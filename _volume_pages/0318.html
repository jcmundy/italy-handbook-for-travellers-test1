{% raw %}Piazza dei Signori. VERONA. 35. Route. 201{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supported by four columns, in the centre of the Piazza, was an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciently used as a seat of judgment. The Casa dei Mercanti (1301),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the corner of the Via Pelliciai, has been recently restored and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now contains the commercial court.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the Casa Mazzanti rises the Tower of the Municipio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 320 ft. in height. A short street to the left of the latter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the *Piazza dei Signori (PI. E, F, 3), a small square paved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with flag-stones, and surrounded by imposing edifices. Immediately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right of the tower is the Palazzo della Ragione (seat of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}assize-courts), founded in 1183, and lately restored and extended.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The interesting court contains some relics of the old market and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase of the 14th century. Adjacent is the Tribunal, and on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other side of the piazza is the Prefettura, both erected by the Sca-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ligers. The original architecture is seen to best advantage in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}courts, which have been restored. The portals are by Sammicheli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the N.E. corner of the piazza stands the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Palazzo del Consiglio, or Old Town Hall, usually called La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Loggia, erected before 1500, probably from designs by Fra Giocondo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 200) , and restored in 1873. It is considered one of the finest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of the early-Renaissance architecture of N. Italy, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinguished rather for richness and beauty of detail than for strict{% endraw %}
{% raw %}harmony of composition. Beside the portal are two bronze statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Campana, representing the Annunciation. Over the door is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscription, placed here at the instance of the Venetians: 'Pro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summa fide summis amor 1592'. Above are five statues of celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}natives of ancient Verona: Cornelius Nepos, Catullus Vitruvius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the yonnger Pliny, and iEmilius Macer, the poet and friend of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Virgil. In the interior of the loggia are busts of celebrated Veronese{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of mediaeval and modem times. On the upper floor are several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apartments which have been tastefully restored (porter in the court).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The entrances to the Piazza dei Signori are spanned by arch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ways. Above the arch in the N.W. corner is a Statue of Scipione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maffei, the historian. Near the same arch are a picturesque Foun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain of the 15th cent, and the Volto Barbaro, under which Mastino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Scala was assassinated in 1277.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the middle of the piazza rises a marble Statue of Dante,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Zannoni, erected in 1865; the poet, as recorded by the inscrip¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions on the monument and on the palace adjoining the Loggia at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right angle, found an asylum here with the Scaligers after his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banishment from Florence in 1316. — Opposite is the old Pal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de' Giureconsulti, erected in 1263, but altered in the 16th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The passage adjoining the Tribunal leads direct to the Lombardic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Maria Antica, with a Romanesque campanile, and to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the imposing Gothic Tombs of the Scaligers, or della Scala family,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who for upwards of a century were presidents of the republic of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verona (see p. 200). The ladder, which forms their crest, recurs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently on the elaborately-executed railings.{% endraw %}

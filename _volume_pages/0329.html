{% raw %}Part of the castle is now used as Archives (open during office-hours{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only), and part of it was a prison during the Austrian supremacy. Most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the frescoes by Andrea Mantegna (1474), which once adorned the rooms,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are now obliterated. The only ones which have been preserved and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored are those on two walls of the Camera degli Sposi (first floor),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing the "Family of the Gonzagas with their courtiers: on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, Lodovico Gonzaga with his wife Barbara of Hohenzollern; on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, Lodovico meeting his son Cardinal Francesco at Rome. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceiling is an illusive painting, consisting of an apparent opening, at which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cupids and girls are listening.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Andrea (PL a; C, D, 3, 4), in the Piazza delle Erbe, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of very imposing proportions, the finest in Mantua, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1472 from designs by the Florentine Leon Battista{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alberti, but the dome was not added till 1782. The white marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade, with its spacious portico, resembles that of an ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple ; adjoining it is a square tower, built of red brick, and sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mounted by an elegant octagonal superstructure with a Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spire. The summit affords a good survey.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, 110 yds. in length, is covered with massive barrel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting, the panels of which are partly painted. 1st Chapel on the right:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arrivabene, St. Antony admonishing the tyrant Ezzelino (painted in 1844).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the sides are frescoes representing Hell, Purgatory, and Paradise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}according to Dante. — 3rd, Cappella S. Longino: on the left, Sarcophagus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the inscription: 'Longini ejus, qui latus Christi percussit, ossa'. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right is the sarcophagus of Gregorius of Nazianzus. The frescoes, designed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Giulio Romano, represent the Crucifixion; below is Longinus; on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite side the finding of the sacred blood. The saint is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought hither some drops of the blood of Christ. — The Right Transept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains the monument of Bishop Andreasi (d. 1549), executed in 1551 by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Clementi, a pupil of Michael Angelo. The swan is the heraldic emblem in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the armorial bearings of Mantua. — Choir , Martyrdom of St. Andrew, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fresco by Anselmi, a pupil of Paolo Veronese. In the corner to the left by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the high-altar is the marble figure of Duke Guglielmo Gonzaga, founder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the church, in a kneeling posture. The Burial Chapel, beneath the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high-altar, where the drops of the sacred blood were preserved, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a marble crucifix and an interesting statue of the Madonna and Child,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carved in wood. — Left Transept. Chapel on the left: Monument of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro Strozzi, with caryatides, designed by Giulio Romano (best seen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the middle of the nave). Another monument, with the recumbent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure of a Count Andreasi, was also designed by G. Romano. — The first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small chapel to the left of the W. portal contains the tomb of the painter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andrea Mantegna (d. 1506), with his *Bust in bronze. — The walls are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with frescoes of different periods.{% endraw %}

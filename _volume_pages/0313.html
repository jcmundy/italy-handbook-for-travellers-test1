{% raw %}VENETIA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Veneti, with whose language and nationality we are unacquaint¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed , kept entirely aloof from the immigrating Celtic tribes. The seaports{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Hatria and Spina, at the mouths of the Po, carried on a considerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trade at an early period, and several canals on a large scale were con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed as early as B.C. 380. In the 3rd cent, the Veneti together with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Cenomani, a Celtic tribe which occupied Brescia and Verona, entered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into an alliance with Rome. While the Romanisation of Lombardy and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piedmont was attended with violent struggles, it was rapidly effected here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without opposition. The Roman colony of Aquileia was founded as early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as 181 B. C., and the boundary of Italy was thus laid down at the point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to which it still extends. Owing to its industries, cattle-breeding, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}agriculture, Venetia prospered greatly under the emperors. Padua was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wealthiest town in Italy next to Rome, and was rivalled in W. Eu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rope by Cadiz alone, as it numbered during the reign of Augustus no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fewer than 500 citizens of knightly fortune (i. e. upwards of about 45002).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The city was afterwards destroyed by Attila, and then razed to the ground{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Lombards, and a similar fate befel Altinum, an important com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mercial town in the Lagoons, and Aquileia, which in ancient times was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a similar importance as the modern Trieste. The Romans sought re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fuge from their Lombard conquerors in the islands of the Lagoons. Re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moved from Teutonic influences, and under the protection of the Byzan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine Empire, the most famous of mediaeval states took its rise here from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apparently insignificant beginnings. Its earliest history is involved in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obscurity. The first Dux or Doge is said to have been Paulucius Anafeslus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 716). In 809 the islands warded off an attack of King Pepin, the son{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Charlemagne, and virtually threw off the yoke of the Eastern emper¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ors. At this period the inhabitants were crowded together in the is¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lands of Rivoalto, Malamocco, and Tor cello, which were the most secure.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rivoalto was selected as the seat of government, and here accordingly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}city of Venice was founded. Angelus Participotius (819) is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been the first doge whose residence occupied the site of the present Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lace of the Doges. Situated between the Byzantine and Franconian em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pires, Venice became a connecting link between the trade of both, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great depot of the traffic between the East and the West. In 828 a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venetian fleet brought the body of St. Mark to Venice, and thenceforth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Venetians revered him as their tutelary saint, using his emblem, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lion (Rev. iv. 7) as their cognizance , and his name as synonymous with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the republic, while their supreme official functionary was styled 'Procu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rator of St. Mark1. In the interests of her commerce Venice was at length{% endraw %}
{% raw %}induced to make foreign conquests. These were at first confined to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Istrian and Dalmatian coasts for the purpose of procuring timber and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suppressing piracy. The rivalry that sprang up with Genoa during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crusade led the Venetians to obtain a footing in the Levant, and to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}establish extensive colonies. At the same time the constitution of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}state developed into a rigorous oligarchy, which with terrible impartial¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ity contrived to keep both the nobility and people in check, and effectu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally to curb the national desire for liberty. In the neighbouring towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the supreme power rested on a foundation altogether different. The re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}publics had been overthrown by the despots, who, supported by merce¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nary troops and the favour of the lower classes, had founded principali¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ties in the modern sense of the word. Such were the Visconti in Milan,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Scala in Verona, the Carrara in Padua, the Gonzaga in Mantua, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Este in Ferrara. The danger of collision with warlike princes, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the support they afforded to every attempt to overthrow the Venetian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constitution, led to their own downfall. Venice, having made conquests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the mainland (terra ferma) for the sake of her own safety, soon be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}came one of the chief Italian powers, and was thus involved in all the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interminable wars caused by the rivalry of the different states. She ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained permanent possession of Treviso in 1339, Vicenza in 1404, Padua{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Verona in 1405, Udine in 1420, Brescia in 1426, Bergamo in 1428,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crema in 1454, and Rovigo in 1484. In the market-places of these towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lion of St. Mark was erected as a token of their subjugation, and Ve¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}262 Route 40. VENICE. 8. Zaccaria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Canareggio, which diverges from the Canal Grande at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Geremia, rises, to the left, the Pal. Labia (PL 73), of the 17th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent., with frescoes by Tiepolo. Farther on, also to the left, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pal. Manfrin (PL 75), containing a picture-gallery, the best works{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which were sold in 1856. It still contains about 200 pictures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some of them valuable, in seven rooms :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3. Lorenzo Lotto, Madonna and Child with two saints, and the donor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between them; 28. Bernardo da Milano (?), Madonna enthroned; 25. Boni¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facio, Allegory; 51. Girolamo da Santa Croce, Adoration of the Magi; 79.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Good old copy of Titian's Entombment in the Louvre; 160. Raphael (?),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Noah entering the ark. All for sale (admission daily 10-3, 72 fr-)-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite, on the right side of the canal, diverges the Ghetto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vecchio (PL D, 1). Following the Canareggio farther, we pass the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pal. Savornian on the left, and reach the church of S. Giobbe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL C, 1), an early-Renaissance structure with a fine portal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is embellished with fine stone-carving, particularly in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first chapel on the left, constructed by Pietro Grimani (d. 1553), above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first and second altars, and in the choir, which was adorned in 1462{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Doge Moro, who is buried here. Above the fourth altar to the left, SS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peter, Andrew, and Nicholas, by Paris Bordone. The sacristy contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three early-Venetian paintings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the church is the entrance to the Botanical Garden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL C, 2), famed for its gigantic cacti.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Pal. Correr (PL 57; D, 2) a side-canal leads to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Giacomo dell' Orio (P1.D,2, 3), the interior of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is Gothic in style, with a timber-roof.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the entrance-wall, to the right, Giov. Buonconsigli, St. Sebastian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and two other saints. The right aisle contains a vestibule adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a column of verde antico, above which runs a richly-gilded frieze; Franc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bassano, John the Baptist. The wall to the left, above the sacristy-door,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is embellished with frescoes by Paolo Veronese. In the chapel farther to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left; "Lorenzo Lotto, Madonna with saints; opposite, a pulpit in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of a drinking-glass.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity is the Fondaco de' Turchi, see p. 260.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the following description of the churches and other sights at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venice the Piazza of St. Mark is taken as a starting-point (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also p. 237).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Skirting the N. side of the church of St. Mark, proceeding to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. of the small piazza in which rises the monument of Manin (p.240),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and passing the Pal. Patriarcale on the right, we observe opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}us the Pal. Trevisani, or Bianca Capello, built in the style of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lombardi about 1500. We cross the bridge (fine view of the back{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the palace of the doges and of the Bridge of Sighs), and traverse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two small piazzas to the Campo and the church of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Zaccaria (PL 36; G, 4), erected by Martino Lombardo in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1457-1515 in the round-arch style, supported by six Corinthian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, and possessing a remarkable and somewhat discordant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade. The recess of the high-altar is in the Gothic style. Over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance the statue of St. Zacharias by Aless. Vittoria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The walls of the Nave are covered with large pictures, all of them,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}except those over the altars, representing memorable events in the history{% endraw %}

{% raw %}440 Route 53. FLORENCE. Nat. History Museum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the W. of the Abbondanza we reach an open grass-plot, also afford¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing a fine view, whence the visitor about to leave the garden may de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scend direct. We follow a beautiful avenue to the S., adorned with nu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merous statues (copies of old works, as well as modern originals), and,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passing the gardener's house, reach a charming Basin (la vasca dell' iso-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lotta), enlivened by swans and other water-fowl. In the centre, on an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island planted with flowers, rises a fountain surmounted by a colossal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statue of Oceanus , by Giovanni da Bologna. The surrounding walks are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly embellished with 'genre' works. A path leads from this basin in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a straight direction to a grass-plot with two columns of granite, and thence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Porta Romana, which however is usually closed; in the vicinity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several ancient sarcophagi. To the right of the Oceanus basin a broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}path, parallel with the palace, is reached, by which the principal en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trance may be regained. Another exit, near a fountain with Bacchus on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lion, leads into the Via Romana. — To the left is the Botanic Gar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}den, which, on public days, the custodian invites visitors to inspect (fee);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a permesso for other days is obtained at, the Museo di Storia Naturale.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above the Boboli garden is the Fortezza di Belvedere, constructed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1590 by Buontalenti by command of Ferdinand I. Near it is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}closed Porta S. Giorgio. Beyond the gate, which may be reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Viale dei Colli, is the little church of S. Leonardo in Ar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cetri (i.e. arce veteri, PL D, 8), the chancel of which contains note¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worthy reliefs of the 12th cent., from the church of S. Piero Scher-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aggio (beside the Pal. Vecchio). The Via Costa S. Giorgio, in which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(No. 13) Galileo's House (PL E,7), debouches at the Porta S. Giorgio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At Via Romana 19, to the W. of the Pitti Palace, is the Mu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seum of the Natural Sciences {Museo di Fisica e di Storia Natu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rale ; PL C, 6; open Tues., Thurs., Sat., 10-4; to strangers daily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by permission of the secretary), founded by Leopold I., and greatly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}augmented at subsequent periods.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The public museum is on the Second Floor; the mineralogical, geo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logical, and palasontological collections occupy 9, the zoological 13 rooms.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There is also an admirable anatomical collection in 12 rooms, consisting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly of preparations in wax, by Clemente Susini and his successors Ca-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lenzuoli and Calamai.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the First Floor (r.) is situated the "Tribuna of Galileo, inaugu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rated in 1840, on the occasion of the assembly at Florence of the principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scholars of Italy, constructed by Giuseppe Martelli, and adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings by Giuseppe Bezzuoli, Luigi Sabatelli, etc., illustrating the history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Galileo, Volta, and other naturalists; also a statue of Galileo by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Costoli, numerous busts of celebrated men, and mosaics in the pavement,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Sabatelli, and executed by Giov. Batt. Silvestri. Along the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls are six cabinets containing instruments from the time of Gali¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leo downwards. There is also an Observatory here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite stands the church of S. Felice (PL C, 6), with a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}porch, rebuilt in the 15th cent., by a disciple of Michelozzo: 1st{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar to the left, School of Botticelli, Four Saints; 2nd altar to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, Fra Paolino, Relief in clay; above, opposite the high-altar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a *Crucifix by Giotto.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately adjoining the Ponte alle Grazie (recently widened;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}PI. E, F, 6, 7) is the Piazza de' Mozzi, to the right in which rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Torrigiani (PL E, 7), containing a valuable picture-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gallery on the first floor (open on Thurs. 10-4; written catalogues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the use of visitors).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}He died at Careggi on Ap. 8th, 1492, at the age of 43 years, an absolute{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prince in all but the name.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Scarcely nine months after his death the Florentine love of liberty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expelled his feeble son Piero, with his brothers Giovanni and Giuliano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1494 Charles VIII. of France occupied Florence on his campaign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against Naples. On the king's departure Hieronymus Savonarola, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated prior of S. Marco, founded his theocratic republic at Florence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but his career was terminated in 1498 by his death at the stake. The re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public maintained its freedom under the Gonfaloniere Pietro Soderini till{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1512, but in that year the party of the Medici regained the upper hand{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and recalled the brothers Giuliano and Giovanni. The former soon resigned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his authority, the latter became pope, and they were followed by Lorenzo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}son of Pietro II. and afterwards Duke of Urbino (d. 1519), Giulio, the son{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Giuliano who was murdered in 1478 (elected pope in 1523), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alessandro, a natural son of the last-named Lorenzo. The family was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again banished in 1527, but Emp. Charles V., who had married his nat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ural daughter to Alessandro, attacked the town and took it in 1530 after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a siege of eleven months, during which Michael Angelo, as engineer on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the side of the republic, and the brave partisan Ferruccio greatly distin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}guished themselves. The emperor then appointed Alessandro hereditary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sovereign of Florence. The assassination of the latter, perpetrated by his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}own cousin Lorenzo, 7th Jan., 1537, did not conduce to the re-establishment{% endraw %}

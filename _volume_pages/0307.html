{% raw %}1 92 Route. 33. BRESCIA. Museo Patrio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now follow the Corso Garibaldi, whence the first cross-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street on the right leads to the Torre della Palata, a mediaeval{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tower with a modern spire. To the N. stands the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Giovanni Evangelista (PI. 11), with several admirable pictures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We begin on the right. 3rd Altar: '"Moretto, Massacre of the Inno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cents, a youthful work, conceived in the spirit of Raphael. High-Altar:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}""Moretto, John the Baptist, Zacharias, St. Augustine, and St. Agnes; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre the Madonna; above, God the Father and a prophet, unfor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunately damaged by retouching. — At the next Altar: School of Bellini,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pieta; the frescoes on the right are by Moretto (youthful works of 1521,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}showing the influence of Romanino): Collecting the manna, Elijah, the Last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Supper, Two Evangelists, and prophets above; those on the left are by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romanino: Raising of Lazarus, Mary Magdalene before Christ, the Sacra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment, two Evangelists, and prophets above. At the next altar: Romanino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nuptials of Mary, freely treated. In the Battistero: "Francesco Francia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Trinity adored by saints.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little farther to the N. lies the church of S. Maria del Car¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mine (PI. 4; C, 2), with a Renaissance portal and tasteful brick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ornamentation on the facade. The lunette is filled with a fresco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Ferramola, and the third chapel on the right contains a ceil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing-painting by Foppa, representing the four Fathers of the Church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The buildings to the left of the church enclose two fine courts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Proceeding to the E. from the Piazza Vecchia, and straight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}past the N. side of the Broletto, we come to a small piazza, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left in which is the entrance to the *Museo Patrio (PI. 17;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, 3 ; shown daily, 10-3, in summer 10-4, on payment of a fee of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 c.; open to the public free on the first Sunday in each month and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on each Sun. and Thurs. in August; visitors knock at the door),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}established in a Corinthian temple of Hercules (?), which was ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cavated in 1822. The temple, which, according to inscriptions, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Vespasian in A.D. 72 (Tempio di Vespasiano), stands on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a lofty substructure with a projecting colonnade of ten columns and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}four pillars to which the steps ascend. The substructions, portions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the steps, and the bases and parts of the shafts of the columns,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in white marble, are still well preserved. The Cella consists of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three sections, each of which was dedicated to a different god (per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}haps Jupiter, Juno, and Minerva).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The pavement of the Principal Hall has been restored with the aid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the original remains. An ancient mosaic has also been placed here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the walls are altars and Roman inscriptions from the province. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room on the right contains mediaeval and other curiosities, ornaments,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the monument of Count Pitigliano, weapons, medals (those of the Napo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leonic period very numerous). In the Central Room and the Room on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left are ancient sculptures, including some interesting marble busts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a relief of a naval battle; the most valuable of all, however, is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine statue of "Victory, excavated in 1826, a bronze figure about 6 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height, with a silver-plated wreath of laurel round her head, a (restored){% endraw %}
{% raw %}shield, on which she is about to write, in her left hand, and a (restored){% endraw %}
{% raw %}helmet under her left foot. This is one of the most admirable specimens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the ancient plastic art now in existence. Also a number of coins and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}medals, ornaments, busts in gilded bronze, fragments of a colossal figure{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a temple, portions of sarcophagi, decorated breastplate of a horse, etc.{% endraw %}

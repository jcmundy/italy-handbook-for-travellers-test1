{% raw %}to Pisa. CARRARA. 19. Route. 123{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Sarzana and the next stat. Avenza are the ruins of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luna, situated on the coast. This old Etruscan town fell to decay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the Roman emperors, and was destroyed by the Arabs in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1016; its episcopal see was transferred to Sarzana in 1465. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}site of the ancient tcwn is still marked by the ruins of an amphi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theatre and circus. From the town of Luna the district derives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its name of La Lunigiana.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7472 M. Avenza is a small town on the brook of that name,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above which rises an old castle of Castruccio Castracani , of 1322,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with bold round towers and pinnacles. On the coast to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a small harbour for the shipment of the Carrara marble.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Branch Railway from Avenza, 12 min. (3 M.; fares 60, 40, 30 c) to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carrara (Locanda Nazionale, with the Trattoria del Giardinetto, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal street on the right; travellers are cautioned against spending the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night here, as the mosquitoes are insufferable). The town of Carrara con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains the studios of numerous sculptors, some of which should be visited.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Most of the inhabitants obtain their livelihood by working the marble.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The following churches should also be inspected: S. Andrea, in a half{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Germanic style of the 13th cent., like the cathedral of Monza, with inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting facade and good sculptures; Madonna delle Grazie, with sumptuous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorations in marble. The Accademia delle Belle Arti contains many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copies from antiques, as well as works by sculptors of Carrara and.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several Roman antiquities found in the mines of Fantiscritti (see below),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}e. g. a *Basrelief of Jupiter with Bacchus. The piazza in front of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Academy is embellished with a statue of Pellegrino Rossi of Carrara, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}papal minister, murdered at Rome in 1848.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A visit to the celebrated and interesting quarries requires 3 hrs. at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}least (somewhat fatiguing). Guides demand 5 fr., but for a mere super¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficial survey their services may be dispensed with. Leaving the station,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we [turn to the right and follow the street in a straight direction, past{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the theatre, to the Piazza, which is adorned with a statue of the grand-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duchess Maria Beatrice, over life-size, erected in 1861. The bridge to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left at the end of the piazza should then be crossed, and the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with deep ruts, ascending on the right bank of the Torano, followed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At (1/4 M.) a group of houses a path diverges to the right to extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarries of an inferior kind of marble, but we continue to follow the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road, passing numerous marble cutting and polishing works. Beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the village of Torano, round which the road leads, the first mines,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recognisable by broad heaps of rubbish, are situated on both sides of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley. The blocks are detached, drawn out by oxen, and rolled down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill. The finer description is called marmo statuario. About 400{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mines with 6000 workmen are at present in operation. The working{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hours are from 5 a. m. to 2 or 3 p. m.; the forenoon is therefore the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best time for a visit (a supply of copper coins is desirable). A horn is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blown as a signal when the rock is about to be blasted. The mines of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Crestola and M. Sagro yield the best and largest blocks. The mines{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Fantiscritti, 3 M. from Carrara, were worked by the ancient Romans.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7872 M. Massa (Albergo Giappone), formerly the capital of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Duchy of Massa-Carrara, which was united with Modena in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1829, with 4,800 inhab., is pleasantly situated amidst mountains,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and enjoys a mild climate. The Palace was once occupied by Na¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poleon's sister Elisa Bacciocchi when duchess. The marble-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarries here are very valuable, rivalling those of Carrara.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Country fertile and well cultivated. The picturesque ruins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the castle of Montignoso become visible on an abrupt height{% endraw %}

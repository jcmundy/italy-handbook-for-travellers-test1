{% raw %}but still adhered to its ancient privileges, as was especially evident in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wars of the Ligue, against Henry IV. In 1660 Louis XIV. divested the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town of its privileges, so that it retained its importance as a sea-port only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1720 and 1721 it was devastated by a fearful pestilence. During the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}revolution it remained unshaken in its allegiance to royalty and was there-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}.fore severely punished. In 1792 hordes of galley-slaves were sent hence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Paris, where they committed frightful excesses. It was for them that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rouget de VIsle, an officer of engineers, composed the celebrated Marseil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}laise, 'Allons, enfants de la patrie', which subsequently became the battle-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hymn of the republican armies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The docks and quays (comp. plan) are very extensive. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}harbour has been quadrupled in size since 1850, notwithstanding{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which there is still a demand for increased accommodation. In 1853{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Port de la Joliette was added to the Ancien Port, and is now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the starting-point of most of the steamboats. The Bassin des Docks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and d'Arenc were added next, in 1856 the Bassin Napoleon (now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}National), and in 1872 the Bassins de Radoub. Other extensions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are projected. — The old harbour is long and narrow. Its entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is defended by the forts of St. Jean and St. Nicolas. Near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}former is the Consigne (PI. 6; D, 5; adm. 50 c), or office of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Intendance Sanitaire' (quarantine authorities).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The principal hall contains several good pictures: Horace Vernet, The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cholera on board the frigate Melpomene; David, St. Rochus praying to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Virgin for the plague-stricken, painted in Rome, 1780; Puget, The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plague at Milan, a relief in marble; Girard, Bishop Belsunce during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plague of 1(20 (see below); Tanneurs, The frigate Justine returning from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the East with the plague on board; Guirin, The Chevalier Rose directing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sepulture of those who have died of the plague.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A few paces farther N. is the Cathedral (PI. C, D, 5), a new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifice constructed of alternate courses of black and white stone, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a mixed Byzantine and Romanesque style, from the designs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vaudoyer and Esperandieu. The towers are surmounted by domes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The terrace commands a pleasant survey of the Bassin de la Joli¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ette. — To the E., in front of the old harbour, is the former 'Resi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dence Imperiale', now Chateau du Pharo, a hospital (PI. E, 5). In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vicinity are the sea-baths (p. 18).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*La Cannebiere (PI. D, 3), a broad and very handsome street,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intersects the town from W. to E. , from the extremity of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ancien Port to the centre of the town where the ground rises. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this street, a few paces from the harbour, stands the Bourse (PI. 5),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a portico of Corinthian columns, erected 1854-60.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A short distance further the Cours (PL C, D, 3) is reached on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, a shady promenade generally thronged with foot-pass¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}engers, at the S. end of which stands the statue of Bishop Belsunce,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who during the appalling plague in 1720, which cairied off 40,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}persons, alone maintained his post and faithfully perfoimed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}solemn duties of his calling. — Fiom this point the Rue d'Aix{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends to the Arc de Triomphe (PI. 1 ; C, 3), erected in 1823, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards adorned with representations of Napoleonic batties in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief by Ramey and David d'Angers.{% endraw %}

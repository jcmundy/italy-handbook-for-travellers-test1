{% raw %}The *Baptistery (Battistero; PL 2; F, 3), constructed of Vero¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nese marble, externally octagonal, with three round-arched portals,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and consisting of five stories with colonnades, and a flat roof sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mounted by seven pyramidal turrets and a belfry, was designed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Benedetto Antelami, and erected in 1196-1270. Around nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the whole exterior of the building runs a series of medallions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing various animals of symbolical import. The portals are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with the following scriptural subjects: —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the N. Portal (towards the Piazza), above, is the Nativity of Christ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then the History of John the Baptist; on the door-posts are genealogical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trees of Jacob and of Jesse. — At the W. Portal, above, a curious repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sentation of the Last Judgment. On the door-posts to the left, Christ per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forming works of mercy; on the right, the six ages of man. — On the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side is an Allegory of Death from the biblical romance of Barlaam and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Josaphat. All these are probably by Benedetto Antelami, whose name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appears as the master from the inscription on the portal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior (closed; key in the house opposite the S. entrance) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sixteen-sided, with thirteen niches and three doorways below and two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}galleries above, and graceful columns on the walls. The sculptures have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}only been partly completed. The old frescoes in the dome (13th-14th cent.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}represent the history of John the Baptist and prophets, with a number of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saints below. The whole population of Parma since 1216 is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been baptised here. The font dates from 1294. Altar-piece by Filippo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mazzuola (15th cent.), father of Parmeggianino.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}may be regarded as a symptom of the universal withdrawal of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italians from public life. Artists, too, henceforth occupy an isolated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}position, unchecked by public opinion, exposed to the caprices of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}amateurs, and themselves inclined to an arbitrary deportment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Several qualities, however , still exist of which Italian artists are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}never entirely divested; they retain a certain address in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arrangement of figures, they preserve their reputation as ingenious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorators, and understand the art of occasionally imparting an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ideal impress to their pictures; even down to a late period in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18th century they excel in effects of colour, and by devoting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attention to the province of genre and landscape-painting they may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boast of having extended the sphere of their native art. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same time they cannot conceal the fact that they have lost all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}faith in the ancient ideals, that they are incapable of new and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earnest tasks. They breathe a close, academic atmosphere, they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}no longer labour like their predecessors in an independent and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}healthy sphere, and their productions are therefore devoid of ab¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sorbing and permanent interest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This slight outline of the decline of Italian art brings us to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the close of our brief and imperfect historical sketch, which, be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it again observed, is designed merely to guide the eye of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enlightened traveller, and to aid the uninitiated in independent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discrimination and research.{% endraw %}

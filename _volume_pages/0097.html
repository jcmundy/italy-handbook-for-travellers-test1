{% raw %}42 M. Toulon (*Grand Hotel, near the station; Victoria; Croix{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Or; Louvre; Railway Restaurant), the war-harbour of France for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Mediterranean, with 70,500 inhab., possesses a double harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}protected by eleven forts which crown the surrounding heights. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1707 the town was besieged in vain by Prince Eugene, and in 1793{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the inhabitants surrendered to the English Admiral Hood. In De¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cember of that year it was gallantly defended by a small body of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English soldiers against an enemy of tenfold number, but was at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}last taken by storm. The attack was conducted by Bonaparte, lieu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tenant of artillery, then 24 years of age. Beautiful *View from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill on which stands the fort of La Malgue.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}where our line diverges from the main railway (see below). 13 M. Hyeres-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ville, 1 M. from the station. 18 M. Les Salins d'Hyeres.{% endraw %}

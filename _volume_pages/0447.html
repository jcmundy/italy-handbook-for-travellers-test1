{% raw %}*S. Petronio (PL E, 5), the largest church in the town, begun in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emulation of the cathedral of Florence in the Tuscan-Gothic style{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1390 from a design by Antonio Vincenzi, but never completed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The projected length was upwards of 200 yds., and an imposing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transept and an octagonal dome rising above the centre between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}four towers were to be erected. The work was discontinued in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1659, when the nave and aisles as far as the transept only were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed, and they are now terminated by an apse of the breadth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the nave. Length 128 yds., breadth with the chapels 52 yds.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The nave is of vast dimensions, and the aisles are flanked with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels. The church is supported by twelve pillars, and below the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pointed vaulting are small round-arch windows. The *Sculptures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal entrance are by Jacopo della Querela, 1429; side-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}doors by Niccolb Tribolo, 1525. — Over the principal entrance a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronze statue of Pope Julius II. with the keys and a sword in his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left hand, by Michael Angelo, was placed in 1508, but it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed by the populace three years later, and sold as old metal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Duke of Ferrara, who used it in casting a piece of ordnance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}('Giuliano').{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is adorned with numerous sculptures and pictures. Most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the chapels are enclosed by handsome marble screens, dating from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 14th, 15th, and 16th centuries. 1st Chapel on the right: altar-piece{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(God the Father with angels) by Giacomo Francia; 2nd Chapel (r.) curious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}old frescoes of the year 1417. 4th Chapel: Old stained glass by Jacob of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vim. 8th Chapel: good inlaid stalls by Fra Raffaele da Brescia. 9th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chapel (di S. Antonio): Statue of the saint, an early work of Sansovino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the eight Miracles wrought by him, in grisaille , by Girolamo da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Treviso; fine stained glass from designs by Pellegrino Tibaldi. 11th Chapel:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assumption of Mary, a high-relief, the lower part by Niccolb Tribolo;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the two angels by his pupil Properzia de" Rossi; opposite to it is a Pieta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Vincenzo Onofri. — The sacristy contains pictures of no great value.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the canopy of the Choir , Charles V. was crowned emperor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Pope Clement VII. on 24th Feb., 1530, this being the last occasion on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which an emperor was crowned in Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Reverenda Fabbrica (workshop), at the end of the N. aisle,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains forty sketches of the unfinished facade, of the 15th-17th cent., by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palladio, Giulio Romano, Vignola, etc., an interesting collection; also a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}model of the church in wood, and a relief of Joseph's Temptation by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Properzia de' Rossi (the best time for inspecting it is about noon).{% endraw %}

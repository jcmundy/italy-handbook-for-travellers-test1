{% raw %}Popular Festivals. Saturday before Easter. 'Lo Scoppio del Carro'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a chariot laden with fireworks, is driven to the front of the cathedral'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and its contents ignited by a dove ('La Colombina'), which descends from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the high-altar along a string. The course of the 'dove', which is made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to return to the altar, is watched with great interest by the thousands of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}country people assembled in the piazza, as its regularity or irregularity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is supposed to presage a good or a bad harvest respectively. The remaining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fireworks are let off at the Canto de' Pazzi. — On the eve of the Feast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Epiphany (Befana; Jan. 6th) the 'Street Arabs' perambulate the streets{% endraw %}

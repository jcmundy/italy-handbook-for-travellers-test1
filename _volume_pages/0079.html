{% raw %}VIENNE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Route. 9{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Railway to Marseilles (Gare de Perrache) descends the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Rhone, which flows on our right.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}337 M. Vienne (Hotel du Nord; Hotel de la Poste), the Vienna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Allobrogum of the ancients, was the seat of the old Gaulish families{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was almost constantly at strife with its younger rival Lugudu-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}num. The town, with 26,600 inhab., lies on the left bank of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rhone, at the influx of the Gere. Several interesting mementoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its former greatness are still extant. The finest of these is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Temple, of the Corinthian order (88 ft. long, 49 ft. wide, 56 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high), with 16 columns, and hexastyle portico, dedicated, according{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the remains of an inscription on bronze, Divo Augusto Optimo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maximo et Div^e Augusts. It is approached from the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forum by twelve steps, in the middle of which stands an altar. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple was used in the middle ages as a church, but has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored as nearly as possible to its original condition. — The an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cient abbey-church of St. Pierre, of the 6th cent., altered in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18th and now restored, contains an interesting museum of Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antiquities (inscriptions, architectural fragments, sculptures). In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Hotel-de-Ville is a collection of smaller Roman antiquities. ■—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— The Cathedral of St. Maurice (between the temple and the bridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}across the Rhone), was begun at the close of the 11th cent., but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was not completed till 1515. — The church of Notre - Dame - de-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Valette commands a beautiful view. On the high-road, !/4 M. S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the town, stands an archway surmounted by an obelisk called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the *Plan de VAiguille, which some authorities regard as the meta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(goal) of a circus, while others believe it to be a Roman tombstone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A small part only of Vienne is visible from the railway , which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes under the town by a tunnel. Immediately beyond the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises the Plan de 1'Aiguille, mentioned above. The banks of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rhone rise in gentle slopes, planted with vines and fruit-trees. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right bank, at some distance from the river, towers Mont Pilat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(3750 ft.), a picturesque group of mountains, at the base of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lie the celebrated vineyards of La CoteRotie. —356 M. St. Rambert{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Albon (Rail. Restaurant), whence a branch-line diverges to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grenoble. — 373y2 M. Tain, where the valley of the Rhone con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tracts ; on the left rises the extensive vineyard of Ermitage, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the well-known wine of that name is produced. In the distance to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left the indented spurs of the Alps are conspicuous, above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which in clear weather the gigantic Mont Blanc is visible. Tain is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connected by means of a suspension-bridge with Tournon, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite bank, a small town with picturesque old castles of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Counts of Tournon and Dukes of Soubise.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On our left, in the direction of the Little St. Bernard, now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opens the broad valley of the turbid Isere, which is also traversed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a railway to Grenoble. In September, B.C. 218, Hannibal{% endraw %}

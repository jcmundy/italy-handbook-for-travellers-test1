{% raw %}10(3 Route 53. FLORENCE. S. Croce.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}600th anniversary of the birth of the great poet (b. 1265), 14th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}May, 1865, a white marble statue 19 ft. in height, on a pedestal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}23 ft. high, the corners of which are adorned with four shield-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bearing lions with the names of his four most important works after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Divina Commedia: the Convito, Vita Nuova, De Vulgari Elo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quentia, De Monarchia. Round the pedestal below are the arms of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal cities of Italy. — To the right is the Palazzo dell'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antella (now del Borgo), with a facade decorated with frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which were executed in 1620 within the short space of 27 days by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giovanni da S. Giovanni and other masters. To the W. is the *Pa-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo Serristori, by Baccio d'Agnolo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church of *S. Croce (PI. G, 6), a cruciform edifice borne by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, was begun in 1294, from a design by Arnolfo di Cambio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the Franciscans, who at that time were the popular favourites{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among the monkish bodies. It was completed in 1442, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exception of the facade, which has quite recently been skilfully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed by Niccolo Madas from the old design by Cronaca. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tower has also been well restored. Over the central door is a bas-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief (Raising of Christ), by Dupre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, consisting of nave and aisles 163 yds. in length, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each 9 yds. in width and 65 ft. in height, with a transept 14 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}width, and open roof, rests on 14 octagonal pillars at considerable inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vals, and produces an impressive effect, enhanced by its numerous monu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments of celebrated men. This church may be called the Pantheon of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern Italy, and its interest is greatly increased by the frescoes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giotto and his successors (Taddeo Gaddi, Maso di Banco, Giovanni da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Milano, Agnolo Gaddi, etc., which were discovered within the last twenty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}years under the whitewash (best light in the morning). In 1566 Giorgio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vasari, by order of Cosimo I., made several alterations on the altars,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which however hardly accord with the simple dignity of the interior and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are therefore to be removed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall. Over the central door are a window filled with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stained glass from drawings by Lorenzo Ghiberti, and a bronze statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Louis by Donatello.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right Aisle. At the entrance, small honorary monument to Daniele{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Manin, with portrait-medallion (his tomb at Venice, p. 240). — On the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther on, *Tomb of Michael Angelo, whose remains repose below it (d.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Rome, 1564), erected in 1570, the bust by Battista Lorenzi, the fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure of Architecture by Giovanni delV Opera, the painting and sculptures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Lorenzi and Valerio Cioli. — On the pillar opposite, the 'Madonna del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Latte', by Rossellino, above the tombstone of Francesco Neri. — Monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Dante (interred at Ravenna, p. 327), with the inscription 'Onorate l'altis-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}simo poeta!', by Stefano Ricci, erected in 1829. — Alfieri (d. 1810), by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Canova (erected by his friend the Countess of Albany). — *Marble pulpit,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the pillar to the left by Benedetto da Majano, described as 'the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful pulpit in Italy'. The five reliefs represent the Confirmation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Franciscan Order, the Burning of the books, the 'Stigmata', the Death{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Francis, and Execution of brothers of the Order; below are sta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tuettes of Faith, Hope, Charity, Fortitude, and Justice. — Macchiavelli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1527). by Innocenzo Spinazzi, erected in 1787, with inscription, 'Tanto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nomini nullum par elogium'. — The learned Lanzi (d. 1810). — Benedetto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de' Cavalcanti; above it, is a fresco by Domenico Veneziano, representing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}John the Baptist and St. Francis. — Adjacent is an Annunciation, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early relief by Donatello. — 'Monument of Leonardo Bruni (d. 1444){% endraw %}
{% raw %}surnamed Ardino from his birth-place, by Bernardo Rossellino; above it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Madonna, basrelief by Andr. del Verrocchio (V). — The naturalist{% endraw %}

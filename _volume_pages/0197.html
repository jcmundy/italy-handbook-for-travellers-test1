{% raw %}Climate. Mentone is protected on the N. by a girdle of rocky moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains , and is considered one of the most favourable spots for a winter-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence on the Riviera. The E. bay in particular is thoroughly shelter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed, and has a mean temperature in winter of 50° Fahr. A cool and re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}freshing breeze, however, generally springs up about noon, and the cold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Brise' is also an occasional visitor. Between Nov. 1st and the end ot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}April the average number of rainv davs is 40, while snow scarcely ever{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falls. Fogs are unknown, but heavy dews are frequent. The W. bay is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cooler than the E. bay, but has a greater choice of houses at a distance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the sea, and also affords pleasanter walks. About 2000 English,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}American, and German families regularly spend the winter in Mentone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mentone, a small town with 5600 inhab., formerly belonging{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the principality of Monaco, and afterwards under the Sardinian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supremacy, was annexed to France in 1860. It is charmingly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated on the Bay of Mentone, which is divided into the Baie de{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I'Est and the Baie de I'Ouest by a rocky promontory. Several small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brooks empty themselves into the W. bay. The vegetation is luxu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}riant, consisting chiefly of orange and lemon groves interspersed with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gnarled carob-trees (ceratoria siliqua), figs, olives, etc. The Pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}menade du Midi and the Jardin Public are favourite walks in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afternoon. The ruined castle on the above-mentioned rocky pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}montory, which has been converted into a burial-ground, affords a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine view, embracing S. Agnese on a lofty hill, erected for defence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against the Saracens. Another picturesque point is the monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of *S. Annunziata, to which a tolerable path ascends (in !/2 hr.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Turin road (to the left immediately beyond the railway).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pleasant and sheltered walks may also be taken to the Vallee de Gor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bio (practicable for driving), Vallee Cabrole, and Vallee de Menton,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and to the Cap Martin, which bounds the Bay of Mentone on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W.; another to Grimaldi (p. 107), to the E., immediately beyond{% endraw %}

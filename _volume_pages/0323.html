{% raw %}206 Route 35. VERONA. S. Zeno Maggiore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Portal, the columns of which rest on lions of red marble, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embellished with rude but interesting marble reliefs of scriptural subjects{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed by Nicolaus and Wiligelmus in 1139. In one of them Theodoric,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'degnissimo re d'ltalia', is represented as a wild huntsman speeding head¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long to the devil. At the top of the lintels are reliefs of the months.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The doors are covered with rude reliefs in bronze.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is borne by alternate pillars and columns. In the corner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right is an ancient octagonal font, behind which is a fresco of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zeno, dating from the 14th century. The holy-water basin, by the 1st{% endraw %}
{% raw %}column to the right, rests upon the inverted capital of an antique column.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite is a large ancient vase of porphyry, 28 ft. in circumference, be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yond which is a fine Gothic crucifix. — On the choir-screen are statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Christ and the 12 Apostles, of the 13th cent., in marble, some of them paint¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed. — The walls to the left of the choir are covered with frescoes of the 16th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent., behind which are traces of others of the 12th; to the right are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes of the 11th and 13th centuries. To the right of the steps to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir is an altar, on each side of which are four columns of brown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble, resting on lions and bulls, each in one block. — To the right in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Choir, above the crypt, is the very ancient painted marble figure of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Zeno, Bishop of Verona and patron-saint of fishermen (about 9th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent.), holding his episcopal staff, from which is suspended a silver fish.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Behind the high-altar is a fine "Picture (covered) by Mantegna (1460),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in excellent preservation, but unfortunately hung too high. On a throne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of stone in the middle of a colonnade sits the Madonna with the Infant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christ, with angels playing on instruments at her side and on the steps.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the left wing are SS. Peter, Paul, John, and Augustine; in the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wing are SS. John the Baptist, Gregory, Lawrence, and Benedict. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}striking effect of this great work is enhanced by remarkably rich accesso¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ries. (The three lower pictures are copies.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}The approach to the spacious Crypt, in accordance with the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plan which has been followed in the restoration of the building, occupies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entire width of the church. It contains the tomb of St. Zeno and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient sculptures and frescoes ; the capitals of the 40 columns are mediaeval,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some of them bearing the name of the sculptor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A door in the N. aisle leads to the admirably-preserved ''Cloisters,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with elegant double columns and a projecting structure, restored (accord¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing to an old inscription) as early as 1123. Immediately to the right two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tombstones are recognised as pertaining to the Scaliger family by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ladder represented on them. — On the S. side of the church is a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disused Churchyard, whence a general view of the church with its cam¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}panile of 1045 (restored in 1120) is best obtained. At the entrance to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disused Mausoleum, with a sarcophagus and two columns (descent by 12{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steps), a stone bears the inscription, '■Pipini Italiae regis, Magni Caroli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imperatoris filii piissimi sepulcrum'. Adjacent is a very large Roman sarco¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phagus.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We next visit the S. E. Quarters of the town. To the S. E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Piazza delle Erbe (p. 200) runs the Via S. Sebastiano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL E, 3, 4), in which a hat over the gateway of a court opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Sambonifazi is said to indicate the house of Juliet's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parents (Capuletti; p. 208). — Farther on, close to the church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Sebastiano (PL F, 4) is the Biblioteca Comunale (open in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter 9-3 and 6-9, summer 9-4), founded in 1860, which contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous documents from the suppressed monasteries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via Ponte Navi, the S. prolongation of the Via S. Sebastiano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the Ponte delle Navi. In this street, on the left, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner of the Corticella Leoni, and built into the side of a house{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Cemetery. TURIN 9. Route. 73{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cemetery {Campo Santo, open 12-4 o'cl. in winter in fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}weather ; in March and April 1 - 5 ; in summer 3-8 ; in Sept. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oct. 2-4 only; single cab fare), l1/^ M. N.E. of Turin, and reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Ponte delle Benne by a shady avenue (the road to Chi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vasso, see p. 77), deserves a visit. The front part is enclosed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a wall with arches, while the more interesting portion beyond is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surrounded by arcades covered with small domes. To the left by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wall in the first section is the tomb of Silvio Pellico (d. 1854);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the other section we observe the names of many celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern Italians, such as d'Azeglio, Bavo, Brofferio, Gioberti, Pepe,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Pinelli. A separate space on the N. side is reserved for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interment of non-Romanists.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Superga, or Soperga (2555 ft.), the royal burial-church, a hand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some edifice with a colonnade in front, and surmounted by a dome, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spicuously situated on a hill to the E. of Turin, is well worthy of a visit,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and commands a splendid view (comp. the Map, p. 61). The building was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun in 1718, from designs by Juvara, and was completed in 1731 (closed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12-2,). Adjacent are a seminary for priests and a trattoria. — It was near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Superga that the famous battle of Turin between the Italians and French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was fought, 7th Sept. 1706, in which the latter were signally defeated,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and by which the House of Savoy regained the Duchy, which was created{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a kingdom in the Peace of Utrecht, 1713. It is said that Prince Eugene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reconnoitred the hostile camp from this height before the commencement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the battle, and that, observing symptoms of irresolution in their move¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments, he observed to Duke Amadeus II. '/J me semble, que ces gens-la{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sont a demi battus\ The latter, it is said, on this occasion vowed to erect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a church here in honour of the Virgin, in case of his success in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}battle. An annual thanksgiving still takes place in the church on 8th Sept.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pedestrians require three good hours to reach the Superga. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pleasantest way is to take the tramway as far as the Madonna del Pilone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 3/4 M. below Turin, where donkeys (somarelli, 2- 3 fr.) may be en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gaged for the ascent of the hill. Two-horse carriage from Turin and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, 25 fr. (not recommended, as the road is very rough at places).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Excursion from Turin to the Valleys of the Waldenses ( Valines Vau-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}doises), extending along the French frontier, about 30 M. to the S.W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The well-known and interesting Protestant communities (about 25,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}souls) who have occupied these valleys for 600 years, have steadily ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hered to the faith for which they were formerly so cruelly persecuted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Their language is French. Railway from Turin to Pignerol, Ital. Pine-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rolo (with a monument to Gen. Brignone by Tabacchi), in IV2 hr. (fares{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3fr. 45, 2fr. 60, lfr. 75c); omnibus thence once daily in 1 hr. (fare lfr.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one-horse carr. there and back 7'/2 fr.) to La Tour, Ital. Torre Pellice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly Torre Luserna (L'Ours; Lion d'Or), the chief of these commun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ities , which possesses excellent schools. — From Pignerol a road ascends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the valley of the Clusone by Perosa and Fenestrelle, a strongly fortified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place, to the Mont Genevre and the French fortress of Briangon in the lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Durance. At Cesanne this road unites with that from Susa.{% endraw %}

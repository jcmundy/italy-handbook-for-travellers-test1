{% raw %}At the corner of the Via della Sanita (No. 2) and the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giuseppina* stands the sumptuously furnished Palazzo Revoltella{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. G, 6), built in 1857 from the plans of Hitzig of Berlin, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bequeathed to the town along with its valuable contents of paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and sculptures by Baron Revoltella (adm. daily, 11-2). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal facade of the edifice is turned towards the Piazza Giusbp-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pina , which is embellished with a *Monument to Emperor Maxi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}milian of Mexico (d. 1867), in bronze, designed by Schilling, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1875. The unfortunate prince, who was a rear-admiral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Austrian navy, generally resided at Trieste before he under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}took his ill-starred expedition to Mexico.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Passeggio di S. Andrea, a much frequented avenue 3 M. in length,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirting the coast and commanding a succession of beautiful views, leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the E. side of the town, past the Villa Murat, the Lloyd Arsenal, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Gas-Works, to Servdla. The extensive "Wharves of the Lloyd Co.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite Servola (472 M.), may be visited daily, except holidays, Sundays,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and between 11 and 1 o'clock, by permission obtained at the offices in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Tergesteo (guide 72-1 fl.). — On the road to Zaule, famous for its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oyster-beds, are the handsome Cemeteries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another favourite place of resort is the Boschetto, to the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, which is most easily reached by the tramway traversing the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Corso, Piazza della Legna, and Corsia Stadion, and passing the shady{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giardino Pubblico (PL C, G, 1; fare 10 kr.). In the Boschetto is a large{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brewery. A shady road ascends hence in 30-40 min. to the Villa Ferdi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nandea (Rest. Cacciatore), situated upon a small plateau 750 ft. above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea. Adjacent is the Villa Revoltella, now belonging to the town, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a park and chapel, commanding a charming view of the town and the sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A very pleasant excursion (railway station, see p. 55; boat 3 fl., one-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horse carr. 2fl., two-horse 3 fl.) may be made to the chateau of *Mirainar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly the property of Emp. Maximilian of Mexico (see above), charm¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ingly situated to the N.W. near Grignano, and commanding a fine view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Trieste, the sea, and the coast. The park is open to the public daily.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The sumptuously furnished chateau is shown to visitors (Sundays and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}holidays excepted) on sending their cards to the steward. To the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the entrance to the garden is a small 'Museum1 of Greek and Egyptian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antiquities. — Barcola (restaurant) is a favourite resort, halfway between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trieste and the chateau.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}(imp erial stables), etc. — A very interesting excursion, occupying one day{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(starting early in the morning), is by steamer (p. 55) to Muggia; over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill on foot (beautiful view from the top) to Oltre (1 hr.), thence by boat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(15 kr.) to Capo d'Istria (Citta diTrieste; Radetzky; Caffe in the principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza). The town itself, situated on an island, with 7500 inhab., is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Justinopolis of the Romans, and is connected with the mainland by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone embankment. The chief objects of interest are the Cathedral, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palazzo Pubblico, occupying the site of a temple of Cybele, and the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tensive salt-works. We now proceed by the road on the shore, passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Se/nedella , to (3 M.) Isola (good Refosco wine), and (6 M. farther) Pirano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and return to Trieste by steamer in the evening. — About 2 M. from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pirano lies the sea-bathing place of S. Lorenzo, established in 1864, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome building in an extensive park.{% endraw %}

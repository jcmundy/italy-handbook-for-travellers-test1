{% raw %}The Monti Pisani, a range of hills to the E., are very picturesque;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among them, about 5 M. from the town, in the Valle dei Calci, lies La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Certosa, or the Carthusian Abbey, a fine structure of the year 1367, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church and cloisters, restored in 1814. — To the right above it is La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verruca, a mountain 1765 ft. above the sea-level, crowned with ruins of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a castle of the 15th cent, and commanding a delightful prospect.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 3 M. S.W. of Pisa, on the old post-road to Leghorn, to the right of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the railway, in the direction of the Arno and opposite S. Rossore, is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient basilica of *S. Pietro in Grado, erected before the year 1000,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing beautiful antique columns and capitals, occupying the spot,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}according to tradition, where St. Peter first landed in Italy. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly much frequented as a pilgrimage-church. The faded paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the interior are of the 14th century. The ancient estuary of the Arno,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the harbour of Pisa, must once have been at this spot, before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present coast was formed by alluvial deposits.{% endraw %}

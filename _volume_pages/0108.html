{% raw %}The most interesting part of the railway begins here. Immedi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ately beyond Amsteg the train penetrates a rocky spur of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Windgelle, by means of a tunnel 187 yds. long, and crosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kiirstelenbach, a tributary of the Reuss flowing out of the profound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and narrow Maderaner Thai, of which, as well as of the Great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Windgelle (10,465 ft.) to the left, an excellent view is obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the bridge (150 yds. long, and 175 ft. high). We now pierce{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the slope of the Bristenstock by means of the two Bristenlaui Tun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nels (435 yds. and 232 yds. long respectively), and cross the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foaming Reuss by a lofty lattice-girder bridge (82 yds. long; 255 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above the river). The railway then runs along the left side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesque Reuss valley, sharing the narrow space with the road,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until the latter again crosses to the right bank. After passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through four short tunnels (Intschi, Zgraggen, Breiten and Meitsch-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}linger) we now reach —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 M. Gurtnellen (2525 ft.). Beyond Gurtnellen the train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends the hill ofGoschenen (see below) by means of three curved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunnels and an immense double bend. It crosses the Gorneren-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bach and the Hagrigen-Bach (fine waterfall on the right) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enters the spiral Pfaffensprung Tunnel (1606 yds.), near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pfaffensprung bridge, by which the road re-crosses to the left bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Reuss. On emerging from the upper end of the tunnel,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is about 115 ft. above the lower, the train proceeds through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the short Muhle-Tunnel, again crosses the Hsegrigen-Bach, with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view of the Pfaffensprung bridge below us to the left, and threads the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Miihren-Tunnel (93 yds. ; 2820ft. above the sea-level). Immedi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ately afterwards we cross the deep gorge of the Maienreuss, whic 1{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends from the Maien-Thal, penetrate the hill of Wasen by tho{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kirchberg Tunnel (330 yds.), and after passing to the right bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Reuss, enter the spiral tunnel of Wattingen (1199 yds. long;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}79 ft. of ascent). A part of this tunnel fell in during its con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struction, and on account of the peculiarly fissured nature of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock, it required to be carefully lined with substantial masonry.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond the tunnel, the train again crosses the Reuss, penetrates{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rohrbach Tunnel (240 yds.), and reaches the station of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}55 M. Wasen (2780 ft.), above the village of that name (Hotel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}des Aipes; Ochs), the church of which, through the frequent wind¬{% endraw %}

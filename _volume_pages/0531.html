{% raw %}The 'Madonna del Cardinello', the 'Madonna al Verde' at Vienna,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 'La belle Jardiniere' in the Louvre form a group nearly allied in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point of conception. To the earlier and simpler representations of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna, in which Mary and her Son alone appear, the child John the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baptist has been added. This not only admits of the delineation of ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ditional features of child-life, but also makes possible the construction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a regularly-arranged group. The two children, standing at the feet of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Madonna, form a broad base for the composition, which tapers up¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards easily and naturally to the head of the Virgin. This arrangement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first found expression within the realms of sculpture, whence it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eagerly adopted by the Florentine painters. — Springer.{% endraw %}

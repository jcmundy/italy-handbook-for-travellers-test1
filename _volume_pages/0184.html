{% raw %}Acqua Sola. GENOA. 15. Route. 99{% endraw %}
{% raw %}new and shadeless Passo Nuovo promenade, which, together with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Via Milano (PL C, B, 2), runs above the extensive railway{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magazines (Maggazzini Generali), and commands a fine *View. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In this road, beyond the railway, lies the Palazzo dello Scoglietto,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the property of Sign. Vitale Rosazza, the charming gardens of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also command a fine view (gardener, 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Molo Nuovo and the Lighthouse, see p. 98.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the coast, farther to the W., lies the suburb of S. Pier d'Arena or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sampierdarena (cab with one horse 2, with two horses 2l/t fr.), with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}17,900 inhab. and numerous palaces and gardens, including the Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spinola, and the Palazzo Scassi, formerly Imperiali, with a pleasant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garden, both probably erected by Gal. Alessi. The church of S. Maria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Cella contains frescoes of the Genoese school. There is a large sugar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refinery here. — Railway station, see p. 83; tramway, see p. 87.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A favourite promenade is the small park of Acqua Sola (PL G,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3, 4; 137 ft. above the sea), adorned with a fountain, situated on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an eminence at the N.E. end of the town (approached most con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}veniently from the Piazza delle Fontane Morose by the Salita S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Caterina). The grounds were laid out in their present form on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part of the old ramparts of the town in 1837. During the military{% endraw %}
{% raw %}concerts (p. 87) on Sunday afternoons the grounds are crowded.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pleasant views to the E. and S., finest towards the sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N. of Acqua Sola is the *Villa Negro (PL 46 ; reached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Piazza Mazzini, or from the Via Garibaldi, by the Salita{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delle Battistine), the property of the city, and open to the public,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a well-kept garden, a small museum of Natural History{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(open on Sundays), and the beginnings of a Zoological Garden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Winding promenades ascend hence to a bastion at the back of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}villa, about 150 ft. above Acqua Sola, commanding a fine survey of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the city, the harbour, and environs. — The walk may be pleasantly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extended thus: from Acqua Sola proceed to the S. by Mura S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stefano, then by Mura Sta. Chiara (turn to the left and follow the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town walls), Mura del Prato (to the left, below, is the Manicomio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or lunatic asylum), and then by Mura delle Cappuccine, and Mura{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Strega, to the Piazza della Cava (PL F, 6). From this point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we may proceed either to S. Maria in Carignano (p. 92), or to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Molo Vecchio (p. 89).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Via di Girconvallazione, a magnificent route on the hills{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the back of the town, vies in beauty with the Corso Vittorio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emmanuele at Naples. It begins on the E. at the Piazza Manin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL H, 3 ; 328 ft. above the sea-level), and leads thence along the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slope, across a viaduct and in long windings, under various names{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Corso Solferino , Corso Magenta, Corso Paganini), to the Albergo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dei Poveri (PL E, F, 1,2; 318 ft. above the sea), a hospital founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 17th cent., and last extended in 1835, accommodating 1300{% endraw %}
{% raw %}persons. Thence it descends to the Piazza Annunziata (PL E, 2;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 96) and to the Piazza Acquaverde. — Another fine street in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}course of construction is the Via di Circonvallazione al Mare, lead¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing from the harbour to the mouth (foce) of the Bisagno.{% endraw %}

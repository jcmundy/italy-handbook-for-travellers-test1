{% raw %}The beauty of its situation, and the interesting reminiscences{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of its ancient magnificence, render a visit to Genoa very attractive,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially to the traveller who is visiting Italy for the first time.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the historian of art the Renaissance palaces of the Genoese{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nobility are objects of extreme interest, surpassing in number and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnificence those of any other city in Italy. Many of the smaller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churches are of very ancient origin, though usually altered in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gothic period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the Genoese palaces were erected by Galeazzo Alessi (a pupil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Michael Angelo, born at Perugia 1500, d. 1572), whose example was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally followed by subsequent architects. In spite of occasional de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fects, the architecture of the city is of an imposing and uniform char¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acter, and great ingenuity has been displayed in employing an unfavour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able and limited site to the best advantage. The palaces moreover con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain a considerable number of works of art, while Rubens, who resided at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genoa in 1606-8, and Van Dyck at a later period, have contributed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserve the memory of many members of the noblesse. The native{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school of art, however, never rose to importance, and was far from being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}benefited by the zeal of its artists in painting facades. The chief painters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were Luca Cambiaso (1527-85), Bernardo Strozzi, surnamed II Cappuccino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Prete Genovese (1581-1644), Giov. Batt. Paggi, and Benedetto Castiglione.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Harbour (Porto) consists of a semicircular bay, about 2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in diameter, which is protected from the open sea by two long and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}substantial piers. That on the E. is the Molo Vecchio, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small old lighthouse and the Porta del Molo, erected by Galeazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alessi in 1550; that on the "W. the Molo Nuovo , adjoining which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises the new lighthouse , or Lanterna , with its dazzling reflectors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}410 ft. above the sea-level. The summit, reached by 375 steps,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands a fine view, especially by evening-light (fee 1 fr.), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the arrangements of the interior may also be inspected.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Duke of Galliera (d. 1876) having presented 20 million{% endraw %}
{% raw %}francs for the improvement of the harbour, on condition that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}government and the city would advance the remainder of the re¬{% endraw %}

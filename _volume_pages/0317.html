{% raw %}Verona, founded by the Ehsetians and Euganeans and afterwards occu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pied by the Celtic Cenomani, was made a Roman colony in 89 B.C., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soon became one of the most prosperous towns of Upper Italy. Its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of S. Pietro was a favourite residence of the Ostrogoth Theodoric{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Great, the 'Dietrich of Bern1 (i.e. Verona) of German lore (d. 526). In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}568 the town was taken by the Lombard king Alboin, who fell a victim to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vengeance of his wife Eosamunde, daughter of the conquered ruler{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Verona, whom he had forced to drink wine from her father's skull.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Frankish monarch Pepin ruled here, and, after the Carlovingian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}epoch, Berengarius I. Verona headed the league of Venetian cities against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick Barbarossa. During the fierce contests of the Guelphs and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghibellines the terrible Ezzelino da Romano endeavoured to establish a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lordship at Verona. After his death in 1259 Mastino della Scala was elected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Podesta; and the great princes of his house inaugurated a happier and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brilliant period for the city. Mastino was murdered in 1277, but his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brother and successor Albert secured the supremacy of his line. Romeo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Juliet are said to have loved and died in the reign of Albert's son{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bartolommeo (1301-1304). The most eminent member of this illustrious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family was Can Francesco, called Can Grande (1312-1329), who captured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vicenza and subdued Padua after a long struggle. His brilliant court num¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bered Dante among its members. Mastino II. at first conquered Brescia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parma, and Lucca, but his rule was afterwards restricted to Verona and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vicenza by a league formed by Florence, Venice, and Milan. Can{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grande II., his successor, was murdered by bis brother Can Signorio in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1359; and in 1387, the latter's son Antonio, who had also endeavoured to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}secure his possession by fratricide, was expelled by Gian Galeazzo Vis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conti, Lord of Milan. Through the widow of the last the town passed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1405 to the Venetians, to whom, with short interruptions, it remained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subject down to the end of the Republic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the history of Architecture Verona is a place of considerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}importance, not only on account of its mediaeval buildings, but as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}birthplace of Fra Giocondo (1435-1514), one of the most typical masters of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the early Renaissance, whose works are to be found at Venice, Paris, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome, and as the residence of Michele Sammicheli (1484-1559), the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}famous military architect of Upper Italy, who imparted to the palaces some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the features of fortified castles. In judging of the Verona palaces, we must{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bear in mind that it was customary here; as at Genoa and other towns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to adorn the facades with paintings. The painted facades of houses near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Fermo, the Porta Borsari, Piazza delle Erbe, the Palazzo Tedeschi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 205) and others, recall the style of Paduan masters of the 15th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are perhaps traceable to the influence of Mantegna. — The most distinguished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Veronese Painters of the 15th cent, were Vittore Pisano (Pisanello) , the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated medallist, Liberate da Verona, Fr. Morone, and particularly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Girolamo dai Libri (1474-1556). The artists of a later period, such as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paolo Cagliari, surnamed Veronese (1532-88), belong more properly to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venetian school.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The High Road from Sestri to Spezia, which is far superior to the rail¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the scantily wooded mountains in long windings, affording fine retrospects{% endraw %}

{% raw %}on, the village of Bracco becomes visible on the left; then to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a view is again disclosed of the sea, near which the road leads. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}village on the coast below is Moneglia (see below). Then a gradual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascent through a somewhat bleak district to the Osteria Baracca (2236 ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence the road descends into a pleasant valley in which lies the village{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Baracca. After a slight ascent it next traverses a well cultivated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}district to Pogliasca (Europa), in the valley of the impetuous Vara, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}affluent of the Magra, which falls into the sea near Sarzana. The road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirts the broad, gravelly channel of the river for some distance, then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diverges to the left and enters a wooded tract, in which beautiful chestnuts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}predominate. Beyond Baracca the sea does not again come into view,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until the last height before Spezia is attained, whence a magnificent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prospect is enjoyed of the bay and the precipitous mountains of Carrara,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Alpi Apuane, as the whole range is called.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Frutti and the Piazza delle Erbe. On the E. side of the latter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Palazzo del Municipio (PL 39; E, 3), of the 16th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the W. side the modern Palazzo delle Debite. Between the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delle Erbe and the Piazza dei Frutti lies the Palazzo della Ragione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 37; E, 3), briefly known as II Salone, a 'Juris Basilica1 as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscription records, erected in 1172-1219. It is celebrated for its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great Hall with vaulted wooden ceiling, formed by the removal of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two division-walls after a fire in 1420, and perhaps the largest in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Europe, 91 yds. in length, 30 yds. in breadth, and 78 ft. in height.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This hall contains a large wooden model of a horse by Donatello,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which has given rise to various conjectures, but was probably used by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the artist as a model for the horse in the monument of Gattamelata (it close¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ly resembles the third horse to the right on St. Mark's at Venice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 239, which was probably the prototype). Behind the horse is the tomb¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone of T. Livius Halys, a freedman of the family of the historian Livy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who is believed to have been born at Abano (p. 299). The walls are ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orned with about 400 pictures in fresco, painted soon after 1420 by Giov.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Miretto and others (frequently retouched), representing the influence of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constellations and the seasons on mankind (custodian 1/-j> fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the loggia towards the Piazza dei Frutti, and that towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Piazza delle Erbe, both added in 1306, are Roman antiquities,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly inscriptions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The University (PL 47; E, 4), opposite the Caf^ Pedrocchi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is established in a building called II Bo', from a tavern which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}once existed in the vicinity with the sign of the ox. Below the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome colonnades in the court, erected in 1552 by Jac. San¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sovino, are numerous inscriptions and armorial bearings of disting¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uished 'cives academicV.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Via delle Torricelle (PL D, 3), near a small bridge over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Bacchiglione, is a small tablet, immured in the wall, marking{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the spot where Ezzelino doffed his helmet and kissed the town-gate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on capturing Padua in 1237. — This street ends on the S. at the ■—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Prato della Valle (PL C, 3, 4), now called the Piazza Vittorio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emmanuele II., originally a grassy dale, now a promenade adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a double series of 82 statues. In the inner row to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 76. Steph. Bathori, 75. John Sobieski 'qui Patav. academ. alum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nus ingenio, patriam rex etc. illustr.'; in the external row Tasso,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ariosto, Petrarch, Galileo, Gustavus Adolphus, Livy, etc. A few of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these statues only possess artistic value, such as those of Poleni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Capello by Canova. This spacious Piazza presents a busy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scene at the time of the fair (fiera), which begins on the festival{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Anthony (13th June) and lasts for a fortnight.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. side of the Prato is the Loggia Amulea (PL 40), a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern Gothic structure, used by the judges at the horse-races{% endraw %}
{% raw %}held on the Prato annually, on 12th June. Below are the marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Statues of Dante and Giotto, by Vincenzo Vela.{% endraw %}

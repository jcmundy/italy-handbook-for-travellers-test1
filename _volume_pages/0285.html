{% raw %}The *Certosa di Pavia, or Carthusian monastery, the splendid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}memorial of the Milan dynasties, founded in 1396 by Gian Galeazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visconti (p. 126), and suppressed under Emperor Joseph II., was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored to its original destination in 1844 and presented to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carthusians. Since the recent suppression of the Italian monasteries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it has been maintained as a 'National Monument'. A vestibule,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embellished with sadly - damaged frescoes by Bern. Luini (SS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sebastian and Christopher), leads to a large inner court, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}farther end of which rises the celebrated facade of the church.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}perhaps the most masterly creation of its kind of the 15th century.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}projecting pillars and transverse arcades, while within these well-{% endraw %}

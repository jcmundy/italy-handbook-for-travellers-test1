{% raw %}*Madonna del Sasso (1168 ft.), on a wooded eminence above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, commands a remarkably fine view. The busy market held at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Locarno every alternate Thursday affords the visitor an opportunity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of observing a variety of costumes of the peasantry of the neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bourhood. Great national festival on 8th Sept., the Nativity of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Virgin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The W. bank of the lake, to the S. of Locarno, is studded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with country-houses, villages, and campanili. On the bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lake runs the new carriage-road from Locarno to Pallanza; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the angle lies Ascona with its castle and seminary; higher up, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the slope , Ronco. Passing two small islands , we next reach Bris-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sago (*Albergo Antico; Alb. Brissago), a delightful spot, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesque white houses conspicuous from a great distance, and an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}avenue of cypresses leading to the church. The slopes above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}village are covered with fig-trees, olives, and pomegranates, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even the myrtle flourishes in the open air. — On the E. bank,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite, is situated Pino (railway to Bellinzona, see p. 36), on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grassy slope.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Agdta and Ganobbio (Hotel Canobbio, R. 172~3, 'pens.' 6fr.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Albergo delle Alpi, moderate; Villa Badia, I72 M. from Canobbio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'pens'. 5-7 fr.) are on Italian territory. The latter is one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oldest and most prosperous villages (2600 inhab.) on the lake,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated on a plateau at the entrance of the Val Canobbino, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}overshadowed by richly-wooded mountains. In the church Della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pieta, the dome of which is ascribed to Bramante, is a Crucifixion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Gaud. Ferrari.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pleasant walk of 72 hr. up the beautiful Val Canobbino to the hydro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pathic establishment of La Salute, and thence to the (20 min.) Orrido, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wild rocky scene with a bridge and in spring a waterfall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The boat now steers for the E. bank, and touches at Maccagno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(railway to Bellinzona, see p. 36), whence a walk of 2 hrs. may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taken to the loftily-situated Lago Delio (Inn; extensive view). —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on, Casneda, in a wooded ravine; then —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Luino (Hotel du Simplon, R. 2, L. & A. lfr.; Posta; Vittoria),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the Palazzo Crivelli surrounded by pines, the station for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lugano (p. 164; railway to Bellinzona, p. 36), and a favourite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summer-resort on account of the beauty of its environs. The Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Garibaldi is adorned with a statue of that hero. — About 72 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the S., at the mouth of the Margorabbia, lies Germignaga, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the large silk-spinning (filanda) and silk-winding (filatoja) factories{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cesare Bozotti and Co. of Milan (visitors admitted by the written{% endraw %}
{% raw %}permission of the proprietor).{% endraw %}

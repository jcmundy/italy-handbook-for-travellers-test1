{% raw %}Farther on is the Palazzo Lanfreducci (PL 47), now Uppezinghi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Cosimo Pagliani, with the fragment of a chain over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance, with the motto 'alia giornata'. It contains a small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection of pictures (including Guido Rents 'Divine and Earthly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Love') which are offered for sale.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N. rises La Sapienza (PL 58; D, 4), or the University,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large edifice of 1493, extended in 1543, with a handsome Renais¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sance court. The Library contains 50,000 vols, and several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valuable MSS. (including the famous Statuto di Pisa, or funda¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mental law of the city).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The University, mentioned in history as early as the 12th cent., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extended by Cosimo I. in 1542. is now provided with a staff of about 60{% endraw %}
{% raw %}professors, and attended by 600 students. The celebrated Galileo was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appointed professor of mathematics here in 1610. — Connected with it are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Museum of Natural History, founded in 1590, chiefly illustrative of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ornithology and geology of Tuscany, and the "Botanical Garden (both{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Via S. Maria, PI. 37; C, 3), one of the oldest in Italy, founded in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1547, remodelled in 1563 by the celebrated Cesalpino, and transferred in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1595 to the present site, which was laid out by Giuseppe Benincasa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Nicola (PI. 11; C, 4), founded about the year 1000 by Count{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hugo of Tuscia as a Benedictine Abbey, has an obliquely placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campanile, which contains an admirable winding-staircase ascribed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Niccolb Pisano. — The Piazza in front of the church is adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a Statue of Ferdinand I., 1595, by a pupil of Giambologna.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the Left Bank of the Arno is situated —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Maria della Spina (PL 26; C, 5), so called from a fragment{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the veritable 'Crown of Thorns' preserved here, an elegant little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church in the Pisan Gothic style, erected in 1230 by the senate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the noble families Gualandi and Gattosi, for sailors about to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}go to sea. It was enlarged in 1323, and adorned with sculptures by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pupils of Giovanni Pisano and by Nino, the son of Andrea Pisano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(key kept at the opposite house, No. 22). The church has re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cently been skilfully restored.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Passing the new Ponte Solferino (p. 343), we proceed towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Porta a Mare, at the end of the town, near which rises —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Paolo a Ripa d'Arno (PI. 31 ; B, 6), dating from the 12th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or 13th cent., with a fine facade embellished with three rows of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns, the finest at Pisa after that of the cathedral. The interior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is adorned with badly-preserved frescoes of 1400.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the Ponte di Mezzo (see above, and p. 343; PL D 4) are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated the Loggia de' Banchi (PI. 39), erected in 1605 by Buon-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}talenti, now the corn-exchange, and the handsome Palazzo del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Comune (PL 39; formerly Gambacorti). The latter contains the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}newly-arranged Archivio diStato, or the city-archives, which occupy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ten rooms, and comprise 15,994 parchment charters (one granted by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frederick Barbarossa in 1162, one by Richard Oouir de Lion in 1192{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and others of very early date: catalogue kept by the custodian).{% endraw %}

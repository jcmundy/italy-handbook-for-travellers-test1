{% raw %}1 58 Route 23. VAL TELLINA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Colico to Bormio, 67 M. Diligence in summer (June to Sept.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}daily in I672 hrs.; fares 17 fr. 20 c. (to Sondrio thrice, to Tirano twice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daily). Extra-Post, with two horses, 102 fr. The road ascends the Vat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tellina, which belonged to the Grisons down to 1797, then to Austria,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and since 1859 has been united to Italy. The broad valley is watered by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Adda, the inundations of which often cause considerable damage, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}make the lower part of the valley marshy and unhealthy. — A little way{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Colico the Spliigen road (p. 40) joins ours on the left. IO72 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Morbegno (850 ft.; Regina d'Inghilterra or Posta) is noted for its silk-cul¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ture. About 172 M. beyond Morbegno the road crosses the Adda, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Val Masino opens on the left. The road again crosses and recrosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}river, and afterwards follows the right bank. On a rocky eminence farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on, to the W., rises the church of Sassella, erected on galleries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2672 M. Sondrio (1140 ft.; "Posta; Maddalena), the capital of the Val{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tellina, is situated on the Malero, an impetuous torrent, at the mouth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the picturesque Val Malenco. A large building outside the town, once{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a nunnery, is now private property. The old residence of the bailiffs is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now a barrack.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Sondrio the churches of Montagna and Pendolasco rise on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left. Between S. Giacomo and Tresenda, about halfway up the N. slope{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the valley, rises the ancient watch-tower of Teglio, which gives its name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the valley ( Val Teglino). At Tresenda the road over the Passo d'Aprica{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diverges to the right (R. 34). The road next crosses the Poschiavino, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends from the Bernina glaciers, and soon reaches Madonna di Tirano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}('"S. Michele), a small village with a large and handsome pilgrimage-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of the 17th century. Interesting fairs, lasting 3 days, are held{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here at Whitsuntide and at the end of October. The mountain-road which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here diverges to the right leads to Poschiavo, and across the Bernina to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Upper Engadine; see Baedeker''s Switzerland. The ' Confine Svizzero''{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is 3/4 M. to the N.W. of Madonna di Tirano. About 1/t hr. after leaving{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna di Tirano we reach —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}43 M. Tirano (1506 ft.; Posta, or Angelo; Due Torri, by the post-office;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stelvio, by the lower bridge), a small town with old mansions of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visconti, Pallavicini, and Salis families, where inundations of the Adda{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have also frequently occasioned serious damage.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road now ascends more rapidly along the vine-clad slopes, pass¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing Sernio, Lovero, and Tovo. To the S.W. rises the precipitous Piz Ma-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}succio (9245 ft.), a landslip from which in 1807 blocked up the narrow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}channel of the Adda, and converted the populous and fertile valley into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a vast lake. At Mazzo the road crosses to the right bank of the Adda,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and at the large village of (I72 M.) Grossotto (Leone d'Oro) it crosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roasco, which here issues from the Val Grosina. To the right, at the mouth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the latter, is the imposing ruined castle of Venosta. Beyond (IY2 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grosio the road recrosses to the left bank. In 172 hr. more we reach —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}55 M. Bolladore (2840 ft.; Posta or Angelo). On the hill on the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the river rises the picturesque church of Sondalo. Beyond Mon-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dadizzo we again cross the Adda. The valley now contracts; to the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies Le Prese, at the mouth of tho Val di Rezzo. We now enter the de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}file of Serra di Morignone, about 1 M. in length, which separates the Val{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tellina from the 'Paese Freddo\ or 'cold region', of Bormio. We cross{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Adda for the last time by the Ponte del Diavolo. The road enters the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}green Valle di Sotto, passes the hamlets of Morignone and S. Antonio, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Ceppino reaches the level, green valley (Piano) of Bormio, enclosed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lofty mountains, the lower slopes of which are clothed with pines, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the upper in part with snow. The road traverses the valley, crosses at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2 M.) S. Lucia the muddy Frodolfo, just above its confluence with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adda, and in 20 min. more reaches —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}67 M. Bormio, Ger. Worms (4012 ft.; Posta; Cola, in the market), an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antiquated little Italian town, with numerous dilapidated towers, pictur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esquely situated at the entrance to the Val Furva. — The diligence goes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on hence, ascending in numerous windings, to (2 M.) the —{% endraw %}

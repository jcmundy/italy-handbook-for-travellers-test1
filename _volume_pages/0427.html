{% raw %}Modena, a town with 30,000 inhab. (commune 50,000), situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a fertile plain between the Secchia and the Panaro, formerly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capital of the duchy of that name, and now that of the province of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emilia, possesses broad streets, spacious arcades, a university, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an academy of art.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Modena, the ancient Mutina, in the dominions of the Gallic Boii,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became a Roman colony in B.C. 183, and, being situated on the high¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road from Rome to Mediolanum (Milan), was a place of some importance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the murder of Csesar, Brutus was besieged bere by Antony for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}four months, Dec. 44 to April 43 B.C. (Bellum Mutinense); but the latter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was defeated by Octavian with the consuls Pansa and Hirtius, and compel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}led to raise the siege. — In the middle ages Modena belonged to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}estates of the Countess Matilda, but eventually obtained its independence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and became the scene of violent conflicts between the Guelphs and Ghi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bellines. In 1288 Obizzo II. a"Este gained possession of the supreme power,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which his descendants continued to enjoy (see p. 302). — On the death of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alphonso II., without issue (1597), the states of Modena and Reggio (but not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that of Ferrara) fell to his kinsman Cesare dPEste (1598), husband of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Virginia de1 Medici, daughter of Grand-duke Cosimo I. of Florence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hercules 111. (d. 1803), who by the Peace of Luneville lost Modena in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1801, was the last of the family of Este. Through his daughter Beatrice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who married Archduke Ferdinand, the duchy came into the possession of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the younger branch of the House of Austria in 1814. The insurrections{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 1848 and 1851 were quelled with cruel severity. Francis V., the last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duke, quitted his dominions in 1859 and went over to the Austrians.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A specialty of Modena with regard to art-history, was the sculptur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing of Terracottas , the aim of the artists being to represent dramatic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groups rather in accordance with pictorial than plastic principles, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}therefore calculated only for being exhibited in niches. This branch of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}art was first fully developed by the strongly realistic master Guido Maz-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}zoni, some of whose works may be inspected in the crypt of the cathedral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see below), and in S. Giovanni Decollato (PL 7). The art was next{% endraw %}
{% raw %}practised in a more refined style by Antonio Begarelli (d. 1565), who re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jected the aid of painting, and brought it to the utmost perfection of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which it seems capable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Cathedral (PL 1; D, 5), begun in the Romanesque style in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1099 by Lanfranco, consecrated in 1184, has a superstructure of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later date. The facade is relieved by a large rose-window and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}simple colonnade (three arches resting on columns in the wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and enclosed by a larger arch), which is continued round the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole building. The portals are adorned with the often recurring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble lions. The rude sculptures of the facade, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Creation, and the history of the first men down to Noah, are by JVi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colaus and Guilelmus (about 1099); on the S. side, to the right near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the choir, is the history of St. Geminianus, a relief by Agostino da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Firenze, 1442 (perhaps Agostino di Duccio). The sculptures on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. side-portal are old and interesting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is low and heavy, but of handsome proportions. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nave and aisles are supported by alternate pillars and columns, over which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}runs a triforium, and the vaulting is pointed. In the 2nd chapel on the{% endraw %}

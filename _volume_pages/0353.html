{% raw %}The Cathedral contains several works by Jacopo Bassano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near its N. entrance rises the once fortified tower of Ezzelino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cruel Ghibelin leader, now partly occupied by ecclesiastics of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cathedral, and affording a lovely view.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Villa Rezzonica, I72 M. from the town, contains, amongst{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other works of art, an oil-painting by Canova, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Death of Socrates. In the suburb of Borgo Leone lies the Villa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parolini, with a beautiful park.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bonaparte defeated the Austrians under Wurmser at Bassano on Sept.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}8th, 1796, four days after the battle of Roveredo, having marched hither{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Trent in two days. The covered wooden bridge over the Brenta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupies the place of one which the French blew up on that occasion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— In 1809 Napoleon elevated the district of Bassano into a duchy and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conferred it upon his secretary of state Maret.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fossagno, Canova's birthplace, is beautifully situated at the base of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Monte Grappa, 12 M. N.E. of Bassano. A good road to it leads by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romano, the birthplace of Ezzelino, and Crespano. The church, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of a circular temple, and designed by Canova, contains his tomb, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar-piece painted by him, and a handsome bronze relief of the Entomb¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment. The church and the bridge at Crespano (see above), which crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the river by a single arch (118 ft. in span), were built with funds be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}queathed by Canova for the purpose. The Palazzo, as his house is called,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains models and casts of his works.{% endraw %}

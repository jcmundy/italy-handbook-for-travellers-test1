{% raw %}The Hospital of St. Anna (entrance in the Strada della Giovecca, next{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door to the Europa; PL 29; E. 4) is interesting as the place where Tasso{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was kept in confinement for seven years (from 1579) by order of Al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phonso II. He is supposed to have incurred the displeasure of his patron{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by his passion for the Princess Leonora, the sister of Alphonso, or to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have suffered from periodical attacks of insanity. A dungeon is shown{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which he is said to have been incarcerated, with the names of Byron{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other poets written on the walls. — Adjoining the hospital is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Casino dei Negozianti, formerly Palazzo Roverella, erected in 1508, with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine facade. — At the end of the Strada della Giovecca, to the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands the Palazzina, now an school for engineers, with the remains of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tasteful decorative painting (executed after 1550).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}At the beginning of the Via Cavour, formerly named the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Larga, is the *Palazzo Riccardi (PL F, 3, 4), formerly Medici,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which has been in possession of the government since 1814. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected about 1430 under Cosimo Pater Patrice by Michelozzo, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here introduced the practice of tapering the rustica in the different{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stories. The unsymmetrical facade is surmounted by a rich and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heavy cornice. Here Cosimo's grandson Lorenzo il Magnifico was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}born on 1. Jan., 1449, and here he maintained his brilliant estab¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lishment. Lorenzo's sons Piero, Giovanni, and Giuliano also first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saw the light within its walls. Giulio, Ippolito, and Alessandro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de Medici (comp. p. 372) subsequently resided here, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family continued in possession of the palace until it was sold in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1659 by the grand-duke Ferdinand II. to the Marchese Riccardi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who extended it considerably, enclosing within its precincts the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strada del Traditore, where on 7th Jan., 1537, Duke Alexander{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was assassinated by Lorenzino de' Medici. The original structure,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, is still in great part recognisable, particularly its beautiful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court and the staircases.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}An imposing gateway leads to a Court surrounded by arcades, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient busts, statues, sarcophagi, Greek and Latin inscriptions from Rome,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. were placed by the Marchese Riccardi in 1719. The sarcophagus in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the corner to the left, with the representation of the Calydonian Hunt,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly contained the remains of Guccio de' Medici, an ancestor of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family, who lived at the beginning of the 14th cent.; the cover, bearing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the arms of the Medici and of the guild of the workers in wool, is modern.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Four of the frames for inscriptions resembling windows were designed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Michael Angelo. The relief-medallions above the arcades are by Donatello.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— The passage to the Second Court contains ancient busts; the Third{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Court contains a fountain and the statue of Duke Alexander.{% endraw %}

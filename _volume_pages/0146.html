{% raw %}Turin (785 ft.), the Roman Augusta Taurinorum, founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Taurini, a Ligurian tribe, destroyed by Hannibal B.C. 218,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and subsequently re-erected, was the capital of the County of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piedmont in the middle ages , and in 1418 became subject to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dukes of Savoy, who frequently resided here. From 1859 to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1865 it was the capital of Italy and residence of the king. Turin,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the seat of a university and of a military academy, and the head¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarters of the 1st Italian Corps d'Armee, is situated in an ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tensive plain on the Po, which receives the waters of the Dora{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riparia below the city. The plain of the Po is bounded on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. by the Graian and Cottian Alps , and on the E. by a range{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of hills rising on the right bank, opposite the city (hill of the Ca¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}puchins, p. 72; Superga, p. 73). Turin has always been the focus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the national struggles for unity, and by the industry and per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}severance of its citizens has recovered from the severe losses conse¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quent on the removal of the court. The population in 1882 in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding surrounding villages, was 252,900, of the town itself about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}220,000 (in 1377, 4,200; in 1631, 36,447; in 1799, 80,752: and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1848, 130,849").{% endraw %}

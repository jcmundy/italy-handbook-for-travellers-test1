{% raw %}S. Stefano. VENICE. 40. Route. 269{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Barbaro family ('barbaro monumento del decadimento dell' arte',{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as it has been called). The niches of the facade contain statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of members of the family. At the base of the lower row of columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are plans of Zara, Candia, Padua, Rome, Corfu, and Spalato, hewn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the stone; on the bases of the columns are representations of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}naval battles. The interior of the church contains nothing worthy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of note.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leaving this church, we cross the Campo S. Maurizio, where the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small church of that name is situated, to the larger Campo S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stefano (PL E, D, 4). The church of S. Vitale (PL D, 4), on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, contains a painting by*Carpaccio, representing St. Vitalis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and his family worshipping the Madonna. On the right rises —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Stefano (PL 35; E, 4), a Gothic church of the 14th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with an elegant facade in brick, good window mouldings in ter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}racotta, and a peculiarly constructed vaulting of wood, restored in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ancient style, imparting a very pleasing appearance to the interior.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance-Wall, above the principal door, equestrian statue of Dom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Contarini, middle of 17th cent.; adjacent, (1.) the -;Tomb of the physician{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jacopo Suriano (d. 1511). On the Pavement of the nave is the large tomb¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stone of the Doge Francesco Morosini 'Peloponnesiaci' (d. 1694), with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cap and baton of office in bronze. — Adjacent to the Sacristy in the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aisle a Madonna with saints, a relief in bronze of the 16th cent.; in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sacristy small marble statues of John the Baptist and St. Antony by Pietro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lombardo; on the right Madonna and saints by Palma Vecchio (freely re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}touched). — Choir. On the lateral walls statues of the twelve Apostles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and four saints, and reliefs of the four Evangelists and two Fathers of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church. In front of the high-altar two candelabra in bronze, on marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pedestals, by Al. Vittoria, 1577; behind it, choir-stalls of the 15th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carved and inlaid. — 3rd altar (1.) statues of St. Jerome and St. Paul by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro Lombardo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the church on the left is a handsome ^Monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Court, restored in 1532, and once adorned with frescoes by Porde¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}none, of which there are remains on the S. and E. wall over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnade (four saints on the E. wall, particularly those to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very good); below the windows 'putti', the subjects on the S. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being from the Old Testament. — Crossing the court, we reach the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campo S. Angelo (PI. E, 4), with a monument of Paleocapa, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}minister, and to the left the Pal. Grimani (p. 257). Farther to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E., near the Teatro Rossini (PL 102; E, 4), is a large new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piazza adorned with a Monument of Manin (p. 240) in bronze.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Interior, borne by 12 circular columns, is remarkable for boldness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and symmetry of proportion; the vaulting is painted in the late-Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. On the first column to the left is an ancient capital, used as a basin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for consecrated water, and supported by a humpbacked dwarf (Gobbo), exe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cuted by Gabriele Caliari, father of Paolo Veronese. By the first altar to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right is the monument of Fregoso, by Danese Cattaneo (1565). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd and 3rd altars are enclosed in admirable frame-works of white marble.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The frame-work of the 4th altar is a reproduction of the Arco de' Gavi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 207); the altar-piece is a St. Martin by Caroto. The small adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel contains a painted group of the Entombment, executed in the 15th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}century. In the right transept is a Madonna with saints by Girolamo dai{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Libri (1512). The chapel of the Pellegrini, on the right by the high-altar,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is adorned with terracotta reliefs of the 14th cent., representing the history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Christ from the Nativity to the Resurrection; on the outside, above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the arch, are the remains of a fresco of St. George, by Pisanello. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir, to the left, is the monument of General Sarego (1432), with fine{% endraw %}

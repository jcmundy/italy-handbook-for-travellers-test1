{% raw %}More elaborate and studied, and in every sense grandiose, the 'Ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donna di Casa Pesaro' reveals more surely than the 'Annunciation' (Scuola{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di S. Rocco, p. 268) the breadth of Titian's talent, and takes us, not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without preparation, to the height of his pictorial fame. He has brought{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to perfection the last and finest of all forms of presentation pictures, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}noblest combination of the homely and devotional with palatial archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tecture — the most splendid and solemn union of the laws of composition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and colour with magic light and shade. . . . Far away from those humble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conceptions of place which mark the saintly pictures of earlier times, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesari kneel in the portico of a temple, the pillars of which soar to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sky in proportions hitherto unseen. . . . The Virgin sits on her throne,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bending down in a graceful kindly way, and directs her glance towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the kneeling 'Baffo' (Jacopo Pesaro, Bishop of Paphos), her white veil{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falling over one shoulder, but caught on the other by the infant Christ,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who peeps with delightful glee from beneath it at St. Francis (behind{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom, in the background, is St. Anthony of Padua). ... To the left front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the throne St. Peter at a desk interrupts his reading, and marks the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line with his finger as he turns to look down at Baffo, who kneels in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prayer on the floor below. In the rear between both an armed knight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the standard of the church unfurled and a captive Turk bound by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a rope symbolizes the victory of the Pesari. Below, to the left, are{% endraw %}

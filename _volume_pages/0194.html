{% raw %}to Nice. SAN REMO. 16. Route. 105{% endraw %}
{% raw %}San Remo, although apparently a small place, contains 15,000{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab. , densely crowded in the older parts of the town, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consist of a labyrinth of quaint and narrow lanes , flights of steps,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}archways, lofty and sombre houses, and mouldering walls. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arches by which the houses are connected high above the streets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are intended to give them stability in case of earthquakes. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, which was formerly fortified, stands on a hill between two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}short Valleys, and the houses rising one above another receive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their modicum of light and air from the back only. Castigliuoli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a smaller quarter on the "W. side, is similarly situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The E. part of the town terminates in an eminence approached{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by broad roads shaded by cypresses, commanding charming views{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the bay and mountains, and crowned with the white dome-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered church of the Madonna della Costa, in front of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there is a large hospital for lepers. On a more prominent point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands the Villa Carbone , with a low octagonal tower (fee !/2 fr.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the panorama from which conveys a good idea of the peculiarities{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the situation. Corsica is visible in the distance to the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another walk may be taken to the "W- pier of the small harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is defended by the fort of St. Tecla, erected by the Genoese,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and now used as a prison. A survey from the upper platform of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Molo will convey an idea of the sheltered position of the town,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which renders the climate as genial as that of Mentone and has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought the place into notice as a winter-residence for invalids{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(comp. p. 104). In the rich vegetation the olive predominates, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hills above are chiefly clothed with pines. Country-houses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and churches peep from amidst the olive groves in every direction,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the highest being at San Romolo at the foot of the Bignone, to which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the few visitors who remain throughout the summer resort in order{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to escape from the heat. Several fine palms rise in the principal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street of the lower and modern part of the town, and others in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'palm-quarter' of the old town, and other places (comp. below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Walks numerous and pleasant, but occasionally rough. Near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station are the Giardino Pubblico, containing palms, eucalyptus, etc., and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a small fountain, and the Corso Mezzogiorno, which is planted with palms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pepper-trees and terminates towards the W. in the Giardino delV{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Imperatrice, a garden laid out under the auspices of the late Empress{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Russia. The most sheltered walk higher up in the basin is the Berigo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Road. A similar walk has also been constructed on the E. bay. — A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful point of view easily reached is the '"Madonna della Guardia on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Capo Verde, returning by Poggio. — To S. Romolo 3 hrs., an excursion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for which a donkey may be hired. About 2 hrs. higher rises the Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bignone (4235 ft.), which commands a beautiful panorama of the sea to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. and the Maritime Alps to the N., on the way back from which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piano del Re, a celebrated point of view, may also be visited. — Good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roads lead to Ceriana and to Taggia (p. 103). — To La Colla by Ospeda-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}letti (see below) 2 hrs.; or direct, by a very ancient road, 3 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train passes through a tunnel under the Capo Nero, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road winds over the promontory at a considerable height. —{% endraw %}

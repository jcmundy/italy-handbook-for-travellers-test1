{% raw %}18 M. Carmagnola, with 4000 inhabitants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carmagnola was the birthplace (1390) of the celebrated Condottiere{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Francesco Bussone, son of a swine-herd, usually called Count of Carmag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nola, who reconquered a considerable part of Lombardy for Duke Filippo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maria Visconti, and afterwards, as Generalissimo of the Republic of Venice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conquered Brescia and Bergamo, and won the battle of Macalo (1427).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At length his fidelity was suspected by the Council of Ten, and he was be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}headed between the two columns in the Piazzetta (p. 241) on 5th May,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1432. Bussone's fate is the subject of a tragedy by Manzoni. — The 'Car¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnole', the celebrated republican dance and song of the French Revol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ution, was named after this town, the home of most of the street-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}musicians of Paris. The refrain of the song was: 'Dansons la Carmagnole !{% endraw %}

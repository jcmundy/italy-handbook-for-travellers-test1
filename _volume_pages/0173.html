{% raw %}The situation of Genoa, rising above the sea in a wide semi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circle, and its numerous palaces, justly entitle it to the epithet of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'La Superha'. The city is surrounded by extensive fortifications,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dating from the beginning of the 17th cent., which have recently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been strengthened. From the lighthouse on the W. side, where the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large barrack of S. Benigno affords quarters for 10,000 men, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}broad rampart extends at some distance from the town up the hill,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}past the Forte Begato (1618 ft.) to the Forte dello Sperone (1693 ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the highest point, and then descends past the Forte Castellaccio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1253 ft.) to the mouth of the Bisagno, which falls into the sea to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. of Genoa, a circuit of about 9^2 M. in all. The heights{% endraw %}
{% raw %}around the town are crowned with ten detached forts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genoa is the chief commercial town in Italy, and contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}140,000 inhab., or with the neighbouring suburbs 179,500. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}annual imports are valued at 330 million francs (13,200,000 £.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the exports at 70 million francs (2,800,000 L). Of the imports{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about one-third is from England, and the rest chiefly from France{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and North America.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From a very early period Genoa has been famous as a sea-port, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even in the time of the Romans it formed an outlet for the products of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extensive Ligurian coast-district. The town is believed to derive its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name from the fact that the sea penetrates into the land here somewhat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the shape of a knee (genu). The smaller towns on the Ligurian coast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}looked up to Genoa as their champion against the Saracens, who ravaged{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the country from their settlement at Frassineto, but in 936 Genoa itself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had to submit to being plundered. In 1015 the Genoese made themselves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}masters of Corsica, and in 1119 they waged a victorious war against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisa, which was then the mistress of the Tyrrhenian Sea. From that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}date the rival cities were almost permanently at war down to 1284, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a terrible naval battle took place between them at Meloria, on which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occasion the Genoese captured 29 Pisan galleys, and sank 7 others. From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this disaster Pisa never recovered, and Genoa now obtained the supre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}macy over the W. islands, Corsica, and nominally over Sardinia also.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At a still earlier period Genoa had participated in the Crusades, and se¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cured to herself a busy trade with the Levant. She also possessed settle¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments at Constantinople and in the Crimea, in Syria and Cyprus, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tunis and Majorca. The rivalry of the Genoese and Venetians was a fruit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful source of wars and feuds during the 12-14th centuries, which at length{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were terminated by a decisive victory gained by the latter in 1380.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The internal history of the city was no less chequered than the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ternal. The party conflicts between the great families of the Doria and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spinola (Ghibellines) on one side, and the Grimaldi and Fieschi (Guelphs){% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the other, led to some extraordinary results. The defeated party{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used, at the expense of their own independence, to invoke the aid of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some foreign prince, and accordingly we find that after the 14th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the kings of Naples and France, the counts of Monferrat, and the dukes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Milan, were alternately masters of Genoa. Nor was this state of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}matters materially altered by the revolution of 1339, by which the ex¬{% endraw %}

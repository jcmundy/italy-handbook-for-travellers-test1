{% raw %}72 Route 9. TURIN. Capuchin Monastery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Massimo (PL 15; F, G, 4), built in 1849-54 in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style of a Roman temple, surmounted by a dome. The facade is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with statues of the Four Evangelists. Good modern fres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coes in the interior, and several statues by Albertoni.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Corso del Re, which leads from the iron bridge to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Carlo Felice, on the left, is the handsome Protestant Church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Tempio Valdese,- PL 18, F 4; see p. 73), completed in 1854, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first erected at Turin since the establishment of religious toleration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1848.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A favourite promenade, especially in the evening, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Nuovo Giardino Pubblico (PL G, 4, 5), above the iron bridge on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left bank of the Po (Cafe). It comprises the Botanical Garden,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and extends beyond the royal chateau II Valentino, a turreted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building of the 17th cent., now occupied by the Polytechnic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}School. In the adjacent Corso Massimo d'Azeglio is the Tiro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nazionale, a well equipped rifle-range.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the spacious Piazza Vittorio Emmanuele (see p. 63;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}PI. G, 3) the Po, here 175 yds. wide and 10 ft. deep, is crossed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a Bridge of five arches, constructed of granite in 1810. (Above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge are the swimming-baths, p. 62.) Beyond the bridge, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right bank of the river is a flight of 32 steps ascending to the spa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cious dome-church of Gran Madre di Dio (PL 14; H, 3), erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1818 in imitation of the Pantheon at Rome, to commemorate the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return of King Victor Emmanuel I. in 1814. The groups sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tured in stone on the flight of steps are emblematical of Faith and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charity. The lofty columns of the portico are monoliths of granite.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— A few hundred yards farther is the Villa della Regina, now a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school for the daughters of officers who have fallen in battle. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right a new road leads to the *TrattoriaS. Margherita, command¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing a fine view of the Alps.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Following the Via di Moncalieri to the right, we reach the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wooded hill on which rises the Capuchin Monastery, II Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL H, 3, 4), !/4 hr.'s walk from the bridge. Two paths ascend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill, the wider of which, to the left, is preferable, being shady{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and unpaved. At the top is a station of the Italian Alpine Club{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(open when the flag is flying; adm. 25c), which commands a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Survey of the river, city, plain, and the chain of the Alps in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}background, above which (right) the snowy summit of Monte Rosa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(15,217 ft.) is prominent, then the Grand-Paradis (13,780 ft.), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Levanna (11,942 ft.) ; farther W the valley of Susa (p. 26),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Michele della Chiusa (p. 26), rising conspicuously on a hill{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1042 ft.), above it the Roche-Melon (11,660 ft.) to the right of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mont Cenis, and farther S.W. Monte Viso (12,670 ft.). Morning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}light is most favourable for the view. The club-house contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several interesting maps. This hill of the Capuchins has always{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been a point of great importance in the military history of Turin,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was fortified down to 1802.{% endraw %}

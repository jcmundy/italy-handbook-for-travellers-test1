{% raw %}Theatres. Performances in the large theatres begin at 8, 8.30,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or 9, and terminate at midnight or later, operas and ballets being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exclusively performed. The first act of an opera is usually suc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceeded by a ballet of three acts or more. Verdi is the most popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}composer. The pit (platea), to which the 'biglietto d'ingresso' gives{% endraw %}
{% raw %}access, is the usual resort of the men, while the boxes and some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times the stalls (scanni chiusi, sedie chiuse,poltrone, or posti distinti){% endraw %}
{% raw %}are frequented by ladies. A box (palco) must always be secured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in advance. — A visit to some of the smaller theatres, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dramas and comedies are acted, is recommended for the sake of ha¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bituating the ear to the language. Performances in summer take{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place in the open air, in which case smoking is allowed. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theatre is the usual evening-resort of the Italians, who seldom ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serve strict silence during the performance of the orchestra. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}instrumental music is rarely good.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XL Post Office. Telegraph.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Letters (whether lposte restante", Italian lferma in posla\ or to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the traveller's hotel) should be addressed very distinctly, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of the place should be in Italian. When asking for letters the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller should present his visiting-card instead of giving his name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orally. Postage-stamps (francobolli) are sold at the post-offices and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at many of the tobacco-shops. — Letters of 15 grammes (!/2 oz.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about the weight of three sous) to any of the states included in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}postal union (now comprising the whole of Europe) 25 c.; post-card{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(cartolina postale; for foreign countries, per Vestero) 10 c.; book-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}packets (stampe sotto fascia) 5 c. per 50 gr. ; registration-fee (rac-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comandazione) 30 c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Letters by town-post 5 c. ; throughout the kingdom of Italy 20 c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prepaid, or 30c. unpaid. Post-card 10c, with card for answer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attached 15 c. Book-packets, 20 c. per 40 grammes (li/3 oz.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the larger towns the post-office is open daily from 8 or 9 a. m.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to 10 p.m. (also on Sundays and holidays); in smaller places it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally closed in the middle of the day for two or three hours.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Telegrams. For telegrams to foreign countries the following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rate per word is charged in addition to an initial payment of 1 fr. :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great Britain 47 c, France 14, Germany 25, Switzerland 14, Austria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6-22, Belgium 26, Denmark 37, Russia 66, Sweden 44, Norway{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 c — To America from 33/4 fr. per word upwards, according to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the state.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Within the kingdom of Italy, 15 words 1 fr. , each additional{% endraw %}
{% raw %}word 10 c. ; telegrams with special haste (telegrammiurgenti) which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}take precedence of all others, may be sent in Italy at, five times to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreign countries at thrice the above rates.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}274 Route 40. VENICE. S. Giorgio Magg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gherardo; the 5th chapel (at the principal door), a Madonna and four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saints, by Paolo Veronese.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little to the S. in the Rio della Pieta is the church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lorenzo (on the right), containing two good statues of SS. Law¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rence and Sebastian by Girol. Campagna (at the high-altar). On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left is the church of S. Giorgio degli Schiavoni (PL 13; G, 3),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a good Renaissance facade of 1551, a low wooden ceiling, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pictures by Carpaccio, on the right, three scenes from the life of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Jerome , on the left, three from the life of St. George; altar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piece, St. Tryphon and St. Matthew; above the high-altar, a Ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donna by Vine. Catena; the ceiling of the oratory beautifully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted by Palma Vecchio. — Farther S. we next come to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Antonino, where we cross the bridge to the right to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Giorgio dei Greci (PL 13a; G, 4), with an elegant campa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nile of the 16th cent., and an ikonostasis adorned with gorgeous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Byzantine mosaics. The head of Christ in the dome is said to have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been designed by Titian. — Returning to S. Antonino, and again{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pursuing a S. direction, we soon reach —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Giovanni in Bragbra (PL 13d ; G, 4), a church of early origin,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but entirely restored at the beginning of the 18th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1st Chapel on the right: Alwise Vivarini (according to Mr. Crowe; usu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally attributed to Giov. Bellini), Madonna and Child; farther on, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall of the church, Bissolo (or Ant. Vivarini), St. Andrew with SS. Jerome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Martin, the latter on horseback; "Paris Bordone, Last Supper. — On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pillar before the chapel of the choir: "Cima da Conegliano, Constantine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and St. Helena by the side of the Cross, 1502. At the back of the high-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar: "Cima da Conegliano, Baptism of Christ, 1494. On the pillar to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, L. Vivarini, Resurrection; on the wall to the left, Bart. Vivarini,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna with St. Andrew and John the Baptist; under it, Cima da Coneg¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liano, Finding of the Cross, originally a predella of the above-named picture.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We may now proceed towards the E. , past S. Martino (erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sansovino in 1540; font with four kneeling angels by Tullio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lombardo, 1484; Last Supper by Girolamo da Santacroce, 1549){% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the entrance to the arsenal (PI. 3, H4; see p. 247); or to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. to the Riva degli Schiavoni (p. 247).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the Piazzetta, on an island fortified in 1848, is situ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ated —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Giorgio Maggiore (PL 12; G, 5),belonging to the adjacent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suppressed Benedictine monastery, now an artillery-barrack, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cruciform church with a dome, and apses terminating the transepts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun by Palladio in 1560. The facade was finished by Scamozzi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1575.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior (when closed, ring the bell to the right; the person{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who shows it is a well-informed Benedictine) is very beautiful, and has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not been spoiled by decorations of a later date. Over the door a portrait{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pope Pius VII., who was elected by a conclave of Cardinals held here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on 14th March, 1800. To the right, the monument of Lorenzo Venier (d.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1667). Over the 1st altar, Nativity, by Bassano; 2nd, Crucifix in wood, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Michelozzo; 3rd altar, Martyrdom of SS. Cosmas and Damianus and their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}companions; 4th altar, Coronation of the Virgin, the two last by Tinto¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retto; 5th altar, Adoration of the Madonna, by Rizzi. — Choir: (r.) Last{% endraw %}

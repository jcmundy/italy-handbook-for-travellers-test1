{% raw %}From Bastia to Rogliano, Capo Corso, S. Fiorenzo, and Calvi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Bastia to Rogliano, 35 M., diligence daily in 672 hrs. (coupe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 fr. 55, inte>ieur 4 fr. 45 c).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A very pleasant excursion may be made to the long and narrow pen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}insula in which the Serra Mts. rise, culminating in the Monte Alticcione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Monte Stello (5250 ft.), and terminating on the N. in the Capo Corso{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Promontorium Sacrum). Beautiful valleys descend on the E. and W. sides{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these mountains. A good road leads along the coast from Bastia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passing several ancient watch-towers of the Pisans and Genoese, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}affording a view of the picturesque islands of Elba, Capraia, and Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cristo. At Brando there is a Stalactite Cavern, surrounded by pleasant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gardens. Luri possesses a charming valley, watered by several streams,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and producing a luxuriant growth of grapes, oranges, and lemons. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the promontory (about 25 M. from Bastia), to the N. beyond Rog¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liano and Ersa, rises a lighthouse. An ancient, half-ruined circular struc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ture near it is popularly called the 'Tower of Seneca'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A road leads from Bastia along the W. coast and across the Serra{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the (13 M.) small seaport of S. Fiorenzo, charmingly situated on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bay of that name, and commanded by a fort. In the neighbouring low{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground formerly lay the mediaeval town of Nebbio, the ruined cathedral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which (S. Maria Assunta), of the 12th cent., stands on an eminence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond this the road skirts the sea, crosses the small river Ostriconi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and reaches the small, but thriving seaport town of Isola Rossa, founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1758 by Pasquale Paoli, to whom a monument has been erected. Its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name is derived from three red cliffs rising from the sea in front of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}harbour. The environs are delightful; the view from the Monte S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reparata, surmounted by a deserted church, is finest by evening-light.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road then leads to Algajola, a deserted old town on the coast,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with marble quarries in the vicinity. During the Genoese period it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortified, and formed the central point of the fertile district of Balagna.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The loftily situated village of Lumio, farther on, with its orange-planta¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions and hedges of cactus, commands a beautiful view of the valley and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town of Calvi (57 M. from Bastia), an important and fortified place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the Genoese period, noted for its faithful adherence to the Re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public, and in 1794 bravely defended against the English by the French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commandant Casabianca. Traces of the English bombardment are still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}observable. The principal church contains the tombs of the Baglioni fam¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ily, who bore the surname Liberta, from having distinguished themselves{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 15th and 16th centuries. The environs of Calvi are marshy. Charm¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing view of the bay, with the promontory of Rivellata, and of the rocky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountains of Calenzana, to the E. of the town.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}is now in the Academy (p. 417), stood here. On the right is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}group of Hercules and Cacus by Michael Angelo's rival Baccio Ban-{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The two insignificant statues by Bandinelli and Rossi on each side{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The outer Court was constructed by Michelozzo in 1454. The elabor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ate decorations of the columns and the arabesques on the ceiling were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}added by Marco da Faenza in 1565, from which period also date the faded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}views of Austrian towns. In the centre, above a large basin of porphyry,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a *Boy with a fish as a fountain-figure, by Verrocchio, originally made{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for a villa of Lorenzo de1 Medici. At the back are Samson and a Philis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine by Rossi (the latter being a caricature of Michael Angelo). The ar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}morial bearings above the colonnade include those of Florence (lily),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the People (cross), the Parte Guelfa (eagle), and the Medici (balls), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the combined colours (red and white) of Florence and Fiesole.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. On the First Floor is the Great Hall (Sala del Consiglio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or dei Cinquecento), constructed in 1495 for the Council, which had been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enlarged by Savonarola's partisans. It was to have been decorated with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes from the celebrated cartoons of the 'Cavalry Skirmish' by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leonardo, and the 'Bathing Soldiers' by Michael Angelo (1504). The walls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are adorned with historical frescoes by Vasari and others, and with por¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trait-statues of the Medici by Rossi and Caccini. From 1860 to 1869 this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hall was used for the sittings of the Italian Chamber. The colossal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble Statue of Savonarola, by PassagHo, was placed here in 1881. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left of the staircase is a small but fine marble doorway (15th cent.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leading into a small ante-chamber (Madonna in mosaic over the doorway),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence we enter the Sala dei Dugento (1411), with a ceiling by Michel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ozzo, marble doors, and a series of scenes from the history of Joseph in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tapestry, from drawings by Bronzino. — The 'Quartiere di Leone X.' (private{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apartments of the Medici) are not always shown to the public. — On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Second Floor is the Sala de1 Gigli with beautiful door-posts in marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Benedetto da Majano, to whom the intarsias of Dante and Petrarch on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wings of the door are also ascribed. The frescoes are by Domenico{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghirlandajo , and represent St. Zenobius and heroes of a Roman history,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a fine architectural framework. A very handsome door adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intarsia work (portraits of Dante and Petrarch) and enclosed in a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble framework by Benedetto da Majano now leads into the Sala d'Udi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enza, which contains frescoes by Salviati (story of Camillus). Above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door is a statuette of Peace. Then the Cappella de' Priori di S. Ber¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nardo, with a ceiling painted in imitation of mosaic by Rid. Ghirlandajo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a crucifix over the altar attributed to Giov. da Bologna. — Adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Sala de' Gigli are the Sala delle Carte Geografiche and a room adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with grotesque scenes by Pocetti.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the N. corner of the edifice is a lion in marble, a modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copy of the original in bronze by Donatello, known as 11 Marzocco,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and now preserved in the Museo Nazionale (p. 401). To the left is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the *Great Fountain with Neptune and Tritons, erected by Barto¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lommeo Ammanati under Cosimo I. (1564-75). The fountain oc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cupies the site of the stake at which Savonarola and two other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dominican monks were burned on 23rd May, 1498. Adjoining it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Equestrian Statue of Cosimo I. (PI. E, 5) in bronze, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bas-reliefs, by Giovanni da Bologna, 1594. — Opposite the statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the *Palazzo Uguccione, with a rustica lower story and coupled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pilasters between the windows, erected about 1550 from designs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attributed to Raphael.{% endraw %}

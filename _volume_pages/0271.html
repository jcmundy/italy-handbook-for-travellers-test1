{% raw %}to the N.W. (carriage-road to Fogliardi, then a bridle-path). Fourteen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels or stations of various forms, adorned with frescoes and groups in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stucco, have been erected along the broad path, by which the monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and church on the mountain (2841 ft.) are attained. The view hence is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not less celebrated than the peculiar sanctity of the spot. The small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lakes of Comabbio, Biandrone, and Monate, that of Varese, two arms of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lago Maggiore, part of the Lake of Como, and the expansive and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fruitful plain as far as Milan are visible. — A far more comprehensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view, including the glacier-world also, is obtained (best by morning-light){% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Tre Croci (3966 ft.), 1 hr. to the N.W. of the Madonna. Several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taverns adjoin the monastery. Donkeys and guides (unnecessary) are to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be found at the foot of the mountain. Comp. the Map.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}4 or 372 fr.). — The road crosses the Olona and then the Lanza, near its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}influx into the Olona, and ascends rapidly to Malnate. Farther on it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes Binago and Solbiate, and attains its culminating point (900 ft. above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lake of Como) at Olgiate. It then turns E. to Lurate Abbate, Lucino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Rebbio, traversing a luxuriantly fertile district with numerous villas.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}by the ruins of the Castello Baradello, and descends through the long S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suburb of S. Bartolommeo to Como (p. 148).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the right of the road. Farther on, the Boesio, which flows through the{% endraw %}

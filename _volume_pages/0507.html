{% raw %}Opposite the cathedral is the octagonal *Battistero [San Gio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vanni Battista; PL 3), erected after 1339 by Cellino di Nese in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian-Gothic style, according to Vasari from a design by Andrea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisano. On the exterior is a pulpit. The large square font (older{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the building, and probably dating from 1256) is embellished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with richly-decorated slabs (others of the same kind on the wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right). The principal portal is also worthy of notice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent is the *Palazzo Pretorio (PL 4), formerly del Podesta,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a building of the 14th cent. , now containing the courts of justice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The quadrangle is enclosed by four round arches; the arcades are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with numerous painted armorial bearings of the Podesta's,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkable for their admirable heraldic style, restored in 1844.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}cient tribunal, bearing the inscription of 1507 :{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hie locus odit, amat, punit, conservat, honorat,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nequitiam, leges, crimina, jura, probos.{% endraw %}

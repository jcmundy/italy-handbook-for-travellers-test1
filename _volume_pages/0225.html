{% raw %}of the crops indeed is hardly possible, except when the summer is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unusually cold. Meadows yield as many as twelve crops in the year,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their growth being unretarded by the winter. The so-called Parmesan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cheese is one of the well-known products of Lombardy. In the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages the importance of Milan was due to its woollen industries, but sheep-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breeding has in modern times been superseded by the silk-culture, an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}industry which has so materially increased the wealth of the country,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that it used to be said during the Austrian regime, that the army and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}officers lived on mulberry leaves, as their produce alone sufficed to pay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the land taxes. Under these circumstances the population is unusually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dense, being about 380 persons to the sq. mile, exclusive of the capital.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The central situation, and the wealth of the country, have ever ren¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dered it an apple of discord to the different European nations. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earliest period known to us, it was occupied by the Etruscans, an Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}race, which about the 6th cent. B.C. was subjugated or expelled by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Celts from the W. These immigrants founded Mediolanum (Milan), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traces of their language still survive in the modern dialect of the coun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}try. It was but slowly that the Italians subdued or assimilated these{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreigners, and it was not till B.C. 220 that the Romans extended their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supremacy to the banks of the Po. In the following century they consti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tuted Gallia Cisalpina a province, on which Caesar conferred the rights{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of citizenship in B.C. 46. Throughout the whole of the imperial epoch{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these regions of Northern Italy formed the chief buttress of the power of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome. Since the 4th cent. Milan has surpassed Rome in extent, and,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in many respects, in importance also. It became an imperial residence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the church founded here by St. Ambrosius (who became bishop in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}374), long maintained its independence of the popes. The Goths, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards the Lombards, made Pavia their capital, but their domination,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after lasting for two centuries, was overthrown by Charlemagne in 774. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lombard dialect also contains a good many words derived from the German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(thus, bron, gast, grit, pib, smessor, stora-, and stosa-, from the German Brun-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nen, Gast, Greis, Pflug, Messer, storen, and stossen). The crown of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lombardy was worn successively by the Franconian and by the German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kings, the latter of whom, particularly the Othos, did much to promote{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the prosperity of the towns. When the rupture between the emperor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the pope converted the whole of Italy into a Guelph and Ghibelline{% endraw %}
{% raw %}camp, Milan formed the headquarters of the former, and Cremona those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the latter party, and the power of the Hohenstaufen proved to be no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}match for the Lombard walls. The internal dissensions between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nobles and the townspeople, however, led to the creation of several new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principalities. In 1287 Matteo degli Visconti of Milan (whose family{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was so called from their former office of 'vicecomites', or archiepiscopal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}judges) was nominated 'Capitano del Popolo', and in 1294 appointed gov¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ernor of Lombardy by the German King. Although banished for a time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Guelph family Della Torre, both he and his sons and their poster-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nty contrived to assert their right to the Signoria. The greatest of this{% endraw %}

{% raw %}his uncle in 1385, and extended his duchy to Pisa and Bologna and even{% endraw %}

{% raw %}AJACCIO.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corsica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The family of Bonaparte appears to have emigrated in the 16th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Sarzana in Tuscany, perhaps with the powerful Malaspinas, to Corsica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Messire Francesco Bonaparte, the first member of the family who resided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Corsica, died at, Ajaccio in 1567. Napoleon's father, Carlo Maria Bona¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parte, born at Ajaccio, 29th March, 1746, was educated at a school founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Paoli at Corte, and afterwards studied law at Pisa. He then became an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}advocate at Ajaccio, where he enjoyed considerable popularity, but was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}soon appointed by Paoli his secretary at Corte. After the disastrous battle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Ponte Nuovo, 9th May, 1769, in consequence of which Corsica lost its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}independence to France, Carlo fled with his young wife Letitia Ramolino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Monte Rotondo. He shortly afterwards returned to Ajaccio, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the French General Marbeuf, the conqueror of Corsica, accorded him{% endraw %}
{% raw %}protection, and where, about two months later, Napoleon was born. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1777 Carlo was appointed deputy of the nobility for Corsica, and travelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}via, Florence to Paris. He died at Montpellier in February, 1785. Napoleon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then 16 years of age, having quitted the school at Brienne two years pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}viously, was studying at the Ecole Militaire at Paris. The letter of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dolence which he wrote to his mother on the occasion is still extant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the storming of the Bastille in 1789 and the great subsequent crisis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Napoleon with his elder brother Joseph warmly espoused the popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause at Ajaccio. He then repaired to Marseilles to welcome Paoli on his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}return from exile, and the latter predicted on this occasion that a great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destiny was in store for the youth. In 1791 Napoleon obtained the com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mand of the newly-constituted Corsican battalions, and in this capacity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}practically began his military career. In 1792, Paoli, dissatisfied with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the proceedings of Napoleon, sent him to S. Bonifacio, to join the expe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dition against Sardinia. This, however, proved an utter failure, and on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}22nd January, 1793, Napoleon narrowly escaped being slain by insurgents.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Shortly afterwards he broke off his connection with Paoli and was com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pelled to quit Corsica with his family. During the zenith of his power{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Emperor evinced little partiality for his native island, which he vi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sited for the last time on 29th September, 1799, on his return from Egypt.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}During his exile in the island of St. Helena, however, his thoughts appear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently to have reverted to Corsica. 'What reminiscences Corsica has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, to me'.' he was frequently heard to exclaim; 'I still think with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pleasure of its mountains and its beautiful scenery; I still remember the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fragrance which it exhales.' Autommarchi, Napoleon's physician in St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Helena, and the priest Vignale, who performed the last offices of religion,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were Corsicans, and shared the fate of their illustrious compatriot.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cathedral, where Napoleon was baptised on 22nd July,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1771, dates from 1585.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In a side street of the Place du Marche" is situated the palace of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Pozzo di Borgo, one of the most distinguished Corsican families.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carlo Andrea Pozzo di Borgo, born on 8th March, 1768, an early friend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Napoleon, a democrat and adherent of Paoli, afterwards became the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emperor's bitterest enemy. He subsequently became a Russian counsellor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of state, and in 1802 was created a count and appointed ambassador, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which capacity he indefatigably devoted his energies to opposing his am¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bitious countryman. He died at Paris in 1842.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Rue du Marche* leads from the Place du Marche to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Place Bonaparte (formerly Diamant), adorned with an equestrian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Statue of the Emperor with his four brothers, in bronze, by Barye,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1865. — The tree-lined Cours Napoleon, which crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rue du Marche at the Place Bonaparte, is prolonged thence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the high-road to Bastia. To the right, outside the gate, is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monument of General Abbatucci, a Corsican who fell in 1796,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whilst defending the town of Huningen. The Botanic Garden, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Cours Napoleon, merits a visit.{% endraw %}

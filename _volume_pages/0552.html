{% raw %}VII. Saloon : In the centre : Donatello, a Cupid treading on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}snake ; *Giov. da Bologna, Mercury, a bold but thoroughly success¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful work. *Andr. Verrocchio, David, attractive by its truth, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tender handling of the youthful limbs, and the Leonardesque head,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but not so dignified either in bearing or form as Donatello's David{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Room VI. By the walls, to the left of the entrance : Crucifixion,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a basrelief (partly gilded) ; two cabinets with imitations of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Renaissance sculpture ; below, Hercules and Antaeus by A. Pol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lajuolo ; between these a colossal bust of the Grand Duke Cosimo I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in bronze, by Benvenuto Cellini; the model in wax and bronze for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that master's Perseus (somewhat differing from it; p. 377") is also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}placed here; Bertoldo (pupil of Donatello), Ancient battle-scene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in relief; below, cinerary urn with angels by Lor. Ghiberti. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cabinets by this and the following wall contain portraits of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}15th cent, and other admirable small reliefs. End-wall: Abraham's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sacrifice by Lor. Ghiberti, and the same by Fil. Brunelleschi, spe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cimens produced in their competition for the execution of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gates of the baptistery (p. 395).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The composition of Ghiberti is the less harmonious but the calmer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the two. Its dignified draped figures, especially that of Isaac, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}full of a true antique feeling for beauty, while in Brunelleschi's relief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the principal figures are represented in violent movement, and Isaac is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}besides remarkably ugly. The subordinate figures, including the ram, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also in positions of over-strained activity. In technical execution Ghiberti{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is superior.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between the two last, a *Crucifixion, by Agostino di Duccio (?),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a Frieze of children by Danese; below, a recumbent figure of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mariano Soccino in bronze, by Lor. Vecchietta. — Window-wall:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Francis Xavier, Death of Joseph, and St. Theresa, reliefs by Sol-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dani; in front, two handsome candelabra. —By the exit: *Bust{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Michael Angelo in bronze (once the property of his servant).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turkey-cock and eagle by Pietro Tacca (17th cent.). — We now{% endraw %}

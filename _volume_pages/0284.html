{% raw %}Varallo (1515 ft.; Posta, well spoken of; Italia; *Croce Bianca,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moderate; Falcone Nero), the principal village (3200 inhab.) in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the valley of the Sesia, a stream rising on the Monte Rosa, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the chief tributaries of the Po , into which it flows beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Casale (p. 77), but frequently dry in summer. A bridge with three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arches crosses the river. The old town and the Sacro Monte are very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesque when seen through the arches of the bridge. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collegiate church contains an altar-piece representing the Nuptials{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Catharine by Gaudenzio Ferrari, who was born here in 1484{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1549). The churches of *S. Maria delle Grazie (in the choir),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria di Loreto, and S. Marco also contain frescoes by this master{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(those in the last being of his earlier period).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Sacro Monte, the object of numerous pilgrimages, rises in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}immediate vicinity of the town. It is attained in 74 hr. by a path shaded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by beautiful trees, but the enjoyment is somewhat marred by the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portunities of beggars. The summit, surmounted by a chapel and crucifix,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands a magnificent view of the surrounding mountains towering one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above another. Besides the church there are a great number of Chapels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Oratories on the summit and slopes of the Sacro Monte, many of them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}buried among the trees, containing scenes from the life of the Saviour, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terracotta, with life-size figures arranged in groups. Each chapel is devoted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to a different subject; the 1st to the Fall, the 2nd to the Annunciation,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and so on to the 46th , containing the Entombment of the Virgin. Some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the frescoes by Pellegrino Tibaldi and Gaudenzio Ferrari (Chapel of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Magi, *Chapel of the Crucifixion) are worthy of inspection. This '■Nuova{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gerusalemme nel Sacro Monte di Varallo' was founded by Bernardino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Caloto, a Milanese nobleman, with the sanction of Pope Innocent VIII.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It did not become a pilgrim-resort until after the visits of Card. Borromeo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 167) in 1578 and 1584, from which period most of the chapels date.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}accessible (comp. Baedeker s Switzerland).{% endraw %}

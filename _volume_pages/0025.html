{% raw %}SIGHTS AND THEATRES.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}xxi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The usual viands for lunch are ham, sausages, cutlets, beefsteaks, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eggs (uova da bere, soft; toste, hard; uova al piatto, fried).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ices (sorbetto or gelato) of every possible variety are supplied at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cafes at 30-90 c. per portion; or half a portion (mezzo) may be ordered.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Granita, or half-frozen ice (limonata, of lemons; aranciata, of oranges), is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}much in vogue in the forenoon. The waiters, who expects a sou or more,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}according to the amount of the payment, are apt to be inaccurate in chang¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing money.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The principal Parisian newspapers are to be found at all the larger{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cafes, English rarely.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cigars in Italy are a monopoly of Government, and bad. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prices of the home-made cigars (Scelti Romani, Virginias, Vevays,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pressati, Cavours, Napoletani, Toscani, Minghetti, etc.) vary from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 to 15 c. Good imported cigars may be bought at the best shops{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the large towns for 25-60 c. each. — Passers-by are at liberty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to avail themselves of the light burning in every tobacconist's,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}without making any purchase.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}X. Sights, Theatres, etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Churches are open in the morning till 12orl2. 30, and generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again from 4 to 7 p. m. Visitors may inspect the works of art{% endraw %}
{% raw %}even during divine service, provided they move about noiselessly,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and keep aloof from the altar where the clergy are officiating. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the occasion of festivals the works of art are often entirely concealed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the temporary decorations. The verger (sagrestano, or nonzolo){% endraw %}
{% raw %}receives a fee of 30 - 50 c. from a single traveller, more from a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}party, if his services are required.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museums, picture-galleries, and other collections are usually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open from 10 to 4 o'clock. By a law passed in 1875 all the col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lections which belong to government are open on week-days at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}charge of 1 fr., and on Sundays (and sometimes on Thursdays also){% endraw %}
{% raw %}gratis. Artists are admitted without charge. The attendants are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forbidden to accept gratuities, but are sometimes very importunate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in endeavouring to sell photographs (dear and often bad).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The collections are closed on the following public holidays: New{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Year's Day, Epiphany (6th Jan.), the Monday and Tuesday during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carnival, Palm Sunday, Easter Sunday, Ascension Day, Whitsunday, Fete{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de Dieu (Corpus Christi), the Festa dello Statute (first Sunday in June),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Assumption of the Virgin (15th Aug.), Nativity of the Virgin (8th Sept.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Festival of the Annunciation (25th Mar.), All Saints'1 Day (1st Nov.), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on Christmas Day. A good many other days are also sometimes observed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as holidays, such as the Thursday before the Carnival (Giovedi grasso),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the day sacred to the local patron-saint, and the birthdays of the king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(14th Mar.) and queen (20th Nov.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Valets de Place (servitori di piazza) may be hired at 5-6 fr. per{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day. They are generally respectable and trustworthy, but, as they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are seldom good judges of what is really worth seeing, the traveller{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should specify to them the places he desires to visit. Their services{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may generally well be dispensed with by those who are not pressed{% endraw %}

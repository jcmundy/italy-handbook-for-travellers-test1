{% raw %}The train then crosses the small river Pesa. On the left, before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reaching Montelupo, we perceive the Villa Ambrogiana, erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ferdinand I. on the site of an ancient castle of the Ardinghelli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and surmounted by towers and pinnacles. 45 M. Montelupo; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of this place was fortified by the Florentines in 1203 in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}order to keep in check the hostile Capraj a on the opposite side.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hence the appellation Montelupo, 'mountain of the wolf, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was desirous of devouring the goat (capra).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train now crosses the Arno, and slowly winds through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defile of the Gonfolina, through which the Arno flows. The heights{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are clad with rock-pines, below which is quarried the pietra Sere¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}na, a kind of sandstone frequently employed in the construction of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the palaces of Florence. The Ombrone, which falls into the Arno,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is next crossed. 52 M. Signa, with its grey towers and pinnacles,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded in 1377 by the Florentines to command the road at this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point. This place, as well as the opposite village of Lastra, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}noted for its straw-plait. — Near (54 M.) S. Donnino is Brozzi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with numerous villas which proclaim the proximity of the capital.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train now approaches the Cascine, the park of Florence, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enters the station near S. Maria Novella.{% endraw %}

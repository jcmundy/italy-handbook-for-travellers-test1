{% raw %}Donato Montorfano (Crucifixion) of 1495, opposite the Last Supper,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is in much better condition and affords an insight into the state of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painting in Lombardy before the advent of Leonardo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Deplorable as is the condition of the Last Supper, the chief work{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed by Leonardo during his stay at Milan, the original alone ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hibits to its full extent the emotions which the master intended to ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}press, and which even the best copies fail to reproduce. The motive of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the work has been well explained by Goethe: 'The shock by which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artist represents the company at the sacred repast as deeply agitated has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been produced by the Master's words, One of you shall betray me. They{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been pronounced; the whole party is in dismay, while he himself{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bows his head with downcast eyes. His whole attitude, the motion of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his arms and hands , all seem to repeat with heavenly resignation, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his silence to confirm, the mournful words — It cannot be otherwise.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One of you shall betray me!' Comp. also p. Hi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via delle Oche and the Via S. Vittore lead hence to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S.E. to the Piazza S. Ambrogio, with the church of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Ambrogio (PL C, 6), founded by St. Ambrose in the 4th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent, on the ruins of a temple of Bacchus, and dating in its present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romanesque form, with its peculiar galleries, from the 12th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The fine atrium in front of the church, containing ancient tomb¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stones, inscriptions , and half-obliterated frescoes (probably by Ze-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nale), seems, like the facade, to have preserved the architectural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms of the original building. The gates of this church are said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be those which St. Ambrose closed against the Emp. Theodosius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after the cruel massacre of Thessalonica (389). There is a portrait{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the saint on the left side of the principal entrance. The Lom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bard kings and German emperors formerly caused themselves to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowned here with the iron crown , which since the time of Fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derick Barbarossa has been preserved at Monza (p. 147).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. On the right and left of the side entrance on the right:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes by Gaudenzio Ferrari, representing the Bearing of the Cross, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three Maries, and the Descent from the Cross. 2nd Chapel on the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Cappella delle Dame): a kneeling *Statue of St. Marcellina, by Pacelti.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5th Chapel on the right: *Legend of St. George, frescoes by Bernardino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lanini. In the entrance to the sacristy is the Cappella S. Satiro with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosaics of the 5th century. 6th Chapel: Madonna with St. John and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jerome, by Luini. By the pulpit are a bronze eagle, a figure of St. Ambrose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(12th cent.), and an early Christian sarcophagus of the 6th century. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canopy over the high-altar, which is adorned with reliefs of the 8th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently gilded, is borne by four columns of porphyry. The high-altar still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}retains its original decoration intact, consisting of reliefs on silver and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gold ground (in front), enriched with enamel and gems, executed in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carlovingian period by Volfoinus, a German (covered, shown only on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}payment of 3 fr.). In front of the high-altar is the tombstone of Emp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lewis II. (d. 875). The choir contains an ancient episcopal throne. By{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the high-altar is an *Ecce Homo, al fresco, by Luini, under glass. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Tribuna "Mosaics of the 9th cent., earlier than those of St. Mark's at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venice: Christ in the centre, at the sides the history of St. Ambrose. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the entrance to the Crypt, Christ among the scribes, a fresco by Bor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gognone. The modernised crypt contains the tombs of SS. Ambrose, Pro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tasius, and Gervasius. The brazen serpent on a column in the nave is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to be the one which was raised by Moses in the wilderness.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Mantua, Ital. Mantova, a very ancient town founded by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etruscans, with 28,150 inhab. (3000 Jews), is a provincial capital{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and strongly fortified place, bounded on the N.W. by the Lago{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Superiore, on the N.E. by the Lago di Mezzo, on the E. by the Lago{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Inferiore, and on the S. and S.W. by marshy land, which in case{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a siege is capable of being laid under water.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mantua is mentioned in ancient times as the home of Virgil, who is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to have been born at the village of Pietole (the ancient Andes?), 3 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the S.E., but it was not till the middle ages that it became a place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of importance. In the conflicts of the Hohenstaufen period the town em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}braced the cause of the Guelphs. In 1328 the citizens elected Luigi, Lord{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Gonzaga, as the 'Capitano del Popolo', and to him the town was in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}debted for its prosperity. The Gonzagas fought successfully against Milan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Venice, and succeeded in extending their territory, while they were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the liberal patrons of art and science. Giovanni Francesco II. (1407-1444),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first marquis, invited the learned Vittorino da Feltre to reside at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mantua, and through him made his court a renowned centre of culture and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}education. The beautiful and accomplished Isabella d'Este (1474-1539),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sister of Alphonso, Duke of Ferrara, and mother of Eleonora of TJrbino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was the wife of Giovanni Francesco III. (1484-1519). She carried on a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lively correspondence with many of the most eminent men of her time, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collected with most judicious taste numerous valuable books, pictures,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and antiquities. In 1530 Federigo II. was raised to the rank of duke by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charles V., and in 1536 was invested with the county of Monteferrato{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1540); the chief monument of his reign is the Palazzo del Te (p. 213).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1627, when Charles de Nevers, a member of a French collateral line,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}took possession of the throne, the Mantuan war of succession broke out,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Emperor Ferdinand II. declared the fief forfeited. On 18th July,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1630, Mantua was taken by storm and sacked by the Austrians. Although{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the emperor, being hard pressed by the Swedes, was obliged to conclude{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a peace in 1631, the town never recovered from this blow. Carlo IV.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last duke, having taken the French side in the Spanish war of suc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cession , was declared an outlaw in 1703, and Monteferrato was aw arded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Piedmont, while Mantua was annexed to Austria, and afterwards be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}came the chief support of the Imperial domination in Italy. After a long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and obstinate defence by General Wurmser, the fortress capitulated to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French on 2nd February 1797. In accordance with the Peace of Villafranca{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Austrians retained Mantua, although deprived of the rest of Lombardy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but they were compelled to cede it to Italy in 1866.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Climate. Cannes is protected on the N.W. by the Esterel Mts. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. and N.E. by other ranges of hills, but the beach is somewhat ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posed to the Mistral and the N.E. wind. It is thus at times, particularly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in spring , cooler and more windy than Mentone or San Remo, but its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter-climate is usually mild, equable, and dry. The warmest and most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sheltered part of Cannes, and consequently that most suitable for patients{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with pulmonary complaints, is the space between the N. side of the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the village of Le Cannet. Its comfortable accommodation, its excellent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drinking-water, and the numerous pretty drives in the vicinity, have co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}operated with its sheltered situation in making Cannes a most popular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter-resort, especially among the upper classes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cannes, a small but rapidly increasing town with 15,000 inhab.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picturesquely situated on the Golfe de la Napoule, consists chiefly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a main street, parallel with which , along the coast, runs the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Boulevard de la Plage, terminating on the W. in the Cours, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'place' with promenades and fountains. The W. end of the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is chiefly occupied by English families. The best French society is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also well represented.{% endraw %}

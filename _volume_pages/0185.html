{% raw %}The -'Carriage Road along the charming Riviera di Ponente, the famous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Route de la Corniche, will however still be preferred by many travellers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if not for the whole distance, at least for the most beautiful parts of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}route, especially where the view is lost in passing through the numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}railway-tunnels, as between Savona and Loano, and between San Remo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Nice. A carriage-and-pair for the whole journey, which takes 3 days,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}costs about 150 fr., with gratuity. A carefully-worded written con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tract should be drawn up with the coach-hirer in Genoa (Guelfo Andrea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rango, near the Piazza dell' Annunziata, may be recommended). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller should take a picnic basket (cold meat, etc.) with him. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This journey is very attractive. The road affords a delightful succession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of varied landscapes, traversing bold and lofty promontories, wooded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hills, and richly cultivated plains near the coast. At some places it passes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}precipitous and frowning cliffs, the bases of which are washed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surf of the Mediterranean, while the summits are crowned with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}venerable ruins of towers, erected in bygone ages for protection against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pirates. At other places extensive plantations of olives, with their gro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tesque and gnarled stems, bright green pine-forests, and luxuriant growths{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of figs, vines, citrons, oranges, oleanders, myrtles, and aloes meet the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view, and even palms are occasionally seen (at S. Remo and Bordighera).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Many of the towns are picturesquely situated on gently sloping heights{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Porto Maurizio, S. Remo, Bordighera, Ventimiglia); others, commanded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by ancient strongholds, are perched like nests among the rocks (Rocca-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bruna, Eza). Small churches and chapels peering from the sombre foliage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of cypresses, and gigantic grey pinnacles of rock rising proudly above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the smiling plains, frequently enhance the charms of the scenery. Finally,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the vast expanse of the sea, with its ever varying hues, forms one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief attractions. At one time it is bathed in a flood of sunshine, at an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other its beautiful blue colour arrests the eye; or while the shore immedi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ately below the spectator is lashed with wild breakers, the snowy crests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the waves are gradually softened to view in the purple distance.{% endraw %}

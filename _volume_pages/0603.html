{% raw %}The monastery of Vallombrosa was founded by <S. Giovanni Gualberto,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the scion of a wealthy and powerful family of Florence, who after a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}career of youthful profligacy resolved to devote the remainder of his life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the most austere acts of penance. His brother Hugo having fallen by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the knife of an assassin, Gualberto was bound by the customs of the age{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to follow the bloody law of retaliation. Descending one Good Friday{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the church of S. Miniato near Florence, accompanied by armed fol¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lowers, he suddenly encountered the assassin at a narrow part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road. The latter fell at his feet and implored for mercy. The knight,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suddenly moved by a generous impulse, forgave his enemy, and resolved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for ever to renounce the world and its passions. He accordingly retired{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the cloister of S. Miniato; but finding the discipline there too lax, he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}betook himself to this lonely spot and founded Vallombrosa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II Paradisino, or Le Cette, a small hermitage situated on a rock,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}!/4 hr. to the left above the monastery, and now uninhabited, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mands an admirable survey of the latter, which lies 266 ft. below,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and of the broad valley of the Arno as far as Florence, half of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cathedral-dome of which is visible behind a hill. The horizon is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bounded by the marble-hills of Carrara.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Another good point of view is situated 1 M. to the S. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monastery. The path leads to the left of the inn, and immediate¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ly beyond it passes a spring (to the right), then traverses the wood,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and reaches a projecting rock commanding an extensive view of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Arno. The pretty villa of De Resi is next passed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ascent of the Pratomagno from Vallombrosa occupies 4-5 hrs. (guide{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2 fr.). The path traverses dense pine-forest, then dwarfed beech-under¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wood, and finally green pastures. The view from the summit, which is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5323 ft. above the sea-level, is obstructed on the N. and S. by peaks of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equal elevation. To the E. lies the green Casentino Valley, bounded on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N.E. by the lofty Monte Falterona, where the Arno rises; W. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fertile and richly-cultivated valley of the Arno stretches as far as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dome of the cathedral of Florence, beyond which the blue Mediterranean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is visible in the extreme distance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Pratomagno a steep path descends through woods and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ravines (lVz hr.), skirting the brook Solano, passing Cetica and several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other mountain-villages, and leading to the picturesque market-town of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Niccolb, commanded by the ancient fort of that name, and situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at. the confluence of the Solano and Arno, where the fertile Casentino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expands. A good carriage-road leads from S. Niccolo to Poppi (33/4 M.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carr. 2'/2 fr.). We cross the river, beyond which a good road leads to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, passing the ancient church of Campaldino, where in a sanguinary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conflict, on 11th June, 1289, Dante distinguished himself by his bravery,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and aided his Guelph countrymen to crush the might of Arezzo and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscan Ghibellines. The next place of importance is Poppi (see below).{% endraw %}

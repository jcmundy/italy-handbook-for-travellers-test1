{% raw %}102 Route 16. SAVONA. From Genoa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20y2 M. Varazze, or Voragine, a town with 9800 inhab., is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerable ship-building place. The coast on both sides of it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rocky, and there are numerous cuttings and tunnels.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}23 M. Celle; 25 M. Albissola, at the mouth of the Sansobbia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where pottery is largely manufactured.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}27*/2 M. Savona (Rail. Restaurant; Albergo Svizzero, Roma,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both well spoken of; Italia), a town with 16,000 inhab., the capi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tal of the Montenotte department under Napoleon I., is charmingly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated amidst lemon and orange gardens. The harbour, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}manded by a fort, presents a busy scene. The Cathedral of 1604{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains several good pictures. The handsome theatre, erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1853, is dedicated to the poet Chiabrera (1552-1637), a native of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the place. The church of Madonna degli Angeli affords a fine view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the town. Santuario di Savona, see p. 84.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Savona to Turin, see p. 81; to Alessandria, see p. 82.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}31 M. Vado. On this side of the extensive Capo Bergeggi a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}^Retrospect of the Riviera as far as Genoa is enjoyed. Then a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunnel and galleries, through the arches of which the sea and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small island of Bergeggi are seen. The construction of the line{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was attended with much difficulty here, and several long tunnels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are traversed. 35 M. Spotorno; 37 M. Noli, a small town shaded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by dense olive-groves, with the ruins of a castle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}42 M. Finalmarina (Hotel Garibaldi, indifferent) is the seaport{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and principal part of the town of Finale, which consists of three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}different villages; it contains a cathedral, by Bernini, with double{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns of white marble, a dome, and rich gilding. To the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies Finalborgo , the oldest part, with a castle; and farther to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. is Finalpia. In the neighbourhood are interesting caverns, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prehistoric remains. — 43 M. Borgio Verezzi; 45V2 M. Pietraligure;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}48 M. Loano, with a ruined castle; to the right of the line are two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suppressed monasteries, of which Monte Carmelo, the higher, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Dorias in 1609, commands a fine view. The large twelve-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sided church of the village was also erected by the Dorias. Beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(50 M.) Ceriale, with its market-gardens, the mountains recede.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}53 M. Albenga [Albergo Reale, Vittoria, both thoroughly Italian),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Albingaunum of the Romans, an ancient town and episcopal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence, 1 M. to the W. of the station. Between the station and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town are extensive remains of the Ponte Lungo, a Roman bridge.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Several chateaux of the old noblesse with lofty towers; cathedral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with towers and elegant facade, all of brick. — To the left, from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea, rises the rocky island of Gallinaria, crowned with a tower.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The line now quits the coast and traverses olive groves, vine¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yards, and orchards. The train crosses the Centa and skirts the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promontory of S. Croce. Several tunnels.{% endraw %}

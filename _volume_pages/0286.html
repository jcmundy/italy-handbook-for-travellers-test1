{% raw %}CERTOSA DI PAVIA. 28. Route. 177{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porta and Agostino Busti, surnamed II Bambaja, to whom the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal portal is ascribed), and Cristofano Solari, surnamed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II Gobbo. The plinth is adorned with medallions of Roman empe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rors , above which are reliefs representing Biblical history and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scenes from the life of Gian Galeazzo. Below the beautiful win¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dows is a row of angel's heads, and above them are niches with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous statues. This is unquestionably the finest decorative{% endraw %}
{% raw %}work of the kind in N. Italy, although inferior to the facades of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cathedrals of Orvieto and Siena, especially as the upper part is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wanting. The reliefs are on the whole superior to the statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The body of the church, begun in 1396 by Marco di Campione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Gothic style, consists of a nave with aisles and 14 chapels,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is surmounted by a dome, borne by ten slender columns. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior, 272 ft. long and 174 ft. broad, is sumptuously and taste¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fully fitted up. The handsome coloured enrichments were probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Borgognone; the mosaic pavement is modern.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Chapels and altars are richly adorned with valuable columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and precious stones. 2nd Chapel on the right: good altar-piece in six{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sections by Macrino d'Alba (1496); 4th Chapel on the right, "'Crucifixion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Ambrogio Borgognone; 5th Chapel on the right, "St. Sirus with four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saints, by the same. The 2nd Chapel on the left (counting from the en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trance) formerly contained a picture by Perugino in six sections, of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the central part, above, representing !;'God the Father, is alone original,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the other parts being now in France and England. The other frescoes and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings by Borgognone, Procaccini, Guercino, Bianchi, Crespi, father and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}son, and others are of no great value.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The transept and choir are separated from the rest of the church by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a beautiful Screen of iron and bronze. Right Transept : magnificent ''Monu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment of Giangaleazzo Visconti, designed in 1490 by Galeazzo Pellegrini, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed chiefly by Antonio da Amadeo and Giacomo della Porta, and not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed till 1562. Left Transept : Monuments of Lodovico Moro and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his wife *Beatrice d'Este (d. 1497), by Crist. Solari. — The *Choir con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains a fine altar with carving of the 16th century. The *Choir-stalls are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with figures of apostles and saints, from drawings by Borgognone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The handsome bronze candelabra in front of them are by Libero Fontana.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The old sacristy to the left of the choir contains a fine carved ivory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar-piece in upwards of 60 sections by Leon, degli Ubriachi of Florence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(16th cent.). — The door to the right of the choir, handsomely framed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in marble, leads to the Lavatorio, which contains a rich fountain and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Madonna and Child in fresco by Bern. Luini. To the right of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lavatory is a small burial-place.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sagrestia Nuova, or Oratorio, is entered from the S. end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the transept: *Altar-piece, an Assumption by A. Solario, but the upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part is said to be by Giulio Campi of Cremona. Over the door, Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enthroned, by Bart. Montagna; the side-pictures by Borgognone.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The front part of the *Cloisters (della Fontana) possesses slender{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble columns and charming decorations in terracotta. Fine view hence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the side of the church and the right transept with its trilateral end. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Refectory is also situated here. — Around the large Cloisters , farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, are situated the 24 small houses occupied by the few remaining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monks, each consisting of three rooms with a small garden.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The battle of Pavia, at which Francis I. of France was taken{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prisoner by Lannoy, a general of Charles V., took place near the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Certosa on 24th Feb. 1525.{% endraw %}

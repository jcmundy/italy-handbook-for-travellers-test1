{% raw %}The History of the country is closely interwoven with that of its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dynasty. The House of Savoy (or Casa Sabauda), a family of German{% endraw %}
{% raw %}origin, professing even to trace their descent from the Saxon Duke{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Wittekind, the opponent of Charlemagne, first became conspicuous among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the nobles of Upper Burgundy about the year 1000. Humbert I. (d. about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1050) is generally regarded as the founder of the dynasty. In 1101 his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descendants were created imperial counts of Savoy by Henry IV., and by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}judiciously espousing the cause of the pope and the emperor alternately,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}they gradually succeeded in extending their supremacy over Turin, Aosta,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Susa, Ivrea, and Nice. In consequence of a law passed by Amadeus V.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Great, in 1367, which settled the succession on the male line in the order{% endraw %}

{% raw %}subdivisions of the country were at length united. In 1416, during the{% endraw %}

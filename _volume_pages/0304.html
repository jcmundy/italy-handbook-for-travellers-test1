{% raw %}BRESCIA. 33. Route 189{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 10 min. after the steamboat (p. 186) has quitted Riva,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fall of the Ponale, mentioned p. 188, comes into view. Torbole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 188) lies to the left. The steamer now steers S. to Mal-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cesine (2100 inhab.), a good harbour on the E. bank, with an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}old castle of Charlemagne, which was afterwards a robbers'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stronghold. Goethe , while sketching this ruin , narrowly escaped{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being arrested as a spy by the Venetian government. The castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has since been restored. Beyond it is the rock of Isoletto, then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cassone, and a little farther the small island of Trimelone. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}next places of importance are Castello, S. Giovanni, Castelletto,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all belonging to the parish of Brenzone, Montagna (somewhat in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land), and Torri. The banks gradually become flatter. The pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}montory of San Vigilio, sheltered from the N. wind by the Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baldo (p. 188), extends far into the lake, and is the most beauti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful point of view on the E. bank. The surrounding hills are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}planted with vines, olives, and fig-trees. The village of Garda{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1500 inhab.), beautifully situated in a bay at the influx, of the Te-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sino, which descends from the Monte Baldo, gives its name to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lake. To the S. in the distance is the peninsula of Sermione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 186). The next places are Bardolino (2500 inhab.) with a har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bour, Cisano, and Lazise (3100 inhab.), another harbour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peschiera (see p. 185), at the efflux of the Mincio from the lake,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a station on the Milan and Verona railway. The station is on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. side of the town, not far from the landing-place.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Brescia (515 ft.), a manufacturing town with 33,400 inhab.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(commune 60,700), the capital of a province, and the residence of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a bishop, is beautifully situated at the foot of the Alps, and its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous fountains of limpid water lend it an additional charm.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Iron wares, and particularly weapons (hence 'Brescia armata') form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the staple commodities, and a considerable number of the firearms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used by the Italian army are made here. The woollen, linen, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silk manufactories are also worthy of mention.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Bridges. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 375{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. of the Porta S. Gallo. The broad Viale, which under various{% endraw %}
{% raw %}names encircles the town on the right bank and occupies the site{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the old fortifications, is not yet entirely completed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bridges. The oldest of the six bridges which connect the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banks of the Arno is the Ponte alle Grazie (PI. E, F, 6, 7), con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed in 1235, the scene of the union effected between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Guelphs and Ghibellines in 1283. The Ponte Vecchio (PI. D, E, 6 ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 429), which is said to have existed as early as the Roman period,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was reconstructed, after its repeated demolition, by Taddeo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gaddi in 1362, consists of three arches. The Ponte S. Trinita (PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}D, 5; p. 428), was originally erected in 1252, and rebuilt soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after 1567 by Bartolommeo Ammanati. The Ponte alia Carraja{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. C, 4), originally built in 1218, destroyed together with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ponte Vecchio by an inundation in 1333, and restored in 1337, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again erected in 1559 by Ammanati by order of Cosimo I. Besides{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these, two Suspension Bridges have recently been constructed, one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at each end of the town (toll 5c, carriages 42c).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The river is bordered on both sides by broad and handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quays, called the Lung' Arno, of which the different parts are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lung' Arno Corsini, the Lung' Arno Soderini, Lung' Arno Nuovo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. The busiest streets are the Via Tornabuoni (PL D, 4, 5), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Calzajoli (PI. E, 5), the Via Cerretani (PL E, 4), and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Porta Rossa (PL D, E, 5). Many of the other streets also take{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their names from old families, the guilds, public games, trades,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the like. The streets were first paved with tiles in 1237, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the second half of the same century with stones (Lastrico). Num¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erous castle-like houses of Dante's period still exist in the small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}streets between the Arno, Mercato Nuovo, and S. Croce, and one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the ancient amphitheatre is recognisable in the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peruzzi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Piazza della Signoria (PL E, 5, 6), with the Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vecchio and the Loggia dei Lanzi, once the forum of the republic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the scene of its popular assemblies and tumults, is still an im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portant centre of business and pleasure.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Palazzo Vecchio (PL E, 5, 6), a castle-like building with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}huge projecting battlements, originally the seat of the Signoria, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}government of the republic, subsequently the residence of Cosimo I.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and now used as a town-hall, was erected in 1298 by Arnolfo del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cambio. The tower, 308 ft. in height, commands the neighbouring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}streets; the upper part dates from the 15th century. The courts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and vestibule were also greatly altered at a later date, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rustica facade towards the Via del Leone was added by Vasari in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1540. The original inscription over the door 'Jesus Christus Rex{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florentini populi s. p. decreto electus', was altered by Cosimo I. to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Rex regum et Dominus dominantium'. To the left of the entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a tablet showing the result of the plebiscite of 1860. From 1504{% endraw %}

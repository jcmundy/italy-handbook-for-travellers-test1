{% raw %}Environs of Nice. NICE. 17. Route. 115{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N. of the town are the villas Chateau Neuf and Orangini,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both in the Quartier Brancolar. In the Quartier St. Philippe is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Villa Bermond, where Nicholas, heir-apparent to the crown of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Russia, died in 1865. The site of the room in which he died is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now occupied by a tasteful chapel (PL B, 2). The garden contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 10,000 orange-trees.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Environs of Nice , with their attractive villas and luxur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iant vegetation, afford a variety of beautiful excursions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Franciscan monastery of Cimiez, Ital. Cimella, is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3 M. to the N. of Nice. The best route to it is by the road passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Pons (see below). The monastery (two pictures by Bre'a in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chapel; ladies admitted to the chapel only), erected in 1450,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands on the foundations of an ancient temple of Diana in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roman town of Cemenelium, of which part of an amphitheatre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(210 ft. long, 175 ft. wide), and a quadrangular structure, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monly called a ' Temple of Apollo', still remain. Traces of baths and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other buildings have also been discovered. We may return by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}footpath, which on the top of the hill intersects the ruins of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above-mentioned amphitheatre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Villa Clary, to which the public are admitted, below Ci¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}miez , on the road to St. Andre", possesses the finest orange and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lemon-trees at Nice and many rare plants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A good carriage-road ascends on the right bank of the Paglione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the (40 min.) monastery of St. Pons, founded in 775 on the spot{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where St. Pontius, a Roman senator, suffered martyrdom in 261.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was destroyed by the Saracens in 970, and the present edifice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 999. The treaty by which the County of Nice was an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nexed to the Duchy of Savoy was concluded here in 1388. [This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excursion may be combined with a visit to Cimiez (see above) by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}returning by the road which leads from St. Pons to Cimiez through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the olive-plantations on the hill.] — The chateau of St. Andre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(restaurant, closed in summer), which is reached in 1J2 hr. more,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in 1687, is now unoccupied. About Y4 hr. farther up the valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the insignificant grotto Les Cluses de St. Andre (adm. 50 c), or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rather a natural bridge over a brook, crossed by the road. An{% endraw %}
{% raw %}avenue of cypresses leads from the chateau to the grotto.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The excursion may be extended still farther in this direction.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Grotto of St. Andre' we follow the Torretta road in the de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}solate rocky ravine a little farther, and then ascend to the left by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the new road in several windings to the village of Falicon, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highest point of which affords an admirable view. — From Falicon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}we may return by the road to the S. to Nice (or by the less beauti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ful and very steep, but shorter path via Cimiez), or proceed farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towards the N. to *Aspremont, 9y2M. from Nice. The road is good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the whole way, and commands a fine view. Near Aspremontwe obtain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an excellent survey of the valley of the Var and of the Alps.{% endraw %}

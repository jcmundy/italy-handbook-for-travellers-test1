{% raw %}arrests the eye; and the vast and artistically vaulted interiors, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well as the structures of a merely decorative description, must{% endraw %}

{% raw %}may be understood. In the use of columns in front of closed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls (e. g. as members of a facade), in the construction of domes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above circular interiors, and of cylindrical and groined vaulting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over oblong spaces, the Roman edifices have served as models to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posterity, and the imitations have often fallen short of the originals.{% endraw %}

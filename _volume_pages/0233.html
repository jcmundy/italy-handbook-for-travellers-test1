{% raw %}To theS., opposite the cathedral, stands the Palazzo Reale (PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, F, 5, 6), built on the site of a palace of the Visconti in 1772,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with frescoes by A. Appiani, B. Luini, and Hayez, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing several handsome saloons. In the street to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond the palace, are visible the tower (1336) and apse of the fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half-Romanesque church of S. Gotardo, formerly the chapel of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visconti. — Adjacent, on the E., is the large Archiepiscopal Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lace (Arcivescovado; PI. F, 5), by Pellegrini (1565), containing a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome court with a double colonnade and marble statues (Moses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Aaron) by Tandardini and Strazza. In the interior court, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the side next the Piazza Fontana, are several Corinthian columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 15th century. — The W. side of the Piazza del Duomo is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirted by the Via Carlo Alberto (see p. 139), beyond which, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N.W., lies the Piazza de' Mercanti (see p. 139).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the N. side is the imposing new palatial facade which forms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance to the **Galleria Vittorio Emmanuele (PL E, 5),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connecting the Piazza del Duomo with the Piazza della Scala. This{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the most spacious and attractive structure of the kind in Europe.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was built in 1865-67 by the architect Gius. Mengoni, one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most gifted of modern Italian architects, who unfortunately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lost his life by falling from the portal (finished in 1878) in 1877.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The gallery, which is said to have cost 8 million fr. (320,000^.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is 320 yds. in length, 16 yds. in breadth, and 94 ft. in height.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The form is that of a Latin cross, with an octagon in the centre,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over which rises a cupola 180 ft. in height. The decorations are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}well-executed and bear testimony to the good taste of the Milanese.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The octagon is adorned with frescoes, representing Europe, Asia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Africa, and America, while the frescoes on the entrance-arches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are emblematic of Science, Industry, Art, and Agriculture. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gallery contains handsome shops, and is lighted in the evening by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2000 gas-jets. The circle of gas-jets in the dome is lighted by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small engine set in motion by clockwork, which does its work in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}l4/2 ruin., and attracts numerous spectators.{% endraw %}

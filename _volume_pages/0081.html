{% raw %}The town lies on the left bank of the Rhone, a little above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the influx of the Durance, and is connected with Villeneuve{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the opposite bank by a suspension-bridge. The old city-walls,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed in 1349-68 of massive blocks of stone, with towers at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intervals of 100-150yds., are admirably preserved and testify to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the former importance of the place.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The town is commanded by the abrupt Rocher des Doms (PL E, 2;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rupes dominorum), 300 ft. in height, which is surmounted by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cathedral of Notre Dame (PL 10), a structure of the 14th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently restored with moderate success. The portico is of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderably earlier origin. The church contains the handsome *Mon-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ument of Pope John XXII. (Jacques d'Euse of Cahors, d. 1334),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and that of Benedict XII. (d. 1342) in the left aisle. The square{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tower behind the Cathedral, called La Glaciere, was formerly em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ployed as a prison of the Inquisition, and during the Days of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Terror in 1791 became the place of execution of several innocent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}victims of the Revolution.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity of the cathedral rises the Papal Palace (PI. 3 ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, 2), now used as a barrack, a lofty and gloomy pile, erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Clement V. and his successors, with huge towers and walls 100 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in height. The faded frescoes in the Chapelle du St. Office were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed by Simone Memmi of Siena (d. 1339). Rienzi was in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carcerated here in 1351 in the Tour des Oubliettes, at the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time that Petrarch was entertained in the palace as a guest.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pleasant grounds have been laid out on the hill near the cathe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dral (unpretending cafe'). The best point of view is a rocky emin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ence in the centre. The **Prospect, one of the most beautiful in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}France, embraces the course of the Rhone and its banks ; Villeneuve{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the opposite bank, with its citadel and ancient towers; in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distance towards the N.W. the Cevennes; N.E. Mont Ventoux; E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Durance, resembling a silver thread, and beyond it the Alps;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below the spectator the tortuous and antiquated streets of Avignon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the promenades is a statue to Jean Althen, erected in 1846, out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of gratitude to him for having in 1766 introduced the cultivation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of madder, which long formed the staple commodity of the district,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being used extensively in dyeing the French red military trousers.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Outside the Porta Vittoria (PL E, 5) is the Cimitero, with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Doric colonnade and lofty dome-church. The summit of the ped¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}iment is adorned with a marble group of Faith, Hope, and Charity,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Spazzi. — An avenue leads hence along the Adige to the Rail¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way Bridge, which affords a fine view of the town and its environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right bank of the Adige, within a closed garden (visitors ring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the gate facing them, 2-3 soldi) in the Vicolo Franceschine, a side-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street of the Via Cappuccini (PI. D, 6), is situated the suppressed Fran¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciscan Monastery, where a partly-restored chapel contains a rude sar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cophagus in red Verona marble, called without the slightest authority the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tomba di Giulietla, or '■Tomb of Juliet' (fee 25 c). The whole scene is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prosaic and unattractive. Shakespeare's play of 'Romeo and Juliet' is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded on events which actually occurred at Verona. 'Escalus, Prince{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Vemna' was Bartolommeo della Scala (d. 1303). The house of Juliet's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parents, see p. 206.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the E. of the Ponte delle Navi lies S. Paolo di Campo Marzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL F, 4), which contains Madonnas with saints by Girolamo dai{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Libri (3rd altar to the right) and P. Veronese (right transept).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther to the N.E. is S. Nazzaro e Celso (PL H, 4), built in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Renaissance style, with traces of the Gothic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cappella di S. Biagio (in the transept) contains damaged frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Falconetto (processions of Nereids in the dome), and an altar-piece{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Madonna and Saints) by Bonsignori (1519). The apse is adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes by Bart. Montagna of Vicenza (history of St. Blaise). In a side-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}room to the left is a Baptism of Christ by Cavazzola. The two pictures on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 1st altar to the left, representing SS. John the Baptist and Benedic-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tus, Na/.arus and Celsus, are also by Montagna.{% endraw %}

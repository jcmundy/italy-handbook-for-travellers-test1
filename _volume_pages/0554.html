{% raw %}On the opposite side of the Via Proconsolo is the church of La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Badia (PL F, 5, entrance in the passage, to the right), founded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Willa, the mother of the Tuscan Margrave Hugo, who died about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1000 A.D. The present building was chiefly erected by Segaloni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1625), who left nothing of the original edifice (built by Arnolfo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Cambio in 1285) except the termination of the choir, and thus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed a number of frescoes by Giotto, Masaccio, and others.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door next the Bargello is by Benedetto da Ravezzano, 1495; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lunette a relief by Benedetto Buglioni. In the Interior a *Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and saints, by Mino da Fiesole; Monument of Bernardo Giugni (1466) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left that of the Margrave Hugo (14S1), both by Mino da Fiesole;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then, *Madonna appearing to St. Bernard, by Filippino Lippi, 1480. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful wooden ceiling is by Segaloni. — The graceful Campanile (1330){% endraw %}
{% raw %}also deserves inspection. — The Monastery Court contains remains of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monuments of the old noblesse (with whom this was a favourite church in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dante's time) and unimportant frescoes of the 15th century. — In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second passage to the right of the church is a chapel containing a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture of the 14th century.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of Athens (p. 371) from Florence on the festival of St. Anne, 1343',{% endraw %}

{% raw %}chio. The lunette, which is closed, is opened on application to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}custodian of the theatre (50 c). — From the Bargello the Via del{% endraw %}

{% raw %}*Palazzo De Rast, formerly Quaratesi (Pi. F, 0), by Brunelleschi,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Nonfinito (PL F, 5), in the baroque style by Buontalenti (1592).{% endraw %}

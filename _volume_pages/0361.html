{% raw %}The close of the 15th cent, may be designated as the culminating point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the glory of Venice. It was now the grand focus of the entire commerce{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Europe, numbered 200,000 inhab. , and was universally respected and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admired. Its annual exports were valued at 10 million ducats, 4 millions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which were estimated as clear profit. It possessed 300 sea-going vessels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 8000 sailors, and 3000 smaller craft with 17,000 men, as well as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fleet of 45 galleys carrying 11,000 men , who maintained the supremacy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the republic over the Mediterranean. But already, in the middle of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 15th cent., an event had taken place, which cast an ominous shadow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the future of the Republic; the capture of Constantinople by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turks in 1453 completely undermined the supremacy of Venice in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}East. The crowning blow, however, was the discovery of the new sea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}routes to India at the close of the century, by which its commerce was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diverted to the Portuguese. However 'the arts, which had been gradually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rising to perfection, shed a glorious sunset over the sinking form of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Republic'.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}To the right of the Scuola da Santo is the Museo Civico (PL 49;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}D, 4), with a fine facade and staircase, containing the civic Library,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Archives, and the Pinacoteca. The present building was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1881 by Cam. Boito of Milan, on the site of the old monastery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Antonio, the cloisters of which have been retained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Cloisters are columns, friezes, and other remains of a Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple, excavated near the Cafe Pedrocchi (see p. 219); farther on, numer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ous Roman tombstones, the so-called "Monument of the Volumnii, medi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seval coats of arms, memorial stones, etc.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The N.E. part of Italy, named II Veneto after the ancient Veneti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is divided into the nine provinces of Verona, Vicenza, Padova, Rovigo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venezia, Treviso, Belluno, and Udine. Its area, 9059 sq. M., is slightly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}larger than that of Lombardy, while its population of 2,790,300 souls is con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderably smaller. The western and larger portion of the country, between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Mincio and Piave, is indeed about as thickly peopled as the eastern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and less prosperous part of Lombardy between the Adda and the Mincio;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the Friuli, or ancient county of Forum Julii, the border-land to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. of the Piave, consists of very inferior soil, owing to the debris brought{% endraw %}
{% raw %}down by the Alpine streams. The lFurlanians\ the poor inhabitants of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Friuli, speak a patois of their own.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Venetian Dialect no longer contains traces of the Gallic ele¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment like that of the districts from Piedmont to the Romagna, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were once conquered by the Celts. It boasts, however, of having been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently used by men of letters, as for example by Goldoni in his co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}medies, and is the softest of all the Italian dialects, the flattening and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elision of the consonants being very common. Thus nevode for nipote,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suar for sudare, fogo for fuoco, sior for signore; and another characteristic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the conversion of g into z, as zente for gente, zorno for giorno, mazore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for maggiore. The history of the country has always been influenced by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the proximity of the sea, and the peculiar formation of the coast. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower part of its course the Po differs widely from all the other rivers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Europe. Its fall is very gradual, being for a considerable distance 22/3{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inches only, and latterly little more than 74 inch per English mile. To¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards the end of its course, moreover, it receives numerous tributaries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The result is that the adjacent districts are much exposed to inundations,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a danger which has to be averted by the construction of huge dykes; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these works frequently require to be raised, as the bed of the river is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constantly rising. The Po, together with the Adige, Bacchiglione, Brenta,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other coast rivers, terminate in a vast delta which extends along the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole coast of Venetia. The quantity of alluvial deposit is so great, that the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beds of these streams are continually undergoing change and subdivision.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Thus the ancient seaport of Hatria now lies 1572 M. from the coast, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while the Po formerly flowed towards the S., it has formed its present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embouchure since 1150. The extensive lagoons (lagune), separated from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea by narrow strips of land (lidi), and connected with it by outlets,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}would render the whole coast uninhabitable, were it not for the slight{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ebb and flow of the tide (mean difference IV2 ft.), which is perceptible{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Adriatic, and prevents malarious exhalations. This extensive allu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vial territory, which reminds one of Holland, called into activity the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}genuity and enterprise of its inhabitants at an early period, and a temper¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ate and conservative character has thus been imparted to their history.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}PRATO.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Pisa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Biblioteca Fabbroniana (PL 13), and the Biblioteca Forti-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}guerra (PL 14) were founded by two cardinals who were born here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Villa Puccini, 1 M. to the N., has beautiful gardens and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of art by Pampaloni and others.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Railway from Pistoja to Bologna, see R. 49.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Railway to Florence intersects a rich tract at the base{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Apennines. 44y2 M. S. Piero. On the left the picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of Monte Murlo comes into view, near which the Floren¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine republicans Baccio Valori and Fiiippo Strozzi were defeated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and taken prisoners by the troops of Cosimo I. in 1537.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50'/2 M. Prato {Albergo del Giardino, ViaMagnolfi; *Alb. Con-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trucci, Piazza del Duomo; La Colonna, Via dei Lanaioli; Caffe di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marte, Piazza del Duomo), a well-built town of 13,000 inhab.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(commune 42,900), on the Bisenzio, with beautiful environs, is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}manufacturing place, of which straw-plait is one of the staple com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modities, and is also noted for its excellent bread and biscuits{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(biscotti, cantucci). It formerly belonged to Florence, whose fortunes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it shared throughout the middle ages. In 1512 it was taken by storm{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Spaniards under Cardona.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the 15th cent, this small provincial town attracted numerous artists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Florence, so that a visit to it is indispensable to those who desire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be thoroughly acquainted with the Early Renaissance style of Flor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ence. An important work by Donatello and Michelozzo, an extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}composition in the style of Robbia, and a superb bronze screen bear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}testimony to the importance of Prato in the history of Renaissance sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ture. Among the painters of the place were Fiiippo and Filippino Lippi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Botticelli, and Fra Diamante. The church of the Madonna delle Carceri{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Prato also forms a very striking example of Renaissance archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tecture. This edifice (erected by Giuliano da Sangallo) exhibits the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transition from early to high Renaissance, and shows how anxiously the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}architects of the day directed their attention to the design of a Greek cross{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covered with a dome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the station we follow the Via Magnolfi, the first street to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, to the Piazza del Duomo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Il Duomo, begun in the 12th cent., and completed by Gio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vanni Pisano in the 14th, is in the Tuscan-Gothic style. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade, which dates from 1450, is a pulpit, adorned by Donatello{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Michelozzo, after 1434, with *Bas-reliefs (dancing children).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the pulpit the highly-revered Sacra Cintola, or 'girdle of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Virgin' preserved in the cathedral, is periodically exhibited to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people. Over the principal entrance a *Madonna with SS. Stephen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Lawrence in terracotta, by Andrea della Robbia.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The *Campanile, or bell-tower, designed and begun by Giotto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1334 and completed after his death (1336) by Taddeo Gaddi, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}square structure in the style of the cathedral, 292 ft. in height, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}regarded as one of the finest existing works of the kind. It consists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of four stories, richly decorated with coloured marble. The win¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dows, which increase in size with the different stories, are enriched{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with beautiful tracery in the Italian Gothic style. On the W. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are four statues, the three first of which are by Donatello (St. Mat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thew ; *David, the celebrated 'Zuccone' or bald-head ; and Solomon),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the fourth (Obadiah) by Nanni di Bartolo (1420). On the side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are Habakkuk, Abraham, and Isaac by Rosso and Donatello, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two patriarchs by Niccolb d'Arezzo. On the N. and S. are sibyls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and prophets. Below these figures, on the sides of the tower, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bas-reliefs; those on theW., S., and E. sides having been designed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Giotto, and executed partly by him and partly by Andrea Pisano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and those on the N. designed and executed by Luca della Robbia:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Seven Cardinal Virtues, the Seven Works of Mercy, the Seven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beatitudes, and the Seven Sacraments. In the lower series is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}represented the development of mankind from the Creation to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dimax of Greek science (Creation of Eve, Adam and Eve at work,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dwellers in tents, Astronomer, Rider, Weaving, Navigation, Agri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}culture, etc.), while the liberal arts are represented by figures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Phidias, Apelles, Donatus, Orpheus, Plato, Aristotle, Ptolemy, Euc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lid, and a musician.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The campanile is ascended by a good staircase of 414 steps (fee for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1-2 pers. lfr.). Beautiful View from the top, embracing the valley in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the city lies, the neighbouring heights, studded with villas and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}richly cultivated, and the mountains to the N., S., and E. At the summit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are seen the pillars on which, according to Giotto's plan, it was proposed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to raise a spire of 100 ft., but the project was abandoned by Gaddi.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}A little farther on is the Gothic church of S. Fermo Maggiore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. E, F, 4), erected at the beginning of the 14th century. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}architecture of the exterior, with its facade of brick, enriched{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with marble, is worthy of inspection.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, which is destitute of aisles, has been modernised;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful old ceiling in walnut-wood, and remains of good frescoes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 14th century. By the 3rd altar to the right is an Entombment, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief of the 15th century. In the right transept is a reproduction of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient Arco de1 Gavi (at the Castello), which was removed in 1805. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, above one of the arches of the choirs, is a fresco, possibly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed by Giotto, with portraits of William of Castelbarco, who recon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed the church in 1313, and of Prior Daniel Guzman, who furnished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the wooden ceiling. In the chapel to the left of the choir is a St. Anthony{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Liberate da Verona. The chapel adjoining the left transept contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the monument of the physician Girolamo della Torre by Riccio (the ori¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ginals of the bronze reliefs were carried off by the French, and are in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Louvre). Above the left side-entrance is a Crucifixion, the best of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 14th cent, frescoes. The Cappella Brenzoni, to the left of the prin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cipal entrance, contains reliefs by the Florentine Russi (1420), and a ruined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fresco of the Annunciation by Pisanello. — Outside the main entrance, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, is the sarcophagus of Fracastoro, body-physician to Can Grande.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Ponte delle Navi (PL E, 4) in the vicinity, which commands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a good survey of S. Fermo, was erected to replace a bridge across{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Adige, which was destroyed by an inundation in 1757.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately to the right beyond the Adige, at the beginning of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the promenade, is the *Palazzo Pompei alia Vittoria (PL F, 5),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Sammicheli about 1530, presented by the family to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town in 1857, and now containing the Museo Civico (fee 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Ground Floor contains collections of natural history (fossils from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Monte Bolca) and antiquities, the latter including Roman and Etrus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}can bronzes, marble sculptures and vases, Roman silver-plate, and Lom-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bardic gold ornaments.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Pinacoteca or picture-gallery, on the first floor, contains works{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principally of the Veronese school. The first and second rooms contain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Galleria Bernasconi, presented to the town by Dr. Bernasconi.{% endraw %}

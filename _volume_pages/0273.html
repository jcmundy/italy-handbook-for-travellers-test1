{% raw %}The *Lago Maggiore (646 ft., greatest depth 2800 ft.), the Lacus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verbanus of the Romans, is 37 M. in length and averages 472 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in width. The canton of Ticino possesses only the N. bank for a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distance of 9 M. ; this portion of the lake is also called the Lake of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Locarno. TheW. bank beyond the brook Valmara, and the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bank from Zenna belong to Italy. Its principal tributaries are on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. the Ticino (Tessin), on the W. the Tosa, on the E. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tresa , flowing from the Lake of Lugano. The river issuing from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. end of the lake retains the name of Ticino. The N. banks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are bounded by lofty mountains, for the most part wooded, whilst{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. shore towards the lower end slopes gradually away to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level of the plains of Lombardy. The W. bank affords a succession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of charming landscapes. The water is of a green colour in its N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arm, and deep blue towards the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the N.W. angle of the lake, at the influx of the Ticino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies Magadino (Bellevue, on the lake), consisting of Upper and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lower Magadino, at which, since the opening of the railway to Lo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carno (p. 36), the steamers only touch once daily. — Opposite to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it, on the W. bank, lies —{% endraw %}

{% raw %}view; * Corona, on the lake; * Albergo Svizzero, in the piazza,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}(R. 4), situated at the mouth of the Maggia, the deposits of which{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the character of the scenery and population is thoroughly Italian.{% endraw %}

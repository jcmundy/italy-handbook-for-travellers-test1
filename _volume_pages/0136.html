{% raw %}56 Route 8. TRIESTE. Tergesteo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Sea-baths at the Bagno Maria, opposite the Hotel de la Ville; Bagno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Buchler. Ferry to the baths 3 kr. each way (a single person 6 kr.). —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Boats 1-172 A- per hour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Theatres. Teatro Gomunale (PI. 21), opposite the Tergesteo; Teatro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Filodrammatico (PL 23), French and German plays sometimes performed;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Armonia (PI. 24), dramas and operas; Politeama Rossetti, on the Acquedotto.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}British Consul, Capt. Richard Burton. — TJ. S. Consul, Col. Montgomery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English Church Service performed by a resident chaplain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trieste, the Tergeste of the Romans, situated, at the N.E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extremity of the Adriatic, is the capital of Illyria and the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}important seaport of Austria. Pop. of the town proper 72,000, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the suburban villages 133,000, or including the 'commune' and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garrison 144,500.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trieste was made a free harbour by Emp. Charles VI. in 1719, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be termed the Hamburg of S. Germany. About 18,000 vessels, in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cluding 1600 steamers, annually enter and clear the harbour. The yearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}value of its exports amounts to 117 million florins, that of the imports{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to 145 million florins (14,500,000?.). Every European nation, and also the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}United States, has a consul here. The population is very heterogeneous,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the Italian element predominates in the city. About one-sixth of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhabitants are Slavs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Harbour is the centre of business. The quays have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}greatly extended within the last few years to meet the increasing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}requirements of the shipping trade. The Lighthouse (Fanale Marit-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}timo; PI. G7) on the S.W. Molo Teresa is 106 ft. high.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The New Town, or Theresienstadt, adjoining the harbour, is laid{% endraw %}
{% raw %}out in broad, well-paved streets with handsome houses, and is in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tersected by the Canal Grande (PI. 5; D, 3, 4), 360 yds. long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 50 ft. wide, which enables vessels to discharge their cargoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}close to the warehouses. At the E. end of the Canal is the modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Antonio Nuovo (PI. 7; D, 3), in the Greek style.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent to the Hotel de la Ville towards the S. is the Greek{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Church (S. Niccolb dei Greci, PI. 10; E, 4; divine service 6-8.30{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a.m. and 5-7 p.m.), with its two green towers, sumptuously fitted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up. To the left of the Hotel de la Ville is the Palazzo Carciotti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a green dome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A few paces from the harbour is the Molo S. Carlo, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}begun in 1751 on the site of an old Roman mole. Adjacent, in an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open space opposite the Teatro ComunaleCPl. 21), is the *Tergesteo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. 25 ; E, 4), an extensive pile of buildings, on the outside of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are shops, and in the interior a glass gallery in the form of a cross,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the Exchange (12-2 o'clock) is situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Reading Room of the exchange is well stocked with newspapers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(visitors admitted). The principal part of the edifice is occupied by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}offices of the 'Austrian Lloyd', a steamboat-company established in 1833,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by which the postal service and passenger traffic between Austria and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. Mediterranean and India are undertaken.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Borsa (PI. E, 4), where the old Exchange is situated, stands a{% endraw %}

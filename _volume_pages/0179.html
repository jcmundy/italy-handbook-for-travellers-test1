{% raw %}Two broad streets lead towards the N.E. from the Piazza Defer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rari : to the right the new Via Roma, and to the left the Via Carlo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Felice. The Via Roma (PL F, 4) soon reaches the Piazza Corvetto,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing the principal entrance to the Galleria Mazzini (PL 43),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and embellished with a statue of the great agitator of that name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 86), by Costa, erected in 1882. The Salita S. Caterina ascends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hence to the right to the Acquasola Promenade (see p. 99), while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Via Roma is carried through the promenade, unfortunately{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cutting off an angle of the interesting old Palazzo Spinola (Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Caterina, No. 14), and is continued by the Via Assarotti, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads to the loftily-situated Piazza Manin (p. 99).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the left side of the Via Carlo Felice, No. 12, is the Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pallavicini (PL 26; F, 3), now the property of the Durazzo family{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 96). — We next come to the Piazza delle Fontane Morose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL F, 3). No. 17 in the piazza is the Pal. della Casa, originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spinola, adorned with five honorary statues in niches, of the 15th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent. ; No. 27 is Pal. Lud. Stef. Pallavicini, sumptuously fitted up.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the Piazza Fontane Morose begins a broad line of streets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in the 16th cent., extending to the Piazza dell' Acquaverde{% endraw %}
{% raw %}near the railway-station, under the names of Via Nuova, Via Nuo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vissima, and Via Balbi, and forming one of the chief arteries of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern traffic. In these streets are situated the most important{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palaces and several churches; some of the former should be visited{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for the sake of their magnificent staircases , which are among the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most remarkable objects in Genoa. — On each side of these loftily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated streets a complete labyrinth of narrow lanes, occupied by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lower classes, descend to the left to the harbour, and ascend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hill on the right; here, too, the traveller will observe many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting buildings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The first of these main streets is the *Via Garibaldi, formerly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via Nuova (PL F, 3), which is flanked by a succession of palaces.{% endraw %}

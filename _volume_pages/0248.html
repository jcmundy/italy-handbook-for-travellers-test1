{% raw %}8M. Monza (Falcone; Alb. del Castello, near the station) is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town with 15,500 inhabitants. Leaving the station and following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Via Italia to the right, we pass the church of S. Maria in Istrada{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2nd on the right), with a Gothic brick facade of 1327, and soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reach the *Cathedral, the chief object of interest. It was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 14th cent, in the Lombard Gothic style by Marco di Campione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the site of a church founded in 595 by the Lombard queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Theodolinda, and contains double aisles and transept, flanked with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels on both sides. Above the portal is a very curious relief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing Queen Theodolinda amid her treasures; below, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baptism of Christ.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. In the left transept is the plain sarcophagus of Queen Theo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dolinda; in theE. transept reliefs of the 13th cent., supposed to represent the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coronation of Emp. Otho III., or that of Henry III. — In a casket forming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre of a richly-decorated cross over the altar, to the right of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir, is preserved the celebrated Iron Crown , with which 34 Lombard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kings were crowned. This venerable relic was last used at the coronation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Emp. Charles V., of Napoleon in 1805, and of Emp. Ferdinand I. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1838. It consists of a broad boop of gold adorned with precious stones,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}round the interior of which is a thin strip of iron, said to have been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made from a nail of the true Cross brought by the empress Helena from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palestine. In 1859 it was carried off by the Austrians, but after the peace{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 1866 was restored to its former repository. (Fee for seeing the crown,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5 fr.) — The "Treasury (fee 1 fr.) contains several objects of historical in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terest : a hen with seven chickens in gold, representing Lombardy and its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seven provinces, executed by order of Queen Theodolinda; the queen's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crown, fan, and comb; two silver loaves, presented by Napoleon I. after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his coronation; the cross which was placed on the breast of the Lombard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kings at the moment of their coronation ; a richly - adorned book - cover{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with an inscription of Theodolinda; reliquary, cross, and missals of Beren-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garius; goblet of sapphire, with a stem of Gothic workmanship; Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goblet of Gian Galeazzo Visconti; fine diptychs of the 4-6th cent.; Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carvings in ivory; 'ampulles1 from the Roman catacombs (vessels with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a dark-red deposit supposed to be the blood of martyrs); Byzantine pil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grim-flasks from Palestine; model of the iron crown. A cabinet outside{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the treasury contains the mummy of one of the Visconti, who died in 1413.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Broletto, or town-hall, of the 13th cent., with round-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arched windows and tower, is believed to be part of a palace of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Emp. Frederick I. and the Lombard kings. The royal Summer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace near Monza is a large building with an extensive and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful park, traversed by the Lambro. The church of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna di Tirdno contains frescoes by Luini, Gaudenzio Ferrari,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Cesare da Sesto.{% endraw %}

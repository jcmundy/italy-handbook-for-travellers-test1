{% raw %}Riva, a busy harbour with 5000 inhab., is charmingly situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the N. end of the lake , at the base of the precipitous Rocchetta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Church of the Minorites, outside the Porta S. Michele, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 16th cent, and adorned with gilding and stucco mouldings,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains several altar-pieces by Guido Reni, Palma Vecchio(*?), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}others. The Parish Church in the town possesses several modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pictures and frescoes. The watch-tower of La Rocca on the lake,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fortified anew since 1850, at present a barrack, and the old Castello,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high on the mountain to the W., erected by the Scaligers, greatly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enhance the picturesqueness of the place. The situation of Riva is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sheltered and healthy, the heat of summer being tempered by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lake. Private apartments may be procured on moderate terms. —{% endraw %}

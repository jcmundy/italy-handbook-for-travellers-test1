{% raw %}Dyck, Portrait of the Marchesa Durazzo; good portrait of the Lombard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school, attributed to Leon, da Vinci; Perino del Vaga, Holy Family. To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right a handsome gallery with rococo-painting and a few ancient and{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The throne-room is adorned with two large pictures by Luca Giordano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The terrace commands a fine view of the city and harbour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Piazza Acquaverde (PI. 47; D, 1) rises the marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Statue of Columbus, who is said to have been born at Cogoleto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 101) in 1456. It was erected in 1862, and stands on a pedestal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with ships' prows. At the feet of the statue, which rests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on an anchor, kneels the figure of America. The monument is sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rounded by allegorical figures in a sitting posture, representing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Religion, Geography, Strength, and Wisdom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between these are reliefs of scenes from the history of Columbus, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the inscription of dedication: '■A Cristoforo Colombo la Patria\ and '■divi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nato un mondo lo avvinse di perenni benifizi alV antico\ — [On the house{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Around the monument are grounds containing date-palms,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dwarf-palms, and other specimens of tropical vegetation. Opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Palazzo Faraggiana, with a marble frieze representing scenes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the life of Columbus, and an inscription.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Piazza del Principe (PL C, D, 1, 2), to the W. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station, commands a good view of part of the old fortifications and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the old tower of S. Tommaso. No. 4 in the piazza is the long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Palazzo Doria (PL 24), presented in 1522 to Andrea Doria, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'padre della patria' (d. 1560, at the age of 95). It was remodelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1529 from designs by Giov. Ang. Montorsoli, and adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frescoes by Perino del Vaga, a pupil of Raphael.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The long Latin inscription on the side next the street records that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andrea d'Oria, admiral of the Papal, Imperial, French, and native fleets,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in order to close his eventful career in honourable repose, caused the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palace to be rebuilt for the use of himself and his successors. His{% endraw %}
{% raw %}praises were thus sung by Ariosto — 'questo e quel Doria, che fa dai{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pirati sicuro il vostro mar per tutti i lati'. — The finest of the Frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Perino del Vaga (restored in 1845)), which in many respects recall the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings of Raphael, are those on the ceiling, vaulting, and lunettes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great entrance-hall, representing scenes from Roman history; a cor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ridor hung with portraits of the Doria family, a saloon with a large ceil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing-painting representing Jupiter overthrowing the Titans, and a room{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the love adventures of Jupiter. The great hall is further adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with reliefs by Montorsoli and the Titan saloon also contains a portrait{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the aged prince with his favourite cat, and a superb chimney-piece.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The elder branch of the Doria family, to whom the palace now belongs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally resides at Rome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The garden of the palace, extending towards the harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains an extensive Loggia with arcades. The gardens on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill opposite, with a statue of Hercules (lJl Giyante) in a niche,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also belong to the estate.{% endraw %}

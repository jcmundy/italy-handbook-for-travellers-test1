{% raw %}About 3 M. to the S.W. of Battaglia, on the Monti Euganei, is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arqua del Monte, where Petrarch lived and died (1304-74). His monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in front of the church consists of a sarcophagus resting on short columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of red marble, bearing the inscription:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Frigida Francisci lapis hie tegit ossa Petrarce,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Suscipe virgo parens animam! Sate virgine, parce !{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fessaque nam terris celi requiescat in arce.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the top is a bust of Petrarch, dating from 1547. His house in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}upper part of the town, with painted wooden ceilings and faded frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in allusion to his poems, contains a few reminiscences of its former{% endraw %}
{% raw %}illustrious owner.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14!/2 M. Monselice (Scudo d'Oro, tolerable), a town at the base{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Monti Euganei, has remains of fortified walls and a ruined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle, which once belonged to the Patriarch of Venice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18M. Stat. Este. The town, the ancient Ateste, lies 33/4 M. to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N., on the road which here diverges to Mantua. It possesses{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of Este (p. 302), a spacious piazza surrounded with arcades, a Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vecchia with a clock-tower, a Museo Civico in the church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Francesco (containing several interesting Roman inscriptions), a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museo Euganeo-Prehistorico (with a valuable collection of antiqui¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ties), a cathedral of elliptical plan with a lofty choir (with a paint¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Casa Benvenuti (visitors ring) commands a view of the Alps,{% endraw %}

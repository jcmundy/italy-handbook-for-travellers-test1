{% raw %}19. From Genoa to Pisa. Riviera di Levante.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}IO41/2 M. Railway in 41/2-71/4 hrs. (fares 19fr., 13 fr. 30, 9fr. 50c;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}express 20 fr. 90, 14 fr. 65 c). — Some of the trains start from the Stazione{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Principe at Genoa, stopping, with the exception of the express, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Stazione Piazza Brignole, while others start from the latter station. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The finest views are from the side of the train opposite that on which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passengers enter at Stazione Piazza Principe. Travellers by the night{% endraw %}
{% raw %}express loose all the scenery. Beyond Nervi, however, the view is greatly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circumscribed by the numerous tunnels, which also make it dangerous to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stretch the head out of the carriage-window. — Carriage and Pair from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genoa via, Porto Venere to La Spezia, about 125 fr. and fee; with heavy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luggage extra-horses (25 fr. each) are necessary. Comp. p. 100.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genoa, see p. 86. The train backs out of the Stazione Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Principe, and then starts in the opposite (E.) direction, passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through a long tunnel under the higher parts of the town (transit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 4-5 min.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2 M. Stazione Piazza Brignole, where there is generally a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prolonged stoppage. — To the left we obtain a view of the fortress-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowned heights around Genoa (comp. p. 88).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train, which at places runs parallel with the road, now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}follows the *Riviera di Levante , which is less remarkable for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luxuriant vegetation than the Riviera di Ponente (p. 100), but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents almost more striking scenery. The line is carried through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the numerous promontories by means of cuttings and tunnels, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which last there are no fewer than eighty, some of them of consid¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erable length. The villages generally present a town-like appear¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ance , with their narrow streets and lofty and substantial houses,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}closely built on the narrow plain of the coast, or in short and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}confined valleys, and mostly painted externally as at Genoa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train crosses the Bisagno, generally an insignificant brook,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and passes through the hill on which S. Francesco d)Albaro is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated by means of a tunnel. — 4 M. Sturla. To the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stretches the beautiful expanse of the Mediterranean ; to the left we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enjoy a view of the olive-clad slopes of the Apennines , sprinkled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with country houses. A tunnel. — 5 M. Quarto. A tunnel. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6 M. Quinto, with numerous villas, and dense lemon plantations,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among which rise several fine palm-trees. Three tunnels.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}772 M. Nervi. — Hotels. *H6tel Victoria, near the station and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea, R. 2-3, B. I1/4, ddj. 2'/2, D.4, 'pens'. 8-10 fr.; *Hotel et Pension{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Anglaise, 'pens.' in winter 11-16fr.; Albergo Ristorante di Nervi, un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretending. — Pensions Fayod (7-9 fr.), Roeder (with garden; 8-10 fr.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pizzi, Bellevue, Cavour. — Furnished Apartments. Suites of 3-5 rooms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}300-500 fr., villas 400-4000 fr. and upwards for the season. The physician{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should be consulted in taking a dwelling for an invalid.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Physicians. Dr. Thomas, Castello Ponzone; Dr. Schetelig, Pension{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Anglaise. — Chemist: Gallo, in the main street.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post Office, at Gallo's drug-store in the chief street. Telegraph Office{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English Church Service in the Hotel Anglaise.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}groves , has of late come into notice as a winter-residence, owing{% endraw %}

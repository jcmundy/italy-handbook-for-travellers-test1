{% raw %}Tuscany, which covers an area of 9287 sq. M., and contains 2,209,500{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inhab., is divided into eight provinces of very different sizes; viz. Massa-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carrara, 687 sq. M. in area, an independent duchy down to 1829; Lucca,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}575 sq. M. in area, independent down to 1847; Florence , the largest pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vince, 2267 sq. M. in area; Leghorn, the smallest, about 126 sq. M. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}area; and Pisa, Arezzo, Siena, and Grosseto. The density of the popula¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion, too, varies greatly in different parts of the country. In the province{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Lucca there are about 431 inhab. to the square mile, in Florence 295, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Siena 127, and in Grosseto not more than 57. With the exception of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast districts and the valleys, the country is hilly, and intersected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the spurs and ramifications of the Sub-Apennines. The N. part, adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Arno, is most fertile, the plains and slopes of the hills being richly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cultivated. A strong contrast to this smiling region is presented by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marshy coast district below Leghorn, where malignant fevers have wield¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed their destructive sway since the depopulation which took place in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the middle ages. The soil of the inland hill country is also poor, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some compensation is afforded for this by its copper and other mines.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscany, indeed, possesses greater mineral wealth than any other part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy, and to this circumstance is due the fact that it was earlier civilised{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the rest of the peninsula.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscany still retains the name of its first inhabitants, the Tusci or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Etrusci (Greek Tyrrhenians). The excellent iron of Elba and the rich{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copper mines of Volterra, afforded them materials for establishing thriv¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing industries, the products of which were in demand far and wide at an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early period, as for example at Athens and in Germany, where numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discoveries of ancient Etruscan ironwork have been made. The art of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}navigation was simultaneously developed. The earliest naval battle in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the western part of the Mediterranean handed down by tradition (about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B.C. 532), was fought between the Greeks and Etruscans for the possess¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ion of Corsica, and resulted in the victory of the latter, who thus obtained{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supremacy over the sea still known as the Tyrrhenian. The League{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Etruscan Towns, which extended from the foot of the Alps to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bay of Naples, was also instrumental in promoting civilisation, as it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the means of diffusing a knowledge of writing, as well as of the mechan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ical arts, and to some extent influenced even Latium and Rome itself.{% endraw %}

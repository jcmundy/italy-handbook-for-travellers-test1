{% raw %}In the History of Art Pisa occupied an important position at an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early period, but was obliged to yield up its artistic precedence earlier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than its political to the more fortunate Florence. The progress of art at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisa was more rapid than in the rest of Tuscany, owing perhaps to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}influence of its numerous and handsome ancient monuments, as Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms repeatedly recur in the buildings. With the foundation of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cathedral of Pisa began the dawn of mediaeval Italian art. This church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is in the old basilica style, but with the not unimportant innovation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having a dome over the centre of the cross. The magnificent building{% endraw %}
{% raw %}operations of the Pisans continued throughout the whole of the 12th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent., and terminated with the erection of the charming church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maria della Spina (1230), and that of S. Caterina (1253). In the 13th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent. Pisa was also important as a cradle of Sculpture, and gave birth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Niccolb Pisano, a precursor of the Renaissance. Under what influences{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Niccolo was trained is uncertain, but there is a marked difference be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween his works with their somewhat antique cast, and those of his Pi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}san predecessors (such as the bronze door of the cathedral by Bonannus).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}His successors Giovanni and Andrea Pisano adhered to his style, or at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}least to the spirited character of his designs. Pisa also boasted of pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sessing Painters at an early period. The name of Giunta Pisano (first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half of the 12th cent.), for example, was known far beyond the limits of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town, but his works are uninteresting, except to the student of art.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The fact that Cimabue was invited from Florence to embellish the apse{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the cathedral, indicates the decline of native art, the development of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which appears to have ceased entirely in the 14th century. The execution{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the frescoes in the Campo Santo was committed exclusively to foreign{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artists, not indeed to Giotto himself, as Vasari asserts, but to his pu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pils and to Sienese masters. Buffalmacco, the jester among the Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painters, who is not a merely mythical personage, as has been supposed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is said to have assisted in executing the frescoes in the Campo Santo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but to what extent is unknown. In the 15th cent. Benozzo Gozzoli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1420-97) of Florence, a pupil of Fra Angelico, spent 16 years at Pisa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the Campo Santo is graced by his most important work.{% endraw %}

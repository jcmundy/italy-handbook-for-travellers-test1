{% raw %}Corsica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. BONIFACIO.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}457{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One of the pleasantest walks in Ajaccio is afforded by the prolonga¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of the Cours Grandval, which crosses the Place Casone and gradually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends the olive-clad slopes to the spring of Solario, commanding charm¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing views of the town, the harbour, the gulf, and the mountains. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road on the N. side of the bay, passing the Hospice Eugenie, although{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destitute of shade, also affords a charming walk. The gardens here contain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous family burial-places and chapels. About 3 M. from the town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Jardin de Barbicaja, celebrated for its Mandarin and blood oranges.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Ajaccio to S. Bonifacio, and to Bastia by the E. Coast.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carriage-road from Ajaccio to Bonifacio (86V2M.); diligence daily to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(5272 M.) Sartene (Hot. de l'Univers). On Saturdays a steamer plies from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ajaccio to (3 hrs.) Propiano, whence a diligence runs to Sartene in 272 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The fortress of S. Bonifacio (Hdtel de France, pension 6 fr., moder¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ate), with 3600 inhab. is picturesquely situated on a prominent and lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock. It possesses high and dilapidated houses, of the Pisan and Genoese{% endraw %}
{% raw %}periods, and narrow, unattractive streets. The principal street is called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Piazza Doria. The town was founded in 833 by the Tuscan Marquis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bonifacio, after a naval victory over the Saracens. II Torrione, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large tower of that remote date, is still standing. Bonifacio subsequently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}came into the possession of the Pisans, then into that of the Genoese,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by whom it was treated with marked favour. In return for this partiality{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this town, as well as Calvi, remained inviolably faithful to Genoa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as was proved in 1420 by its memorable defence against King Al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phonso I. of Arragon. The house of Fiiippo Cataccioli, in which the Emp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charles V. lodged in 1541 on his return from an expedition against Algiers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is pointed out. The town once boasted of 20 churches, of which the cathe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dral of S. Maria del Fico, the handsome Gothic church of S. Domenico, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous tombstones of Knights Templar and Genoese nobles , and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small church of S. Francesco (with a spring, the only one in the town) now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alone remain. — A stone bench above the Marina of Bonifacio, by the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gate of the fortress, near the chapel of S. Rocco, commands a charming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view, especially by evening-light, of the strait which separates Corsica{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Sardinia. On the opposite coast the town of Longo Sardo is visible;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left lies the island of S. Maddcdena. On the coast below Bonifacio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are situated three imposing Grottoes, which visitors explore by boat (5 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for one or more persons; stipulate for visits to all the grottoes).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The distance from Bonifacio to Bastia is 106 M. The E. coast of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corsica is somewhat bleak and desolate. The road leads past the bay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Manza to (16M.) Porto Vecchio, the only good harbour. It was con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed by the Genoese, and is supposed to occupy the site of the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Portus Syracusanorum. Hence to the mouth of the Tavignano, 4472 M.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where, near the lake of Diana, the ancient town of Aleria was situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fragments of masonry and vaulting, and remnants of a circus are still to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be seen. Coins, vases, and inscriptions have also been found here. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern Aleria consists of the Genoese castle and a few houses only, for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this coast, owing to the want of cultivation, is marshy and unhealthy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Here, in 1736, the adventurer Neuhof (see p. 454) landed from an English{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vessel. About 1572 M. farther is S. Nicolao (2 M. on this side of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a road diverges to Cervione, 2^/4 M.). The river Goto (p. 459), often nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dry in summer, is next crossed. In the extensive plain at its mouth, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left bank, once lay Mariana, the Roman colony founded by Marius,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the remains of which are visible on the shore, 3 M. from the road. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruins of a beautiful chapel, and of a church called La Canonica, a basilica{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of noble proportions in the Pisan style, are situated here.{% endraw %}

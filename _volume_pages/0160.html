{% raw %}The antiquities which still remain testify to its ancient impor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tance. The Town Walls, flanked with strong towers, and forming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a rectangle 790 yds. long by 620 yds. wide, are preserved in their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entire extent, and on the S.W. side the ancient facing and cornice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of hewn stone are still in situ. The walls of the old Theatre and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the arcades of the Amphitheatre are visible above the houses in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}houses in the market-place.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The principal street leads to the E., through the ancient *Porta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pretoria, to the [}/^ M.) handsome Triumphal Arch, adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with ten Corinthian pilasters. It then crosses the Buttier, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has deserted its ancient channel, and reaches the beautiful Arch{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the old Roman bridge , now half-buried in the earth. — In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}suburb lies the church of St. Ours, the choir of which contains the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tomb of Bishop Gallus (d. 546) and finely carved stalls of the 15th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}century. The old crypt is supported by Roman columns. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adjacent cloisters contain early-Romanesque columns (12th cent.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with interesting capitals. Near the church rises a Tower, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed of Roman hewn stones in the 12th cent., opposite which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a sarcophagus and two ancient columns at the entrance of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel. In the same square is the Priory of St. Ours, a handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building of the 15th cent., with terracotta ornamentation and an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}octagonal tower. The interior contains some interesting wood-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carvings and frescoes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cathedral dates in its present shape from the 14th cent¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ury. Above the portal is a painted terracotta relief, and in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir are two mosaics of the 10th cent, and some early-Renaissance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stalls. The cathedral treasury contains two shrines of the 13th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 15th cent., a cameo of a Roman empress in a setting of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}13th cent., and a diptychon of the Consul Probus (406) with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portrait of the Emp. Honorius.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the S. gate rises the tower of Bramafam (12th cent.), in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which Count Challant is said to have starved his wife to death out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of jealousy. By the W. wall is the Tour du Lepreux, rendered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}celebrated by Xavier le Maistre's novel, in which a leper named{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Guasco (d. 1803) and his sister Angelica (d. 1791) dragged out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their miserable existence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The : Becca di Nona (10,354 ft.), which rises to the S. of Aosta, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mands a superb view of the Alps. Good bridle-path to the summit. Two-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thirds of the way up is the Alp Comboe (simple fare); on the top is a new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refuge hut.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}to the Graian Alps, see Baedeker's Switzerland.{% endraw %}

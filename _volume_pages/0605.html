{% raw %}On its S.W. slope, one-third of the way up, and 3906 ft. above the sea-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level, is seen a wall with small windows, the oldest part of the monastery,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built in 1218 by St. Francis of Assisi. The church dates from 1264. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1472 the monastery was entirely destroyed by fire. Beautiful forests are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated in the vicinity, from the openings in which imposing mountain-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}views are often enjoyed. One of the grandest points is the "Penna della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verna (4796 ft.), or ridge of the Verna, also known simply as I'Apennino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'the rugged rock between the sources of the Tiber and Arno', as it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called by Dante (Paradiso ii., 106). To the E. are seen the lofty Sassi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Simone, the mountains which bound the Tuscan Romagna in the di¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rection of the republic of San Marino; N.E. the sources of the Tiber are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated behind the Fumajolo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the monastery are the Luoghi Santi, a number of grottoes and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock-hewn chambers in which St. Francis once lived. The church con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains no pictures worthy of mention, but several excellent reliefs in terra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cotta, especially an "Annunciation by Luca della Robbia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S., not far from the monastery, is situated the ruined castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Chiusi, occupying the site of the ancient Clusium Novum, where Ludo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vico Buonarroti, father of Michael Angelo, once held the office of Podesta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The great master himself was born on 6th March, 1475, at Caprese, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Singorna in the vicinity, but in 1476 his parents removed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Settignano, in the vicinity of the quarries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller is recommended to return from Bibbiena to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence by Arezzo (diligence every afternoon in 33/4 hrs. ; one-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}horse carr. 10 fr.). The road follows the left bank of the Arno, pass¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing several small villages, quits the river at Giovi, and entering the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rich Val di Chiana leads to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arezzo (Inghilterra,; Vittoria; Globo; Cannon d'Oro), a town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 12,000 inhab. (with the adjoining villages 39,000), aprovincial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}capital, and the birthplace of Maecenas, Guido Aretino, Petrarch,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro Aretino, Spinello Aretino, Vasari, and many other celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italians. It possesses several interesting churches. That of S. Fran¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cesco contains noteworthy frescoes of the 15th century, of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief are those by Piero della Francesca in the choir. S. Maria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Pieve, of the 9th cent., has a tower and a very curious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade of 1216. The Cathedral, a remarkable specimen of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian Gothic style, begun in 1177, is embellished with several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful stained-glass windows and well-sculptured monuments; its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade is unfinished. S. Annunziata, a fine Renaissance edifice, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in great part built by Ant. da Sangallo. The church of S. Domenico{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is embellished with frescoes by Spinello Aretino and others. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Museum, in the Piazza Grande, containing a rich palseontological{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection, Roman and Etruscan antiquities, and fine majolica of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 15th cent., and the Pinacoteca Bartolini in the Palazzo Capel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Ferro, also merit a visit. The Accademia Aretina di Scienze,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lettere, ed Arti holds its meetings in the Badia diS. Fiore; in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}library, formerly the refectory, is a fresco by Vasari.{% endraw %}

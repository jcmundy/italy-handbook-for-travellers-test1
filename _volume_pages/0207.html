{% raw %}the Rue de France, commemorating the meeting of Charles V. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Francis I. in 1538, which was effected through the intervention{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pope Paul III., has given its name (Croix de Marbre) to this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarter of the town. Opposite rises a Pius Column, or monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in honour of the promulgation of the doctrine of the Im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}maculate Conception by Pope Pius IX. — The Square Massena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. E, F, 4), a broad space formed by covering in the Paillon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Pont Vieux and Pont Neuf, is embellished by a Statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Massena (p. 113) in bronze, erected in 1867; in front Clio is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}represented on the pedestal writing his name on the page of history ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the sides are reliefs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Jardin Public (PI. D, 4 ; military music, see p. 112) at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embouchure of the Paillon, and the *Promenade des Anglais ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}joining it on the W., which was laid out by English residents in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1822-24, and greatly extended in 1862, are the principal resorts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visitors. These grounds stretch along the coast for l1^ M., as far{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the brook Magnan, and are bordered with handsome hotels and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}villas (at the beginning of the promenades is the Cercle Medi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terranee, mentioned p. 112). On the left bank of the Paillon, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is crossed here by the Pont Napoleon, they are continued by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Boulevard du Midi, which is planted with palms.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To theE. of the town rises the Castle Hill, 320 ft. in height (PI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}F, G,4; ascent from the N. orE. side, 20min.), crowned by the ruins{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a castle destroyed by the Duke of Berwick under Louis XIV. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1706, now converted into beautiful grounds, where palms, oranges,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cypresses, and aloes flourish in profusion. The platform on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summit, erected in honour of Napoleon III., commands an admirable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view in every direction : S. the Mediterranean; W. the coast, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promontory of Antibes, the two lies de Lerins, the mouth of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Var (which down to 1860 formed the boundary between France and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sardinia), below the spectator Nice itself; N. the valley of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paglione, the monasteries of Cimiez and St. Pons, in the distance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the castle of S. Andre, Mont Chauve, the Aspremont, and the Alps ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E., the mountains, Fort Montalban, and the promontory of Mont-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boron (p. 116). The S. slope of the castle-hill, which descends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}precipitously towards the sea, is called the Rauba Capeu ('hat-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}robber', owing to the prevalence of sudden gusts). — The Ceme¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teries, with the exception of the English , are on the N. side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle-hill.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the base of the castle-hill on the E., where a house opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dogana was destroyed by a landslip in the winter of 1871, lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the small Harbour (PI. F, 4), called Limpia from an excellent spring{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(limpida) which rises near the E. pier. It is accessible to small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vessels only ; those of large tonnage cast anchor in the bay of Villa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}franca (p. 116). The harbour is now being enlarged. The Place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bellevue, adjoining the harbour, is embellished with a Statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charles Felix, King of Sardinia, in marble, erected in 1830.{% endraw %}

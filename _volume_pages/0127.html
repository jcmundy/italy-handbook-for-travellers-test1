{% raw %}8. From Vienna to Trieste. Semmering Railway.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}370 M. Austrian S. Railway. Express (1st, in winter 1st and 2nd class){% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 15 hrs. (fares 39 fl. 7, 29 fl. 14, 19 fl. 75 kr.); ordinary trains in 21 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(fares 32 fl. 75, 24 fl. 63, 16 fl. 50 kr). 50 lbs. of luggage free, provided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is at the station at least 72 hr. before the departure of the train;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}otherwise the whole is liable to be charged for. — Best views generally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left. For farther particulars, see Baedekers Eastern Alps.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The station of the S. Railway is between the Belvedere and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Favorite 'Lines', or boundaries of the city. The train, soon after{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starting, affords a good survey of Vienna, and the broad plain with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its innumerable villas and villages, as far as the hills of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leitha, to the S. 3 M. Hetzendorf, with an imperial chateau. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the hills to the right, near (8 M.) Brunn, are several artificial ruins.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— Near (10 M.) Mbdling, the Briihl, a picturesque rocky valley,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opens on the W., and a branch-line diverges to the E. to the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perial chateau and park of Laxenburg. Stations Guntramsdorf and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gumpoldskirchen, famous for its wines. A short tunnel is passed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}17M. Baden (695 ft.; *Stadt Wien; *Griiner Baum; Schwarzer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adler), with handsome villas, celebrated for its warm mineral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}springs, the Roman Thermae Pannonicae. Beautiful environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}19 M. Voslau (800 ft. ; *H6tel Back; Hallmayer), which yields{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the best Austrian wine, is also frequented as a watering - place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(74°Fahr.). The next stations are Kottingbrunn, Leobersdorf (to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right the Schneeberg, 6808 ft.), Felixdorf, and Theresienfeld.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}31 M. Neustadt, or Wienerisch-Neustadt (930 ft.; Hirsch;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Kreuz), with 21,700 inhab., is an important manufacturing town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the E. side lies the old ducal Castle of the Babenberg family,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}converted in 1752 into a military academy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the right beyond Neustadt the Schneeberg is visible almost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from base to summit; on the left rises the Leitha range. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hills to the right, in the distance, stands the well - preserved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of Sebenstein, the property of Prince Liechtenstein. — 35 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Egyden; 40 M. Neunkirchen, a manufacturing place ; then Ter-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nitz and Potschach. On the height to the left, near Gloggnitz, rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the castle of Wartenstein. Schloss Gloggnitz on the hill, with its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous windows, was a Benedictine Abbey down to 1803.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At (47 M.) Gloggnitz (1426 ft.; *Alpenhorn; *Adler; Rossi){% endraw %}
{% raw %}begins the imposing *Semmering Railway, one of the most inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting lines in Europe (best views on the left), completed in 1853.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}factory of Schleglmuhl. On the left the three-peaked Sonnwend-{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The handsome E. side of the Palace of the Doges towards the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canal, which presents a more harmonious appearance than the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side, and has a basement of facetted stone, is connected with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carceri or Prigioni, constructed in 1512-97 by Giov. da Ponte, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}means of the lofty Bridge of Sighs (Ponte dei Sospiri; PI. 97).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Piombi, or prisons under the leaden roof of the Palace, were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed in 1797, but a torture-chamber has recently been restored{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to satisfy the curiosity of tourists. The Pozzi, or half-ruined dun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}geons on the farther side of the narrow canal on the E. side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palace, with the place of execution for political criminals, may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also be inspected, and are full of sombre interest (entrance from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first floor).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}della Paglia, which connects the Molo with the adjacent —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Siva degli Schiavoni (PI. F, G, 4), a quay paved with un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}polished slabs of marble, and presenting a busy scene. Sailors of all{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nations, from the vessels which lie in the vicinity, and numerous id¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lers, are seen lounging here or congregated at the cafes. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Maria della Pieta, situated about the centre of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riva, contains a *Christ in the house of the Pharisee by Moretto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(in the high-choir, above the principal entrance), and a Coronation{% endraw %}

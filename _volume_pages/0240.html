{% raw %}The celebrated *Biblioteca Ambrosiana (PL D, E, 5), open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on week-days 10-3 o'clock (fee 1 fr. ; picture-gallery, or Pinacoteca,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open to the public in summer on Wed., 10-2^2; entrance from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reading-room to the right in the court), contains 160,000 vols, of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}printed books, and 8,000 MSS. and palimpsests, or codices rescripti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some of them very valuable. Director: Cav. Sacerdote Ceriani, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Orientalist. The library was founded in 1609 by the archbishop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cardinal Federigo Borromeo, to whom a statue was erected in front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the building in 1865.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Court, adjoining the porter's lodge, is a fresco of the Mocking{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Christ, by Luini.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Via Montebello, the next cross-street, leads to the new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Synagogue (PL 47; G, 2), begun by Antonelli in 1863, but after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards discontinued for lack of funds , and now being finished at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the expense of the city, as a memorial of Victor Emmanuel; it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a square building resembling a tower, with a singular facade con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sisting of several rows of columns, and will when finished be the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loftiest in Turin (354 ft.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Via di Gaudenzio Ferrari, No. 1, is situated the Museo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Civico (PL 33; F, 2), containing the civic collections (open gratis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on Sun. and Thurs., 11-3; on other days, fee 1 fr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ground Floor. Early sculptures, early mediaeval relief of the Ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}donna, coffin of the poet Vagnone (d. 1499) with reliefs of Orpheus and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Perseus, terracottas, wood-carvings of the 16th cent., a copy of the Bucen-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}taur (p. 247). — FiRST Floor. Modern paintings and sculptures. Marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues of Eve by Fantacchiotti and Dante by Vela. The realistic tendency{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of modern Italian art is well illustrated in the death agonies depicted in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Crucifixion of Eulalia by Franceschi and the 'Femme de Claude' by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mosso. Good water-colours by Bossoli, illustrating the events of 1859-61.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Statuette by Balzico, the 'Plebiscite in Naples'. In the last room are a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}few old paintings by Bart. Vivarini, Bugiardini, Honthorst, and Victors,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a marble bust of Sappho by Canova. — Second Floor. Rooms 12-14:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sculptures in wood, tapestry, bronze and iron work. Room 15: Modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wood and ivory carvings; six pieces of sculpture from the tomb of Gaston{% endraw %}
{% raw %}de Foix (p. 138), by Bambaja. R. 16: Miniatures (missal of Cardinal della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rovere, 15th cent.), enamels, majolica. R. 17: Italian ceramic ware. RR. 18,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}19: Mementoes of Massimo d'Azeglio. R. 20: Interesting collection of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stained glass. RR. 21. 22: Prehistoric and ethnographical collection.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}iele Manin (d. 1857), beyond the Ospedale S. Giovanni Battista,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing the Republic Venice, holding in her right hand a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palm-branch, and leaning, with her left, on the medallion-portrait{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Over the principal portal are "Four Horses in gilded bronze, 5 ft. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height, which were long supposed to be the work of a Greek master{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Lysippus), but are now believed to be of Roman workmanship, probably{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the time of Nero. They are finely executed, and are especially valuable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the sole existing specimen of an ancient quadriga preserved intact.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}They probably once adorned the triumphal arch of Nero, and afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that of Trajan. Constantine caused them to be conveyed to Constantinople,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence the Doge Dandolo brought them to Venice in 1204. In 1797 they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were carried by Napoleon I. to Paris, where they afterwards occupied the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summit of the triumphal arch in the Place du Carrousel. In 1815 they were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brought back to Venice by the Emp. Francis and restored to their former{% endraw %}
{% raw %}position.{% endraw %}

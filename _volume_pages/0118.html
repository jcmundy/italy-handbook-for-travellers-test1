{% raw %}42 Route 6. BRENNER. From. Innsbruck{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12 M. Matrei (3241 ft.), with the chateau of Trautson, the prop¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erty of Prince Auersperg, is charmingly situated. — 14 M. Steinach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(3430 ft.); the village lies on the other side of the valley, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mouth of the Gschnitzthal. — The train now ascends a steep incline,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the Schmimer Thai in a wide curve above the village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Stafflach (two tunnels), and runs high above the profound ravine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Sill to (1972 M.) Gries (4100 ft.). It then, in another curve,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes the small green Brennersee, and reaches —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2372 M. Stat. Brenner (4485 ft.), on the summit of the pass, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}watershed between the Black Sea and the Adriatic. View limited.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sill, which rises on the N. side of the pass, falls into the Inn;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Eisak, rising on the S. side, descends to the Adige. The train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}follows the course of the Eisak and soon stops at (26 M.) Brenner-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bad (4353 ft. ; *Logirhaus), a popular bath-establishment. It then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends rapidly by means of a long embankment and through two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunnels to stat. Schelleberg (4065 ft.), where it turns into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pflersch-Thal. Here it enters the N. slope of the valley by a curved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunnel, 800 yds. long, from which it emerges in the opposite direc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion , soon reaching (33 M.) Gossensass (3481 ft.; *Brauhaus),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which lies 584 ft. below Schelleberg. This is one of the most in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teresting parts of the line , and is most striking when seen in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reverse direction. — The train now runs high above the Eisak,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passing at places through wild rocky scenery, and enters the broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}basin in which lies —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}38 M. Sterzing (3107 ft. ; Goldner Greif; NeuePost; Schwarzer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adler; Stoetter's Hotel, at the station), a clean and picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little town with curious old buildings and arcades, deriving its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prosperity from mines formerly worked here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train now crosses the Pfitscher Bach; on the left rises the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of Sprechenstein, and on the right bank of the Eisak the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruins of Thumburg and Reifenstein are visible. — 4072 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Freienfeld. The train crosses the Eisak; on the left bank rises the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ruined castle of Welfenstein (said to be of Roman origin), and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}village of Mauls. — Beyond (45 M.) Grasstein the train enters the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}narrow defile of Mittewald, where the French were defeated in 1809.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The lower end of the defile, called the Brixener Klause, near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Unterau (2460 ft.), is strongly fortified by the Franzensfeste,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was constructed in 1833, and commands the Brenner route.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Franzensfeste (4772 M. from Innsbruck) is the junction for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pusterthal line (for Carinthia); the station (*Rail. Restaurant,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with rooms to let, D. 1 fl. 20 kr., R. 1 fl.) lies at some distance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the fortifications. The vegetation now assumes a more southern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character, vineyards and chestnuts gradually appearing.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}unfortunately much retouched. The mosaics in the dome (Christ between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mary and St. John) are by Cimabue (begun about 1302). Of the pictures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the choir, SS. Margaret and Catharine on the right in front of the high-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar, and SS. Peter and John on the left, by And. del Sarto, are worthy of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inspection; beyond the high-altar, ^Abraham's Sacrifice, and Entombment{% endraw %}

{% raw %}porphyry columns on the right and left, with figures of children, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Stagi, the designs being attributed to Michael Angelo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left Transept. Over the Cappella del S. Sagramento, the Annunciation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in mosaic by a follower of Cimabue. The altar, richly decorated with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silver by Foggini, was presented by Cosimo III.; behind it, Adam and Eve,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a basrelief by Mosca, by whom the other statues were also executed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Baptistery (PI. 15; Battistero), begun in 1153 by Diotisalvi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but according to the inscriptions not completed till 1278, and with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gothic additions of the 14th cent., is also entirely of marble. It is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a beautiful circular structure (33Y2 yds. in diameter), surrounded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by half-columns below, and a gallery of smaller detached columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above, and covered with a conical dome (190 ft. high, restored in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1856). It has four entrances; at those on the N. and E. are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptures in marble of the 12th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior rests on eight columns and four pillars, above which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there is a simple triforium (restored); in the centre, on a raised base,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a marble octagonal Font, admirably adorned with rich sculpture and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosaic by Guido Bigarelli of Como (1246), and the famous hexagonal "Pul¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pit, borne by seven columns, by Niccolb Pisano, 1260; the reliefs (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. 347) on the pulpit are: (1) Annunciation and Nativity; (2) Adoration{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Magi; (3) Presentation in the Temple; (4) Crucifixion; (5) Last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Judgment; (6) Allegorical figures; in the corners the apostles. — Fine echo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *CampaniIe, or clock - tower, begun by the architects{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bonannus of Pisa and William of Innsbruck in 1174, and completed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Tommaso Pisano in 1350, rises in eight different stories, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like the Baptistery are surrounded with half-columns and six{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnades. Owing to its remarkable oblique position, 13 ft. out{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the perpendicular (height 179 ft.), it is usually known as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leaning Tower. The question whether this peculiarity was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intentional or accidental has frequently been discussed. The most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probable solution is that the foundations settled during the progress{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the structure, and that, to remedy the defect as much as pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sible, an attempt was made to give a vertical position to the upper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part. Galileo availed himself of the oblique position of the tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in making his experiments regarding the laws of gravitation. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*View from the platform, embracing the town and environs, the sea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W., and the mountains to the N.E., is very beautiful; a good{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase of 294 steps leads to the top. Permission is only accord¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed to a party of not fewer than three, but if necessary the cus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}todian (50 c.) will provide a third person (15-20 c). The tower con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains seven bells, the heaviest of which, weighing 6 tons, hangs on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the side opposite the overhanging wall of the tower.{% endraw %}

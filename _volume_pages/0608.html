{% raw %}VIII. Corsica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Steamboats. — I. From Marseilles to Ajaccio (212 M., in 14--20 hrs.);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Compagnie Ginirale Transatlantique every Tuesday, fares, 1st cabin 28 fr.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2nd cabin (also comfortable) 18 fr., food included (private cabin with one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bed 50 per cent more); Compagnie VaUry every Friday. — II. From Mar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seilles to Bastia : Compagnie Fraissinet every Sunday direct, and every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuesday via, Nice (leaving Nice on Wednesday); Compagnie VaUry every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sunday. — III. From Marseilles to Calvi or Isola Rossa (alternately):{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Compagnie Fraissinet once weekly. — IV. From Leghorn to Bastia (72 M.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 6-7 hrs.): Societa Rubattino once weekly (1st cab., 16 fr. 20 c); Compag¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nie Fraissinet and Compagnie Valery, each once weekly (usually Wed¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nesday). — Embarkation by small boat, 1 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Diligences run from Ajaccio and Bastia to all the principal places in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the island.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Railway from Ajaccio to Bastia in course of construction, portions of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which will very soon be opened for traffic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corsica (French La Corse), situated between 43" and'41° 21' N. latitude,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}56 M. distant from Italy and 111 M. from France, and separated from Sar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dinia by the Strait of Bonifacio, which is 9 M. in width, possesses an area of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3386 sq. M., and a population of 258,000 souls (census of 1872). A broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountain-chain, terminating towards the N. in the Capo Corso, consisting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of grey granite and limestone formations, occupies almost the entire is¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land. On the W. it rises abruptly from the sea , forming a number of bold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}promontories and deeply indented bays. On the E. side, towards Italy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the alluvial deposits have been more abundant, and have formed a level{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast of some breadth. The vast height to which the mountains rise{% endraw %}
{% raw %}within a comparatively small space (e.g. Monte d'Oro 8690 ft., Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rotondo 9053 ft.) imparts a wild and imposing character to the scenery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nine-tenths of the area of the island are uncultivated, while the moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains for the most part are clothed with magnificent forests. The Flora{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the island is remarkable for its rare luxuriance and diversity, com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prising specimens of almost, every species of plant found on the shores{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Mediterranean. The timber of Corsica was highly esteemed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancients, and still supplies most of the French and Italian dockyards.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Its mineral wealth, however, is far inferior to that of Sardinia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The character of the natives, notwithstanding the levelling and equal¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ising effects of advancing civilisation, corresponds with the wild aspect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of their country, and, at least in the more remote districts, still retains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many of those peculiar features described by ancient writers. Their in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}satiable thirst for revenge (vendetta), formerly one of the chief causes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the depopulation of the island, has never been thoroughly eradicated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The perpetrators of these dark deeds retire as outlaws (bandili) to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountains, where, hunted like wild beasts by a corps of gensdarmes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constituted for this express purpose, they frequently protract their mis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erable existence for many years. At the same time this revengeful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ferocity is to a certain extent compensated for by bravery, love of free¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dom simplicity of manners, and hospitality, virtues which usually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}characterise a vigorous and primitive race. Their ballads, too, and espe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cially their dirges (voceri), are replete with poetical pathos.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The situation and climate of the island are Italian, as was also its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}history down to the year 1768. Since the beginning of the present cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tury its union with France has been still more closely cemented by its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}connection with the family of Napoleon. It now forms the 86th Depart¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment the capital of which is Ajaccio, and is divided into 5 Arrondisse-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments: Ajaccio, Bastia, Calvi, Corte, and Sartene. An Italian dialect is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still the language of the natives, but French is used for all official pur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poses and is spoken by the educated classes.{% endraw %}

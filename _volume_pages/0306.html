{% raw %}The Broletto (PI. 2; D, 3), adjoining the cathedral on the N.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a massive and spacious building of the 12th cent., but was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards entirely altered. It was anciently the seat of the mu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nicipal authorities, and now contains the courts of justice. Part{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of it is used as a prison. The campanile on the S. side , called La{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Torre del Popolo, belongs to the original edifice. — A well-pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served fragment of Gothic architecture in the street ascending hence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with circular windows and brick mouldings, is also interesting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the W., not far from the Broletto , extends the interesting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Vecchia, in which rises the *Palazzo Comunale (PI. 16;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}C, 3), usually called La Loggia, the town-hall of Brescia, erected by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Formentone of Brescia in 1508 on the ruins of a temple of Vulcan,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a 'putto' frieze by Jacopo Sansovino and window-mouldings by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palladio, of the latter half of the 16th century. The interior was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half destroyed by a fire in 1575. The exterior of this imposing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure is almost overladen with enrichments. On the ground{% endraw %}
{% raw %}floor is a deep hall resting on columns; in front are pillars with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns in the wall. In the angles of the arches is a series of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}busts of Roman emperors as medallions. The upper floor recedes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerably. The handsome adjacent building on the right, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Archivio e Camera Notarile, is probably also by Formentone. (The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller should walk round the whole building.){% endraw %}

{% raw %}bell is struck by two iron figures as at Venice (p. 241). — To the{% endraw %}

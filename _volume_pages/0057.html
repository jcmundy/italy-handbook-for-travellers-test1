{% raw %}ITALIAN ART.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}reach a true appreciation of Michael Angelo's artistic greatness.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Educated as a sculptor, he exhibits partiality to the nude, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}treats the drapery in many respects differently from his professional{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brethren. But, like them, his aim is to inspire his figures with life,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and he seeks to attain it by imparting to them an imposing and im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pressive character. At the same time he occupies an isolated position,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at variance with many of the tendencies of his age. Naturally pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disposed to melancholy, concealing a gentle and almost effeminate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperament beneath a mask of austerity, Michael Angelo was con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}firmed in his peculiarities by the political and ecclesiastical circum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stances of his time, and wrapped himself up within the depths of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his own absorbing thoughts. His sculpture most clearly manifests{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that profound sentiment to which however he often sacrificed sym¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}metry of form. His figures are therefore anomalous, exhibiting a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grand, conception, but no distinct or tangible thoughts, and least of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all the traditional ideas. It is difficult now to fathom the hidden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sentiments which the master intended to embody in his statues and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pictures; his imitators seem to have seen in them nothing but massive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and clumsy forms, and soon degenerated into meaningless mannerism.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The deceptive effect produced by Michael Angelo's style is best ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}emplified by some of his later works. His Moses in S. Pietro in Vincoli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is of impossible proportions ; such a man can never have existed ; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small head, the huge arms, and the gigantic torso are utterly dis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proportionate ; the robe which falls over the celebrated knee could{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not be folded as it is represented. Nevertheless the work is grandly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}impressive; and so also are the Monuments of the Medici in S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lorenzo at Florence, in spite of the forced attitude and arbitrary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moulding of some of the figures. Michael Angelo only sacrifices{% endraw %}
{% raw %}accuracy of detail in order to enhance the aggregate effect. Had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so great and talented a master not presided over the whole, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}danger of an inflated style would have been incurred, the forms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}selected would have been exaggerated, and a professional mannerism{% endraw %}
{% raw %}would have been the result. Michael Angelo's numerous pupils, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their anxiety to follow the example of his Last Judgment in the Sis-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine, succeeded only in representing complicated groups of unnat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}urally foreshortened nude figures, while Baccio Bandinelli, think¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing even to surpass Michael Angelo, produced in his group of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hercules and Cacus (in the Piazza della Signoria at Florence) a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mere caricature of his model.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Michael Angelo lived and worked at Florence and Rome alter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nately. We find him already in Rome at the age of 21 years (1496),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as Florence, after the banishment of the Medici, offered no favour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able field for the practice of art. Here he chiselled the Pieta and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Bacchus. In the beginning of the 16th cent, he returned to his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}home, where he produced his David and worked on the Battle Car¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}toon (Florentines surprised while bathing by the Pisans), which has{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Charges. Gondola for 1-4, or a barca for 1-6 persons, with one rower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(barcajuolo), according to the tariff of 1872, a copy of which the gondolier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is bound to exhibit if desired, for the first hour, or for each trip, by day{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or by night, 1 fr., for each additional hour 50 c. (but a fee is expected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}addition to these low fares), for the whole day (of 10 hrs.) 5 fr. To or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the station, see p. 228. From the steamers to the Piazzetta (two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rowers required) 40 c, luggage 15 c For short distances a bargain should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be made. For a second rower double the ordinary fare is charged. One,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, suffices for the gondola, and even for the barca if not heavily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}laden (lbasta uno'), unless greater speed than usual is desired. For a longer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distance, however, such as to the Lido, two rowers are desirable, and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this case a bargain may be made with the gondolier for a second. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}islands of Murano, S. Lazzaro, and Lido are included in the tariff. — For{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longer distances the charge per hour and per gondolier is 10 c. more. For{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public festivities a bargain must be made. Officious loiterers who assist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passengers to disembark expect a gratuity of a few centimes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The principal station of the gondolas is by the Piazzetta (p. 242; PI. F, 4).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller selects a suitable boat without regarding the importunities{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the boatmen, whereupon the owner will soon present himself. If the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gondola is hired by the hour, which is the most advantageous mode for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sight-seeing, the passenger shows his watch, saying ' all' ora'. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highest demands are generally made at the Piazzetta and Riva and in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vicinity. It need hardly be observed that the intervention of a com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}missionaire or a waiter in the hiring of a boat causes the fare to be con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderably raised. If any difficulty arises it is best to apply to a policeman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Guardia municipale).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The gondoliers are, as a class, respectable and trustworthy, and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small gratuity goes far towards securing their good offices.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Guides ('Guides Autorises', 'Guide Patentate'). In 1881 the guides of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venice formed themselves into a 'Societa di mutuo soccorso ed incre-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mento fra le guide patentate', which has been recognised by the authori¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ties and is designed to correct the abuses which had crept into the rela¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions between guides and tourists. A list of the members of the society is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kept at each hotel. The head-office is at Calle Sei Pretti 5028, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}complaints may be made when necessary. Almost every hotel has its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}own guide. Those not attached to the hotels are generally to be found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Piazza of St. Mark before 9 a.m. or about 8 p.m. The fee for a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day (9-6) is 5 fr., with 10 fr. additional for gondola-fares and gratuities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parties of visitors are frequently formed by the guides, who undertake to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conduct them to all the principal sights of Venice at a charge of 4 fr. each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}person, which includes gondola-fares, gratuities, etc., but, as the number{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is usually unlimited, this wholesale system cannot be recommended, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}members of the party being entirely deprived of their independence. A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}party of 2-6 persons will find it far preferable to have a guide at their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}own disposal. In this case the fee, including all expenses, is about 20 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(i.e. 5 fr. for the guide and 15 fr. for gondolas, fees, etc.).{% endraw %}

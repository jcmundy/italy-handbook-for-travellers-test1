{% raw %}S. Lorenzo. GENOA. 15. Route. 91{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Via Lomellini leads to the left to the Annunziata (p. 96). —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Following the Via di Fossatello and the Via S. Luca , — in a side-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street to the left of which is the church of S. Siro (PL 18; E, 3),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in 1516 , modernised in 1820 , containing statues by Tad-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deo, and frescoes by Giov. Batt. Carlone, — we next reach the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza Banchi , in which is situated the Exchange (Loggia de'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Banchi, Borsa, PL 7; E, 3), erected at the end of the 16th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from plans by Alessi, and adorned with a sitting figure of Cavour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in marble by Vine. Vela. — The narrow but handsome *Via degli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Orefici (PL E, F, 3 ; at the beginning of which , on the right, is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door with an interesting Adoration of the Magi in relief, of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}middle of the 15th cent.), and then the Via Luccoli, lead to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza delle Fontane Morose (p. 94). A little to the N. of the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}degli Orefici lies the church of S. Maria delle Vigne, originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded in the 13th cent.; of the old structure, however, there{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now remain only a tower and a ruined cloister. — To the S. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Exchange we traverse the Via S. Pietro della Porta, passing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}curious church of S. Pietro de' Banchi (1583), to the Via S. Lorenzo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Piazza S. Lorenzo, in which rise the new Banca Nazion-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ale (PL 5), and the cathedral of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Lorenzo (PL 9; E, F, 4), erected in 1100 on the site of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earlier edifice, and subsequently so much altered, that it now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presents three distinct styles, the Romanesque, the French Gothic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Renaissance. The lower part of the facade, which consists{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of alternate courses of black and white marble , was constructed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 13th cent, in the style peculiar to the French churches; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two lower of the recumbent lions with which it is adorned on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right and left of the steps, are modern. The sculptures of the prin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cipal portal date from the end of the 13th century. The entrances to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the aisles are richly decorated with Romanesque sculptures of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12th cent.; the antique ornamentation of the entablature and capi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tals probably came from the older church. A small oriel of 1402,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly belonging to the Hospital of St. John, has been built into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right aisle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, constructed in 1307, is borne by the columns of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earlier church. Beyond the massive substructure of the towers, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms a kind of atrium, lies the nave with its aisles, covered with cylindrical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting and a dome (which last was constructed by Alessi in 1567), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}borne by sixteen Corinthian columns of coloured marble and four buttresses,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above which is another series of columns alternating with pillars. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, over the second side-portal, is the monument of a bishop by Giov. di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Balduccio (1336). In the chapel to the right of the choir a Crucifixion by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fed. Baroccio, and statues by P. Francavilla. In the choir, handsome stalls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with inlaid-work. In the chapel to the left of the choir six pictures and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a statue of Prudence by L. Cambiaso. In the left aisle, seven statues by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gugl. della Porta. — The second chapel to the left of the entrance, that of *S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giovanni Battista, erected in 1451-96, contains in a stone area of the 13th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cent, (below the altar) relics of John the Baptist, brought from Palestine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the Crusades. The six statues at the sides and the reliefs above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them are by Matteo Civitali (p. 352); the Madonna and John the Baptist by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andrea Sansovino (15%); the canopy and the other sculptures by Qiacomo{% endraw %}

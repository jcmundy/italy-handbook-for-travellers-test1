{% raw %}PISA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Climate.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drive lfr. 60 c, first 72 hr. lfr. 40 c, each additional 72 hr. 80 c; at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}night 20 c. more for each 72 hr. With two horses, one-third more.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post Office (PI. 51) on the left bank of the river, below the Ponte di Mezzo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Telegraph Office at the Prefecture, Lung-Arno Galilei, open from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7 a.m. till midnight.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Physicians. Dr. Ahrt, Dr. Hirschl, Dr. Fedeli, Dr. Feroci.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Booksellers. Hoepli, Lung-Arno Regio 9; Ubelhart, Lung-Arno Regio 5.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Photographers. Huguet & Van Lint, Piazza dei Cavalieri (also sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tures in marble).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baths. Bagni Ceccherini, Lung-Arno, N. side; Bagni Lombard, Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Manzoni 11, new.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Theatres. Regio Teatro Nuovo, good operas, prices very moderate.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English Church Service in winter and spring.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Stanze Civiche, to which strangers may be introduced, contain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian and French newspapers. Balls and concerts in winter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Climate. Pisa is partly sheltered on the E. and N.E. by the Monti{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisani (p. 351) , while the lofty town-wall also affords no inconsiderable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}protection from the wind. The mean winter temperature is about 472°{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower than that of the Riviera, and the usual daily range of temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is much less. This equability is due in great measure to the humidity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the atmosphere occasioned by the proximity of the sea, the broad river,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other causes. Pisa has also been known from ancient times for its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}abundance of rain, there being here on an average 73 days of rain,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and one of snow between Oct. and April. Pisa is a well-known wintering-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place for patients suffering from asthma, pneumonia, pleurisy, and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pulmonary complaints, but should be avoided by those who have much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mucous discharge, as well as by rheumatic and gouty subjects. The best{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apartments are on the N. side of the Lung-Arno, that part of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Ponte di Mezzo and the Ponte Solferino, called Lung-Arno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Regio, is the sunniest. The Lung-Arno Mediceo is less favourably situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rents of furnished rooms are highest in October, after which they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gradually fall. The average rent of a single room is 172-3 fr. per day, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many landlords decline to let their rooms except for the whole winter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Living at an hotel is of course more expensive (pension 9-12 fr. per day),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the visitor is more independent. The best situated hotels are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grand Hotel, the Victoria, and the Gran Bretagna. As the Lung-Arno is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief centre of society in winter, invalids are recommended not to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}take rooms at a distance from it.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisa, a quiet town with 26,000 inhab. (commune 54,000),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the capital of a province, is situated 6 M. from the sea, on both{% endraw %}
{% raw %}banks of the Arno. It was the Pisae of the ancients , and once lay{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the confluence of the Amus and Auser (Serchio), which last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has now an estuary of its own.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisa became a Roman colony in B.C. 180. Augustus gave it the name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Colonia Julia Pisana, and Hadrian and Antoninus Pius erected temples,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theatres, and triumphal arches here. At that period the town must have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been a place of considerable importance, but all its ancient monuments,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the exception of a few scanty relics (p. 351) have disappeared. At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the beginning of the 11th cent. Pisa attained the rank of one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}greatest commercial and seafaring towns on the Mediterranean, and became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a rival of Venice and Genoa. It was chiefly indebted for its power to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}zeal with which it took the lead in the wars against the Infidels. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1025 the Pisans expelled the Saracens from Sardinia and took permanent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possession of the island. In 1030 and 1089 they again defeated the Saracens{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Tunis, and in 1063 destroyed their fleet near Palermo. In 1114 they{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conquered the Balearic Islands, and soon afterwards took a prominent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part in the Crusades. In the 12th and 13th centuries their power had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reached its zenith; their trade extended over the entire Mediterranean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and their supremacy embraced the Italian islands and the whole of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast from La Spezia to Civita Vecchia. In the intestine wars of the{% endraw %}

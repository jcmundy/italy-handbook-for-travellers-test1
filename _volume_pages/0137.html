{% raw %}Cathedral.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}TRIESTE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}8. Route. 57{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, 4), containing the handsome hall of the provincial diet. In front{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Municipio is the Maria Theresa Fountain, erected in 1751.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via del Corso (Pl.E, 3,4), the principal street of Trieste,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}together with the two piazzas just mentioned, separates the new town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the old. The latter, nestling round the hill on which the castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises, consists of narrow and steep streets, not passable for carriages.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the left on the route to the cathedral and the castle is situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Jesuits' Church (S. Maria Maggiore, PI. 9; F, 4), containing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large modern fresco by Sante. To the W., a few paces higher up,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the Piazzetta di Riccardo, named after Richard Cceur de Lion,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who is said to have been imprisoned here after his return from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palestine. The Arco di Riccardo (PI. 2) is believed by some to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be a Roman triumphal arch, but probably belonged to an aqueduct.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now ascend by the Via della Cattedrale towards the cathe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dral. A little below the latter, on the right (custodian opposite,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}No. 16; fee 30 kr.), is the entrance to the Museo Lapidario{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. 16 ; F, 3), an open-air collection of Roman antiquities in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}disused burial-ground.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The antiquities on the upper terrace were found at Trieste, those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the lower at Aquileia. Winckelmann, the eminent German archaeo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logist, who was robbed and murdered by an Italian at the Locanda Grande{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1768, is interred here, and a monument was erected to him in 1832.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right and left of this monument are fragments of the sarcophagus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of an Amazon. At the lower end of the burial-ground is the so-called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Glyptothek, containing an inscription from the pedestal of a statue to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Decurio Fabius Severus of Trieste, and also the heads and fragments of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several other statues.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cattedrale S. Giusto(P1. 8; F, 3) occupies the site of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}old Roman temple, part of the substructure and some of the columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which are still visible in the tower. The present building was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed in the 14th cent, by the union of a basilica, a baptistery,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a small Byzantine church of the 6th century. To the right and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left of the portal are six Roman tombstones (busts in relief). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}greater part of the interior has been defaced with whitewash. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}facade is adorned with three busts of bishops in bronze. The altar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}niches contain two ancient mosaics (7th cent.), that to the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing Christ with SS. Justus and Servatius, that to the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mary with the archangels Michael and Gabriel; below, the Apostles.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Some of the capitals are antique, others Romanesque.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fouche , Due d'Otranto, the powerful minister of police of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Napoleon I., died at Trieste in 1820, and was interred on the Ter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}race in front of the church. Fine view thence of the town and sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hill above the cathedral is crowned by the Castle (PI. F, 3),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally built in 1680 and frequently restored.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Piazza Lipsia , which is embellished with pleasure^{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grounds, is the Nautical Academy (PI. 1; G, 5), containing on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the second floor the Ferdinand Maximilian Museum, the chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attraction of which is a complete collection of the fauna of the{% endraw %}

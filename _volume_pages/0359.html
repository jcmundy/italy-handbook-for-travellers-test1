{% raw %}The Climate of Venice is tempered by the proximity of the sea and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lagune. In January, the coldest month, the mean temperature is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}about 37° Fahr., from which it rises to 72-77° in June, July, and August.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In April the average is about 56° Fahr., in May 65°, in Sept. 69°, and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oct. 59°. The air is very humid, and consequently often favourable to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}catarrhal affections. Rheumatism is, on the contrary, rather prevalent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Its perfect immunity from dust is one of the chief advantages of Venice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and nervous patients will find another in its noiseless highways. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drinking-water is, as already mentioned, indifferent. Invalids who intend{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wintering in Venice should be particular as to a S. aspect. The quietest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}apartments are to be found in the Fondamenta delle Zattere (PL C, D, 5).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}During the Carnival no other city in Italy, Rome excepted, presents{% endraw %}
{% raw %}so busy and animated a scene as Venice. The Piazza S. Marco is then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}converted into a kind of vast ball-room. Balls also take place in the Ri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dotto and the Teatro Fenice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plan of Visit. A stay of 3-4 days may suffice when time is limited,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which case the following plan is recommended, but it may be extended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or modified at discretion.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Afternoon or Evening of arrival. In order to gratify their first curiosity,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and obtain a general idea of the peculiarities of Venice, travellers are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recommended to undertake a preliminary voyage from the Piazzetta along{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Grand Canal (see p. 255) to its extremity (near the railway-station is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church Degli Scalzi, see p. 261, which may now be visited on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}account of its remoteness from the other points of attraction); then under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the iron bridge to the Canal di Mestre, to the left of which is the Jews'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarter (the Ghetto, inhabited by the lowest classes); back hence by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grand Canal to the Ponte Rialto, where the gondola should be quitted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Then walk through the Merceria to the Piazza of St. Mark. The whole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}expedition will occupy 2-2'/2 hrs.{% endraw %}

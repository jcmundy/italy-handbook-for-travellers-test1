{% raw %}Modern historians describe the character of the Genoese in the middle-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages in a similar strain. The whole energy of the Genoese seems indeed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tp have been concentrated on commerce and the pursuit of gain. Notwith¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}standing their proud naval supremacy, they participated little in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intellectual development of Italy, and neither possessed a school of art,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nor produced any scholars of eminence. When at length the effete re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public was incorporated with Piedmont, it became the representative of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}radical principles as contrasted with the conservatism of the royalist terri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tory. Giuseppe Mazzini, the chief leader of the national revolutionary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}party, was born at Genoa in 1808, and Garibaldi, though born at Nice{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1807), was the son of a Genoese of Chiavari. The rivalry of the once{% endraw %}
{% raw %}far-famed republic with the upstart Turin, and of the restless harbour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}population with the stolid Piedmontese, have of recent years been pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ductive of very notable results. Modern Genoa has, moreover, regained its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient mercantile importance, and it is in the possession of the Ligurian{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Arrival. There are two stations at Genoa. The Stazione Piazza Principe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Restaurant), or principal station (for Alessandria, Turin, Spezia, Pisa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and for Savona and Nice), is in the Piazza Acquaverde (PL D, 1, 2; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goods-station only is in the Piazza del Principe). The arrangements are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admirable. A long row of omnibuses in the covered hall awaits the arri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}val of the trains. — The second station, called Stazione Piazza Brignole{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL H, 4), at the end of the Via Serra, and connected with the first by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}means of a tunnel below the higher parts of the town, is the first place{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where the Spezia and Pisa trains stop. — Travellers arriving at Genoa by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea, and wishing to continue their journey by rail without delay, may{% endraw %}
{% raw %}immediately after the custom-house examination, which takes place on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the quay, book their luggage there for their destination (taking care to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrust it to a facchino of the dogana, fee 20 c, and not to an un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}authorised bystander), and thus save much trouble.{% endraw %}

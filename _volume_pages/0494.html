{% raw %}Academy. PISA. 57. Route. 349{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Belle Arti (PI. 1 ; D, 3), founded by Napoleon in 1812, and recently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transformed, into a Scuola Tecnica-Industriale. The second floor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains a chronologically-arranged collection of pictures, chiefly of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the earlier Tuscan schools. It is, however, of little value to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}student of art, as Messrs. Crowe and Cavalcaselle and other au¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thorities deny the accuracy of most of the names attached to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room II. Barnaba da Modena, Madonna in trono (1370); Francesco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Traini, St. Dominic; Simone Martine (?), Part of a large altar-piece. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room III. Old drawing after Benozzo Gozzolis fresco of the 'Queen of Sheba{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visiting Solomon' in the Campo Santo (p. 347). In the centre, a Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}candelabrum. — Rooms IV., V., and VI. contain works by masters of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the 15th and of the 16th century. Also in Room IV., a Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sodoma; in Room VI., a St. Catharine, by a Flemish painter. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room VII. Sketches of the pictures in the cathedral, old missals and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}breviaries, tapestry. — Room VIII. Portraits of the ladies of the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grand-ducal court. — On the staircase: Portraits of the Grand-Dukes of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscany.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on are S. Frediano (PL 23), with ancient columns in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interior, and the University (p. 350).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the N.E. Quarter of the town the churches of S. Caterina{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and S. Francesco deserve notice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Caterina (PL 17; E, 2), which was erected about 1253,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possesses an interesting Pisan facade in the Gothic style.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. To the left of the entrance the monument of Archbishop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Simone iSaltarelli, by Nino Pisano, 1342. Altar-piece (3rd on the left) of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Thomas Aquinas, with his glory, by Francesco Traini, 1341. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1st chapel to the right of the choir, a Madonna with SS. Peter and Paul{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Fra Bartolommeo and Mariotto Albertinelli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church stands in a pleasant piazza, shaded with plane-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trees, and embellished with a Statue of the Grand Duke Leopold I.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1792), in Roman garb, by Pampaloni, erected in 1832.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Francesco (PI. 10; E, F, 3) contains frescoes in the choir{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Taddeo Gaddi (1342). Its cloisters are richly embellished with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns dating from the close of the 15th cent. Handsome cam¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}panile.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In and near the Lung-Arno are several other interesting build¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ings with which we may terminate our walk. In the Via del Borgo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}close to the Ponte di Mezzo (p. 343), rises —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Michele in Borgo (Pi. 29; E, 4), a church in the Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style of the 13th cent., which is said to have been designed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Niccolb Pisano (but more probably by his pupil Fra Guglielmo), with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an ancient crypt, supposed to occupy the site of a heathen temple.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The mosaic flooring in S. Pierino (PL 32; E, 4) is of early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Christian origin, and some of the columns are antique.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Lung-Arno Mediceo (to the E. of the Ponte di Mezzo) is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Lanfranchi (now Toscanelli), attributed to Michael{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Angelo, and occupied by Lord Byron in 1822.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Lung-Arno Regio (to the W. of the Ponte di Mezzo){% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises the *Palazzo Agostini, a fine Gothic brick edifice of the 15th{% endraw %}

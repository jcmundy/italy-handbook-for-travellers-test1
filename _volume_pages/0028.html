{% raw %}It would, however, be a mistake to suppose that this strip of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast is entirely free from wind. The rapid heating and cooling of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the strand produces numerous light breezes, while the rarefaction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the masses of air by the strength of the sun gives rise to strong{% endraw %}
{% raw %}currents rushing in from the E. and W. to supply the vacuum. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most notorious of these coast-winds is the Mistral, which is at its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worst at Avignon (p. 12) and other places in the Rhone Valley,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where it may be said without exaggeration to blow on one of every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two days. As a rule this wind lasts for a period of 3-17 days at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}time, rising at about 10 a.m. and subsiding at sunset; and each{% endraw %}
{% raw %}such period is generally followed by an interval of calm and fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}weather. As the Mistral sweeps the coast from W. to E. it gradu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally loses its strength, so that at SanRemo, for instance, it is much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}less violent than at Cannes or Hyeres. The N.E. wind on the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trary is much stronger in Alassio and San Remo than on the coast{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Provence. The Scirocco as known on the Ligurian coast is by no{% endraw %}
{% raw %}means the dry and parching wind experienced in Sicily and even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Rome; passing as it does over immense tracts of sea it is gener¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally charged with moisture and is often followed by rain.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The prevalent belief that the Riviera has a moist climate, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}account of its proximity to the sea, is natural but erroneous. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}atmosphere, on the contrary, is rather dry, especially in the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}half of it, while the humidity rapidly increases as we approach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Riviera di Levante. The same holds good of the rainfall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}While Nice has 36 rainy days between November and April Men-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tone has 44, Nervi 54, and Pisa 63. The average number of rainy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days during the three winter months in the Riviera is 16. Snow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is rarely seen; it falls perhaps once or twice in the course of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}winter, but generally lies only for a few hours, while many years{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pass without the appearance of a single snow-flake. Fogs are very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rare on the Ligurian coast; but a heavy dew-fall in the evening is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rule. In comparison with the Cisalpine districts, the Riviera{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enjoys a very high proportion of bright, sunny weather.{% endraw %}

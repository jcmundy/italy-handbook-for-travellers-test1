{% raw %}Climate. San Remo is surrounded by an unbroken semicircular{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill sloping upwards from the Capo Nero by La Colla to its culminating{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point in the Piano Carparo and Monte Bignone, which attain a height of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nearly 4000 ft., and descending thence to the Capo Verde, the summit of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this barrier being nowhere more than 4 M. distant in a straight line.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The N. winds are therefore entirely excluded from this favoured spot,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}especially as a double range of Alps rises behind the town a little farther{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, while at the same time the violence of the E. and W. winds is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}much broken. Violent E. winds, however, frequently occur at the end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of February and the beginning of March, and the 'Mistral' and 'Brise' are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also unwelcome visitors at this season. Rain is not unfrequent in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter half of October and the first half of November, but December and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}January are usually calm and sunny. — Consumptive and bronchial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}patients are recommended to reside in the E. bay on account of its more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sheltered situation and more humid atmosphere, while sufferers from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liver and similar complaints will find the dry and stimulating air of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. bay more beneficial.{% endraw %}

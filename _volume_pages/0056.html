{% raw %}A reminiscence of his earlier period, when he wrought under{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the most famous of his pictures in the Italian galleries: — in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ambrosiana of Milan the Portrait of Isabella of Arragon, wife of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giov. Galeazzo Sforza; in the Palazzo Pitti the Goldsmith and the{% endraw %}

{% raw %}find Leonardo's drawings in the Ambrosiana exceedingly interesting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The best insight into Leonardo's style, and his reforms in the art of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colouring, is obtained by an attentive examination of the works{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Milan school (Luini, Salaino ; p. 129), as these are far better{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preserved than the original works of the master, of which (his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}battle-cartoon having been unfortunately lost with the exception of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a single equestrian group) the Last Supper in S. Maria delle Grazie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Milan is noAv the only worthy representative. Although now a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}total wreck, it is still well calculated to convey an idea of the new{% endraw %}
{% raw %}epoch of Leonardo. The spectator should first examine the delicate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equilibrium of the composition, and observe how the individual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}groups are complete in themselves, and yet simultaneously point to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a common centre and impart a monumental character to the work;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then the remarkable physiognomical fidelity which pervades every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}detail, the psychological distinctness of character, and the dramatic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life, together with the calmness of the entire bearing of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture. He will then comprehend that with Leonardo a new era{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Italian painting was inaugurated, that the development of art{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had attained its perfection.{% endraw %}

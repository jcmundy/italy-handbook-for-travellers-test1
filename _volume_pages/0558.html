{% raw %}we observe the second row of cloisters, by Brunelleschi, one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the finest Renaissance works of the kind. To thelright (on enter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing), on the other side of the cloisters, is the old Refectory,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing a Last Supper by one of Giotto's best pupils, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crucifixion, with a genealogical tree of the Franciscans, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the legend of St. Francis, by an inferior hand. The Tribunal of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Inquisition, which was abolished by Grand - duke Peter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leopold, once held its sittings here. An adjoining room (to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which a few steps ascend opposite the door) contains a *Miracle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of St. Francis (multiplication of the loaves), a fresco by Giovanni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}da S. Giovanni, with a portrait of the painter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leaving the Piazza S. Croce , we proceed to the S. through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via de' Benci, at the end of which, on the right, No. 1, is the Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo Alberti (once the residence of Leon Batt. Alberti; restored{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1850), and reach the Ponte alle Grazie, see p. 440.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little to the N. of S. Croce, at the corner of the Via Buonar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}roti , Via Ghibellina 64, is the House of Michael Angelo (PL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}G, 6). In the 17th cent, a descendant of his family founded here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a collection of pictures and antiquities, which the last of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Buonarroti bequeathed to the city. This * Galleria Buonarroti{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merits a visit chiefly on account of the designs and other reminis¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cences of Michael Angelo (adm., Mon. and Thurs., 9-3; cat. 50 c).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ante-Chamber: to the right, 19. Pordenone, Conversation-piece; *14.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Battle of the Lapithse and Centaurs, in relief, an early work by Michael{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Angelo, whose delight in bold movements, defiant attitudes, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representation of vehement passion, is already apparent. Adjoining it,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12. the arm of a Discus-thrower (ancient); above, Caryatide by one of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Robbia; *5. a predella representing the *Legend of St. Nicholas by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pesellino. Above these are two portraits of Michael Angelo; on the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2., by his pupil Mar cello Venusti; on the left, 3., a later work, represent¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing Michael Angelo as a young man.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the Left of the ante-chamber is the Room of the Drawings: to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, Head of Cleopatra; then the first design of the monument of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medici in S. Lorenzo; various studies for the Last Judgment in the Sistine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chapel, and also the first design for that work. In the centre of the end-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall , a "Madonna with the Infant Christ (executed in colours); Arch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}itectural sketches, including the first design for the facade of S. Lorenzo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the Right of the ante-chamber is the — I. Room : 57. Statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Michael Angelo in a sitting posture, executed by Ant. Novelli, in 1620; on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the walls are eighteen scenes from the great master's life, some of them{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in colours and others in grisaille, by painters of the 16th and 17th centuries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the ceiling are similar scenes and, allegories by the same artists. Op¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posite the statue is a large oil-painting of the Madonna and Christ, St. John,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and other saints by Jacopo da Empoli, of which Michael Angelo is said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have drawn the design. — II. Room (chapel): 74. Madonna and Child, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}basrelief in marble, another early work of Michael Angelo; 73. Cast of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Descent from the Cross; *81. Bronze bust of Michael Angelo, by Ric-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciarelli. — III. Room (Archives of MSS. of Michael Angelo). — Rooms IV{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and V contain clay models and autographs of the master. In the last{% endraw %}
{% raw %}room are some Etruscan terracottas and majolica.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Above the door of No. 93, Via dell' Agnolo, the next street, is{% endraw %}

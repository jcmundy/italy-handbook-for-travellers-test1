{% raw %}In 1326, Francesco Petrarca, then 22 years of age, visited Avignon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and beheld Laura de Ifoves, who was in her 18th year, at the church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the nunnery of St. Claire. Her beauty impressed the ardent young{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian so profoundly, that, although he never received the slightest token{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of regard from the object of his romantic attachment, either before or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after hert marriage with Hugues de Sade, he continued throughout his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole lifetime to celebrate her praises in songs and sonnets. In 1334 he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quitted Avignon for Vaucluse, travelled in France, Germany, and Italy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and returned to Avignon in 1342 (with his friend Cola di Rienzi), where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}he found Laura the mother of a numerous family. She died in 1348,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bowed down by domestic affliction. Petrarch lived till 1374, and long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after Laura's death dedicated many touching lines to her memory.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}226 Route 38. PADUA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dante's House is No. 3359, Ponte S. Lorenzo. In front of it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a mediaeval sarcophagus, which, as travellers are assured, contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bones of the Trojan Antenor, who, according to Virgil, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the founder of Padua.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S.E. of the Prato is situated the church of *S. Giustina{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 16; C, 4), an edifice of strikingly noble and imposing pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portions, completed in 1516 by Andrea Riccio or Briosco. The un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned facade of brick is approached by a handsome flight of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twelve steps, of the entire breadth of the structure. The interior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consists of a nave and aisles, bordered on each side by a row of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels. The aisles are roofed with barrel vaulting, the nave with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three flat domes. The transept and choir are terminated by semi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular recesses and surmounted by four lofty cupolas.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church is paved with black, yellow, and red marble. In the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transept is the sarcophagus of St. Luke, in the right transept that of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Matthew. Over the high-altar, which contains the tomb of St. Justina,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the "Martyrdom of St. Justina, by Paolo Veronese. Magnificently carved{% endraw %}
{% raw %}"Choir-stalls from drawings of Gampagnola (1552), in 50 different sections,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each representing a subject from the New Testament above, and one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Old below. In the chapel on the right of the choir is represented{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Virgin with the body of Christ, at the sides John and Mary Magda¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lene, a large group in marble by Parodi (17th cent.). The old choir, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sole remnant of the original church, also possesses fine carved stalls.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity is the Botanic Garden (PL 32; C, 4), founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1545 and one of the oldest in Europe, well stocked with trees{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peculiar to the south. It contains a palm, 30ft. high, which served{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a type to Goethe in his theory of the metamorphosis of plants.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tree is now enclosed in a building with a suitable inscription.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}longer axis is 150yds., the shorter 116yds. long; the arena75yds.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long and 43 yds. wide. It possessed five corridors and forty-three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiers of seats, holding 25,000 spectators. The two stories of 60{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arches, the lower being Doric, the upper Corinthian, present a most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imposing aspect. The entrance is on the N. side.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior (the concierge lives opposite the N. entrance) was for¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}merly occupied by a number of dwellings tenanted by poor families, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}these have been almost entirely removed since 1846-47. After the Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period the amphitheatre was employed by the Goths, then by the Sara¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cens, and again by Charles Martel (who expelled the latter in 739), as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stronghold, two of the four towers of which are still standing. A stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case of 103 steps ascends the W. tower, which commands a pleasing sur¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vey of the neighbourhood. The vaults beneath the lowest tier of seats{% endraw %}
{% raw %}served as receptacles for the wild beasts, the gladiators, etc. They com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}municated with the arena by means of six doors. The spectators of high{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rank occupied the front seats and were protected from the attacks of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wild animals by a lofty parapet. Bloodless bull-fights are now occasion¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally exhibited here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Place du Forum, the site of the ancient market-place,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two granite pillars and fragments of a Corinthian pediment are still{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seen (near the Hotel du Nord). ■— On the bank of the Rhone lie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the remains of a palatial building, probably of the time of Con-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stantine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the S.E. side of the town are the Champs Elysees (Aliscamps),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally a Roman burying-ground, consecrated by St. Trophimus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and furnished by him with a chapel. In the middle ages this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cemetery enjoyed such celebrity that bodies were conveyed hither{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for sepulture from vast distances. It is mentioned by Dante in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his Inferno (9, 112): 'Si come ad Arli, ove Rodano stagna, . . .{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fanno i sepolcri tutto il loco varo\ ('As at Aries where the Rhone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is dammed, .... the graves make the whole ground uneven'.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}To this day many ancient sarcophagi are still to be seen in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}environs of the curious old church, although after the first Re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}volution great numbers were sold to relic-hunters from all parts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the world.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Arles to Montpellier (p. 15) a branch-line runs in I72 hr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Below Aries begins the flat delta of the estuary of the Rhone called{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the He de la Camargue. It is protected against the incursions of the sea{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by dykes, and is employed partly as arable and partly as pasture land,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which supports numerous flocks and herds. A canal, constructed in 1864-71,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}admits vessels to the estuary of the Rhone, which had previously been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inaccessible.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Between Aries and Salon the line intersects the stony plain of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crau, which the ancients mention as the scene of the contest of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hercules with the Ligures. Near St. Chamas the line skirts the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long Etang de Berre, an extensive inland-lake on the right. From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(519 M.) Rognac a branch-line diverges to Aix, the ancient Aquae{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sextiae. Beyond (525 M.) Pas-des-Lanciers the train traverses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longest tunnel in France, nearly 3 M. in length, on emerging from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which it passes some grand rocky scenery. The sea now comes in{% endraw %}

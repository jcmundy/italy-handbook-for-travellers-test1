{% raw %}*S. Lorenzo (PI. D,7), the most ancient church in Milan. Whether{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the handsome interior once formed the principal hall of the thermae,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or of a palace of Maximian (4th cent.), to which the above-mentioned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnade belonged, or a very ancient Christian place of worship,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}like S. Vitale at Ravenna, is uncertain. It was subsequently altered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at least three times, the last time by Martino Bassi in the 16th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}century. It is octagonal in form, and covered with a dome. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the four principal sides are large semicircular apses in two stories,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}each borne by four columns alternately octagonal and round, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the whole structure is simple and dignified. At the back of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high altar is the Cappella S. Ippolito, containing the tomb of Maria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visconti. To the right of the church is the Chapel of St. Aqui-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}linus, containing mosaics of the 6th and 7th cent. (Christ and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the apostles), and an ancient Christian sarcophagus supposed to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that of the founder, the Gothic king Ataulph (411-16). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance to the chapel is adorned with an antique marble coping.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther S. is the Porta Ticinese, which was originally intended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to commemorate the Battle of Marengo and in 1814 received the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscription 'Paci Populorum Sospitse'. Adjacent rises the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Eustorgio (PL D, 8), founded in the 4th cent., re-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in the Gothic style by Tosano Lombardo in 1278, renewed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the bad taste of the 17th cent, by Richini, and recently again{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored. The facade is modern.{% endraw %}

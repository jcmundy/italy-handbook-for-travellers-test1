{% raw %}The Interior consists of nave and aisles with transept, and is flank¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed with chapels at the sides and adjoining the high-altar. To the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the central door is a marble altar by Benedetto da Rovezzano; to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, Mary Magdalene, a statue in wood by Desiderio da Settignano; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4th Chapel on the right contains the Annunciation by the Camaldulensian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monk Don Lorenzo. The '"Cappella de' Sassetti, the second on the right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the high altar, is adorned with frescoes from the life of St. Francis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Dom. Ghirlandajo, dating from 1485, and presents a model of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sistent ornamentation. The frescoes are in double rows. We begin with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the upper row, to the left: 1. St. Francis banished from his father's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house; 2. Pope Honorius confirms the rules of the order; 3. St. Francis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in presence of the Sultan. On the right: 1. St. Francis receiving the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stigmata; 2. Resuscitation of a child of the Spini family; 3. Interment of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the saint. It is interesting to compare the last of these scenes with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giotto's rendering of the same subject in S. Croce (p. 407). The sibyls{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the ceiling are also by Ghirlandajo. *Tombs of the Sassetti by Giul. da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}San Gallo (?). Above the altar, Pieta by Barbieri (18th cent.). — Left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aisle : 3rd Chapel, early Christian sarcophagus. — In the Sacristy, form¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erly the Chapel of the Strozzi, is a monument of 1417, in the style of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Donatello.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the church lies the Piazza S. Trinita, adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a column of granite from the Baths of Caracalla at Rome, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here in 1563, and furnished in 1570 with an inscription in honour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cosimo I., who had just been made grand-duke by the pope.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the summit is placed a statue of Justice in porphyry, by Fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Taddi, added in 1581. The figure was afterwards considered too{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slender, and consequently draped with a robe of bronze. — To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right is the Hotel du Nord, formerly the Palazzo Bartolini-Salimbeni,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Baccio d'Agnolo in 1520, and the first secular building{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which pointed and circular gables were alternated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on in the busy Via Tornabuoni (PL C, 4), with its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome palaces and tempting shops, is situated the * Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strozzi (PL D, E, 4), begun in 1489 by Benedetto da Majano for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the celebrated Fiiippo Strozzi (comp. p. 362), but not reaching its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present state of completion till 1553. Lighter and more buoyant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the Pal. Pitti, it presents an example of the Florentine pala¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tial style in its most perfect development. It possesses three im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posing facades (that towards the Via Tornabuoni is 42 yds. in width,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 105 ft. in height), constructed in huge 'bossages', and a hand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some court added by Cronaca. Celebrated cornice by Cronaca. Tha{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corner lanterns (by Caparra), the link-holders, and the rings are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among the finest specimens of Italian ironwork of the period. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}works of art formerly contained here were sold in 1878 and re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moved to Berlin. — In the small piazza at the back of the Pal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strozzi stands the Palazzo Strozzino, a smaller building in a similar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style, with a fine court.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}14. From Turin to Genoa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a. Via Alessandria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}103 M. Railway in 474-7 hrs. (fares 18 fr. 80, 13 fr. 50, 9 fr. 40 c.; ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}press 20 fr. 70, 14 fr. 50 c).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The line , the construction of which was zealously promoted by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Count Cavour in order to bring Genoa into closer relations with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turin (opened in 1853), at first proceeds towards the S., at some{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distance from the left bank of the Po. Near (5M.) Moncalieri, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the line turns to the E. , the river is crossed by a bridge of seven{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arches. On a height above Moncalieri, which is picturesquely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated on the hill-side, rises the handsome royal chateau, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Victor Emmanuel I. died in 1823. A final retrospect is now ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained of the hills of Turin, and, to the left, of the principal snowy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summits of the Alps. At (8 M.) Trofarello branch-lines diverge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Savona(p. 102) and Cuneo (p. 118), and to Chieri. Stations Cam-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}biano, Pessione, Villanova , Villafranca , Baldichieri, S. Damiano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The line then crosses the Borbone, and reaches the valley of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tanaro, on the left bank of which it runs to Alessandria.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}with 17,300 inhab. , and numerous towers , the birthplace of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dramatist Alfieri (d. 1803), is famous for its sparkling wine and its{% endraw %}

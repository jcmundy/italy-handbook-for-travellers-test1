{% raw %}Pallanza, a thriving little town with 3900 inhab., delightfully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated opposite the Borromean Islands, commands a beautiful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}view of them, the lake, and the Alps to the N. As the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sheltered and warmest spot on the Lago Maggiore, it enjoys a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}repute as a winter-resort, especially as an intermediate stage be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the Riviera and more northerly climes. The banks of the lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are skirted by pleasant promenades. The nursery gardens of Ro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}velli, Cerutti, and others deserve a visit (fee xj^-i fr.). Pleasant walk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the new road round the Monte Rosso, ascending by the brook{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Bernardino and past the Madonna della Campagna and the vil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lage of Trobaso to the old Roman bridge of Santino (I72 hr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The lake here forms an extensive bay, 472 M. long and 2'/4M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wide, running in a N.W. direction, at the N. extremity of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the influx of the impetuous Tosa (Toce). On its N.E. bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies Suna, on the S.W. Feriolo, where the Simplon route (p. 27){% endraw %}
{% raw %}quits the lake; the steamboat does not always touch at these two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stations. — Then Baveno (*Grand Hotel Bellevue, R. 4, B. 172,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}D. 472, L. & A. 1 fr.; *Beaurivage, both with gardens; Hotel-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pension Suisse), a village with 1900 inhabitants. The handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Villa Clara (proprietor Mr. Henfrey) was occupied by Queen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Victoria for some weeks in the spring of 1879. This is the usual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starting-point of travellers from the Simplon for a visit to the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Borromean Islands. The steamers touch only at the most S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of these, the Isola Bella, which with the Isola Madre is the property{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Borromeo family. Between these lies (W.) the Isola dei{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pescatori, or Superiore, the property of the fishermen who inhabit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it; to the N. is the Isola S. Giovanni mentioned above.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Loggia, dei Lanzi. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 377{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. side of the piazza rises the Palazzo Fenzi, built by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Landi (1871) in the early-Florentine style, which has been adopted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in many of the newer edifices. In the S. angle of the piazza rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Loggia dei Lanzi (PI. E, 5), originally called Loggia dei Sig¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nori, a magnificent open vaulted hall of the kind with which it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}usual to provide both the public and private palaces of Florence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in order that the inmates might enjoy the open air, or participate in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}public demonstration's, without being obliged to descend to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street. This structure was projected in 1356, having perhaps been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Andrea di Clone (Orcagna), but was not erected till{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1376. Benci di Cione Dami and Simone di Tolenti are said to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been the architects. The style of the architecture shows a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falling off from the Gothic, while the sculptures (Faith, Hope,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Charity, Temperance, and Fortitude, by Agnolo Gaddi, 1383){% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhibit an incipient leaning to the Renaissance forms. The present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name of the loggia dates from the time of the Grand Duke Cosimo I.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when his German spearsmen or 'lancers' were posted here as guards.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the Steps are two lions; that on the right is antique, the other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Flaminio Vacca. — Under the arches, to the right, is the ::Rape of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sabines, a group in marble executed by Giovanni da Bologna in 1583 with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a life like relief on the base; on the left 'Perseus with the head of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medusa, in bronze, by Benvenuto Cellini (1553), who also executed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statuettes and basreliefs of the pedestal; behind it the Rape of Polyxena,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large group in marble by Fedi, erected in 1866. To the left of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter, Judith and Holofernes in bronze, by Donatello, with the inscription{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Salutis Publicae Exemplum', erected in front of the Palazzo Vecchio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after the expulsion of the Medici. In the centre *Ajax with the body of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Achilles, an ancient copy of a Greek work, brought here from Rome in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1570. To the right of it, Hercules slaying the centaur Nessus, in marble,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Giov. da Bologna. By the wall at the back are five antique portrait-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues, and a figure of '*Germania devicta' (the so-called Thusnelda; 3rd{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left), in which the expression of grief in the barbaric but noble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}countenance is admirably depicted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the corner of the Pal. Vecchio begins the Portico degli Uffizi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL E, 6), erected 1560-74 by Vasari, the niches of which were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adorned with *Marble Statues of celebrated Tuscans in 1842-56.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The names of the persons represented and of the respective sculptors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are engraved on the bases. On the side next the Arno is a statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Cosimo I. by Giov. da Bologna, with figures of Justice and Power{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Danti. Fine view hence of S. Miniato.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}$In this colonnade, to the right, is the Post Office, formerly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mint.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Approaching from the Piazza della Signoria, we enter by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second door to the left under this portico, and ascend by a staircase{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 126 steps (lift, 1 fr.) to the **Galleria degli Uffizi (admission,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}see p. 369). The gallery originated with the Medici collections, to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which numerous additions were made by the LoTraine family, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is now one of the best in the world, both in extent and value.{% endraw %}

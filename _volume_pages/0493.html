{% raw %}works of art at Pisa, but he will be amply rewarded by taking a{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The central part of ancient Pisa, and the forum of the republic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is now the Piazza dei Cavalieri (PL D, 3), formerly degli Anziani,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was remodelled in the 16th and 17th centuries. In this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piazza rises —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Stefano ai Cavalieri (PI. 18), the church of the knights of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Order of St. Stephen, begun from designs by Vasari in 1565;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interior completed 1596 ; facade designed by Buontalenti. It contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turkish trophies on the right and left of the door, and ceiling-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings of the battle of Lepanto (1571) and other victories over{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Turks, by Cristoforo Allori, Jacopo da Empoli, and others.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the 2nd altar to the left a Nativity by Alessandro Allori: 'Quem{% endraw %}
{% raw %}genuit adoravit', a finely-conceived work, 1564. Excellent organ.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Palazzo Conventuale dei Cavalieri, adjoining the church,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altered by Vasari, is now a school; above the windows are busts of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}six masters of the order ; in front of the building a marble Statue of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Grand-Duke Cosimo, designed by Giov. da Bologna and executed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Francavilla (1596). Opposite to it once stood (down to 1655) the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ill-famed 'Tower of Hunger', properly Torre dei Gualandi alle Sette{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vie, in which Archb. Ruggieri degli Ubaldini caused Count Vgolino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dei Gherardeschi with his sons and nephews to be starved to death{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1288 as a punishment for treason, as described by Dante in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}33rd canto of his Inferno.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The neighbouring church of S. Sisto (PI. 36; C, 3) was founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Pisans to commemorate several of their victories on the day{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Sisto, 6th Aug., 1089. It contains a number of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns of marble and granite. The church was frequently used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a place of assembly by the Great Council of Pisa.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}valley of the Magnan (p. 113), in which a road ascends to (2 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church of La Madeleine. The beautiful, sheltered banks of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Var, which falls into the Baie des Anges, 33/4 M. to the "W. of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nice, are also worthy of a visit (one day; carr. with two horses,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}18. From Nice to Turin by the Col di Tenda.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}UO1/2 M. — Messagekies to Cuneo (861/2 M.) in 18-22 hrs. (fares 25{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 22 fr.). Railway from Cuneo to Turin (54 M.) in 3hrs. (fares 9fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}95c, 7fr., 5fr.). — Office at Nice in the Hotel de l'Univers (p. 110), not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}far from the French theatre; at Turin, in the Via Cavour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}This is a very attractive route, especially for those coming from Turin.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The views during the descent from the Col di Tenda to the Mediterranean{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are strikingly beautiful. In winter the road is often impassable for a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerable time.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road leads from Nice, on the bank of the Paglione, through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the villages of La Trinite- Vittoria and Drappo, beyond which it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses and quits the river.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12 M. (from Nice) Scarena, Fr. Escarene. The road hence to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sospello traverses a sterile and unattractive district. The barren{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rocks which enclose the bleak valley are curiously stratified at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}places. The road ascends to the Col di Braus (4232 ft.). To{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S., on a lofty rock to the right, is seen the castle of Castillon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Castiglione. At the foot of the pass on the E. lies —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}251/2 M. Sospello, French Sospel (1174 ft.; Hotel Carenco), sit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uated in the valley of the Bevera (affluent of the Roja, see below),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the midst of olive-plantations, and surrounded by lofty moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains. [From Sospello to (14 M.) Mentone, see p. 108.] The road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now ascends to the Col di Brouis (2871 ft.). Near the summit of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pass a final view is obtained of the Mediterranean. Scenery{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unattractive, mountains bleak and barren. Then a descent to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}38 M. Giandola (1250 ft.; Hotel des Etrangers; Poste), in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grand situation at the base of lofty rocks. Breglio, a town with 2500{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inh. and the ruined castle of Trivella, lies lower down on the right.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road now ascends the narrow valley of the Roja, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}falls into the sea near Ventimiglia (p. 106). Saorgio, rising in ter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}races on a lofty rock on the right, with the ruins of a castle in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Oriental style, destroyed by the French in 1792, commands the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road. On the opposite side is a monastery of considerable extent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The valley contracts, so as barely to leave room for the river and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road between the perpendicular rocks. Several small villages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are situated at the points where the valley expands. Beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(43 M.) Fontana the road crosses the Italian frontier. The southern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character of the vegetation now disappears. 48 M. S. Dalmazzo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where an old abbey is fitted up as a hydropathic establishment,{% endraw %}

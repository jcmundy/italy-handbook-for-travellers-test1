{% raw %}These noble portraits were executed in 1537, when the Duke was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appointed Generalissimo of the League against the Turks. The Duke{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has a martial bearing, the look of the Duchess is stately but subdued.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To make the difference apparent between the blanched complexion of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dame accustomed to luxury and ease and the tanned face of a soldier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}habitually exposed to the weather, Titian skilfully varied the details of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}technical execution. Here he is minute and finished, there resolute and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}broad. Here the tinted and throbbing flesh is pitted against a warm light{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground, there the sallow olive against a dark wall. — C. dc C.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}**626. Titian, The so-called 'Flora', painted after 1520.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}There is nothing in this ethereal Flora to shock the sensitive eye.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The proportions and features are of surprising loveliness, reminding us{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in their purity of some of the choicest antiques. The masterly and clear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}light scale is attained by the thin disposal of pigments, the broad plane{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of tinting, and the delicate shade of all but imperceptible half-tones. —{% endraw %}

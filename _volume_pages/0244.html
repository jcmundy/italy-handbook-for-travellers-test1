{% raw %}The Corso S. Celso (PL E, 7, 8) leads back from this point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the interior of the city. To the right in the Piazza S. Eufemia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the church of that name (PI. E, 7), dating from the 5th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but entirely modernised. Farther towards the N. is situated —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Alessandro (PL E, 6 ; in the Via Amedei, to the right), erect¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed in 1602; it is a reduced and in the interior successful copy of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peter's at Rome, with two W. towers. It is the most sumptuously{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorated church in Milan, but destitute of works of art. High-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar adorned with precious stones. — Adjacent is the Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Trivulzio, containing an art-collection in which the most note¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}worthy objects are a portrait by Antonello da Messina, a Madonna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Mantegna, and the tomb of Azzo Visconti (d. 1329) from S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gottardo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We return by the Via Lupetta and the Via Torino to the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Duomo. To the right in the Via Torino is the small church of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Satiro (PI. E, 5, 6), founded in the 9th cent., and re-erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Bramante and his pupil Suardi in the 15th century. The ap¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parent choir is only painted in perspective. The octagonal *Sacristy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a handsome frieze by Caradosso, halfway up the wall, is also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Bramante. At the end of the left transept is a curious little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building with a cupola, belonging, like the belfry, to the original{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure; it contains a Descent from the Cross, in terracotta, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Caradosso.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church of S. Giorgio al Palazzo (PL D, 6), in the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Torino, contains an *Entombment by Luini. — Farther to the N.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Piazza S. Marta, is a Monument by Luigi Belli, erected in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1881 in memory of the Italians who fell at Mentana.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. in the Piazza del Duomo, opposite the cathedral, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Reale and the Archiepiscopal Palace, both already men¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tioned (p. 132). The Piazza Fontana (PL F, 5), to the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pal. Arcivescovile, is adorned with a statue of Beccaria (d. 1794 ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}comp. p. 134) by Grandi, erected in 1871. Adjacent is the Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo di Giustizia (PL F, 5), built by Vine. Seregni; on the portal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a tablet commemorating the Italian patriots committed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Austrians to the fortress of Spielberg in 1821.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Via Brolo leads hence to the S. to the Piazza S. Stefano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the simple Renaissance church of that name (PL F, 6). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via dell' Ospedale leads S.W. to the Corso di Porta Romana.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Ospedale Maggiore (PL F, 6), a vast and remarkably fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brick structure , half Gothic and half Renaissance in style, begun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1457 by Antonio Filarete of Florence, is one of the largest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hospitals in existence, and contains no fewer than nine courts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The extensive principal court, surrounded by arcades, is by Richini{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(17th cent.); the court to the right of it is ascribed to Bramante.{% endraw %}

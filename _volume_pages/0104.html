{% raw %}9 M. Berisal (5006 ft.), the Third Refuge (*H6tel de la Poste,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}R. 272 fr.). Above the Fourth Refuge (5645 ft.) a retrospect is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}obtained in clear weather of the Bernese Alps (to the N.), from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the huge Aletsch Glacier descends. The part of the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Fifth Refuge (6358 ft.) and the culminating point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the most dangerous during the period of avalanches and storms.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road passes through the Kaltwasser Glacier Gallery (6460 ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over which the stream issuing from the glacier is precipitated into{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the depths below, forming a waterfall which is visible through a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side opening. The road then passes through two other galleries.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Sixth Refuge (6540 ft.) a splendid final view is enjoyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Bernese Alps; far below in the Rhone Valley lies Brieg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Simplon Pass (6595 ft.) is 6 M. from Be'risal. About{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3/4 M. beyond the summit is the Hospice (no payment demanded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for hospitality, but travellers should contribute at least as much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the poor-box as they would have paid at an hotel), a spacious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building founded by Napoleon , but not completed till 1825. A{% endraw %}
{% raw %}broad, open valley, bounded by snow-capped heights and glaciers,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms the highest portion of the Pass. The imposing Raut Glacier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a conspicuous object on the mountains to the S.; to the E. rises{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Monte Leone (11,696 ft.). The Old Hospice, a lofty square tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now tenanted by herdsmen, lies on the right far below the road.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}curve to the S., which pedestrians may cut off by a rough path regain¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of the Simplon route begins. It leads through the *Ravine of Gondo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the wildest and grandest in the Alps, becoming narrower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and more profound at every step, until its smooth and precipitous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}walls of mica-slate completely overhang the road, below which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rushes the impetuous Doveria. The most remarkable of the cut¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tings by which the road penetrates the rocks is the Gallery of{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the Fressinone (or Alpienbach) forms a fine waterfall, which is cross¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed by a slender bridge. On both sides the rocks tower to a dizzy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height of 2000 ft. The dark entrance of the tunnel forms a striking{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contrast to the white foam of the falling torrent. This magnificent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Alpine Scene, especially when viewed at a distance of 40-50 paces,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surpasses the Via Mala (p. 37). Gondo (2818 ft.) is the last Swiss{% endraw %}

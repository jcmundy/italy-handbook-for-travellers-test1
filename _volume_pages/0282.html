{% raw %}27. From Stresa to Varallo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Motterone. Lake of Orta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Three days suffice for a visit to this district, which, though seldom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visited, is one of the most beautiful of the S. Alps. Travellers from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Simplon (R. 3) should, after visiting the Borromean Islands, begin this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excursion at Stresa (p. 172) and terminate it at Arona. From Stresa or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Isola Bella by the Motterone to Orta 9, from Orta (or rather from Pella) to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Varallo 472 hrs. walking; from Varallo to Arona 5, to Novara 6 hrs. drive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A Guide (to the summit of Monte Motterone 5-6, to Orta 8 fr.; donkey{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and attendant to. Orta 12 fr. and fee) can hardly be dispensed with. Mules{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Orta at high charges. — The ascent of the Motterone is fatiguing, as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the descent must be made the same day, but presents no difficulty and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is very attractive.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Lago Maggiore is separated from the Lake of Orta by a long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountain ridge, which is crossed by a footpath from Stresa (p. 172){% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 5-6 hrs. via Gignese, Coiro, and Armeno (where the high-road is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reached) to Orta (see below). — Farther to the N. this mountain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}culminates in the grassy *Monte Motterone (4891 ft.), Monterone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Margozzolo. The path from Stresa (guide desirable, see above){% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends opposite the Isola Bella, at first through a chestnut grove;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}then, above the village of Someraro, over fern-clad and grassy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slopes, passing several chalets shaded by lofty trees, and leading to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the W. to a small church, where it turns to the right. Thence to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the summit 1 hr. more.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The extensive prospect commanded by the summit embraces the entire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}amphitheatre of mountains from Monte Rosa to the Ortler in the Tyrol.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}of Monte Rosa appear the snow-mountains of Monte Moro, Pizzo di Bot-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tarello, Simplon, Monte Leone, Gries, and St. Gotthard; farther E. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conical Stella above Chiavenna, and the long, imposing ice-range of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bernina, which separates the Val Bregaglia from the Val Tellina. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spectator's feet lie seven different lakes, the Lake of Orta, Lago di Mer-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gozzo, Lago Maggiore, Lago di Monate, Lago di Comabbio, Lago di Bian¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}drone , and Lago di Varese; farther to the right stretch the extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plains of Lombardy and Piedmont, in the centre of which rises the lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cathedral of Milan. The Ticino and the Sesia meander like silver threads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the plains, and by a singular optical delusion frequently appear{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to traverse a lofty table-land. The simultaneous view of the Isola Madre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Lago Maggiore and the Isola S. Giulio in the Lake of Orta has a re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}markably picturesque effect. — The mountain itself consists of a number{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of barren summits, studded with occasional chalets, shaded by trees. At{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its base it is encircled by chestnut-trees, and the foliage and luxuriant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vegetation of the landscape far and wide impart a peculiar charm to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}38 Route 5. SPLUGEN. From Coire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}♦Second Bridge (2844ft.), built in 1739,1 M. from Rongellen. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rhine, 260 ft. below the road, winds through a ravine so narrow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that the precipices above almost meet. At the third bridge, built{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1834, about 1 M. farther, the Via Mala ends.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road now enters the more open Valley of Schams, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}green meadows and cheerful cottages of which present a pleas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ant contrast to the sombre defile just quitted. To the S. in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}background are the peaks of the Hirli (9373 ft.). Above the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge the Rhine forms a small waterfall. The first village in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of Schams (6 M. from Thusis) is Zillis, Roman. Ciraun{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(3061 ft.; Post), with the oldest church in the valley. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hill to the right, on the left bank of the Rhine, stands the ruined{% endraw %}
{% raw %}castle of Fardiin, or La Turr. Below lies the village of Donat, above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which towers the Piz Beverin. On the same bank are the village{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Clugin and the tower of the ruined castle of Cagliatscha.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2372 M. Andeer (3212 ft. ; *Krone, or Hotel Fravi) is the prin¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cipal village in the valley, with 600 inhabitants. Fine view of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley from the church, built in 1673.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road ascends in windings , passes the ruins of the Bdren-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}burg, and enters the *Rofna Ravine, a gorge 3 M. in length, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the Rhine forms a series of waterfalls. Near the entrance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Averser Rhein descends from the Ferrera Valley and joins the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hinter-Rhein. Towards the end of the gorge, an old bridge crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rhine. Farther on, a rocky gateway (Sassa Plana), 16 yds. in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}length, is passed. The open Alpine landscape of the Rheinwald-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thal ( Val Rhein) is now disclosed; to the right lies Suvers (4673 ft.);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite rise the Pizzo Uccello(8dll ft.) and the Einshorn(9650ft.) ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left of the Spliigen, near the Uccello, is the Tambohorn{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(10,748 ft.); to the W. the Zapporthom (9803 ft.), etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}32i/2 M. Spliigen, Roman. Spluga (4757 ft.; Hotel Bodenhaus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Post), the capital of the Rheinwaldthal, is a busy place, owing to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its position at the junction of the Spliigen and Bernardino routes.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The latter (p. 40) here runs to the W. The Spliigen route turns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left, crosses the Rhine, and ascends in windings, passing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through a tunnel 93 yds. in length. Retrospect of the barren Kalk-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}berg rising above Spliigen. The road then enters a bleak valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and ascends on the W. side by numberless zigzags, passing a soli¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tary Refuge, to the summit of the Spliigen Pass (Colmo dell' Orso;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6946 ft.), lying between the precipitous Tambohorn, or Schnee-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hom (10,748 ft.) to the W., and the Surettahom (9925 ft.) to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the E. This narrow ridge forms the boundary between Switzer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}land and Italy. The pass, which was known to the Romans, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traversed down to 1818 by a bridle-path only. The road was con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed by the Austrian government in 1819-21. About 3/4M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyord the pass is the Dogana (6247 ft.), the Italian custom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house, at the head of a bleak valley surrounded by lofty mountains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The road now descends by numberless zigzags along the E.{% endraw %}

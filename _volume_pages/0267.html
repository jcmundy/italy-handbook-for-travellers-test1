{% raw %}lower hills are covered with vineyards and gardens, contrasting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautifully with the dark foliage of the chestnuts and walnuts in the{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The interior of the town with its arcades, workshops in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open air, and granite-paved streets, is also thoroughly Italian in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character. On market-day (Tuesday) a variety of picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italian costumes and characteristic scenes may be observed here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The once numerous monasteries of Lugano have been suppress¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed with the exception of two. The most important was that of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maria degli Angioli, now the Hotel du Parc. The adjacent church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains beautiful *Frescoes by Bern. Luini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The painting on the wall of the screen, one of the largest and finest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ever executed by Luini, represents the "Passion of Christ, and contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several hundred figures, arranged according to the antiquated style in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two rows. In the foreground, occupying the upper part of the wall,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stand three huge crosses, at the foot of which we perceive Roman war¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}riors, the groups of the holy women, and St. John, and the executioners{% endraw %}
{% raw %}casting lots for the garments. Above, on a diminished scale, from left to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right, are Christ on the Mount of Olives, Christ taken prisoner, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Scourging, the Bearing of the Cross, the Entombment, and the Ascension,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all immediately adjacent. Although the style of the composition strikes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one as old-fashioned, especially after seeing Leonardo's works, the eye{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannot fail to be gratified by the numerous beautiful details. The St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sebastian and St. Rochus, below, between the arches, are particularly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine. To the left, on the wall of the church, is the Last Supper, a picture{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in three sections, formerly in the Lyceum, and in the 1st Chapel on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right is a Madonna, both also by Luini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Lorenzo, the principal church, on an eminence (fine view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the terrace), probably erected by Tommaso Rodari at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the 15th cent., has a tastefully adorned marble facade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the Theatre are the old government-buildings (now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Hotel Washington), with a cool and pleasant colonnaded court.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The hall contains a monument to the architect Canonico di Tes-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}serete, and a marble bust of General Dufour.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}a bust of Washington, 'magnum saeculorum decus\ — The Villa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beausejour, charmingly situated near the Hotel du Parc, of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is now a dependance, has a beautiful and very extensive garden,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}containing fine cedars , magnolias, camellias, etc. — Superb view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the tower in the garden of the Villa Enderlin, to which access{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is permitted by the proprietor.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}122 Route 19. LA SPEZIA. From Genoa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cafes. '"Cafe del Corso, near the Giardino Pubblico; '"Elvetico, near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Teatro Civico.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chemist. Fossati, Via del Prione.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baths. Warm baths at. the two first-named hotels, and adjoining the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hotel Italia. — Sea-baths in summer on the beach to the N., 50 c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Post Office in the Corso Cavour (8-12 a. m. and 2-6 p. m.). — Telegraph{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Office: Piazza Vittorio Emmanuele, and Via Fossi 7 (open till midnight).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Boat with one rower, 1 fr. per hour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}English Church; service also in the Hotel Croce di Malta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}La Spezia, a town with 10,700 inhab., is charmingly situated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the N.W. angle of the Golfo della Spezia, between two rocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowned with forts, and possesses one of the largest, safest, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most convenient harbours in Europe, the Lunai Portus of the Ro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mans. Since 1861 La Spezia has been the chief war-harbour of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy, and extensive improvements are now being carried out. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Royal Dockyard on the S.W. side of the town, constructed by Gen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eral Chiodo, to whom a statue has been erected at the entrance,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is a large establishment, 150 acres in extent (admission on written{% endraw %}
{% raw %}application to the Comando Generale della Darsena). The marine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}artillery magazines in the bay of S. Vito cover an area of 100 acres.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Spezia is also a trading place of some importance. In summer the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea-baths, and in winter the mildness of the climate attract numer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ous visitors. The air is genial and humid, and the vegetation of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}southern character. The olive-oil of the environs is esteemed. Plea¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sant walks on the coast.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A delightful Excursion may be made to Porto Venere on the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side of the bay (two-horse carriage in H/2 hr., 10 fr.; boat in 2-2'/2 hrs.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}8-10 fr.; the former preferable), on the site of the ancient Portus Veneris.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A most charming prospect is enjoyed from the ruins of the church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro, rising above the sea, and supposed to occupy the site of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}old temple of Venus. Opposite lies the fortified island of Palmaria. Beau¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiful excursions may also be taken on the E. side of the bay, to S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Terenzo and Lerici, to which a screw-steamer runs thrice daily (lfr.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slarting from the Molo of the bay.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Four tunnels. — 6472 M. Areola, with a conspicuous campanile.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train passes through a long tunnel, and crosses the broad Magra,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which was in ancient times the boundary between Italy and Liguria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6772 M. Sarzana, Rom. Sergiana, or Luna Nova, from its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}having superseded the ancient Luna, with the picturesque forti¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fication of Sarzonella, constructed by Castruccio Castracani (p. 352),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a handsome Cathedral of white marble in the Italian Gothic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style, begun in 1355. Pop. 10,000. In 1467 the place fell into the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hands of the Florentines under Lorenzo de' Medici, from whom it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was again wrested by Charles VIII. of France ; it subsequently be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed to the Genoese, and then to the Sardinians. Sarzana was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the birthplace of Pope Nicholas V- (Tommaso Parentucelli, 1447-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}55), a great patron of learning, and the founder of the library of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Vatican.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The environs are very fertile. Among the mountains to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left the white rocks and gorges of the neighbouring marble-quarries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are visible. To the right a fine retrospect of the Bay of La Spezia.{% endraw %}

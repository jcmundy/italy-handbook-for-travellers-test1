{% raw %}History. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 371{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1864, when Florence supplanted Turin as the capital of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy, the enterprise of the citizens received a powerful stimulus,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as was shown, for instance, in the rapid extension of its precincts;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but it is well known that financial ruin was the price paid for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the short-lived honour. As early as the 15th cent. Florence con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained 90,000inhab., in 1859 about 112,000, and in 1882 163,200.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Florentines have ever been noted for the vigour of their reason¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing powers and for their pre-eminence in artistic talent; and even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the present day their superiority over the Genoese and the inhabi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tants of other towns of Lombardy is apparent from their manners{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and their dress.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}History. Florence does not lay claim to very great antiquity. It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably founded by the Romans in the first century B.C., under Sulla,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and, as ancient records and some scanty ruins indicate , must at an early{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period have attained to considerable prosperity, owing to its highly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}favourable situation. The town was devastated by the incursions of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}barbarian hordes during the dark ages, but revived about the beginning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the 11th century. In 1010 the Florentines conquered the ancient town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Fiesole, aided the Pisans in their contests with Lucca and Genoa, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}took an active part in the feud which broke out about this period be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the Guelphs and Ghibellines, the town generally supporting the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cause of the pope against the imperial party. The most powerful families{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the town, such as the Buondelmonti, were on the side of the Guelphs,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in opposition to whom the Uberti for a brief period held the supremacy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under Emperor Frederick II. As in consequence of these conflicts the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sway of the nobility proved detrimental to the interests of the city, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}people in 1250 organised a kind of national guard of their own, commanded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a 'Capitano del Popolo1. About the same time (1552) was first coined the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}golden Florin, which soon became a general standard of value, and marks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the leading position taken by Florence in the commerce of Europe. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seven greater Arts, or guilds, among which the Wool-weavers, Cloth-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dealers, Silk-workers, and Money-changers were the most important, soon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made their right to a share in the government unequivocal, and in 1282{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chief executive power was entrusted to their Priori, or presidents. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nobles were held in check by strict regulations , the execution of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was committed to the Gonfaloniere della Giustizia, who after 1300 became{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the president of the Signoria (or Priori). The party-struggles now again{% endraw %}
{% raw %}burst forth, under the new names of the Whites and the Blacks; the Guelphs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Neri) were eventually victorious, and many of the Bianchi, among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom was the poet Dante Alighieri, were banished. In the meantime{% endraw %}
{% raw %}various attempts had been made to secure peace and order by appointing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a foreign prince as lord of the city. Walter of Brienne, Duke of Athens,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last of these governors, abolished the constitution by force in 1342,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but in the following year he was expelled by the people. The Ciompi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or lower classes , were now bent upon securing a share in the govern¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment of the city, and a turbulent and lawless period ensued, during which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the power of the wealthy commercial family of the Medici, who espoused{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the popular side, gradually developed itself (see the Genealogy below).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}1423). His son Cosimo was overthrown by the Albizzi in 1433, but returned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after an exile of one year, and resumed the reins of government with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}almost princely magnificence. He employed his wealth liberally in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}advancement of art and science, he was the patron of Brunelleschi, Dona¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tello, Michelozzo, Masaccio, and Lippi, and he founded the Platonic Aca¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}demy and the Medici Library. Towards the close of his life he was not,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}undeservedly surnamed pater patriae by the Florentines. He was succeeded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by his son Pietro in 1464, ana in 1469 by his grandson Lorenzo, surnamed{% endraw %}

{% raw %}red marble in two stories, one above the other (entrance to the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the facade, then turn to the left again opposite .the side-entrance).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the N. of the choir is a corridor leading to S. Giovanni in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fonte, the ancient Baptistery, of the 12th cent.; the font is embel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lished with *Reliefs of about 1200. The adjacent Vescovado contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Biblioteca Capitolare with its precious MSS. ^palimpsests),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among which Niebuhr discovered the Institutiones of Gains.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In Veronetta, on the left bank of the Adige, to which the Ponte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Garibaldi leads (toll 2 c), is situated S. Giorgio in Braida (PI. F, 1;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if the principal gate is closed, entrance by side-door on the N.), recon¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structed in the 16th cent, under the superintendence of Sammicheli.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The well-proportioned interior contains some admirable pictures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the W. wall, over the door, Baptism of Christ, by Tintoretto;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1st altar on the left, St. Ursula and her companions, the Saviour above,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted in 1545 by Franc. Caroto; 4th altar on the left, "Madonna with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two saints, God the Father above, three angels with musical instruments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below, by Girolamo dai Lib'ri (1529); 5th altar on the left, St. Cecilia, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Moretto. To the right in the choir the Miracle of the Five Thousand, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paolo Farinato; to the left, the Shower of manna, by Fel. Brusasorci, both{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted in 1603. High altar-piece, ''Martyrdom of St. George, by P. Vero¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nese, a masterpiece of the highest rank: — 'Paolo treats the scene as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}much as possible as if it were one which actually happened, restrains the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pathos within the bounds of moderation, avoids any excess of realism,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Via S. Giorgio leads hence to the S.E. to the old cathedral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Sto. Stefano (PI. G, 1), probably erected in the 11th cent, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the site of a still earlier church. The interior has a flat roof and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a raised choir, with the episcopal stall at the back; to the left a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}figure of St. Peter (14th cent.).. The crypt contains the tombs of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old bishops.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nearly opposite this church is the Ponte della Pietra, built by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fra Giocondo (p. 200); the first two arches date from the Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period. At the bridge begins the ascent to the Castello S. Pietro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PI. G, 2; permission obtained at the commandant's office at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance), the ancient castle of Theodoric the Great (p. 200). It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was entirely remodelled by Galeazzo Visconti in 1393, destroyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the French in 1801, and refortified by the Austrians in 1849.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At its base, immediately below the bridge, are the remains of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}semicircular antique Theatre (PL G, 2), excavated in the court of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}private house , and interesting to antiquarians.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On a low eminence in front of the theatre stands the little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of SS. Siro e Libera, in which it is said the first mass was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}read in Verona. The tradition is probably owing to the ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting at the back of the altar.{% endraw %}

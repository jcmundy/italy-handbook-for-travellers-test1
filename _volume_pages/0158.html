{% raw %}74 Route 10. VERREX. From Turin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}39 M. Ivrea (768 ft.; Europa , in the Dora promenade ; Uni-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}verso, well spoken of), a town with 7600 inhab., is picturesquely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated on the Dora. Baltea (French Doire), on the slope of a hill{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowned by an extensive and well-preserved ancient Castle, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three lofty towers of brick, now a prison. Adjacent is the modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cathedral, the interior of which was restored in 1855. An ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sarcophagus adorns the adjoining Piazza. A monument was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here in 1880 to the general and minister Ettore Perrone, who fell{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Novara in 1848. Ivrea was the ancient Eporedia, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonised by the Romans, B.C. 100, in order to command the Alpine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}routes over the Great and Little St. Bernard. Pleasant walk to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Madonna del Monte (pilgrimage-church) and the lake of S. Giuseppe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a ruined monastery (1 hr.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ivrea may be regarded as one of the S. gateways to the Alps. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luxuriantly fertile valley, here IV2 M. in breadth , is flanked with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountains of considerable height. The Road skirts the Dora{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baltea the whole way to Aosta. On a height to the right stands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the well-preserved, pinnacled castle of Montalto (a waterfall near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it); several other ruins crown the hills farther on. The vines{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which clothe the slopes are carefully cultivated. The road leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the villages of Settimo-Vittone and Carema. At —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11 M. (from Ivrea) Pont St. Martin (Rosa Rossa) the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crosses the Lys torrent, which descends from Monte Rosa. The bold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and slender bridge which crosses the brook higher up is a Roman{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure. This and the ruined castle here are most picturesque{% endraw %}
{% raw %}features in the landscape. Several forges are situated on the bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Dora.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Donnaz the road ascends rapidly through a profound{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defile. On the left flows the river, on the right rises a precipitous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rock. The pass is terminated by the picturesque Tort Bard (1019{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ft.), which stands on a huge mass of rock in a most commanding{% endraw %}
{% raw %}position. The fort was taken in 1052 by Duke Amadeus of Savoy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after a long and determined siege, and in May, 1800, before the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}battle of Marengo, it was most gallantly defended by 400 Austrians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who kept the whole French army in check for a week.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The new road, hewn in the solid rock, no longer leads by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}village of Bard, but follows the course of the Dora, below the fort.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the left opens the Val di Camporciero, or Champorcher.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}19 M. Verrex (1279 ft.; Ecu de France, or Poste; *Couronne){% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies at the entrance of the (r.) Val de Challant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The valleys of Aosta and Susa (p. 26) were alternately occu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pied by the Franks and the Lombards, and belonged for a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable period to the Franconian Empire, in consequence of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the French language still predominates in these Italian districts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bard is the point of transition from Italian to French, while at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verrex the latter is spoken almost exclusively.{% endraw %}

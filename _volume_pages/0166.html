{% raw %}82 Route 14{% endraw %}
{% raw %}NOVI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Turin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Next stations Annone, Cerro, Felizzano, Solero. The country{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is flat and fertile. Before Alessandria is reached, the line to Arona{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 167) diverges to the N. The train now crosses the Tanaro by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge of 15 arches, skirts the fortifications, and reaches —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}56^2 M. Alessandria (Hotel de VTJnivers; Europa; Italia;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}^Railway Restaurant), a town with 29,000 inhab., situated on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tanaro in a marshy district, and only remarkable as a fortified{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place. It was founded in 1168 by the Lombard towns allied against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Enip. Frederick Barbarossa, and named after Pope Alexan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der III., with the addition of della paglia, i.e. of straw, perhaps{% endraw %}
{% raw %}because the first houses were thatched with straw. — Alessandria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being a junction of several lines, carriages are generally changed here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Railway to Vercelli by Valenza, p. 77; to Novara and Arona, pp. 79,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}80; to Milan by Mortara and Vigevano, see p. 80; to Pavia by Valenza,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}see p. 179; to Piacenza, Parma, Bologna, etc, see RR. 13 and 42; (o{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cavallermaggiore, see p. 83. — Tramway from Alessandria to Varallo and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Sale (via Marengo).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Alessandria to Savona (via Acqui), 65 M., in 472 hrs. (fares{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11 fr. 95, 8 fr. 40 c, 6 fr.). — As far as Cantalupo the line is the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as to Bra and Cavallermaggiore (see p. 83). — 21 M. Acqui (Moro;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italia), the Aquae Statielae of the Romans, an episcopal town on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bormida with 7000 inhab., is well known for its mineral waters, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resemble those of Aix-la-Chapelle. The Cathedral, with its double aisles,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from the 12th century. Near Acqui the Austrians and Piedmontese{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were defeated by the French in 1794. Good wine is produced in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vicinity. — The line ascends the valley of the Bormida, passing through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ten tunnels. Stations Terzo, Bistagno, Ponti, Montechiaro, Spigno (with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silk and wool factories), Merana, Piana, Dego, Rocchetta, and Cairo. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}52 M. S. Giuseppe di Cairo, see p. 84. — 65 M. Savona, see p. 102.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The line crosses the Bormida, which a short distance below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alessandria falls into the Tanaro. About IV4 M. to the E. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge, in the broad plain between the Bormida and the Scrivia, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated the small village of Marengo, near which, on 14th June,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1800, was fought a battle which influenced the destinies of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole of Europe. The French were commanded by Napoleon, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Austrians by Melas. The battle lasted 12hrs., and the French lost{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Desaix, one of their best generals. — 63 M. Frugarolo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}70 M. Novi (*La Sirena), situated on the hills to the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commanded by a lofty square tower, was the scene of the victory{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gained by the Austrians and Russians under Suvbrov over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French on 15th Aug., 1799. Branch-line to Pavia and Milan via,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tortona and Voghera, see p. 80, and R. 28; to Piacenza, see R. 13.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— At (75 M.) Serravalle the train enters a mountainous district.—{% endraw %}
{% raw %}79 M. Arquata, with a ruined castle on the height. Between this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point and Genoa there are eleven tunnels. The train threads its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way through profound rocky ravines (la Bocchetta), traversing lofty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embankments, and several times crossing the mountain-brook (Scri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}via). The scenery is imposing and beautiful. — 83 M. Isola del{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cantone; on the height to the right the ruins of an old castle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}89^2 M. Busalla (1192 ft.), the culminating point of the line,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the watershed between the Adriatic and the Tyrrhenian Sea.{% endraw %}

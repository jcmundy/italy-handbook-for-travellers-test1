{% raw %}History. For the early history of Venice, see p. 198. The foundation{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the greatness of Venice as an eastern power was laid by the Doge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Enrico Dandolo (1192-1205), who conquered Constantinople in 1204. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consequence of this the Byzantine Empire was divided, and Venice ob¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tained possession of numerous places on the coasts of the Adriatic and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Levant, from Durazzo to Trebisond, and also of most of the islands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Greek Archipelago, including Candia. During the process of con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quering and ruling these new territories there gradually arose a class of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aristocrats or nobles (Nobili), who declared themselves hereditary in 1297{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and shut out the rest of the people from all share in the government.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The supreme authority lay with the Great Council (Consiglio Maggiore),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which consisted of all members of the noble families above the age of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twenty. The executive was entrusted to a Doge or Duke, and six coun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sellors, with whom was also associated the Council of the Pregadi. At a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later period the Pregadi were combined with the higher officials to form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Senate. The Avvogadori di Comune watched that the powers of office{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were used in a constitutional manner. After the conspiracy of 1310 the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highest authority became vested in a secret Council of Ten (Consiglio dei{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Died), who kept the whole administration of the city and also the man¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}agement of its foreign policy entirely under their control. From this{% endraw %}
{% raw %}council the Inquisition was developed in the 16th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the successors of Enrico Dandolo the republic underwent severe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contests with Genoa, which occasioned the loss of many of the Venetian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conquests in the East, but at length terminated in the total defeat of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Genoa in 1352, by the Doge Andrea Dandolo. His successor Marino Falieri{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contemplated the overthrow of the aristocratic form of government, but his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scheme was discovered, and he was beheaded on 17th April, 1355. During{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the reign of Andrea Contarini (1367-82) Padua, Verona, Genoa, Hungary,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Naples formed an alliance against Venice. In 1379 the Genoese took{% endraw %}
{% raw %}possession of Chioggia, but were surrounded in the Lagune and compelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to surrender, 24th June, 1380. Peace was concluded in 1381. In 1386{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Antonio Venier (1382-1400) took possession of the island of Corfu, then of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Durazzo, Argos, etc. Under Michele Steno (1400-14) the Venetian general{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Malatesta conquered Vicenza, Belluno, Feltre, Verona, and Padua (1405);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1408 the republic gained possession of Lepanto and Patras, and in 1409{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Guastalla, Casalmaggiore, and Brescello. In 1421 Tommaso Mocenigo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}waged war successfully against Hungary. In 1416 the Venetian fleet under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Loredan defeated the Turkish at Gallipoli, and in 1421 subjugated all{% endraw %}

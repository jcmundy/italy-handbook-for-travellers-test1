{% raw %}Lake of Lecco.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Como to Lecco by.Bellagio steamboat twice daily; from Colico{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Lecco, once daily, see p. 151.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The S.E. arm of the Lake of Como is worthy of a visit, although{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inferior in attraction to the other parts. Lecco is charmingly situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The precipitous and formerly almost inaccessible E. bank of the lake is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traversed by a road constructed in 1832 and carried along the rocks at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}places with the aid of embankments, tunnels, and galleries. Three of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter near Olcio are together 1000 yds. in length. It affords admirable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}views of the lake.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The steamboat rounds the Punta di Bellagio; on the height{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above is situated the garden of the Villa Serbelloni, and adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it are the Villa Giulia (p. 156) and the village of Visignola. Then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Limonta, and opposite to it (left) Lierna and Sornico, (right) Vassena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Onno, (left) Olcio, and Mandello on a flat promontory. On the op¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posite bank (right) lies the small town of Pare, separated from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Malgrate by the promontory of S. Dionigio. Malgrate itself lies at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance of the Val Madrera, through which a road to Como{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads by Erba (p. 150). The lake gradually contracts into the river{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adda, by which it is drained, and is crossed by the Ponte Grande,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a stone bridge often arches, constructed in 1335 by Azzone Visconti,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and furnished with fortified towers at the extremities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lecco (*Albergo d'Italia; *Croce di Malta, both in the Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style; Due Torri, well spoken of; Leon d'Oro; Corona; omn. be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the station and the pier 50 c.), an industrial town with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7500 inhab. and silk, cotton, and iron manufactories, at the S. end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the E. arm of the Lake of Como, is admirably described in Man-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}zoni's 'I Promessi Sposi'. Pleasant walks to the hill of Castello and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the pilgrimage-church on the Monte Baro.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little below Lecco the Adda again expands into the Lago di Garlate,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and further down, into the small Lago di Olginate. A navigable canal con¬{% endraw %}

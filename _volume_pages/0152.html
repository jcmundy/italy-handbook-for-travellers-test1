{% raw %}The spacious Piazza S. Carlo (PL E, 3; 587 ft. long, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}264 ft. wide), which adjoins the Academy, is embellished with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}equestrian *Statue of Emmanuel Philibert (PL 28), Duke of Savoy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1580), surnamed lTete de Fer\ in bronze, designed by Maroc-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chetti (1838), and placed on a pedestal of granite, with reliefs at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sides. On the W. side the Battle of St. Quentin, gained by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duke under Philip II. of Spain against the French in 1557; on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E. side the Peace of Cateau-Cambre'sis (1558), by which the duchy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was restored to the House of Savoy. The duke as 'pacem redditurus'1{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is in the act of sheathing his sword (his armour preserved at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}armoury is placed in the same attitude).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Castello (p. 63), and (S.) to the Piazza Carlo Felice (p. 70){% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the railway-station. — To the left in the Via dell' Ospedale is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Exchange (PL 6; F, 3), and adjoining it, a Museo Industriale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italiano (PL 34; F, 3), with a technological collection. Farther on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the large Ospedale S. Giovanni Battista (PL 35; F, 3). — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cross-street leads in a N. direction to the Piazza Carlo Emmanuele{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II. (PL F, 3), with a handsome ^Monument to Cavour (PL 26), by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dupre of Florence , erected in 1873 : grateful Italy presenting the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}civic crown to Cavour, who holds a scroll in his left hand with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}famous words 'libera chiesa in libero stato'; the pedestal is adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with allegorical figures of Justice, Duty, Policy, and Independence;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the reliefs represent the return of the Sardinian troops from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crimea, and the Paris Congress. For this fine work the sculptor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was paid upwards of 30,000 I. — In the Via Cavour , at the corner{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Via Lagrange, is the house (PL 7) in which Count Cavour{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was born in 1810 (d. 1861), with a memorial tablet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjoining the Palazzo Reale (p. 63) on the W. side rises the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cathedral of S. Giovanni Battista (PL 10; E, 2), erected on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}site of three ancient churches in 1492-98 by Meo del Caprino (of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence, from Baccio Pintelli's design?) in the Renaissance style,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a marble facade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior consists of a nave and aisles, a transept, and an octa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gonal dome in the centre. Over the W. Portal is a copy of Leonardo da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vinci's Last Supper (p. 140). Over the second altar on the right are 18{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small pictures, blackened with age, by Deferrari (not Alb. Diirer). Fres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coes on the ceiling modern. The seats of the royal family are on the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the high-altar.{% endraw %}

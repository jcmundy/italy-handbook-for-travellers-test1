{% raw %}26 Route 2.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}SUSA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The tunnel is 26 ft. wide, 19 ft. high, and almost entirely lined with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}masonry. It is lighted by lanterns placed at intervals of 500 metres, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the distances are given in kilometres. The carriages are lighted with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gas. The air in the tunnel, although somewhat close, is not unpleasant.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The transit occupies 30 minutes. Travellers are warned not to protrude{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their heads or arms from the carriage-windows during the transit, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are also recommended to keep the windows shut.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The now deserted Mont Cenis Road, which continues to ascend the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley of the Arc, was constructed by Fabbroni in 1802-5, during the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reign of Napoleon I. The culminating point of the Mont Cenis (6950 ft.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}lies 17 M. to the E. of the tunnel, which was therefore hardly appro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}priately called after the mountain. The road then descends to Susa (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below), about 40 M. from Modane.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the S. end of the tunnel is (443 M.) stat. Bardonecchia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(4127 ft.). Two tunnels. Stat. Beaulard. Near stat. Oulx(3497ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Roman Villa Martis, the line enters the valley of the Dora{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riparia. (A road to the S.W. leads hence to Cesanne at the con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fluence of the Dora and Ripa, and over the Mont Genevre to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French fortress of Briancon on the Durance; comp. p. 73.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}The train traverses the picturesque valley of the Dora. Be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}yond a bridge and two tunnels, we reach stat. Salbertrand (3302 ft.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The river is again crossed. Before the next station (Chiomonte),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nine tunnels are traversed. To the left, between the second and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}third, a glimpse is obtained of the small town of Exilles with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frontier fortress of that name ; farther on, a fine waterfall. — 453 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Chiomonte, or Chaumont (2526 ft.). Then a number of tunnels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and aqueducts. The valley contracts and forms a wild gorge (Le{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gorgie) , of which beautiful views are obtained, with the Mont Ce¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nis road winding up the hill on the farther side , and the Roche-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}melon, Roche-Michel, etc., towering above it. When the valley ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pands , Susa with the arch of Augustus comes in sight on the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see below). — 45672 M. Meana (1949 ft.), 1 M. from Susa, lies{% endraw %}
{% raw %}324 ft. higher than the latter. Three tunnels. The train then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descends through beautiful chestnut woods , and crosses the Dora.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}462 M. Bussoleno.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}H* A short branch-line (5 M. in 1/2 hr.; fares, 95, 65, 50 c.) runs hence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Susa (1625 ft. ; Hotel de France; Soleil), a small and ancient town,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Roman Segusio, situated on the right bank of the Dora. A garden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the W. side of the town contains a Triumphal Arch, 44 ft. in height,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}39 ft. in width, and 23 ft. in depth, with projecting Corinthian columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at the corners and sacrificial scenes on the frieze, erected according to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inscription in A.D. 8. There are also a few other Roman relics. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church of S. Giusto dates from the 11th century. On the opposite bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Dora rises the fort La Brunette, which was destroyed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}French in 1798.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Next stations Borgone (where the Dora is crossed), S. Anto-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nino , Condove , and S. Ambrogio, high above which , on a rocky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eminence to the right, rises the abbey of S. Michele della Chiusa, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}La Sagra, remarkable for a peculiar property of its tombs which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}convert dead bodies into natural mummies. At stat. Avigliana the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valley expands into a broad plain. Stations Rosta, Alpignano,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Collegno.{% endraw %}

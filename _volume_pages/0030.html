{% raw %}XXVI{% endraw %}
{% raw %}HEALTH.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Cimiez. Many invalids make the mistake of leaving the Riviera{% endraw %}
{% raw %}too soon, and thus lose all the progress they have made during{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the winter, through reaching home in the unfavourable transition{% endraw %}
{% raw %}period between winter and spring. It is better to spend April and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}May at some intermediate station, such as Pallanza or Lugano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Good opportunities for sea-bathing are offered at many points{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the Mediterranean coast of N. Italy, such as Cannes, Nice,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alassio, Savona, Pegli, Spezia, Viareggio, and Venice. The Me¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diterranean is almost tideless; it contains about 41 per cent of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}common salt, a considerably higher proportion than the Atlantic;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its average temperature during the bathing-season is 71 ° Fahr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The bathing-season on the Ligurian coast begins in April, or at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latest in May, and lasts till November, being thus much longer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the season at any English seaside-resort.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Most travellers must in some degree alter their mode of living{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whilst in Italy, without however implicitly adopting the Italian{% endraw %}
{% raw %}style. Inhabitants of more northern countries generally become{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unusually susceptible to cold in Italy, and therefore should not omit{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to be well supplied with warm clothing for the winter. Woollen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}underclothing is especially to be recommended. A plaid should be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carried to neutralise the often considerable difference of temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the sunshine and the shade. In visiting picture-galleries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or churches on warm days it is advisable to drive thither and walk{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back, as otherwise the visitor enters the cool building in a heated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}state and has afterwards no opportunity of regaining the desirable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temperature through exercise. Exposure to the summer-sun should{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be avoided as much as possible. According to a Roman proverb, dogs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and foreigners (Inglesi) alone walk in the sun, Christians in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shade. Umbrellas, or spectacles of coloured glass (grey, concave{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glasses to protect the whole eye are best), may be used with advantage.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Blue veils are recommended to ladies. Repose during the hottest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hours is advisable, and a moderate siesta is often refreshing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great care should also be taken in the selection of an apartment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carpets and stoves are indispensable in winter. A southern aspect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in winter is an absolute essential for delicate persons, and highly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}desirable for the robust. The visitor should see that all the doors{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and windows close satisfactorily. Windows should be closed at night.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Health. English and German medical men are to be met with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the larger cities, and as already mentioned in most of the winter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing-stations of the Riviera. The Italian therapeutic art does not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enjoy a very high reputation in the rest of Europe. English and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}German chemists, where available, are recommended in preference{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Italian, whose drugs are at once dearer and of poorer quality.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Foreigners frequently suffer from diarrhoea in Italy, which is gener¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ally occasioned by the unwonted heat. The homoeopathic tincture{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of camphor may be mentioned as a remedy, but regulated diet and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thorough repose are the chief desiderata.{% endraw %}

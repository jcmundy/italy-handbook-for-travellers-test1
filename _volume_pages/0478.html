{% raw %}When at length the free constitutions of the greater part of Italy were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}superseded by principalities, Florence did not escape the general fate,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but the change took place in the most favourable manner possible.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among all the Italian dynasties by far the first in rank was that of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Medici, not only owing to their munificent patronage of art and science,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but to their prudent administration, their endeavours A)0 improve the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lower classes, and their care for agriculture, commerce, and the material{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interests of their subjects. At a later period their example was followed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the princes of Lorraine, and down to the present time Tuscany has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enjoyed the enviable lot of being the most enlightened and civilised, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the best-governed state in Italy. The fact that Tuscany unreservedly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}participated in the national aspirations for unity and freedom, and.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}voluntarily recognised the hegemony of a comparatively distant and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unsympathetic section of the Italian race, affords the strongest possible{% endraw %}
{% raw %}evidence of the earnestness of that remarkable revolution which led to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the unity of Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1530, with the aid of the arms of Emperor Charles V., the dynasty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Medici was firmly established in the sovereignty of Florence. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wise Duke Cosimo I. (1537-64) extended his dominions considerably, par¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticularly by the acquisition of Siena in 1557, which was ceded to him by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the emperor. He abdicated in favour of his son Francesco (1564-87), who,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}instead of the coveted title of King obtained that of Grand Duke (gran-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duca) of Florence, in 1569. Francesco was succeeded by his brother{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ferdinand I. (1587-1609), who had previously been a cardinal; Cosimo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II. (1609-21), the son of the latter, Ferdinand II. (1621-70), and Co¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}simo III. (1676-1723) were the next princes. With Giovanni Gaston, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}died in 1737, the house of Medici became extinct. In the wars between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Austria and Spain, the two great powers to which Italy was subject,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscany formed one of the principal objects of contention, but eventually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fell to the share of the former. The emperor annexed the country as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vacant fief, and conferred it on the husband of his daughter Maria Theresa,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Duke Francis Stephen of Lorraine (1737-65), who by the Peace of Vienna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1735) renounced his native principality of Lorraine in return. In 1745 he{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascended the throne of Austria as Francis I., and in 1763 established{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tuscany as an appanage of the second sons of the emperors, in order{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to prevent its being governed in future as one of the immediate domi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nions of Austria. He was succeeded in 1765 by the Grand Duke Leopold,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}who reigned on the same enlightened principles as his brother Joseph II.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and was an active reformer in the administrative, judicial, educational,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and ecclesiastical departments. In consequence of the death of Joseph II.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1790, Leopold was summoned to the throne of Austria, and his de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parture proved a severe loss to the duchy. His son the Grand Duke Fer¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dinand III. was obliged to renounce Tuscany by the Peace of Luneville{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1801), for which he received by way of compensation the Archbishopric{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Salzburg, and afterwards Wurzburg. L'nder the name of Republic,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and afterwards Kingdom of Etruria, the country continued to enjoy osten¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sible independence down to 1807, when it was incorporated with France.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1814 Ferdinand II. was reinstated, and in 1824 he was succeeded by his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}son Leopold II., who was first banished by the revolution of 1849, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally by that of 1859. By the plebiscite of 15th March 1860, Tuscany{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was united to the Kingdom of Italy, then in course of formation.{% endraw %}

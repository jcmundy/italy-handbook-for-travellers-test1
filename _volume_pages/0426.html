{% raw %}University. PARMA. 43. Route. 295{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The (formerly) ducal Tipografia, founded by Bodoni in 1766, is celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for its admirable printing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The custodians of the picture-gallery also keep the keys (fee{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 c.) of the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Convento di S. Paolo (PL 13; F,2), formerly a Benedictine nun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nery, now a school, an insignificant building, containing charming{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Frescoes by Correggio in the Camera di S. Paolo, which was thus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}decorated by order of the abbess Giovanna da Piacenza in 1519 (the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best preserved works of the master) : over the chimney-piece Diana,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the ceiling Cupids and emblems of the chase (the celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Putti del Correggio'), on the frieze the Graces, Fortuna, Adonis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc. The most favourable light is in sunny weather, 10-12 a.m.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'This, his first work of a monumental character, shows the painter{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as already possessed of his full powers. The execution is in the highest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}degree painstaking and minute, his handling even in fresco is delicate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and at the same time broad, soft, and warm. As in the later paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the domes of the Cathedral and S. Giovanni Evangelista, the decorative{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arrangement seems to transcend the limits of the building, and force its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}way through the ceiling, forming an arbour through which the light of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}heaven appears to find free ingress1. — Meyer's Correggio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The adjacent room is adorned with exquisite, slightly-figured{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arabesques on a dark blue ground by Al. Araldi (d. 1528). — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monastery church (S. Lodovico) contains the monument of Count{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Neipperg (d. 1829), the husband of the empress Marie Louise of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}France, afterwards Duchess of Parma, by Bartolini of Florence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. of the Piazza Grande rises the University (PL 29;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, 4), possessing faculties of jurisprudence, medicine, and mathe¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}matics and natural science. The students number about 200. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palseontological and ornithological departments of the natural history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}museum are worthy of inspection. Director, Prof. P. Strobel.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Quitting the museum and crossing the small river Parma by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ponte Verde, we reach the (formerly) Ducal Garden (closed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at 7 p.m.), at the N. end of which is the Palazzo del Giardino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 19; C, 1, 2), erected by Ottavio Farnese, and adorned with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous frescoes. One of the apartments contains the Rape of Eu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ropa, the Triumph of Venus, the Marriage of Peleus and Thetis,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}etc., by Agostino Carracci. (The palace is now a military school and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}often inaccessible.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}The garden adjoins the Rampari, a promenade encircling the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town, and laid out on the site of the former fortifications. To the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S., between the Orto Botanico and the castle, lies Lo Stradone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL F, G, H, 6), another public walk.{% endraw %}
{% raw %},44. Modena.{% endraw %}

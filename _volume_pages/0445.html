{% raw %}The town was founded by the Etruscans, and named Felsina, but was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards conquered by the Gallic Boii, and by them called Bononia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Punic War it espoused the cause of Hannibal, after which, B.C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}190, it was converted into a Roman colony, at the same time as Cremona{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Placentia, by the consul C. Laelius, and as such was a place of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}very great importance. Under the Empire it was even occasionally the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}residence of the monarchs themselves. It afterwards belonged to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greek Exarchate, and then to the Lombards and Franks. Charlemagne{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constituted Bologna a free town (whence its motto '■Libertas''), and its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commerce and prosperity rapidly increased. In 1119 the University , one{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the oldest in the world, was founded, and as a School of Jurisprudence,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where Irnerius and other celebrated jurists taught, soon attained a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}European reputation, and was visited by many thousand students annually.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1262 the number is said to have attained to nearly 10,000; at the present{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day there are 400 only. Irnerius introduced the study of the Roman Law,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while his successors the Glossators devoted their energies to its inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretation. The study of medicine and philosophy was introduced at a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}later period, and a theological faculty established by Pope Innocent VI.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The anatomy of the human frame was first taught here in the 14th cent.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and galvanism was discovered here by Jos. Galvani in 1789. It is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkable fact that the university of Bologna has numbered members of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fair sex among its professors. Thus, in the 14th cent., Novella d'Andrea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a lady of great personal attractions, who is said to have been concealed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by a curtain during her lectures; at a subsequent period Laura Bassi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(mathematics and physical science), Mme. Manzolina (anatomy), and more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recently (1794-1817) Clotilda Tambroni (Greek).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bologna acted a very prominent part in the contests of the Guelphs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Ghibellines, espoused the cause of the former, and allied itself with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Pope against Emp. Frederick II. In a sanguinary encounter at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fossalta, in May, 1249, King Enzio, son of the Emperor, was captured by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Bolognese, and kept in confinement by them for the rest of his life{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(22 years, p. 310). He was the founder of the family of the Bentivogli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards so powerful, who after protracted feuds entered into an alliance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the papal throne. During several centuries the town was the scene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the party-struggles of the Bentivogli, Visconti, and other families, until{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1512 Pope Julius II. incorporated it with the States of the Church.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1515 the interview of Pope Leo X. with Francis I. of France took{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place at Bologna, and in 1529, 1530, and 1532 those of Clement VII. with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Emp. Charles V. Here, too, the Council of Trent held a meeting in 1547.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1796 Bologna was annexed to the 'Cisalpine Republic1 by Napoleon; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1815 it again became subject to the States of the Church; in 1831 and 1849{% endraw %}
{% raw %}revolutions broke out, and in 1859 the town finally united itself to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}kingdom of Italy.{% endraw %}

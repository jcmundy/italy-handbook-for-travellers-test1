{% raw %}276 Route. 40. VENICE. Murano.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marcantouio Grimani (d. 1565), by Vittorio; 2nd altar, Baptism of Christ{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Paolo Veronese; beautiful ceiling-paintings representing the history of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Esther, also by Paolo, aided by his brother Benedetto Caliari.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity is the Campo di Marte, or esplanade (PL B, 4),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a large grassy island surrounded with trees.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the S.E. extremity of Venice (Punta della Motto) are the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giardini Pubblici (PL I, 5), laid out by Napoleon in 1807, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}space having been obtained by the demolition of several monas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teries. They are about 300 yds. in length and 100 yds. in width,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and are planted with six rows of acacias and sycamores. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. end is a small shrubbery, with a cafe. The grounds, which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}generally almost deserted, afford fine views of the city and Lagune.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On Sundays and Mondays they are much frequented, chiefly by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}women of the lower classes (gondola thither from the Piazzetta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50c). They are approached by the Via Garibaldi (formerly Eu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}genia), constructed in 1810 by Eugene Beauharnais, viceroy of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy, by bridging over a canal.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Pietro di Castello (PL 27; I, 4), a church with a dome, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island to the N. of the Giardini Pubblici, begun by Smeraldi in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1596, is said to have been designed by Palladio in 1557. Down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to 1807 it was the cathedral of the Patriarch of Venice, when St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mark's was raised to that dignity by Napoleon I., and the adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palace converted into a barrack. Handsome campanile (1474).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior contains few objects of interest. In the chapel of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left transept are two high-reliefs in marble, executed by Mich. Ongaro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 17th cent., representing the consecration by Pope Paul V. of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Patriarch Vendramin as cardinal, and an allegory of death. To the right,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond the second altar, is a marble throne from Antioch, said to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that of St. Peter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nothing will convey to the traveller a better idea of the situa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of Venice with its islands. than a visit to the Lido, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there are good sea-baths and a restaurant (see p. 231). A small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamer performs the trip in 12 min., starting hourly from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ponte della Pagiia. A gondola takes 1/2 hr. (comp. p. 230). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}excursion may be pleasantly prolonged by a detour by the island{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of S. Elena, with its old monastery and fine garden. The N. end{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Lido is defended by the Forte S. Niccolo and (to the W.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beyond a small arm of the sea) the Forte Andrea di Lido, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sammicheli as architect of the republic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interesting excursion to Murano, on an island about l1/-^ M. to the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Venice (omnibus-boat hourly, starting near SS. Apostoli; PL 5, F 2).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the church of S. Michele, built by Moro Lombardo in 1466, with sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tures on its facade, and the pretty Cappella Emiliana, erected by Gugl.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bergamasco in 1530. — Murano, with 3900 inhab., originally an indepen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dent town, but afterwards dependent on Venice, possesses interesting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}treasures of art in its churches, dating from its most prosperous period.{% endraw %}

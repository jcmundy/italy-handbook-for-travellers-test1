{% raw %}PESCHIERA. 31. Route. 185{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Lecco to Brescia via Bergamo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}51 M. Railway in 3-372 hrs.; fares 9fr. 30, 6fr. 50, 4fr. 65c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lecco, see p. 159. — 4 M. Calolzio, see p. 149; 9 M. Cisano;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14 M. Mapello; 16 M. Ponte S. Pietro, with a tasteful church and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an old castle. The train now crosses the Brembo, which issues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the Valle Brembana. — 20^2 M. Bergamo (p. 183). — Near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(25 M.) Seriate, the Serio is crossed. 28 M. Gorlago; 31^2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grumello, beyond which the Oglio, descending from the Lago d'Iseo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is crossed. — 34 M. Palazzolo, where a branch-line diverges to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paratico (p. 195). Picturesque glimpse of the village in the val¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ley to the left with its slender towers. — 39 M. Coccaglio, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monastery of Mont' Orfano on a height; 40 M. Rovato; 44 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ospitaletto. — 51 M. Brescia, see p. 189.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}31. From Milan to Verona.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}94 M. Railway in 472-572 hrs.; fares 17fr. 10, 12fr. 5, 8fr. 60c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Milan to (20 M.) Treviglio, junction for the lines to Cre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mona (p. 180) and Bergamo (p. 183), see p. 182. — 23 M. Vida-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lengo. Beyond (25*^ M.) Morengo, the train crosses the Serio, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tributary of the Adda. 28 M. Romano; 32 M. Calcio. The Oglio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which issues from the Lago d'Iseo, is now crossed. 3672 M. Chiari,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an old and industrious town of 9500 inhab., with a library. 40 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rovato, junction of the Bergamo and Brescia line described above.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}44 M. Ospitaletto. — 51 M. Brescia, see p. 189.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The slopes near Brescia are sprinkled with villas. — 56!/2 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rezzato, beyond which the Chiese is crossed ; 62 M. Ponte S. Marco.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond (66 M.) Lonato a short tunnel and a long cutting.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A long viaduct now carries the line to (68 M.) Desenzano (p.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}186). The train affords an admirable survey of the Lago di Garda{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the peninsula of Sermione (p. 186).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In this district, extending from the banks of the lake to a point{% endraw %}
{% raw %}considerably beyond Guidizzolo (on the road from Brescia to Mantua), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fiercely-contested Battle of Solferino was fought on 24th June, 1859,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the united French and Italian armies and the Austrians. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defeat of the latter led shortly afterwards to the Peace of Villafranca{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 209). The village of Solferino (Inn, good red wine; guides) lies on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the heights to the S., about 5 M. from the railway; carriage from stat.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Desenzano, there and back, 15 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}771/2 M. Feschiera (station 3/4M. from the town, comp. p. 186),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 2600 inhab., lies at the S.E. end of the Lago di Garda, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the efflux of the Mincio, which the train crosses. On 30th May,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1848, the place was taken by the Piedmontese after a gallant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}defence by the Austrian General Rath (d. 1852).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}was gallantly defended by the 10th Austrian rifle battalion in{% endraw %}

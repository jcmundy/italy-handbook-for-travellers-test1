{% raw %}About 6 M. to the W., on the road from Mantua to Reggio, lies Gua-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stalla (Posta), a small town not far from the Po, with 11,300 inhab., which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the 16th cent, gave its name to a principality of the Gonzagas, Dukes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Mantua. These princes became extinct in 1746, and their territory fell to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Parma. In the market-place is the bronze Statue of Ferdinand I. Gonza¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ga (d. 1557 at Brussels), by Leone Leoni. — At about the same distance from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the station, to the E., on the old road from Verona to Bologna, is situat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed Mirandbla, once the capital of a duchy which belonged to the Pico{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family, a town with broad streets and picturesque, antiquated buildings.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was originally under the jurisdiction of the abbey of Nonantola and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Countess Matilda, and after many vicissitudes came into possession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Counts of Pico, who retained their supremacy for upwards of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three centuries. Count Giovanni Pico (1463-94) was remarkable for his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ability and learning. Alexander I. (1619) was the first of the family who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bore the title of Duke of Mirandola and Concordia. Francesco Maria, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}last duke , sold his dominions to Modena in 1710. The old Palace of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dukes, the Cathedral, and the church of Gesii should be visited.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Sights of Verona may be seen in one day: begin with the Arena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Piazza Bra, then cross the Adige to the Palazzo Pompei (on the way{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to which is S. Fermo Maggiore, p. 207), return by the Via Ponte Navi to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Piazza de' Signori, with the tombs of the Scaligers; see S. Anastasia,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Cathedral, and cross the Ponte di Ferro to S. Giorgio; drive along{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Corso, from the Porta Borsari to the Porta Stuppa and S. Zeno, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finally to the Giardino Giusti.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}and a garrison of 6000 men, situated at the base of the Alps, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rapid Adige, which is crossed by five bridges, is next to Venice the{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Austrians in 1814 Verona was strongly fortified, and along with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Peschiera, Mantua, and Legnago formed the famous 'Quadrilateral',{% endraw %}

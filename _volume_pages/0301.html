{% raw %}188 Route 32. RIVA. Lago di Garda.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Riva to Mori (p. 47; 107a M.), a station on the Trent and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Verona line, omnibus thrice daily in 2!/2 hrs. (fare 90 kr., coupe 1 fl.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two-horse carriage 772 fl., one-horse 4 fl. The road, which is recommended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to pedestrians in cool weather, leads through Torbole ("Bertolini), a har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bour on the N.E. bank of the lake, and then ascends to the left to Nago,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whence, before entering the fort, a magnificent '"Retrospect of the lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is obtained. The road next traverses a wild and stony mountain ridge,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skirts the picturesque little Lake of Loppio (666 ft.), and reaches the village{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Loppio. The village of Mori is 2 M. from the station.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Excursions. To the "Fall of the Ponale (1 hr.), best accomplished by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}boat (there and back 2 fl. and fee). The waterfall itself, which is formed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the Ponale shortly before it flows from the Val di Ledro into the lake, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}insignificant, but its surroundings are picturesque. We disembark at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}point where the disused bridle-path from the Ledro valley reaches the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lake, ascend a little, passing some ruined houses, and beyond the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bridge, just below the fall, reach the best point of view. — The walk to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the fall by the new *Road is also interesting. It leads at a considerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height along the rocky precipices of the W. bank , through a succession{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of tunnels and cuttings, to the Val di Ledro. At the point where it turns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right into the valley, a path descending to the left, then ascend¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing, and again descending, leads to the waterfall, and commands the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful views (shade in the afternoon).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Monte Brione (1184 ft,), a hill 1 hr. to the E. of Riva, affords{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a fine survey of the valley and almost the entire lake. The easiest ascent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is from the N. side. The small village of La Grotta, at the foot of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Brione, IV2 M. from Riva, is a favourite afternoon-resort.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A pleasant excursion may be made towards the N.W. to (I72M.) Var-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rone, where there is a wild and picturesque *Gorge with a fine waterfall,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lately made easily accessible (attendant 20 kr. for each person; ring at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mill). The excursion may be continued by Cologna to (2'A M.) Tenno,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the old castle of which a charming view is enjoyed. The road then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traverses richly cultivated uplands, at a considerable height, and leads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Varignano to (472 M.) Arco (p. 46).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Monte Baldo, a range 45 M. in length, which separates the Lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Garda from the valley of the Adige, is best ascended from Nago (see{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above). The Altissimo di Nago (6970 ft.), the summit towards the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the most beautiful point, is reached hence in 5-6 hrs. (guide). Exten¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sive panorama, comprising a great part of Upper Italy, the lake, the valley{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Adige, and the snow-mountains of the Adamello, Presanella, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ortler. — The ascent of the Monte Maggiore, or Telegrafo (7280 ft.), the cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tral point, from Torri or Garda (p. 189), via Caprino, in 7 hrs., is fatiguing.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Valle di Ledro affords another interesting excursion (carriage to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pieve and back 5 fl.; diligence daily at 3 p. m.). Beginning of the route{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same as to the Fall of the Ponale (see above). The road then turns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W. into a green valley, and leads by Biacesa, Molina, the pretty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lago di Ledro (2135 ft,), and Mezzolago on its N. bank, to (63/4 M. from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riva) Pieve di Ledro (Albergo alia Torre). — At Bezzecca, 3/t M. beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pieve, opens the Val Concei, with the villages of (74 hr.) Enguiso and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(74 hr.) Lenzumo (thence back to Riva direct, by the Mte. Tratta and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campi, in 372 hrs.). From Bezzecca the road leads by Tiarno, and through{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Val Ampola, to (9 M.) Storo (Cavallo Bianco) in the Val Bona, or Chiese,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in which, 3 M. higher, lies Condino (Torre), the capital of S. Giudicaria.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond Storo, and about I72 M. below the bridge over the Chiese, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road crosses the Caffaro near Lodrone (Austrian and Italian frontier), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reaches (I72 M.) the Lago d'Idro, 6 M. long, 74 M. broad, the W. bank{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which it skirts. Opposite (33/4 M.) Anfo, with the mountain-castle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rocca d'Anfo, lies the small village of Idro. At (3 M.) Lavenone, at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. end of the lake, begins the picturesque Val Sabbia, of which the capi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tal is (3 M.) Vestone (Tre Spade). At (3 M.) Barghe the road divides;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that to the E. leads by Sabbio, Vobarno, and Volciano to (12 M.) Said on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lago di Garda (p. 187); that to the W. to Preseglie and through the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Val Garza to (15 M.) Brescia (p. 189).{% endraw %}

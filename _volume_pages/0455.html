{% raw %}Then the Teatro Comunale (PL 60 ; F, 4). — On the right we next{% endraw %}
{% raw %}observe the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}University (PL G, 3, 4), established since 1803 in the old Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo Cellesi, with a court by Bart. Triachini. After that of Salerno,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it is the oldest in Italy, having been founded in 1119, and now pos¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sesses a staff of 50 professors and 400 students (comp. p. 308) and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a considerable number of scientific institutions (clinical hospital,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}anatomical theatre, natural history collections, open on Sundays,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}botanical garden, and observatory).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The extensive Library of 100,000 vols, is open daily, 10-2 o'clock, ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cept Sundays. The oldest of the MSS. is that of Lactantius; also letters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Voltaire to Fred, the Great, miniatures, etc. The celebrated linguist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giuseppe Mezzofanti (born at Bologna 1776, died at Naples in 1849), professor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Oriental languages at the university, was once librarian here. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}age of 36 he is said to have spoken 18 languages fluently, and at the time{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his death no fewer than 42.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Geological Museum was established in 1871 in an adjoining{% endraw %}
{% raw %}building, Via Luigi Zamboni 2530, under the superintendence of Prof.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Capellini, who has enriched it with interesting fossils from the neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bourhood of Bologna, minerals from different parts of Europe and America,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a collection of prehistoric anthropological curiosities. — The Tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands a good survey of the town.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We next proceed to the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}* Accademia delle Belle Arti (PL G, 3), established in the old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}College of the Jesuits. It contains on the Ground-Floor collec¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tions of casts and modern works of art; on the First Floor (1.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}a collection of weapons (Oploteca) , comprising arms captured from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Turks, Venetians, etc., and (r.) a valuable ^Picture Gallery, or{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pinacoteca, consisting chiefly of works of the Bolognese School ar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ranged in eight saloons and rooms (open daily from 9-3 or 4, ac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cording to the season ; admission 1 fr.; on Sundays gratis; catalogue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}60 c). Each picture bears the name of the painter.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The visitor imbued with the modern taste for the historical{% endraw %}
{% raw %}study of art, will find little attraction in the works of the Seven¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}teenth Century, which form the chief boast of the gallery. Al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}though it would be unfair to depreciate the undoubted talent and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}skill of these late masters, their works are unsatisfactory owing to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the absence of any definite aim or indication of progress, and from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the obvious pains which have been taken to reproduce trite themes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in an interesting manner. In the department of fresco painting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the works of these Bolognese eclectics (see p. 309) are most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous at Rome, but they are admirably represented here by a{% endraw %}

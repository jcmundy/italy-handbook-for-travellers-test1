{% raw %}The scenery around the Borromean Islands rivals that of the Lake of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Como in grandeur, and perhaps surpasses it in softness of character. Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rosa is not visible; the snow-mountains to the N.W. are the glaciers and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peaks of the Simplon; of the nearer mountains the most conspicuous are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the white granite-rocks near Baveno (p. 29). The traveller coming from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. cannot fail to be struck with the loveliness of these banks, studded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with innumerable habitations, and clothed with southern vegetation (chest¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nuts, mulberries, vines, figs, olives); the extensive lake with its deep blue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}waters and beautiful girdle of snowy mountains combining the stern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grandeur of the High Alps with the charms of a southern clime. Rousseau{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at one time intended to make the Borromean Islands the scene of his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Nouvelle Heloise', but considered them too artificial for his romance, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which human nature is pourtrayed with such a masterly hand.{% endraw %}

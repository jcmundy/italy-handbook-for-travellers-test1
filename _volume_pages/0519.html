{% raw %}Florence, formerly the capital of the Grand Duchy of Tuscany,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1865-70 that of the Kingdom of Italy, and now that of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}province of its own name, the seat of an archbishop, and the head¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quarters of the VI. Corps d'Armee, ranks with Rome, Naples, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venice as one of the most attractive towns in Italy. While in ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}times Rome was the grand centre of Italian development, Florence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has since the middle ages superseded it as the focus of intellectual{% endraw %}
{% raw %}life. The modern Italian language and literature have emanated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chiefly from Florence, and the fine arts also attained the zenith of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}their glory here. An amazing profusion of treasures of art, such as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}no other locality possesses within so narrow limits, reminiscences{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a history which has influenced the whole of Europe, perpetuated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by numerous and imposing monuments, and lastly the delightful{% endraw %}
{% raw %}environs of the city combine to render Florence one of the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interesting and attractive places in the world.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Who can describe the enchanting view of this art-city of Tuscany{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the world, Florence, with its surrounding gardens? who paint the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distant horizon, from Fiesole smiling at us with its fair towers, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}blue ridge of the Lucchese Mountains standing out against the golden back¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ground of the western sky? Here everything betrays the work of gen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eration after generation of ingenious men. Like a water-lily rising on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mirror of the lake, so rests on this lovely ground the still more lovely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence, with its everlasting works, and its inexhaustible riches. From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the bold airy tower of the palace, rising like a slender mast, to Brunel-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leschi's wondrous dome of the Cathedral, from the old house of the Spini{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the Pitti Palace, the most imposing the world has ever seen, from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garden of the Franciscan convent to the beautiful environs of the Cascine,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all are full of incomparable grace. Each street of Florence contains a world{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of art; the walls of the city are the calyx containing the fairest flowers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the human mind; — and this is but the richest gem in the diadem{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with which the Italian people have adorned the earth.1 (Leo).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florence, Italian Firenze, formerly Fiorenza, from the Latin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Florentia, justly entitled 'la bella', is situated in 43°46' N. lati¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tude, and 11° 15' E. longitude, on both banks of the Arno, an in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}significant river except in rainy weather, in a charming valley of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moderate width, picturesquely enclosed by the spurs of the Apen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nines, the highest of which {Monte Morello, about 3000 ft.) rises to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N. On the S. the heights rise more immediately from the river,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the N. they are 3-4 M. distant, while towards the N.W., in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}direction of Prato and Pistoja, the valley expands considerably. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sudden transitions of temperature which frequently occur here are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trying to person in delicate health. Thepleasantest months are April,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}May, and the first half of June, September, October, and November.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}prevail, while in July and August the heat is very oppressive. On{% endraw %}

{% raw %}On the S. side of the piazza is the Municipio (PL 18); at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entrance is a marble bust of General Cialdini, who was born here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Proceeding to the right past the Municipio, and following a broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}street to the right, we next reach the church of the *Madonna della{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghiara (PL 6; A, 3), built in 1597 from a design by Balbi, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}form of a Greek cross covered with a dome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is adorned with frescoes in the nave as far as the dome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and in the N. aisle by Luca Ferrari (1605-54) of Reggio, a pupil of Guido{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reni. The altar in the latter, presented by the town in 1621, has an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar-piece by Guercino. The frescoes in the choir are by Tiarini of Bo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}logna, of the school of the Carracci; the Annunciation at the back of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high altar is by Carlo Caliari (brother of Paolo Veronese), and the fres¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coes in the S. transept are by Lionello Spada and others.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Passing through the arches to the right of the cathedral, we{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reach the Piazza Minore, with the church of S. Prospero (PL 14;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}C, 3, 4), re-erected in 1504 by Gasparo Bisi on the site of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}earlier Lombard edifice, to which the six marble lions of the facade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}originally belonged. The choir contains damaged frescoes by Campi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Procaccini, and pictures by Tiarini.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Madonna della Concezione (PL 7; C, 2) is a handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern church near the theatre. — The Theatre (PL C, 2), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chief boast of Reggio, is a remarkably fine edifice for so small a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town. — The Museum (PL 16; C, 2) contains the natural history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}collection of the celebrated Spallanzani (b. at Reggio in 1729, d.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1799) and a palaeo-ethnological collection illustrative of the history{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the province. — The Library (PL 3 ; B, 4) contains 56,000 vols.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 1066 MSS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Correggio, 9 M. to the N.E. of Reggio, formerly the capital of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principality belonging to the Duchy of Modena, was the birthplace (in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1494) of the celebrated painter Antonio Allegri da Correggio. The piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is embellished with a statue of the master by V. Vela, erected in 1880.{% endraw %}

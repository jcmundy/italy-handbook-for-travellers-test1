{% raw %}LYONS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Paris{% endraw %}
{% raw %}probably a Celtic settlement and became a Roman colony in B. C.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}43. As the seat of the sanctuary 'Ara Romas et Augusti' it was the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}religious centre of all Gaul. Lyons is now the second city, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most important manufacturing place in France , with 342,900 in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hab., silk being its great staple commodity. It is also an archi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}episcopal see, and the headquarters of a corps d'armee. As an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}episcopal residence it is mentioned as early as the 2nd century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The situation of the city at the confluence of the Rhone and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Saone is imposing. The Saone is crossed by thirteen, the Rhone by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eight bridges. Lyons is one of the best built towns in France.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Great alterations have taken place within the last 30-40 years, so{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that the general aspect of the city is modern. It consists of three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinct portions , the original town on the tongue of land between{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rhone and Saone, the suburbs of Les Brotteaux and La Guillo-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiere on the left bank of the Rhone, and the suburb of Vaise on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right bank of the Saone. The military defences of the city consist{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a wide girdle of eighteen forts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The beauty of the situation and the extent of the city are best{% endraw %}
{% raw %}appreciated when viewed from the **Height of Fotjrviere (PI. 25;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}E, 4), crowned by its conspicuous church. The hill is ascended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by several different paths, and also by a wire-rope railway, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}starts near the Cathe'drale St. Jean (PI. 41; E, 4). On the slope{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are a number of fragments of Roman masonry , with explanations{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attached to them, which however are not to be implicitly trusted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The church of Notre Dame de Fourvfere (PI. 25), a modern{% endraw %}
{% raw %}structure surmounted by a gilded statue of the Virgin, contains a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}highly revered 'miraculous' image (visited by upwards of l1^ mil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lion pilgrims annually) and numerous votive tablets. Adjacent is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the New Church, a huge Romanesque building with double aisles,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as yet unfinished. The tower of Notre Dame commands a magni¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficent View (fee 25 c.; visitors may ascend to the statue); and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}still finer prospect may be obtained from the neighbouring Obser¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vatory (fee 50 c.; restaurant). At the feet of the spectator lie the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imposing city, with the two rivers and their bridges, and the well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cultivated district in the neighbourhood; to the E. in fine weather{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mont Blanc, 90 M. distant, is sometimes visible; farther S. the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alps of Dauphine", the Mts. of the Grande Chartreuse and Mont{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pilat, and to the W. the Mts. of Auvergne.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Cathedral of St. Jean Baptiste (PI. 41 ; E, 4) on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right bank of the Saone, adjoining the Palais de Jiistice, dates from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 12th-14th centuries. The Bourbon chapel (1st on the right),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected by Cardinal Bourbon and his brother Pierre de Bourbon,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}son-in-law of Louis XI., contains some fine sculptures.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the left bank of the Saone, about ^ M. lower down, is sit¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uated the church of the Abbey d'Ainay (PI. 24 ; F, 4), one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}oldest in France, dating from the 10th cent., the vaulting of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is borne by four antique columns of granite.{% endraw %}

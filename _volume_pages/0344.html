{% raw %}Padua, the capital of a province, with 44,700 inhab. (commune{% endraw %}
{% raw %}72,200), is situated on the Bacchiglione, which flows through it in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several branches, and occupies an extensive area. Its tortuous streets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are generally flanked with low and narrow 'Portic? or arcades, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many of the more important thoroughfares have recently been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}widened by the removal of the portici on one side. Some of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numerous bridges, which cross the different arms of the river, date{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as far back as the time of the Romans. Padua enjoys the reputa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tion of being the cheapest town in N. Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Padua traces its origin to Antenor, the mythical King of Troy, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brother of Priam, and in the reign of Augustus was the wealthiest town{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Upper Italy. At a later period all the ancient monuments were de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stroyed during the immigration of the barbarian hordes. In the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages the town took the part of the Guelphs, and in 1318 appointed Ja¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copo da Carrara to the Signoria. The princes of this family were much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}harrassed by the princes of Verona and the republic of Venice, and were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at length obliged to succumb in 1405, when the town was annexed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venetia. The University, founded by the Emperor Frederick II. in 1238,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rendered Padua a very famous seat of learning throughout the whole of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the middle ages.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The ancient abbey of Camaldoli lies in a grassy valley surrounded by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forest. It was founded soon after the year 1000 by St. Romuald, but fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently destroyed by fire and devastated by war, in consequence of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church was re-erected in 1523, and again in 1772. It has recently{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been suppressed by the Italian government, like all the other monasteries{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in Italy. The environs are wild and beautiful. A zigzag path ascends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rapidly (3/t hr.) to the Sacro Eremo, a second monastery with hermitages,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded by St. Romuald in 1046. The name of the place is said to be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derived from Campus Maldoli, after a certain pious Count Maldolus, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}presented it to his friend St. Romuald. From this spot the reputation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the order for austere discipline, sanctity, and erudition extended throughout{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the whole of Italy, although the number of their cloisters was never{% endraw %}
{% raw %}great. Camaldoli, as well as Vallombrosa, lost its valuable library and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many treasures of art through the rapacity of the French in 1808.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}220 Route 38. PADUA. S. Antonio.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the History of Art Padua is also a place of importance, its re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}putation as the great focus of Italian science having attracted artists from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many other places. Thus the Florentine masters Giotto, Donatello, F. Lippi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Uccelli, found abundant occupation here. The school of art found¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ed here by Squarcione in the first half of the 15th cent, exhibits a strange{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bias towards scholastic elements. Squarcione, though not a professional ar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tist, made a valuable collection of works of art during his travels, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caused a number of young artists to make drawings from these models.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The greatest Paduan master was Andrea Mantegna (p. 210), and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}school exercised no inconsiderable influence on that of Venice. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}austere style peculiar to the Paduan pictures is perhaps due to the doc¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trinal mode in which the artists were instructed, and to their predilection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for richness of decoration, for which Squarcione's collection doubtless{% endraw %}
{% raw %}supplied abundant models.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*S. Antonio (PL 1; D, 4), the Basilica of St. Anthony of Padua{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1231; a contemporary of St. Francis of Assisi), commonly known{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as 'Il Santo\ is supposed to have been designed by Niccolo Pisano{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1237, but was not begun till 1256. The principal part of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church was completed in 1307, the remainder not before 1475{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(when the domes were raised); the whole was restored in 1749{% endraw %}
{% raw %}after a fire. This vast structure with its seven domes is larger than{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Marco at Venice. Over the portal of the facade, which is 117 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in width, stands a statue of the saint; in the lunette Madonna with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}SS. Bernardino and Antonio, a fresco by Mantegna (1452). The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}church is 100 yds. in length, 49 yds. in width across the transepts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 123 ft. high in the centre.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior is whitewashed. The nave and aisles are supported by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}twelve pillars; the semicircular choir contains eight clustered columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a series of eight chapels.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the entrance, in the nave on the right and left, are two handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}benetiers, with statuettes of St. John the Baptist and Christ, dating from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the beginning of the 16th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right Aisle. By the 1st pillar a 'Madonna in Trono with SS. Peter,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Paul, Bernard, and Anthony, an altar-piece by Antonio Roselli of Bergamo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— 1st Chapel : Altar with reliefs in bronze by Donatello, representing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}miracles of St. Anthony; on the left the sarcophagus of General Gattamelata{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(p. 222), and on the right, that of his son.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right Transept. "Cappella S. Felice, restored in 1773, with a hand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}some altar of 1503, and frescoes from the history of Christ and St. James,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Altichieri da Zevio and Jac. d'Avanzo, painted in 1376. The paintings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the left wall are "somewhat stiff and constrained; but those on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back-wall are free and dignified.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left Transept. "Cappella del Santo, designed by Sansovino; the facade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has four columns and two elegant corner-pillars adorned with reliefs by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Matteo and Tommaso Garvi; between the five arches are the Evangelists.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}rounded by women, by Sansovino; 5. Resuscitation of a child, begun by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Minelli, and completed by A. Sansovino (1528); 6. Tullio Lombardo 'Dis¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Den-tone, and finished by Stella; *9. St. Anthony causes a child to bear{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Ravenna, a town of ancient origin, and formerly the capital of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a province, with 12,000 inhab. (commune 60,300), is situated in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the plain between the rivers Lamone and Ronco (Rom. Bedesis),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a somewhat unhealthy locality. The town is 3 M. in circum¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ference, but nearly one-half of the area is occupied by gardens. It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was originally a seaport, but is now nearly 6 M. distant from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea. After the Porto Candiano had become choked up, the Canale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Naviglio was constructed in 1737, in order to connect Ravenna with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sea. The present harbour of Ravenna is used for the coast-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traffic only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ravenna is one of the most ancient towns in Italy, but under the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Republic was a place of little importance. Augustus constructed the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Portus Classis and a canal, connected with the Po, round the S. side of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the town, and appointed Ravenna the headquarters of the Adriatic fleet.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The commerce of the place now improved, and a new quarter was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected between the town and the harbour (Ccesarea, a name per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}petuated by the ruined church of S. Lorenzo in Cesarea). The harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, having been gradually filled up by the deposits of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Po, Classis and Csesarea fell to decay, while Ravenna continued to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be the capital of the province Flaminia. As early as A.D. 44 Ravenna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became an episcopal see, St. Apollinaris, a disciple of St. Peter, being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first bishop. The Emp. Honorius transferred his residence hither{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Rome in 402 on account of the great strength of the place, and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}438 Ravenna was erected into an archiepiscopal see. After the fall of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Western Empire the town was taken by the Herulian Odoacer, king{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Italy, and again in 493 by Theodoric the Great, king of the Ostro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goths , after which it regained much of its former splendour and was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the residence of the Gothic kings till 539. It then became the seat of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exarch or governor of the Eastern Roman, or Greek Emperors, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}continued under their sway until 752, when the Lombard Aistulph banished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eutychius the last exarch and took possession of the town. Shortly after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards, however, Ravenna was retaken by Pepin, king of the Franks, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handed over to the pope, under whose rule it remained, excepting when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his authority was disputed on several occasions by the Guelphs and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ghibellines. In 1275 the Polenta family, of whom favourable mention is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}made by Dante, obtained the supreme power. In 1318 Ravenna began to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be governed by its own dukes; in 1440 it came into possession of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Venetians, under whom its prosperity materially increased; in 1509 it was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conquered by Pope Julius II., and it belonged to the States of the Church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}till the treaty of Tolentino in 1797. It was, however, restored in 1815,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but again severed from the papal dominions in 1860.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the History of Early Christian Art of the 5-8th century, Ravenna is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the most important place in Italy next to Rome. Being less under the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}influence of the mighty traditions of the past here than at Rome, art was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in a position to develop itself more freely, and even to venture on inno¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vations. The connection of Roman and Byzantine art may best be studied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Ravenna, where the traveller will observe how the capitals of the col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns were gradually remodelled, and a new style of ornamentation intro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}duced. Besides the basilicas there are also dome-structures, which form{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a link between Byzantium and some of the churches of western Europe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(such as the cathedral at Aix-la-Chapelle). The ancient Buildings of Ra¬{% endraw %}

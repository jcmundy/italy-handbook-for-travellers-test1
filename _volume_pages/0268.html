{% raw %}to Luino. MONTE S. SALVATORE. 24. Route. 163{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Lugano to Lucerne (St. Gotthard Railway), see R. 4.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 3 M. to the N.E. of Lugano lies Soragno, with the "Pension{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Therapia ('pens.' 5-7 fr.), beautifully situated at the base of Monte Boglia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It may be reached either by the road via Trevano and Davesco (a drive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 40 min.) or by the foot-path via, Pazzolino and Pregassone (50 min.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Delightful excursion to * Monte S. Salvatore (2982 ft.), ascent 2 hrs.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}descent I72 hr., guide (4fr.) superfluous (comp. Map, p. 150); horse 9fr.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mule 8fr., incl. fee. About 10 min from the Hotel du Parc, between a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}detached house and the wall of a garden, a good paved path diverges to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right from the road to Melide (see below); 2 min. farther, where the path{% endraw %}
{% raw %}divides, we go not to the right, but straight on to the houses; be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween these the road ascends, past the handsome and conspicuous (25 min.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Villa Marchino, to (5 min.) the village of Pazzallo, from which Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rosa is visible through a mountain-gorge. Here the path diverges to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left from the broad road, passes through the gateway of the fourth{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house, and leads to the left by a stony but easy ascent in I72 hr. to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pilgrimage Chapel on the summit (refreshments and a few beds at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small house near the top). The *View embraces all the arms of the Lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Lugano, the mountains and their wooded slopes, especially those above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lugano, sprinkled with numerous villas. To the E. above Porlezza is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Legnone (p. 157), to the left of which, in the extreme distance, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the snow-peaks of the Bernina; N. above Lugano the double peak of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Camoghe (p. 162), to the left of this the distant mountains of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gotthard; W. the chain of Monte Rosa, with the Matterhorn and other{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alps of the Valais. This view is seen to best advantage in the morning,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}when Monte Rosa gleams in the sunshine. The construction of a carriage-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road and of a hotel on the summit is projected. In descending, the route{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through Carona and Melide (somewhat longer) may be chosen.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A Drive (or Steamboat Journey, p. 164) round the Monte S. Salva¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tore (472 hrs.) is much recommended. We proceed by (72 hr.) Pambio,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where a monument by Vela has been erected near the church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pietro to Capt. Carloni, who fell at Somma Campagna in 1848, to (1 hr.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Figino, where we approach the W. arm of the lake. The road then skirts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lake and passes round the Monte Arbostora to (3/4 hr.) Morcote, charm¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ingly situated and commanded by a ruined castle (view from the top),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and to (1 hr.) Melide. Thence to Lugano, by the high-road. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}churchyard of S. Abbondio, 1 M. to the W. of Pambio (see above), con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains a fine monument of the Torriani family, a woman praying, by Vela.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The ascent of "Monte Bre (3100 ft.), to the N.E. of Lugano, is another{% endraw %}
{% raw %}easy excursion, scarcely less interesting than that to Mte. S. Salvatore. (It{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is advisable to take a boy as guide from Bre.) A road runs inland towards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several mills at the foot of the mountain. Thence a broad and well-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed path winds upwards to the right to the small village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Desago, passing a few groups of houses. Above Desago the path divides;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}both routes are broad, and well-constructed, leading round the mountain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the village of Bre (2630ft.) on its farther side (Inn, bread and wine only).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The route to the right, above the lake, is very beautiful, while that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the left commands a fine inland view. Near the church of Bre a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}narrow forest-path ascends to the summit of the mountain. This path also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}divides; the branch to the right traverses the highest crest of the hill,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}that to the left leads to a spur of the mountain in the direction of Lu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gano. The summit may be attained by either. The view of the several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arms of the Lake of Lugano, especially in the direction of Porlezza,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the surrounding mountains, is remarkably fine. Lugano itself is not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}visible from the summit, but from the above-mentioned spur a good view{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of it may be obtained. From Lugano to Bre about I72 hr.; from Bre to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the summit about 1 hr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monte Caprino, opposite Lugano, on the E. bank of the lake, is much{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequented on holidays by the townspeople, who possess wine-cellars (can-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tine) in the numerous cool grottoes by which the side of the mountain is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}honeycombed. These receptacles are guarded by numerous huts, which{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Luca Cambiaso and other pictures (Castiglione, Shepherd and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shepherdess ; Van Dyck, Portrait of a lady).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left, No. 10, Palazzo Adorno, also by Gal. Alessi(?), contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}several good pictures by Rubens, Palma Vecchio, Bassano, Bordone,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Seb. del Piombo, and others, but is not always shown to visitors.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left, No. 12, Palazzo Serra, by Alessi, remodelled in the interior{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by De Wailly (d. 1798) and Tagliafico, contains a fine hall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right, No. 9, Palazzo del Municipio (PL 23), formerly Doria{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tursi, erected by Rocco Lurago (16th cent.), has a handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}staircase and court, ingeniously adapted to the rising ground on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which it stands.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Vestibule is adorned with five frescoes from the life of the Doge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grimaldi, the court with a statue of Mazzini in marble, and the stair¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}case with a statue of Cataneo Pinelli. — In the large Council Chamber{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the upper floor are portraits of Columbus and Marco Polo in mosaic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the adjacent room a Madonna between two saints, by Gerard David of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bruges (not Van Eyck), and a Crucifixion with SS. Mary and John, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a good early Netherlands master (not Diirer); two other pictures inferior.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Two letters of Columbus; large bronze tablet of A.D. 117, recording the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}judgment of Roman arbiters in a dispute between Genoa and a neigh¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bouring castle. A cabinet to the left contains the violin of Paganini. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the loggia to the left is a Bacchic sarcophagus-relief from the tomb of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Franc Spinola. (Permessi for the cathedral-treasury are procured in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}loggia to the right.){% endraw %}

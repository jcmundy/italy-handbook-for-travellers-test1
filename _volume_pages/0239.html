{% raw %}pictures, sketches of academicans, casts from the antique, Renais¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sance and modern sculptures. (An annual exhibition of art takes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}place in these rooms, generally in September.) — Room XX : Ca-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nova, Vestal Virgin; Thorvaldsen, The Graces and Cupid. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Room XXIV. contains a copy of Leonardo da Vinci's Last Supper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Marco da Oggionno. — The last but one of the rooms with mo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dern pictures contains portraits, the best of which are those of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Niccolini by Ussi, Cavour and Manzoni by Hayez, and D'Azeglio{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sala. — Returning hence to the ante-chamber, the visitor{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enters the Galleria Oggioni to the right: Luini, Holy Family ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crivelli, Coronation of Mary (1493).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Museo Archeologico on the ground-floor (admission daily{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12-3, 50 c.; Sundays free ; entrance in the small Piazza di Brera,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or through a passage to the right on the ground - floor) contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a rich but imperfectly arranged collection of antique, mediae¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}val , and modern works of art, including some fine Renaissance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sculptures.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}J. Gothic bell of 1352; in the middle, four ancient porphyry columns from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Cristoforo. Next wall: Roman and mediaeval architectural fragments.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fourth wall: Portions of the monument of Gaston de Foix (who fell at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the battle of Ravenna in 1512, see p. 333), from the monastery of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marta, the most important being (*E.) a recumbent figure of the hero by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bambaja. D. Monument of Lancino Curzio (d. 1513), by the same master.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}F. Marble framework of a door from the Palazzo Medici, with the arms{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and portraits of Francesco Sforza and Bianca Maria Visconti, attributed{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Fragment of a cippus , a youth leaning on a staff (Greek); to the left,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Head of Zeus (nose modern). B. Monument of Regina della Scala, wife{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Bernabo Visconti; bust of a lady (15th cent.). In the centre: A. Large{% endraw %}

{% raw %}carvings in marble and ivory; majolica; mediseval goldsmith's work;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Egyptian antiquities.{% endraw %}

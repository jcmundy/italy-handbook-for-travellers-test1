{% raw %}*S. Sisto (PL 9; D, 1), the richest in Piacenza, erected in 1499-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1511, with a fine Ionic atrium in front of the modern facade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 1518 Raphael painted for this church his masterpiece, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sistine Madonna (Madonna with St. Sixtus and St. Barbara, now at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dresden), which was sold in 1753 to King Augustus III. of Poland{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for 20,000 ducats and replaced by a copy by Avanzini (beginning{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 18th cent.). The choir contains pictures by Camillo Procaccini,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Palma Giovane, etc. ; also several good intarsias and (in the left{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transept) the unfinished monument of Margaret of Austria (d. 1586),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daughter of Charles V. and wife of Ottavio Farnese, Duke of Parma,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the father of Alessandro Farnese. On each side of the entrance is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tiny chapel with a dome and a Greek cross.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A little to the E. of S. Sisto is the Palazzo Farnese (PL E, 2),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected in a magnificent style by Vignola during the reign of Mar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}garet in 1558, one of his first great works. It was never completed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and is now a barrack. — In the Via delle Benedettine, farther to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the N.E., is the Palazzo dei Tribunali (formerly Landi), with two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dilapidated courts, a handsome frieze, and a rich early-Renaissance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portal (on the side next the church of S. Lorenzo).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Biblioteca Pubblica contains 120,000 vols., including a val¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}uable psalter on red parchment, bound in silver, which once be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}longed to Angelberga, the consort of Emp. Lewis II. (857), and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copy of Dante ('Codex Landiano'), supposed to date from 1336;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also a small archaeological and palaeontological collection.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A Steam-Tramway, opened in 1881 and starting near the railway-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station (fares lfr. 60 c, lfr.), connects Piacenza with (l'/^hr.) Ponte dell'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Olio (Albergo del Sole, poor), a small manufacturing town, picturesquely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated at the mouth of the Val Nure, 14 M. to the S. On market-days{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Tuesdays) it presents a very interesting picture of Italian peasant life.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— From Ponte dell1 Olio a road leads to Borgonure (Alb. dell' Agnello,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rustic), formerly called Bettola, the chief depot for the local traffic on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. side of the Apennines.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A diligence plies daily in 6 hrs. from Piacenza to Bobbio, 25 M. to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S.W., once famous for the library in the monastery.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The remains of the ancient town of Velleia, which is believed to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}have been buried by a landslip in the reign of the Emp. Probus (about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}278), lie 25 M. to the S.E. of Piacenza. Various antiquities excavated here{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1760-75 are now in the museum at Parma (p. 293). An amphitheatre,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}temple, forum, etc., have also been discovered. The route to Velleia is{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Vignola, Rezzano, and Badagnano (where the carriage-road terminates). —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Velleia may also be reached from Fiorenzuola (see below), via Gastel{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arquato.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Opposite is the Statue of Lorenzo de' Medici, who in contrast to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giuliano is represented in profound meditation (hence called il pensiero);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}below it his tomb with -Statues of Evening and Dawn (Crepusculo e Aurora),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also by Michael Angelo. The significance which Michael Angelo meant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to convey by the allegorical figures is somewhat obscure and artificial.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The periods of the day represent as it were the various members of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}universe, which are sunk in grief at the death of the heroes. The statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are not portraits, but ideal forms, in which are reflected the two chief{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sides of a heroic nature, — self-devoted absorption in noble designs, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}confident energy. It is certain, as already mentioned, that sorrow at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fate of his country exercised a great influence on the master's chisel, even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}if the theory that Michael Angelo was bent upon producing a purely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}political monument cannot stand the test. — The remaining statues in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chapel, consisting of an unfinished Madonna, by Michael Angelo, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the two patron saints of the Medici, St. Damianus (1.) by Raffaello da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Montelupo, and St. Cosmas (r.) by Era Giovanni Angiolo da Montorsoli, were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also sculptured for the mausoleum, but have never occupied the positions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for which they were destined.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Chapel of the Princes (Cappella deiPrincipi), the burial-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapel of the grand-dukes of the Medici family, was constructed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1604 by Matteo Nigetti, from the designs of Giovanni de' Medici.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is octagonal in form, covered by a dome, and gorgeously decorated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with marble and valuable ''Mosaics in stone. The paintings in the dome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Creation, Fall, Death of Adam, Sacrifice of Noah, Nativity, Death and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Resurrection, Last Judgment) are by Pietro Benvenuti. In six niches below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the granite sarcophagi of the princes, some of them with gilded bronze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues, from Cosimo I. (d. 1564) to Cosimo III. (d. 1723; comp. p. 337).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the coping round the chapel are placed the armorial bearings of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}16 Tuscan towns in stone-mosaic. — A sum of 22 million lire (about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}880,000Z.) was expended by the Medici family from their private resources{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the construction and decoration of this chapel.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The *Lake of Como (699 ft.), Italian Lago di Como or II Lario,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Lacus Larius of the Romans, is extolled by Virgil (Georg. ii.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}159), and is in the estimation of many the most beautiful lake in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}N. Italy. Length from Como to the N. extremity 30 M., from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Punta di Bellagio (p. 156) to Lecco 122/3 M.; greatest width be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween Menaggio and Varenna nearly 2y2M. ; greatest depth 1929 ft.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Numerous gay villas of the Milanese aristocracy, surrounded by luxuri¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ant gardens and vineyards, are scattered along the banks of the lake. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the forests above, the brilliant green of the chestnut and walnut contrasts{% endraw %}
{% raw %}strongly with the greyish tints of the olive, which to the unaccustomed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eye bears a strong resemblance to the willow. The mountains rise to a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}height of 7000 ft. The scenery of the lake, as seen from the deck of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}steamboat, though on a far grander scale, faintly resembles that of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rhine, the banks on both sides being perfectly distinguishable by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller. At Bellagio (p. 154) the lake divides into two branches,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called respectively the Lakes of Como (W.) and Lecco (E.). The Adda{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enters at the upper extremity and makes its egress near Lecco. The W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}arm has no outlet. — The industrious inhabitants of the banks of the lake{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are much occupied in the production and manufacture of silk. Many{% endraw %}
{% raw %}young artisans (masons and carpenters chiefly) emigrate from this district{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Cuba and the Spanish-American islands, whence they return to their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}native land as soon as they have accumulated enough to enable them to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}purchase a small property. — The lake abounds in fish, and trout of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}20 lbs. weight are occasionally captured. The 'Agoni' are small, but{% endraw %}
{% raw %}palatable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The prospect from the quay at Como is limited, but as soon as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the steamer has passed the first promontory on the E. the beauty{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the lake is disclosed to view.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}From Coire to Biasca by the Lukmanier.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}77 M. Diligence to (39 M.) Disentis twice daily in 83/4 hrs. (fare 14 fr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}85 c, coupe 18 fr.); from Disentis to (38 M.) Biasca once daily in 874 hrs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(fare 13 fr. 10 c ; coupe 16 fr. 20 c).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Coire to (6 M.) Reichenau, see p. 33. The road, one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most picturesque in Switzerland, ascends the valley of the Vorder-Rhein,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is plentifully sprinkled with castles. Numerous villages and ham¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lets are passed. Above (274 M.) Trins rises the ruined castle of Hohentrins.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}13 M. Flims (3616ft.), a small and ancient town. — The pensions of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Waldhduser, 1 M. farther, are in great request in summer. — At Schleuis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2507 ft.) is the chateau of Lowenberg.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2072 M. Ilanz (2355 ft.; Oberalp; Lukmanier), prettily situated at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the mouth of the Lugnetz Valley. — To the right, near the village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Waltensburg, are the ruins of Jbrgenberg. The Rhine is crossed near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tavanasa, and again near Zignau or Rinkenberg. The Rinkenberg bridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}commands one of the finest views in the valley.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}32 M. Trons (2822ft.; Krone; Zum Todi). — At Somvix the valley of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the same name opens on the S. The road between Somvix and Disentis{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is remarkable for the boldness of its construction. Several tributaries of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rhine are crossed.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3872 M. Disentis (3773ft.; "Disentiser Hof; "Hdtel Condrau, zur Post;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Hdtel Condrau, zur Krone), a market-town with a Benedictine Abbey,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}situated at the confluence of the Medelser, or Mittel-Rhein, and the Vor¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der-Rhein. The Lukmanier road ascends the valley of the former, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road to Andermatt leads through the Vorder-Rhein valley.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The New Road over the Lukmanier Pass (opened in 1878) crosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vorder-Rhein just above its confluence with the Mittel-Rhein, and enters{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the * Val Medel, the profound and wild ravine of the latter stream.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eleven tunnels are passed through before Curaglia is reached, and numerous{% endraw %}
{% raw %}magnificent views are enjoyed. At the end of the ravine the road crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right bank of the Rhine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4172 M. Curaglia (4370 ft.; Post), at the entrance to the Val Platta.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— 46 M. Platta (4528ft.; Post). Several hamlets are passed. — 4872 M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Perdatsch (5093ft.), a group of hovels, at the mouth of the Val Cristallina.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}mounts to the hospice of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}50 M. S. Maria (6043ft.; :Tnn). About r/4 M. farther, the road crosses{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the summit of the Lukmanier (6289 ft.), the boundary between the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Grisons and Canton Ticino, and, with one exception (the Maloja, 5941 ft.),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the lowest of the Alpine passes from Switzerland to Italy. The road is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}now level for some distance, and then leads high above the Brenno,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the precipitous N. side of the Val S. Maria, being hewn at places in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the face of the rock. The road next descends to (472 M.) the hospice of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Camperio (4028 ft.), where it crosses the Brenno.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}68 M. Olivone (2927ft.; 'Hdtel Olivone), the highest village in the Val{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the Brenno, passing numerous villages. The lower part of the Val Blegno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is monotonous.{% endraw %}

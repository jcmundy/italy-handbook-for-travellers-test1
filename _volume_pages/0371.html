{% raw %}I. "Sala del Maggior Consiglio (door generally open; if not, ring).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In this large hall (55 yds. long, 26 yds. broad, 47 ft. high), the Nobili,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose names were entered in the 'Golden Book', and who constituted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the highest authority in the Republic, formerly sat. In 1848-49 the House{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Representatives under the Dictator Manin also met here. On the frieze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the portraits of 76 doges, beginning with Obelerio Antenoreo (d. 810);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the walls 21 large pictures by Bassano, Paolo Veronese, Tintoretto, etc.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted to commemorate the achievements of the Republic. On the E.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wall Jac. Tintoretto's Paradise, said to be the largest oil-painting in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}world, containing a perplexing multitude of figures, several of the heads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which are admirably done. — The Historical Pictures consist of two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}series. The first illustrates in a somewhat boastful style the life of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Doge Sebastiano Ziani (1173-79), who accorded an asylum to Pope Alexan¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der III. when at variance with the Emperor Frederick Barbarossa, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(in league with the towns of Lombardy) resisted the imperial demands.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The second series depicts the exploits of the Doge Enrico Dandolo (p. 233).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As works of art they are somewhat tedious.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The first series begins on the upper part of the wall to the right of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance, and runs to the left in the direction of the opposite end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the rooms : 1. Meeting of Pope Alexander III. and the Doge Ziani at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Monastery della Carita, and — 2. Parting audience of the ambassadors of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pope and the Doge on their departure for Parma, both by Pupils of Paolo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Veronese; 3. (over the window) Presentation of the consecrated candle,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Leandro Bassano; 4. Ambassadors of the Pope and the Doge presenting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Fred. Barbarossa at Pavia a petition for a cessation of hostilities, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jacopo Tintoretto; 5. The Pope presenting a sword to the Doge, by Franc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bassano; 6. (over the window) Departure of the Doge with the papal bene¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diction, by Paolo Fiammingo; 7. Battle of Salvore (Pirano), defeat of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Imperial fleet, and capture of Otho, 1177, by Dom. Tintoretto; 8. (above{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the door) The Doge presenting the son of the Emperor to the Pope, by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Andrea Vicentino; 9. Pope Alexander grants permission to the captive Otho{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to repair to his father in order to negotiate a peace, by Palma Giovane;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10. Fred. Barbarossa kneeling before the Pope (p. 239), by Federigo Zuc-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}caro; 11. (over the door) Conclusion of peace between the Pope, the Doge,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Emperor, at Ancona, by Girolamo Gambarato; 12. The Pope pre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sents gifts to the Doge, including the ring, the symbol of supremacy with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the Doge annually 'wedded the Adriatic', 1177, by Giulio dai Mora.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The cycle of pictures in honour of the Doge Dandolo also begins on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the entrance wall, to which we return after having inspected the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}series. They run from left to right as follows : 1. The Doge and French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crusaders swear an oath of alliance at St. Mark's in 1201, for the purpose{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of liberating the Holy Land, by Giov. Le Glerc; 2. Storming of Zara in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1202, by Andrea Vicentino; 3. Surrender of Zara to the Crusaders in 1202,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Dom. Tintoretto (placed over the door to a balcony, which affords a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fine "View of the Lagune and the islands of S. Giorgio and Giudecca); 4.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alexius, son of the dethroned Greek Emp. Isaac Angelus, invoking the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aid of the Venetians in behalf of his father in 1202, by Andrea Vicentino;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5. Taking of Constantinople by the Venetians and French, 1204, by Dom.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tintoretto; 6. Second taking of Constantinople, 1204, by Dom. Tintoretto;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7. Count Baldwin of Flanders elected Greek Emp. in the church of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sophia, 1204, by Andr. Vicentino; 8. Coronation of Baldwin by the Doge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Enrico Dandolo, 1204, by Aliense. (Above this, a black tablet on the frieze{% endraw %}
{% raw %}among the portraits of the Doges bears the inscription: Hie est locus Ma-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rini Falethri decapitati pro criminibus; comp. p. 233.) — Lastly, as an ad¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}ARLES.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Paris{% endraw %}
{% raw %}483 M. Aries (*H6tel du Nord, D. with wine 4, B. iy2, dtj.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3, A. 1, L. li/2 fr.; Hotel du Forum), the Arelate or Arelas of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancients, once one of the most important towns in Gaul and a flour¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ishing seat of commerce in the 5th cent, of our era, is now a some¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}what dull place (25,100 inhab.) on the Rhone, 24 M. from its mouth.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is connected with Trinquetaille on the opposite bank by a bridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of boats.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The principal sights of Aries, for which 3-4 hrs. suffice, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}all within easy distance from the hotels: to the E. St. Trophime,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the extensive Museum, and the Theatre of Augustus; N. the Am¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phitheatre, and S.E. the Champs-Elysees.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Place of the Hbtel-de-Ville, which was erected in 1673,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises an *Obelisk of grey granite from the mines of Esterel near{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fre"jus (p. 21), an ancient monument of unknown origin, found in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Rhone in 1676. It is destitute of hieroglyphic inscriptions.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity stands the *Cathedral op St. Trophime (Tro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phimus is said to have been a pupil of St. Paul), founded in the 6th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or 7th cent., possessing an interesting Romanesque * Portal of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}12th or 13th cent., of semicircular form, supported by twelve columns{% endraw %}
{% raw %}resting on lions, between which are apostles and saints (St. Tro-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}phimus, St. Stephen, etc.); above it Christ as Judge of the world.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior contains little to interest the visitor, with the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ception of several sarcophagi and pictures. — On the S. side (entered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the sacristy) are the *Cloisters, with round and pointed arches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and remarkable capitals, dating from various epochs. The N. side is in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the half antique style of the Carlovingian period (9th cent.), the E. side{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dates from 1221, the W. side (the most beautiful) from 1389, and the S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side from the 16th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Museum, established in the old church of St. Anna, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains numerous antiquities found in and near Aries. The following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relics deserve special mention: *Head of Diana (or Venus); Augustus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(found in 1834); recumbent Silenus with pipe, once used as a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fountain-figure; figures of dancing women (found in the theatre);{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and sarcophagi from the ancient burial-ground (see below), etc.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Theatre (commonly called that of 'Augustus'), a most pic¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}turesque ruin, is in a very dilapidated condition. The most perfect{% endraw %}
{% raw %}part is the stage-wall, which according to the ancient arrangement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}had three doors. In front of it was a colonnade, of which two col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns, one of African, the other of Carrara marble, are still stand¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing. The opening for the letting down of the curtain is distinctly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}recognisable. The orchestra, paved with slabs of variegated marble,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contained the seats of persons of rank. The lower tiers only of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}seats of the ordinary spectators are preserved.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The theatre once possessed a second story, indications of which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}observed when the ruin is viewed from the Saracens' Tower (in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}direction of the public promenade). The dimensions of the building when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perfect were very extensive (breadth from N. to S. 33772 ft.), and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}effect it produces is extremely striking.{% endraw %}

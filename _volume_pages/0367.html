{% raw %}240 Route 40. VENICE. S. Marco.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Left Transept contains two handsome bronze Candelabra of 1520;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above, on the left, a Mosaic of 1542, representing the genealogy of Mary;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}adjoining it are some fine Byzantine mosaics. — The Right Transept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also contains two bronze Candelabra, of the end of the 16th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the arched Parapet on each side of the Choir are three reliefs in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronze, by Sansovino (d. 1570), representing events from the life of St.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mark. On the parapet of the Stalls are the four Evangelists in bronze,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Sansovino, and four Fathers of the church, by Caliari (1614).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The High Altar (Altare Maggiore) stands beneath a canopy of verde{% endraw %}
{% raw %}antico, borne by four columns of marble (with reliefs of the 11th cent.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Pala d'Oro, enamelled work with jewels, wrought on plates of gold{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and silver, executed at Constantinople in 1105, constitutes the altar-piece,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is uncovered on high festivals only, or, at other times, for a fee{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 6 fr. (It was originally intended to embellish the front of the altar.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Under the high-altar repose the relics of St. Mark, as the marble slab at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the back records. — Behind the high-altar is a second altar with four spiral{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns of alabaster, of which the two white ones in the middle are semi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}transparent, and are said to have once belonged to the Temple of Solomon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Sacristy (Sagrestia), to the left, contains some fine mosaics on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting; cabinets with inlaid work of 1523; on the door leading from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high-altar, reliefs in bronze by Sansovino (1556); to the right of the handle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the portrait-head of the maker of the door; in the right corner the head{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Titian. Entrance to the Crypt, see below.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right of the high-altar: Cappella di S. Clemente, with altar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}relief of the 16th cent., representing SS. Nicholas, James, and Andrew,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the Doge Andr. Gritti.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the right aisle, close to the principal entrance, is the Battistero, in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the centre of which is a large bronze font of 1545; above it is John the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baptist. Also the monument of the Doge And. Dandolo (d. 1354). The stone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over the altar is from Mt. Tabor. To the left of the altar the head of John{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Baptist, of the i5th cent.; below it is the stone on which he is said{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to have been beheaded. — From the Baptistery we enter the "Cappella{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zeno, containing the handsome "Monument of Cardinal Giambattista Zeno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(d. 1501), wrought entirely in bronze; on the sarcophagus is the figure of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the cardinal, over life-size; below are the six Virtues. The "Altar and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canopy are also cast in bronze, with the exception of the frieze and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bases of the columns. Over the altar are groups in bronze, of the Madonna,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Peter, and John the Baptist; on the altar itself a relief of the Re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}surrection. To the right and left two lions in coloured marble.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the right transept is the entrance to the Treasury ( Tesoro di S. Marco,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}open on Mondays and Fridays 12V2-2 o'clock, except on festivals), containing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two candelabra by Benvenuto Cellini (?); cover of the books of the Gospels{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the church of St. Sophia at Constantinople, decorated with gold and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jewels; a crystal vase with the 'Blood of the Saviour'; a silver column{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with a fragment of the pillar at which Christ was scourged; a cup of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}agate with a portion of the 'skull of St. John'; the sword of the Doge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Morosini; cuneiform writings from Persepolis ; an episcopal throne of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7th cent., said to be that of St. Mark; and a number of other curiosities.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Crypt , freed from water and restored in 1868 (but still often{% endraw %}
{% raw %}underwater), also deserves a visit; open 12-2 o'clock, entrance by the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door to the right in the Sacristy (see above); at other hours it is shown by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sacristan. To the right a well-executed Christ in relief by Sansovino.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A walk (sacristan 1/2 fr.) round the Gallery inside the church is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}strongly recommended in order that the mosaics may be more closely{% endraw %}
{% raw %}inspected. The ascent is from a door to the right in the principal portal,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which the sacristan opens. The gallery on the outside of the church{% endraw %}
{% raw %}should then be visited for the sake of examining the bronze horses.{% endraw %}

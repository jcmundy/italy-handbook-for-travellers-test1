{% raw %}The *Scala dei Giganti, the flight of steps by which the palace{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is entered, derives its name from the colossal statues of Mars and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Neptune at the top, executed by Sansovino in 1554. It was on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the highest landing of these steps that the coronation of the doges{% endraw %}
{% raw %}used to take place. Opposite the landing are statues of Adam and{% endraw %}

{% raw %}chiefly occupied in embellishing the Palace of the Doges. Their{% endraw %}

{% raw %}most attractive array of the Venetian painters, so far as their ener¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gies were enlisted in the service of the state.{% endraw %}

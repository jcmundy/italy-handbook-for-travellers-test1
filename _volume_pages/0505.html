{% raw %}358 Route 52. PISTOJA. From Pisa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the station lfr.; 1st hour lfr. 40 or lfr. 70c, each additional hour lfr.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or lfr. 30c.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pistoja, an ancient town with 13,500 inhab. (commune 54,000),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is loftily situated in the vicinity of the Ombrone, a small tributary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Arno , in a fertile district. It has broad, well-built streets,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and important manufactories of guns and iron-wares. Pistols are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}said to have been invented at Pistoja, and thence to derive their{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pistoja, the Roman Pistoria, near which Catiline was defeated and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slain, B.C. 62, was in the middle ages the centre of the fiercest struggles{% endraw %}
{% raw %}between the Guelphs and Ghibellines. In the year 1300 the Cancellieri{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Panciatichi, or Black and White parties, mentioned by Dante (Inferno{% endraw %}
{% raw %}xxiv. 143), who afterwards extended their intrigues to Florence and in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fluenced the fortunes of the poet himself, were formed here. Pistoja had{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to surrender to Florence in 1351. It was the birthplace of the celebrated{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jurist and poet Cino, a contemporary of Dante, and of the satirist Niccolb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Forteguerri (1674-35), author of the Ricciardetto.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the History of Art, Pistoja, which somewhat resembles Florence{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in miniature, held an important rank in the early part of the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages, and was foremost among the Tuscan republics in fostering artistic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}progress. The older churches, such as the Cathedral and S. Andrea,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exhibit a leaning to the Pisan style, which was extensively in vogue in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 12th century. At Pistoja we also meet with many of the earliest at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tempts at sculpture in Tuscany, which are much ruder than contem¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}poraneous German and French works of the same kind, and with several{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the names of the oldest artists (Gruamons and Adeodatus). After the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14th cent. Pistoja became dependent on Florence both politically and in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the province of art. The town continued to be wealthy and ambitious{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enough to patronise artists, but thenceforth those of Florence were always{% endraw %}
{% raw %}employed. Of the Goldsmith's Art we have an important specimen in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}silver altar in the Cathedral.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We follow the Via Cino, leading from the station and intersect¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing the Corso Vittorio Emmanuele at a right angle, as far as the Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cino, and turning here to the right into the Via Cavour, soon reach{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the old Romanesque church of —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Giovanni Fuoricivitas (Evangelista; PL 1), erected about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1160 , with a somewhat overladen facade adorned in Pisan fashion{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with rows of columns. Over the side-entrance is a relief repre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}senting the Eucharist by Gruamons, as an inscription on the archi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}trave records (12th cent.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. 'On the right is the *Pulpit, adorned with ten reliefs on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}three sides by Fra Guglielmo, a pupil of Niccolb Pisano, about 1270, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}series beginning to the left with the Annunciation, Conception, etc.; in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}front, the symbols of the evangelists; on the left, a handsome basin for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}holy water by Giovanni Pisano, with figures of the virtues at the four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}corners. On the right, the 'Visitation of Mary, a life-size group in terra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cotta, by Andrea della Robbia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite the church is the Pal. Panciatichi-Celesi. — Follow¬{% endraw %}

{% raw %}in the 13th, with a tribuna added in 1599 by Jacopo Lafri. In the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vestibule are faded frescoes by Giovanni Cristiani da Pistoja (14th{% endraw %}

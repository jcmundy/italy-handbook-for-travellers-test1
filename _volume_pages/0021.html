{% raw %}RAILWAYS.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}xvii{% endraw %}
{% raw %}find it convenient to have as nearly as possible the exact fare ready{% endraw %}
{% raw %}before taking tickets. In addition to the fare, a tax of 5 c. is payable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on each ticket, and the express fares are 10 per cent higher{% endraw %}
{% raw %}than the ordinary. It is also very important to be at the station{% endraw %}
{% raw %}early. The ticket-office at large stations is open 1 hr., at small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stations y2 hr. before the departure of the train. Holders of tickets{% endraw %}
{% raw %}alone have the right of admission to the waiting-rooms. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of the journey tickets are given up at the uscfta, except in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the case of the very large stations, where they are collected before{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the passengers alight.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller should, if possible, know the weight of his lug¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gage approximately, in order to guard against imposition (1 kilo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gramme = about 2!/5 lbs.). No luggage is allowed free, except{% endraw %}
{% raw %}small articles (which must not exceed 20 X 10 X 12 inches) taken{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the passenger into his carriage. Porters who convey luggage to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and from the carriages are sufficiently paid with a few sous, where{% endraw %}
{% raw %}there is no fixed tariff. Those who intend to make only a short{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stay at a place, especially when the town or village lies at a con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}siderable distance from the railway, had better leave their heavier{% endraw %}
{% raw %}luggage at the station till their return (dare in deposito, or de-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}positare; 10 c. per day per cwt. or fraction of a cwt.).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The best collection of time-tables is the LIndicatore Ufficiale{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delle Strade Ferrate', etc. (published monthly by the Fratelli Pozzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Turin; price 1 fr.), with which every traveller should be pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vided. A smaller edition, confined to the railways of N. Italy (Fer-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rovie delV Alta Italia), is also issued.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Through Tickets to various parts of Italy are issued in London{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(at the principal railway-stations; by Messrs. Cook & Son, Fleet{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Street; etc.), in Paris, and at many of the principal towns in Ger¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}many and Switzerland. They are generally available for 30 days,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and each passenger is allowed 56 Engl. lbs. of luggage free. Luggage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may be registered either to the traveller's final destination or to any{% endraw %}
{% raw %}one of the stations for which there are separate coupons in his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticket-book. Travellers about to cross the frontier in either direction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are strongly recommended to superintend the custom-house exami¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nation of luggage in person. — Tickets from Italy to Switzerland,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Germany, etc., must be partly paid for in gold, the amount being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stated in the Italian time-tables in the case of the most important{% endraw %}
{% raw %}foreign towns. The traveller should provide himself with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}necessary amount of gold beforehand , as the money-changers and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticket-clerks at the station charge a very high percentage on bank¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}notes. It is, however, usually possible to book to the frontier-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}station only, and there take a fresh ticket. Information on this and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other points may be obtained in the larger towns from the Agenzie{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Citta.{% endraw %}

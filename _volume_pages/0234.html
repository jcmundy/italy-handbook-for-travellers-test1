{% raw %}Macchiavelli, Marco Polo; Raphael, Galileo, Dante, Michael Angelo; Volta,{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Piazza della Scala (PI. E, 4) is embellished with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Monument of Leonardo da Vinci (1452-1519) by Magni, erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1872. The statue of the master in Carrara marble, over life-size,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands on a lofty pedestal, surrounded by Marco da Oggionno, Cesare{% endraw %}
{% raw %}da Sesto , Salaino , and Boltraffio , four of his pupils , and adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with copies of his principal works in relief. — In the piazza, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}W. of the statue, is the Teatro della Scala (p. 128); to theE. is the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large Palazzo del Marino, in which the Municipio(P\. E, 4) has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}established since 1861 , erected in 1555 from designs by Galeazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alessi, with a massive facade and interesting court.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Beyond it is the Jesuit church of 5. Fedele (PI. E, F, 4) in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piazza of that name, erected by S. Carlo Borromeo in 1569 from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designs by Pellegrini, containing a sumptuous high-altar. The ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}joining Palazzo del Censo ed Archivio, formerly the Jesuit college,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains part of the government archives, chiefly documents relat¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing to the history of Milan. — To the N. of this point is the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}degli Omenoni with the palace of the same name, erected by Leone{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leoni and adorned with Caryatides. The Via degli Omenoni ends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Piazza Belgiojoso, which contains the Palazzo Belgiojoso{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Manzoni's house (No. 3).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Adjacent, at Via Morone, No. 10 (PI. F, 4), is the Museo Poldi-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pezzoli, bequeathed to the town by Cavaliere Poldi-Pezzoli in 1879{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and exhibited in the tastefully-furnished house formerly occupied{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the founder. The collections are open to the public daily 10-4,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on holidays 12-3 (adm. 1 fr.; catalogue 1 fr.).{% endraw %}

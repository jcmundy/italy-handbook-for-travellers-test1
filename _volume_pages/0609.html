{% raw %}CORSICA.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The great attractions of Corsica are its beautiful scenery and its inter¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}esting historical associations, for it can boast of no antiquities or trea¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sures of art. A visit to the island is now easily accomplished. A week's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stay will enable the ordinary traveller to become acquainted with Ajac¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cio , Corte (ascent of Monte Rotondo), and Bastia. Those who desire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a more thorough insight into the resources of the country and the char¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}acter of the natives will encounter serious difficulties, and should en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}deavour to obtain introductions to inhabitants of the island.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corsica, like its sister-island Sardinia, which was peopled by the same{% endraw %}
{% raw %}race, never attained to a high degree of civilisation in ancient times. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whole island is depicted as having been a wild and impenetrable forest,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of very evil reputation. Its possession was nevertheless keenly contested{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the great naval powers of ancient times. The Phocseans, banished{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from Asia by the Persians, founded the town of Alalia (afterwards Aleria){% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the E. coast, at the mouth of the Tavignano, B. C. 556. After a great{% endraw %}
{% raw %}naval battle in 536, however, they were compelled by the allied Etrus¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cans and Carthaginians to abandon their settlement and migrate to Italy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}where they founded the town Elea, or Velia, in Lucania. The island then{% endraw %}
{% raw %}became subject to the Etruscans, and subsequently to the Carthaginians,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from whom it was wrested by the Romans in 238. Under Marius and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sulla the colonies of Aleria and Mariana were established on the E. coast,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but both were subsequently destroyed. The island was frequently used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as a place of banishment, as in the case of the philosopher Seneca, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spent eight years here during the reign of the Emp. Claudius. His account{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the country and its inhabitants is by no means flattering, and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corsicans sometimes declare that 'Seneca era un birbone'. The following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lines written by him are to this day partially true:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Prima est ulcisci lex, altera vivere raptu,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tertia mentiri, quarta negare deos'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the fall of the Western Empire Corsica frequently changed mas¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ters; the Vandals, Byzantines, Ostrogoths, Franks, and Saracens rapidly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}succeeded each other in its possession. In 1070 the Pisans, and in 1348{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Genoese obtained the supremacy, which the latter retained till the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}18th century. Their oppressive sway, however, gave rise to a long series of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}conspiracies and insurrections, in many of which a number of remark¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able characters and bold adventurers distinguished themselves. Thus{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Arrigo della Rocca, Vincentello d'Istria, and Giampolo da Leca in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}14th and 15th cent., and Renuccio della Rocca and Sampiero di Bastelica{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(killed on 17th Jan., 1567) in the 16th century. At length, in 1729, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}universal disaffection to Genoa began to assume a more serious aspect,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}notwithstanding the efforts made by the Republic to stifle it with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}aid of German auxiliaries. The last of a long succession of adventurers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was a Baron Theodore Neuhof, from Westphalia, who landed on 12th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}March, 1736, at Aleria, near the mouth of the Tavignano, attended by a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}number of followers, and provided with warlike equipments. He was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}shortly afterwards proclaimed King of Corsica, under the title of Theo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dore I., but his success was short-lived, and he was soon compelled to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quit the island, for the Genoese were assisted by the French. Theodore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}returned twice subsequently to Corsica, but was ultimately compelled{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to seek an asylum in London, where he died in obscurity in 1756.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Meanwhile the Corsicans, under the command (from 1755) of the heroic{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pasquale Paoli (born in 1724 at Stretta, a village among the mountains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S.W. of Bastia; died in London in 1807), fought so successfully against{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the <iinoese, that the latter lost the whole island with the exception of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bastia. By the Treaty of Compiegne in 1768 Genoa ceded Corsica to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the French, who however were still strenuously opposed by Paoli and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other leaders, and were unable thoroughly to assert their supremacy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}until 1774. After the French Revolution Paoli returned from England{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to Corsica, after an exile of 20 years, and became president of the is-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ind. Internal dissensions, however, again springing up, the English were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}invited by Paoli to his aid, and in 1794, under Hood, conquered the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}island. In 1796 they were compelled to abandon their conquest, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}since that period Corsica has belonged to France.{% endraw %}

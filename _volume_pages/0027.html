{% raw %}CLIMATE.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XX1U{% endraw %}
{% raw %}XII. Climate. Winter Stations. Seaside Resorts. Health,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Dr. Hermann Reimer.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is a common error on the part of those who visit Italy for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}first time to believe that beyond the Alps the skies are always blue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the breezes always balmy. It is true that the traveller who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has crossed the Spliigen, the Brenner, or the St. Gotthard in winter,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and finds himself in the district of the N. Italian lakes, cannot fail{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to remark what an admirable barrier against the wind is afforded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the central Chain of the Alps. The average winter-temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}here is 37-40° Fahr. as compared with 28-32° on the N. side of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mountains. Places nestling close to the S. base of the Alps, such{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as Arco, Cadenabbia, Lugano, and Pallanza, thus form an excellent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intermediate stage between the bleak winter of N. Europe and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}semi-tropical climate of the Riviera or S. Italy. A peculiarity of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}climate here is afforded by the torrents of rain which may be ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pected about the equinoctial period. The masses of warm and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}moisture - laden clouds driven northwards by the S. wind break{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against the Alpine chain, and discharge themselves in heavy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}showers, which fill the rivers and occasion the inundations from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which Lombardy not unfrequently suffers. If, however, the trav¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eller continues his journey towards the S. through the plain of Lom¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bardy he again enters a colder and windy region. The whole plain{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Po, enclosed by snow-capped mountains, exhibits a climate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of a thoroughly continental character; the summer is as hot as that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Sicily, while the winter is extremely cold, the mean temperature{% endraw %}
{% raw %}being below 35° Fahr. or about equal to that of the lower Rhine.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Changes of weather, dependent upon the direction of the wind, are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequent; and the humidity of the atmosphere, occasioned in part{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the numerous canals and rice-marshes, is also very considerable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A prolonged residence in Turin or Milan should therefore be avoided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by invalids, while even robust travellers should be on their guard{% endraw %}
{% raw %}against the trying climate. As we approach the Adriatic Sea the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}climate of the Lombard plain loses its continental character and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}approximates more closely to that of the rest of the peninsula. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}climatic peculiarities of Venice are described at p. 232.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}As soon as we cross the mountains which bound the S. margin{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Lombard plain and reach the Mediterranean coast, we find a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remarkable change in the climatic conditions. Here an almost un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interrupted series of winter-resorts extends along the coast from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hyeres to Genoa: and thence to Leghorn, and these are rapidly in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}creasing both in number and popularity. The cause of the mild{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and pleasant climate at these places is not far to seek. The Maritime{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alps and the Ligurian Apennines form such an admirable screen{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the N., that the cold N. winds which pass these mountains do{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not touch the district immediately at their feet, but are first per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceptible on the sea 6-10 M. from the coast. It is of no unfrequent{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occurrence in the Riviera that the harbours are perfectly smooth{% endraw %}

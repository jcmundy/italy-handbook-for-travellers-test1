{% raw %}the seventeen statues of the far-famed ancient **Group of Niobe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with her seven sons and seven daughters and their pedagogue, who{% endraw %}

{% raw %}This group probably once adorned the pediment of a temple (per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}haps of Apollo), the unhappy mother occupying the centre of the group,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with her slain and expiring children and their pedagogue on either{% endraw %}
{% raw %}side. The statues, which appear to have been copied from a work{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Scopas or Praxiteles (4th cent. B. C.), were found at Rome outside{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Porta S. Paolo in 1683 and placed by Cardinal Ferdinand de' Medici{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in his villa on the Monte Pincio (now the property of the French Academy),{% endraw %}

{% raw %}regarding the proper arrangement of the group, especially as statues{% endraw %}
{% raw %}belonging to the group were found elsewhere and some of those found{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Rome are in duplicate.{% endraw %}

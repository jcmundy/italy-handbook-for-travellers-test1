{% raw %}IlBargello. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 401{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Immediately on the right in the Via Proconsolo rises the Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}del Podesta, commonly known as *I1 Bargello (PL F, 5), begun in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1255, and from 1261 the residence of the Podesta, or chief ma¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gistrate of Florence. The building was repeatedly damaged by fire{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and water during the riots of the 14th cent., but was afterwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}restored and strengthened. From the end of the 16th cent, down{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to 1859 it served as a prison and seat of the head of the police{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Bargello). The oldest part of the building, towards the Via Pro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}consolo, is of ashlar, the upper story (added in 1332) and the ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tension towards the E. are of rough, unhewn stone. Between 1859{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and 1865 the imposing structure was judiciously restored and fitted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}up for the new *National Museum, illustrative of the mediaeval and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern history of Italian culture and art. Part of the collection,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which is still in course of formation, belongs to the state, and part{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to private individuals, and it is therefore by no means uniformly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valuable. It contains several admirable works, such as the Renais¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sance bronzes formerly in the Uffizi and the Palazzo Vecchio (comp.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}p. xlviii). Entrance in the Via Ghibellina, daily 10-4, 1 fr.; on Sun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}days, gratis. No catalogue has yet been published. The most im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}portant works are labelled with the masters' names.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Ground Floor, contains a valuable collection of weapons{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly in the possession of the Medici, comprising many pieces{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of great worth and beauty. To the right, an interesting monster{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannon in bronze, cast in 1638 by Cosimo Cenci; in the middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cabinet, wheel-lock muskets inlaid with ivory; in the last cabinet,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}helmet and shield of Francis I. of France, of Milan workmanship.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The room in the tower contains several suits of armour and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Turkish saddle.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We then enter the picturesque Court , embellished with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}armorial bearings of former Podesta's, and forming with its fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colonnades and flight of steps an eloquent picture of the spirit of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 14th century. The colonnades contain a fountain and a marble{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door of the 15th cent.; the walls are painted with the armorial{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bearings of the different quarters of the town. The small colonnade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite the tower-room contains a portal of the Palazzo Pazzi by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Donatello, the 'Marzocco' (see p. 376) of the same artist, figures of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saints in the style of the Pisani, and a copy of the Grinder in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Uffizi (p. 382). — The Staircase, halfway up which is a triumphal{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Trent (685 ft.), or Trento, Lat. Tridentum, with 19,600 inhab.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formerly the wealthiest and most important town in the Tyrol,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}founded according to tradition by the Etruscans, and mentioned by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Strabo , Pliny, and Ptolemy, possesses numerous towers, palaces of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble, dilapidated castles, and broad streets, and bears the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}press of an important Italian town. The Piazza del Duomo in par¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ticular presents a very imposing appearance.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The *Cathedral, founded in 1048, begun in its present form in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1212, and completed at the beginning of the 15th cent., is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Romanesque church surmounted by two domes. The portal, as at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Botzen (p. 43), is adorned with a pair of lions. In the S. transept{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are several old monuments , half-faded frescoes , and on the wall{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the porphyry tombstone of the Venetian general Sanseverino,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom the citizens of Trent defeated and killed at Calliano (p. 47){% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1487. In the Piazza of the cathedral, which is embellished with{% endraw %}

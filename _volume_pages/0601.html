{% raw %}In the midst of a wood about 6 M. to the N. of Fiesole and a little{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right of the road from Florence to Bologna, near the village of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Macioli, lies Pratolino, a villa belonging to the former grand-dukes. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chateau built by Buontalenti at the instance of Francesco de' Medici, son{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Duke Cosimo I., for the reception of Bianca Capello (p. 428) has long{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been in ruins; and almost the only relic of its former splendour is a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}colossal crouching figure (62 ft. high) representing the Apennine Mts.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the way back we may visit the Badia di Fiesole, 1/'i M.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the W. of S. Domenico (p. 447), a monastery founded in 1028,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}occupied first by Benedictine, afterwards by Augustinian monks.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was re-erected by Brunelleschi in 1462, by order of Cosimo the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Elder, and forms a remarkably attractive pile of buildings. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*Loggia near the garden, and the charming view from the garden{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the peaceful valley, to Florence, are noteworthy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Church, with a transept, but destitute of aisles, is covered with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}circular vaulting, and is of noble proportions throughout. The part of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the facade which is decorated with black and white marble belongs to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the older structure, and is older than S. Miniato (p. 443). In the in¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terior are several tombstones of the celebrated families of the Salviati,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Marucelli, Doni, etc. — The Refectory contains a quaint fresco by Gio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vanni da San Giovanni (d. 1636), representing angels ministering to Christ{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the wilderness; the reading-desk is by Brunelleschi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The monastery, which was highly favoured by the Medici, was fre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quently the residence of members of the 'Platonic Academy'. Pico of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mirandola here worked at his exposition of Genesis. After the sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pression of the monastery (1778), the printing-office of the learned Fran¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cesco Inghirami, where a number of important works were published, was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}established here. It is now occupied by a farm.{% endraw %}

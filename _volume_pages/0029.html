{% raw %}The geological character of the Riviera is also of sanitary signi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ficance. The prevailing formation is limestone, which absorbs the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sun's rays with remarkable rapidity and radiates it with equal speed,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}thus forming an important factor in making the most of the winter-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sunshine. On account of its softness it is also extensively used{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for road-making, and causes the notorious dust of the Riviera, which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}forms the chief objection to a region frequented by so many per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sons with weak lungs. The authorities of the various health-resorts,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, take great pains to mitigate this evil as far as practicable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After heavy rain the roads are apt to be very muddy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The advantages that a winter-residence in the Riviera, in contra¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}distinction to the climate of northern Europe, offers to invalids and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}delicate persons, are a considerably warmer and generally dry at¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mosphere, seldom disturbed by storms, yet fresh and pure, a more{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cheerful sky, and comparative immunity from rain. The 'invalid's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}day', or the time during which invalids may remain in the open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}air with impunity, lasts here from 10 a.m. to 4 p.m. The general{% endraw %}
{% raw %}effect of a prolonged course of open-air life in the Riviera may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}described as a gentle stimulation of the entire physical organism.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It is found particularly beneficial for convalescents, the debilitated,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the aged ; for children of scrofulous tendency ; and for the mar¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tyrs of gout and rheumatism. The climatic cure of the Riviera is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also often prescribed to patients with weak chests, to assist in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}removal of the after-effects of inflammation of the lungs or pleurisy,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or to obviate the danger of the formation of a chronic pulmonary{% endraw %}
{% raw %}discharge. The dry and frequently-agitated air of the Riviera is,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}however, by no means suitable for every patient of this kind, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the immediate vicinity of the sea is particularly unfavourable to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cases of a feverous or nervous character. The stimulating effects of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the climate are then often too powerful, producing sleeplessness{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and unwholesome irritation. The dry air of the Riviera di Ponente{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is also prejudicial to many forms of inflammation of the wind-pipe{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and bronchial tubes, which derive benefit from the air of Nervi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pisa, or Ajaccio. Cases of protracted nephritis or diabetes, on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contrary, often obtain considerable relief from a residence here.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}One of the advantages of the wintering-places on the Riviera{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is the presence of good English and German physicians, most of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whom have themselves undergone the beneficial results of a resi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dence here, and are consequently able to use their own personal ex¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}perience in giving advice as to the choice of a dwelling, the proper{% endraw %}
{% raw %}diet, and the amount of time to be spent in the open-air.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}hot, and in March it is so windy that many patients are obliged to{% endraw %}

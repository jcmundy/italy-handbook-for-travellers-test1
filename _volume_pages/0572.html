{% raw %}The church of *S. Maria Novella (PL D, 3), begun in 1278 on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the site of an earlier edifice, from designs by the Dominican monks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Fra Sisto and Fra Ristoro, and completed in 1349 by Giovanni da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Campo, is 'perhaps the purest and most elegant example of Tuscan{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gothic'. In 1456-70 it was furnished with a beautiful marble facade{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a fine portal, designed by Leon Battista Alberti, who first em¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ployed volutes here to connect the nave and aisles. A quadrant and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two concentric meridians on the right and left were constructed by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}P. Ignazio Danti in 1572. (The visitor is warned not to overlook{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the two steps halfway up the church.) — The closed pointed ar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cades('avelli') of black and white marble which adjoin the church on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, were originally constructed from designs by Brunelleschi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but were frequently altered at subsequent periods, and have re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cently been restored. The best view of the mediaeval building,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with its campanile, is obtained from the N.E. side.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The spacious Interior, a Latin cross with pointed vaulting, consists of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nave and aisles resting on twelve slender pillars, to which chapels were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards added by Vasari and others. It is 325 ft. long and 93 ft. wide;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the transept is 202 ft. in length. The unequal distances between the pil¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lars, varying from 37 ft. to 49 ft., are an unexplained peculiarity.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Entrance Wall : over the central door, a crucifix in the style of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giotto; on the right the Trinity with the Virgin and St. John, one of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}best works of Masaccio; on the left, Annunciation, fresco of the 14th cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tury. — The altar-pieces in the Right Aisle are of the 17th cent.; 6th{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar to the right, Resuscitation of a child, by Ligozzi. — In the Right{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Transept, to the right, bust of St. Antoninus; above, monument of bishop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Aliotti (d. 1336) by Tino di Camaino; farther on, the Gothic monument of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Patriarch Joseph of Constantinople (d. 1440), who died while attending{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the great Council of 1439, which was first held at Ferrara in 1438, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}afterwards at Florence, with a view to the union of the Western and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Eastern churches. Above the monument is a Madonna by Nino Pisano.{% endraw %}

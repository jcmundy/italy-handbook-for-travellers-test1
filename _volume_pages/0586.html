{% raw %}Palazzo Pitti. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 437{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'La Bella di Titiano' at the Pitti is one of Titian's likenesses in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}every feature tells of high lineage and distinction. The pose, the look,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the dress are all noble. We may presume that the name was accepted for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}want of a better. The face was so winning that it lurked in Titian's{% endraw %}
{% raw %}memory, and passed as a type into numerous canvases in which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painter tried to realize an ideal of loveliness. . . . The tones are har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monised and thrown into keeping by a most varied use and application of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}glazings and scumblings. — C. dc C.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Parma, situated on the river Parma, a small tributary of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Po, the capital of a province (formerly a duchy), is a town of en¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tirely modern appearance, but of very ancient origin, with broad{% endraw %}
{% raw %}streets, and 45,200 inhabitants. It possesses a university founded{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1549, and the felt-hat manufactories are important.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The foundation of Parma reaches back to prehistoric time, as was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}proved in 1864 by the discovery of a lake-dwelling of the bronze period.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was afterwards occupied by the Etruscans and at a later period con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}quered first by the Gauls, and then by the Romans, and in B.C. 183 was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}erected into a Roman colony at the same time withMutina (Modena). It was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}subsequently extended by Augustus, and called Colonia Julia Augusta Parma.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Both in ancient and modern times its woollen manufactories have rendered{% endraw %}
{% raw %}it a place of some consequence. It participated in the general develop¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ment of the towns of Upper Italy, zealously espoused the cause of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Guelphs, and in 1247-48 was long unsuccessfully besieged by Emp. Freder¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ick II. In 1303 Giberto da Correggio obtained possession of the supreme{% endraw %}
{% raw %}power. In 1341, after various vicissitudes, Parma came into the hands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Visconti, and from that period down to 1512 was generally united{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the Duchy of Milan. In 1545, after it had been annexed to the States{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the Chureh, it was presented by Pope Paul III., with Piacenza, to his{% endraw %}
{% raw %}infamous son Pier Luigi Farnese. This prince was assassinated in 1557,{% endraw %}

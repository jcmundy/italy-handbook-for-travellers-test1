{% raw %}The Emilia includes the former duchies of Parma and Modena, as well{% endraw %}
{% raw %}as the papal Romagna, and is now divided into the eight provinces of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Piacenza, Parma, Reggio, Modena, Bologna, Ferrara, Ravenna, and Forli,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covering an area of 7921 sq. M., with a population of 2,187,000 souls. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dialects spoken here form the third main group of the Gallic languages{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Upper Italy, and the nasal sound of the vowels will at once strike the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traveller as indicating the original affinity of the people with the French.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Celts seem to have crossed the Alps in several different detachments.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}After the Insubri had conquered the district of Milan, and the Cenomani{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Brescia and Verona, the tribe of the Boii crossed the Po in the 5th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B.C., and subjugated the Etruscans and Umbrians who were settled to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. of that river. They chose Bologna for their capital, in the name{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of which is still preserved that of the conquerors. The Senones next inÂ¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaded Italy, and took possession of the coast-district to the S. of the Boii,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}extending nearly to Ancona. It was a horde of these Gauls who destroyed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rome in B.C. 389. About a century later Italy, united under the guidance{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Rome, began to reconquer the lost territory. In 283 the Senones were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exterminated. In 269 a colony was established at Ariminum, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constituted the strongest frontier fortress in the peninsula, and connected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with Rome by the Via Flaminia. In 224 the Boii were subjugated, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in planting the colonies of Placentia and Cremona in 218, Rome extended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}her frontier as far as the Po. This process of Latinisation was interrupted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by the invasion of Hannibal, but vigorously resumed after his defeat; and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 189 Bologna, and in 183 Modena and Parma received Roman colonies.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}M. uEmilius Lepidus, who was consul in B.C. 187, constructed a military{% endraw %}
{% raw %}road from Rimini to Piacenza, via Bologna, Modena, Reggio, and Parma,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a distance of 150 M., called the Via ^Emilia, whence the whole district{% endraw %}
{% raw %}derived the ancient name which it still retains. Down to the time of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Csesar, although the Roman language and customs had spread rapidly here,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the district was officially known as the 'Province of Gaul on this side of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Po1, and the Rubicon formed the frontier of Italy; but in B.C. 43 it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was finally united with the latter.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}part of Italy. In 404 the Emperor Honorius transferred his residence to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ravenna, which also continued to be the capital of the Gothic Kings.{% endraw %}

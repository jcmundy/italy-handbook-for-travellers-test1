{% raw %}to Colico. S. BERNARDINO. 5. Route. 41{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3872 M. Hinterrhein (5302 ft.; Post), the highest village in the valley.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The source of the Hinler-Rhein (7270ft.), which issues from the Rheinwald{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Zapport Glacier, may be reached hence in 37* hrs. The road crosses the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rhine, about 72 M. beyond the village, and then winds up the steep S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}slope of the valley, finally leading through a bleak upland glen to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Bernardino Pass (6768 ft.), which was known to the Romans, and was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}called the Vogelberg down to the 15th century. When S. Bernardino of Siena{% endraw %}
{% raw %}preached the gospel at that period in this region, a chapel was erected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the S. slope and gave its name to the pass. The small Lago Moesola{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(2 hrs. from Hinter-Rhein) lies on the summit of the pass (Inn). From{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the S. end of the lake issues the Moesa, which the road follows down to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its confluence with the-Ticino above Bellinzona. The new road descends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in windings, crossing lower down to the right bank of the Moesa.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4972 M. S. Bernardino (5335 ft.; "Hotel Brocco; Ravizza; Desteffanis),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4 M. from the summit of the pass, the highest village in the Val Mesocco,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}or Mesolcina. Several waterfalls are observed. Near S. Giacomo the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}again crosses the river, and then descends rapidly to —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}58 M. Mesocco, or Cremeo (2559 ft.; Toscani; "Desteffanis), a charming¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ly situated village, where walnut-trees, chestnuts, vines, and maize-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fields begin to indicate the Italian nature of the climate. On a rocky{% endraw %}
{% raw %}eminence to the left of the road, 72 M. below Mesocco, stand the im¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}posing ruins of the Chdteau of Mesocco with its four towers, which was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}destroyed by the inhabitants of the Grisons in 1526. Beyond (2 M.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}Soazza (2067 ft.) the bottom of the valley is reached, and the road becomes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}level. Near the second bridge below Soazza the Buffalora forms a fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cascade near the road. Near Cabbiolo is another waterfall.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}68 M. Cama (1260ft.). The next villages are Leggia and Grono, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}latter at the entrance to the Val Calanca.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}71 M. Roveredo (974 ft.; "Angelo; Croce), the capital of the lower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Val Mesocco, with the ruined castle of the Trivulzio family.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Vittore (882ft.) is the last village in the Grisons, Lumino the first{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the Canton Ticino. On this side the bridge over the Moesa the road{% endraw %}
{% raw %}unites with the St. Gotthard route (p. 35). Below the confluence of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Moesa and the Ticino lies Arbedo, where a battle was fought in 1422 be¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tween the Milanese and the Swiss, in which 2000 of the latter fell.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}7672 M. Bellinzona, a station on the St. Gotthard Railway, see p. 35.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}6. From Innsbruck to Verona by the Brenner.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}166 M. Railway in 9-12 hrs.; express fares 39 fr. 55, 29 fr. 30 c.; ordi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nary 33 fr. 50, 24 fr. 95, 16 fr. 80 c Views on the right as far as the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}summit of the Brenner. Information as to through-tickets, which are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paid for in Italian money, see Introd. vii.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Brenner, the lowest pass over the principal chain of the Alps, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}traversed by the oldest of the Alpine routes, which was used as early as{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Roman period, and rendered practicable for carriages in 1772. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}railway, opened in 1867, one of the grandest modern works of the kind,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}affords the most direct communication between S. E. Germany and Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Within a distance of 78 M. the line is carried through 22 tunnels, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}over 60 large and a number of smaller bridges. The greatest incline,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1:40, is between Innsbruck and the culminating point.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Innsbruck (1912 ft.; *Tiroler Hof; *Europdischer Hof, both{% endraw %}
{% raw %}near the station; Goldene Sonne, Goldener Adler, in the town;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hirsch, second-class), see Baedeker's Eastern Alps. The train{% endraw %}
{% raw %}passes the Abbey of Wilten (on the right) and penetrates the hill{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Isel by a tunnel 750 yds. in length. It then passes through an¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}other tunnel, and crosses to the right bank of the Sill, on which it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ascends. On the S. rises the Waldraster-Spitze (8907 ft.). Five{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tunnels. Beyond (5 M.) Patsch (2550 ft.), the valley becomes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}narrower and wilder. Four more tunnels. The Sill is crossed twice.{% endraw %}

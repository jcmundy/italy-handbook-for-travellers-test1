{% raw %}The Road from Mentone to Nice, 19 M. (by carr. in 3 hrs.; 25-30fv.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}2-3 fr. gratuity), the so-called '■Route de la Corniche\ traverses the most{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful part of the Riviera, and is far preferable to the railway. It ascends{% endraw %}
{% raw %}through the most luxuriant vegetation , and commands a charming retro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spect of Mentone and the coast as far as Bordighera. Then, as the top of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the first hill is gained, a view of Monaco (see below), to which a road de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}scends to the left. To the right of the road, higher up, Roccabruna (see below){% endraw %}
{% raw %}is visible. Then Turbia with its huge Roman tower, now a mere shell, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}remains of the Tropaea Augusti (whence the name 'Turbia'), erected to com¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}memorate the subjugation of the Ligurian tribes (A.D. 13). Another very{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful view is enjoyed here. To the E. the wild mountains and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}entire coast from Ventimiglia to Bordighera ; W. (view in this direction{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from a point a few steps above the tower) the Mediterranean, the French{% endraw %}
{% raw %}coast near Antibes, the island of St. Marguerite, the Montagnes de l'Es-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}terel, and other distant coast-hills. The road now enters a bleak moun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tain-district. On the left is Eza (p. 110), a group of grey and venerable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}houses with a white campanile, perched on an isolated rock rising abruptly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the valley. The culminating point of the road is now reached,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and the wooded promontory of St. Jean (p. 116), Beaulieu (p. 116), and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Villafranca (p. 116) become visible. Beyond these a view is obtained of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the beautiful valley of Nice (p. 110), with its villas, monasteries, villages,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and green hills.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}exploits, exercise sovereign rights, but the customs and post-office{% endraw %}

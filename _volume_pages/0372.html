{% raw %}Pal. of the Doges. VENICE. 40. Route. 245{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Corridor contains a bust of the Emp. Francis. — The Sala dello{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Serutinio, or Voting Hall, is decorated similarly to the preceding saloon.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the frieze are the portraits of 39 doges, down to Lodovico Manin (1797).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the wall of the entrance: "Last Judgment, by Palma Giovane. On the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left wall, towards the Piazzetta: 1. Victory of the Venetians over King{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Roger of Sicily in 1148; 2. Subjugation of Tyre under Domenico Michieli{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1125; 3. (over the door to the balcony, which affords a good survey{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Sansovino's library), Victory of Dom. Michieli over the Turks at Jaffa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1123; 4. Victory in the lagoons over Pipin, son of Charlemagne, in 811;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5. Siege of Venice by Pipin in 809.— Opposite the entrance: Monument to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Doge Francesco Morosini 'Peloponnesiacus1, who in 1684-90 conquered the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Morea and Athens (p. 234). — On the right wall: 6. Lazaro Mocenigo conquers{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Turks near the Dardanelles in 1657; 7. (over the window towards the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}court), Destruction of Margaritino in 1571; 8. Battle of Lepanto, in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}same year; 9. (over the second window), Conquest of Cattaro in Dalmatia{% endraw %}
{% raw %}during the war against Genoa in 1378; 10. Re-capture of Zara in 1346. —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the ceiling several other scenes from the history of the Republic.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The celebrated Library of St. Mark, containing many rare MSS. and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}beautiful miniatures, and also some ancient cameos (two heads of Zeus),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is open to the public daily from 10 to 3 (Sundays and holidays excepted).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Among its chief treasures is the "Breviario Grimani, embellished with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}miniatures by Hans Memling (?), Lievin de Witte, and other Netherlandish{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painters of the 15th century. — The extensive and choice Collection of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Coins is shown by special permission only.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II. The Archaeological Museum, established in 1846 in the apart¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ments in which the doges resided till the close of the 16th cent., contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ancient sculptures in marble, of Greek and Roman origin, most of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were brought home as booty by the Venetians from their various campaigns.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}I. Room: 94. One of the Dioscuri, a portrait-statue; 90. Colossal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Minerva; "85. Bacchus and a satyr; "80. Apollo reposing; 70, 68. Elegant{% endraw %}
{% raw %}candelabra-bases, with armed cupids; "51, 56. Muses from the Amphitheatre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pola; 46. Dancing Silenus; 35. Cupid bending his bow; 32. Boy with a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}goose, a fountain-figure; 29. Venus and Cupid.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}II. Room: 169. Hermaphrodite, fragment of a spirited group; "148.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Rape of Ganymede, freely restored; "153. Gaul sinking from exhaustion:{% endraw %}
{% raw %}*145. Dead Gaul lying on his shield; "144. Gaul, in his last desperate{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struggle; these three resemble the Dying Gladiator in the Capitol at Rome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and similar statues at Naples, and probably belonged to the groups de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dicated to the Acropolis of Athens by Attalus, King of Pergamos, about{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B.C. 239, after his victory at Sardes in Asia Minor over the invading{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gauls; 138. Leda with the swan; 133. Apollo; 113, 187. Two comic masks;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}102. Cupid bending his bow, in Parian marble. — The chimney-piece dates{% endraw %}
{% raw %}from the end of the 15th century.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}III. Room : old maps; among them the celebrated "Map of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}World by the Camaldulensian monk Fra Mauro, 1457-59; six tablets of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}carved wood by Haji Mehemet of Tunis (1559), representing the globe ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Plans of Venice of 1500 and 1728. The next room is entered by a door{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to the right. — IV. Room: 190. Warrior sacrificing; 195. Fragment of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sarcophagus, rape of Proserpine; 196. Another with the destruction of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}children of Niobe; 220. Greek tomb-relief; 222. Centaurs fighting, and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}female Centaur asleep; 231. Fragment of a Greek frieze, battle of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Greeks and Trojans around the ships; 239. Four-sided base of a candelabrum.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now return to the 3rd room, from which the next is entered to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}right. — V. Room; chiefly busts of emperors, the best being 292. Vitel-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lius; 250,299. Bacchantes; 245. Replica of the archaic Diana at Naples.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}— To gain admission to the last rooms (closed), which contain interesting{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bronzes, application must be made to the director.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Upper Floor contains the apartments in which the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}authorities of the republic once held their meetings, and which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are still in a great measure preserved in their ancient splendour.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}xii TRAVELLING EXPENSES. MONEY.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Best Money for the Tour. Circular Notes, obtainable at the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal English banks, form the proper medium for the transport{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of large sums, and realise the most favourable exchange. English{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and German banknotes also realise more than their nominal value.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A moderate supply of French Gold will also be found desirable.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sovereigns are received at the full value (about 26-28 fr.) by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal hotel-keepers, but not in out-of-the-way places.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Exchange. Foreign money is most advantageously changed in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the larger towns, either at one of the English bankers or at a re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spectable money-changer's (^cambiavaluta^. As a rule, those money¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}changers are the most satisfactory who publicly exhibit a list of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}current rates of exchange. The traveller should always be provided{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with an abundant supply of small notes (1, 2, and 5 fr.), as it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}often difficult to change those of large amount. When a railway-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fare has to be paid it is a wise precaution to be provided with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}exact sum beforehand in order that mistakes or imposition may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}prevented. Besides the small notes, l-l1^ fr. in copper should also{% endraw %}
{% raw %}be carried in a separate pocket or pouch.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Money Orders payable in Italy, for sums not exceeding 10?., are now{% endraw %}
{% raw %}granted by the English Post Office at the following rates: up to 21., §d.;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bl., Is. Gd.; 11., 2s. 3d.; 10Z., 3s. These are paid in gold. The identity{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the receiver must sometimes be guaranteed by two well-known resi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dents , but an exhibition of the passport often suffices. The charge for{% endraw %}
{% raw %}money-orders granted in Italy and payable in England is 40c. per ll. sterling.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}A convenient and safe method of carrying money for a journey in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Italy is afforded by the Libretti di Recognizione Postale, which may be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}procured at the post-offices of the principal Italian towns for any sum{% endraw %}
{% raw %}not exceeding 10,000 fr. (400?.). The holder of one of these small books{% endraw %}
{% raw %}may then draw what sum he requires (from 200 fr. upwards) at any post-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}office in the kingdom, until the amount for which the book is issued has{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been exhausted. In case of loss the traveller should immediately inform{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the postal authorities, giving his name and the number of the book, when{% endraw %}
{% raw %}measures will at once be taken to stop payment.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}il. Period and Plan of Tour.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Season. As a general rule the spring and autumn months are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the best season for a tour in N. Italy, especially May and Sep¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tember, before or after the heat of summer has attained its climax.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Winter in Lombardy and Piedmont is generally a much colder{% endraw %}
{% raw %}season than it is in England, but Nice and the whole of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Riviera, Pisa , and Venice afford pleasant and sheltered quarters.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The height of summer can hardly be recommended for travelling.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The scenery, indeed, is then in perfection, and the long days are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hailed with satisfaction by the enterprising traveller; but the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fierce rays of an Italian sun seldom fail to impair the physical and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mental energies. This result is not occasioned so much by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}intensity as by the protracted duration of the heat, the sky being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frequently cloudless and not a drop of rain falling for many weeks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in succession. The heat generally moderates about the end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}August, when the first showers of autumn begin to refresh the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}parched atmosphere.{% endraw %}

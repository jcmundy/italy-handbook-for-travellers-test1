{% raw %}The *View from the highest gallery of the dome (368 ft. above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sea; 119 steps to the first gallery, thence to the top 130, ascended{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by an easy and well-lighted staircase), embraces the city, harbour,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and fortifications, and the well-peopled coast (W. the Riviera di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ponente, E. the Riviera di Lev ante), bounded on the S. by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vast blue expanse of the Mediterranean. (Sacristan 25 c.; his atten¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dance for the ascent is unnecessary; best light in the morning.){% endraw %}
{% raw %}From the Piazza Nuova we proceed to the left through the Via{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Sellai (PL F, 4) to the Piazza Deferrari (with a palace of that{% endraw %}
{% raw %}name, of the 18th cent., on the left), formerly Piazza S. Domenico{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(79 ft. above the sea).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From this piazza the Salita di S. Matteo, the second side-street to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the left, leads to the small church of S. Matteo (PL 14), originally{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Gothic (1278), which contains numerous reminiscences of the Doria fa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}mily, the facade being covered with inscriptions to their memory. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}interior was altered in 1530 by the Florentine Giov. Angelo Montorsoli, who{% endraw %}
{% raw %}was invited to Genoa by Andrea Doria, and who, with his assistants,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed the whole of the fine sculptures with which the church is embel¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lished. The balustrade of the organ-loft is especially good. Above the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}high-altar is Doria's sword. To the left, of the church are handsome{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cloisters with double columns in the Transition style, dating from 1308-10,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with 17 ancient inscriptions relating to the Dorias, and remains of two{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statues of Andrea Doria, which were mutilated during the Revolution in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1797. An ancient sarcophagus-relief, with an inscription in honour of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lamba Doria, who defeated the Venetians at Curzola in 1297, has been{% endraw %}
{% raw %}built into the right side of the facade. — The small piazza in front of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the church is surrounded with palaces of the Doria family; one nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}opposite, the lower half of which is covered with black and yellow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble, bears the inscription, iSenat. Cons. Andreae de Oria, patriae{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liberatori munus publicum1 (1528).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the right in the Piazza Deferrari is situated the Teatro Carlo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Felice (PL 36), built in 1826-28 (see p. 87). Adjacent is the —{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Accademia delle Belle Arti (PL 1; F,4). The vestibule below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains medieval sculptures from the suppressed church of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Domenico. On the first floor is the Biblioteca Civica, well stocked{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with modern works (about 40,000 vols. ; open daily), and on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}second floor a Picture Gallery (shown by the custodian).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The copying-room, to the right, contains a coloured marble relief of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 15th cent, and a Coronation of the Virgin by Luca della Robbia. In{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the room to the left are mediaeval reliefs, Renaissance sculptures (door¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}frame, chimney-piece), and casts. The room to the right of the copying-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}room contains ancient pictures, still unarranged, some of them only being{% endraw %}
{% raw %}numbered, chiefly by German and early Netherlands masters. The finest{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are: 69. Last Supper; 19. St. Anthony; *20. Two saints; 68, 97, 99. Mi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}racles of St. Philip. Then 28 (9). Manfredino da Pistoja (1292), Annun¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ciation, Christ in the house of Martha; 21. Umbrian School, Crucifixion;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ant. Scarini, Entombment; L. Cambiaso, Holy Family. In the centre{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modern statues. Next a circular room and a saloon with large pictures{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Genoese painters (Piola, Deferrari, Ferrari, Fiasella, etc.), and lastly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two rooms with paintings, terracottas, bronzes, etc., chiefly modern (Museo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Principe Odone).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Environs of Lucca are beautiful, and many of the pleasant villas{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are comfortably furnished for the reception of strangers, but in summer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the country is hot and destitute of shade.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The traveller should visit the (3 M.) royal :Villa di Marlia, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its beautiful grounds, fine points of view, and fountains, resembling Marly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at, Paris (whence the name), and with a Greek chapel containing old{% endraw %}
{% raw %}paintings, etc. (permission must be obtained at Lucca). The road thither{% endraw %}
{% raw %}leads by the Porta S. Maria (see below).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the road to Viareggio, about 6 M. to the W., near the Lake of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Massaciuccoli, are situated the Roman ruins known as the * Bagni di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Nerone, with beautiful environs. — The Aqueduct to the S. of Lucca, with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its 459 arches, recalling the Campagna of Rome, is worthy of notice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}About 15J/2 M. to the N. of Lucca, in a hilly district, lie the Baths{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Lucca (diligence from June to September several times daily in 2!/2{% endraw %}
{% raw %}hrs. , fare 3 fr.; carr. in 2 hrs., fare 15 fr.). We quit the town by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Porta S. Maria (PI. E, 1). The excellent road was constructed by the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Princess Elisa. A road to the right diverges to the Villa Marlia (see above).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Farther on we reach the Serchio, a stream which is generally very low{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in summer, but sometimes swells to a violent torrent, and is confined by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}embankments for a long distance. The road ascends the left bank of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the stream, and passes the village of Moriano. We now traverse charm¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing hill-country, passing the opposite villages of Val d'Ottavo, Diecimo,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Borgo a Mozzano. Immediately above Borgo is the bridge della Mad-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dalena, which is said to have been built in 1322 by Castruccio; it is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sometimes called the Ponte del Diavolo, from the peculiarity of its con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}struction, and is hardly practicable for carriages. About 1 M. beyond it{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the road enters the valley of the Lima, another stream which is nearly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dry in summer, and which is crossed near Farnoli by a suspension-bridge{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed in 1860. Between this point and the baths there are roads{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on both banks of the river.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}*Vittore Carpaccio, Nine scenes from the legend of St. Ursula,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}painted in 1490-95 for the Scuola di S. Ursula in Venice.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}539. The ambassadors of the pagan king of England bring to King{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Maurus, father of S. Ursula, the proposals of their master for the hand{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of his daughter; 533. S. Ursula's vision; 537. The ambassadors depart{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the answer that the bride desired the postponement of the marriage{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for three years, in order to make a pilgrimage to Rome; 549. Return of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the ambassadors to England and their report to the king; 542. Double{% endraw %}
{% raw %}picture, representing the Departure of the English monarch, who has re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}solved to share in the pilgrimage, and his Meeting with Ursula (on ship¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}board) ; 546. Ursula, her companions, and the prince receive the blessing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pope Cyriacus; 544. Arrival of S. Ursula at Cologne; 554. Martyrdom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the saint and her virgins, who are pierced with arrows; 560. Apo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theosis of S. Ursula. — The style in which the legend is narrated is al¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most too simple, but interesting on account of the admirable perspective{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and faithful rendering of real life. The traveller who has visited Belgium{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cannot fail to compare this work with the celebrated shrine of S. Ursula{% endraw %}
{% raw %}at Bruges, painted by Hans Memling about the same time (1489) for the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Hospital of St. John there. The execution of the northern artist is ten¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}der and graceful, almost like miniature-painting, while the extensive{% endraw %}
{% raw %}canvases of his Venetian contemporary are vigorous, almost coarse in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}character.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}*S. Apollinare in Classe, erected in 534 by Julianas Argentarius{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the site of a temple of Apollo, consecrated in 549, and restored{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in 1779. This is the most imposing of the basilicas still existing at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ravenna. It consists of a nave and aisles, with a vestibule at the W.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end, and a round campanile. The exterior exhibits traces of an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}attempt to relieve the surfaces of the walls with pilasters and arches.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The spacious Interior rests on twenty-four cipollino columns, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}has an open roof. The Walls are adorned with portraits of bishops and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}archbishops of Ravenna, an unbroken series of 126, from the first bishop{% endraw %}
{% raw %}St. Apollinaris, who suffered martyrdom in 74 under Vespasian, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present archbishop. Each aisle contains four marble sarcophagi of arch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bishops. In the left aisle is an inscription relating to the penance per¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}formed here by Emp. Otho III. at the instigation of St. Romualdo. Ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jacent is an ancient capital used as a holy water basin. At the end of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the aisle is a tabernacle of the 9th cent., with an altar of the 14th cen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tury. — The Nave contains a marble altar in the ancient style. — The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Crypt, a species of corridor in which the remains of St. Apollinaris once{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reposed, is in winter sometimes under water. The bronze window-gratings{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are ancient. — Above the crypt is the broad flight of steps leading to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Tribuxa1 with the high-altar. The canopy of the latter is borne by four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}columns of black and white Oriental marble. The dome of the tribuna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is adorned with well-preserved '-'Mosaics of the 6th cent.: in the centre a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}large cross on a blue ground with gilded stars, at the sides Moses and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Elias, below whom is St. Apollinaris preaching to his flock; below, on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the right, are the sacrifices of Abel and Melchisedech; on the left, Con-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stantine and other Roman emperors, among whom are the four arch¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bishops Ursicinus, St. Ursus, St. Severus, and Ecclesius. — The Arch of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Choir is also embellished with mosaics: in the centre a bust of Christ, at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the sides the emblems of the four evangelists, and below them two flocks{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of sheep hastening to Christ from the towns of Jerusalem and Bethlehem.{% endraw %}

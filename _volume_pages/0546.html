{% raw %}Cathedral. FLORENCE. 53. Route. 397{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'Basrelief of the glorified Madonna, over the door, is ascribed to Nanni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Antonio di Banco. The mosaic is by Domenico and Davide Ghirlandajo (1496).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Interior, though somewhat bare, is very impressive owing to its{% endraw %}
{% raw %}grand dimensions. The gallery detracts from the effect of the arches. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}choir, instead of being at the end of the church, is appropriately placed{% endraw %}
{% raw %}under the dome.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}On the entrance-wall to the right, an equestrian statue of John Hawks-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wood (d. 1394), an English soldier-of-fortune who served the Republic in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1392, by Paolo Uccelli; to the left, the monument of Nic. Marucci da To-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lentino (d. 1434), by Andrea del Castagno. Over the principal portal: Coro¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nation of the Virgin in mosaic, by Gaddo Gaddi; at the sides frescoes{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(angels) by Santi di Tito, restored in 1842. — The designs for the arches{% endraw %}
{% raw %}were drawn by Lor. Ghiberti; the design of the coloured mosaic pavement{% endraw %}
{% raw %}is attributed to Baccio d'Agnolo and Francesco da Sangallo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right Aisle. Monument of Fiiippo Brunelleschi, with his portrait in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}marble, by his pupil Buggiano. Monument of Giannozzo Manetti, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}statesman and scholar, by Ciuffagni; (v.) Bust, of Giotto by Benedetto da{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Majano (1490), with a fine inscription by Angelo Poliziano; (1.) on the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pillar a fine receptacle for holy water by Arnolfo or Giotto, (v.) Monument{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of General Pietro Farnese (over the door), by Agnolo Gaddi and Pesello{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(1395); farther on, King Hezekiah, by Nanni d'Antonio di Banco. Bust of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the learned Marsilius Ficinus, by A. Ferrucci (1521). Over the following{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door (r.) the Mausoleum of Antonio Orso, Bishop of Florence, by Tinodi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Camaino of Siena, with the figure of the deceased in a sitting posture.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the pillar of the dome, towards the nave, St. Matthew, a statue by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Vincenzo de' Rossi; opposite to it, St. James, by Jacopo da Sansovino (1524).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Right Transept: (r.) St. Philip, (1.) St. James the Great, by Giovanni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bandini. Each of the four side-chapels is adorned with two saints, painted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}al fresco by Bicci di Lorenzo (1427). The stained-glass windows are from{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designs by A. Gaddi. — Over the door of the Sacristy a bas-relief (Ascen¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sion) by the Robbia's. In the sacristy, St. Michael by Lor. di Credi (1523),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and two angels by Luca della Robbia.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Nave, E. branch, statues of (r.) St. John and (1.) St. Peter by Bene¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}detto da Rovezzano. 1st Chapel: St. Luke, by Nanni di Banco (1415); in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the 2nd Chapel, St. John, by Donatello. Below the altar of the Tribuna is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the chapel of St. Zenobius, with a reliquary containing the remains of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}saint, in bronze, by Lorenzo Ghiberti (1440). The stained-glass windows are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}also from designs by Ghiberti. Last Supper on a golden ground, painted{% endraw %}
{% raw %}'a tempera' by Giovanni Balducci. On the right St. Matthew by Niccolb{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Arezzo; on the left, St. Mark, by Bernardo di Pietro Ciuffagni (1416).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The marble screen of the octagonal Choir, designed by Giuliano di{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Baccio d' Agnolo, and adorned with basreliefs by Bandinelli (with the initials{% endraw %}
{% raw %}B. B. and date 1555) and his pupil Giovanni delV Opera , was erected to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}replace the original wooden screen of Ghiberti. Behind the high-altar{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an unfinished group (Pieta) by Michael Angelo. — The paintings in the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}octagonal dome, begun in 1572 by Vasari, and continued by Federigo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Zuccheri (prophets, etc.), diminish its impressive effect. The windows in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the drum of the dome were executed by Bernardo di Francesco from de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}signs by Ghiberti (Presentation in the Temple), Donatello (Coronation of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Virgin), and Uccello (Adoration of the Magi).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bronze door of the N. Sacristy by Luca della Robbia, aided by Maso{% endraw %}
{% raw %}di Bartolommeo and Michelozzo (1467). Above it a basrelief in terracotta{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(Resurrection) by the Robbia. The intarsia cabinets in this sacristy were{% endraw %}
{% raw %}executed from designs by Finiguerra and Baldovinetti. The *Putti, or figu¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}res of children on the cabinets, are by Donatello. Near the door, to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}left, is a fine marble fountain. In this sacristy Lorenzo de' Medici sought{% endraw %}
{% raw %}refuge in 1478, on the outbreak of the conspiracy of the Pazzi, to which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}his brother Julian fell a victim, while attending mass in the choir.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}Andrew and St. Thomas by Andrea Ferrucci. In the chapels, frescoes by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Lorenzo di Bicci. The ten stained-glass windows are by Lor. Ghiberti. In{% endraw %}

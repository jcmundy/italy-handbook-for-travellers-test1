{% raw %}the dynasty of the Medici'. — The population amounts to upwards{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of 80,000, or, including the suburbs, 97,700 souls (many of whom{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are Jews), exclusive of a fluctuating sea-faring community of fully{% endraw %}
{% raw %}3000. The town carries on a brisk trade with the Levant in cotton,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wool, and unbleached silk, and with the Black Sea in grain. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}most important industries are the manufactures of coral ornaments{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and oil.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Leghorn, which is a well built, thoroughly modern place, con¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tains little to detain the traveller. The Harbour is a very busy{% endraw %}
{% raw %}spot. The inner harbour (Porto Vecchio, or Mediceo) is too shallow{% endraw %}
{% raw %}to admit vessels of large tonnage; the Porto Nuovo was therefore{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed during the present century, to the W. of the old har¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bour, and protected from the open sea by a semicircular mole.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Picturesque glimpses are obtained hence of the sea with the islands{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Elba, Gorgona, and Capraja. An excursion by boat will be{% endraw %}
{% raw %}found pleasant in fine weather (IV2 fr- Per hr., bargain necessary).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}By the harbour is the Statue of the Grand Duke Ferdinand I. (PL{% endraw %}
{% raw %}10), by Giov. delV Opera, with four Turkish slaves ('I quattro{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Mori') in bronze by Pietro Tacca. On the pier, which is 500 yds.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in length, rises the Lighthouse {Faro or Lanterna; PL B, 3), the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}platform of which affords a good survey of the town, harbour, and sea.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The town is intersected by canals, and connected by a navi¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gable canal with the Arno, the influx of which is 7 M. to the N.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Corso Vittorio Emmanuele, the principal street (PL D, E, 3),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}contains tempting shops, where objects in coral, scagliolo (an{% endraw %}
{% raw %}imitation of mosaic), Oriental shawls, etc., may be purchased at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}reasonable prices. It leads from the harbour to the spacious Piazza{% endraw %}
{% raw %}d'Armi (PL D, 3), in which the cathedral, the town-hall (PL 13),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and a small palace formerly owned by the royal family, are situated.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It proceeds thence to the Piazza Carlo Alberto (PL E, 3) , adorned{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with colossal Statues of Ferdinand III. (d. 1824) and Leopold II.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the last but one, and the last grand-duke of Tuscany respectively.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The original inscription on the latter was replaced in 1860 by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}another to the effect that the 'dinastia Austro-Lorenese si e resa{% endraw %}
{% raw %}assolutamente incompatibile con l'ordine e la felicita. della Tos-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cana'. — The large Synagogue (PL 22), founded in 1581, dates in{% endraw %}
{% raw %}its present form from 1603. — The Protestant Cemetery contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the graves of Tobias Smollett and Francis Horner.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Walks. Pleasant grounds to the S., outside the Porta a Mare,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and along the coast by the road to Ardenza; also in the Giardino{% endraw %}
{% raw %}dei Bagni (adm. 50 c), in the same neighbourhood, where a band{% endraw %}
{% raw %}plays every evening during the bathing-season (Caffe). Farther on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are the sea-bathing establishments mentioned at p. 338, and beyond{% endraw %}
{% raw %}them Ardenza, with numerous villas. Comp. Plan B, 4, 5.{% endraw %}

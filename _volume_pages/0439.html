{% raw %}S. Francesco (PI. 7; E, 5), erected in 1494 by Pietro Benve-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}nuti, is entirely covered with domes, and each aisle is flanked with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chapels. The frieze of 'putti' and foliage, lately restored, is by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Girolamo da Carpi. 1st Chapel on the left, frescoes by Garofalo, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Donors and the Kiss of Judas. The other pictures are copies, of which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the originals are preserved in the Pinacoteca. The church contains{% endraw %}
{% raw %}monuments of the family of Este and that of Giambattista Pigna,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the secretary of Alphonso II. and rival of Tasso (a simple slab,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}outside, to the right of the entrance). A famous echo here (under{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the second dome in the nave) answers sixteen times if awakened{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with due energy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Maria in Vado (PL 11; F, 6), one of the oldest churches at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Ferrara, but altered after 1475 by Biagio Rossetti and Bartolommeo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tristani, consists of a nave divided into three parts, with a flat{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ceiling resting on ten columns, and surmounted by a dome sup¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ported by buttresses. It contains frescoes by Bononi.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}At the back of the church, No. 23, Strada della Scandiana, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Schifanoja (PL 35; F, 6), once a chateau of the Este,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and now containing a deaf and dumb institution. It was begun by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Alberto d'Este in 1391, and completed by Borso in 1469. Over the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome portal is the unicorn from Borso's armorial bearings. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}principal saloon contains *Frescoes by Cosimo Tura, Lorenzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Costa (7), and others, discovered in 1840 under the whitewash,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}representing the twelve months of the year, the signs of the zodiac,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and scenes from the life of Borso.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}To the S. of S. Maria in Vado, in the Corso Porta Romana, is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Palazzo Costabili (PL 33; F, 7), sometimes called Palazzo{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Scrofa, after its former proprietors, or Palazzo Beltrame from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}family to which it now belongs. It was erected in 1502, but is un¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}completed. Handsome court.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}About 2 M. from Ravenna, on the bank of the Ronco, rises the Co-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lonna di Gaston de Foix, a memorial of the victory gained on lilh April,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1512, by the united armies of Louis XII. of France and the Duke of Ferrara{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(at which the poet Ariosto was present) over the Spanish troops and those{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pope Julius II. At the moment when the victory was decided, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brave Gaston de Foix fell, and 20,000 men were left dead on the field.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}From Ravenna to Rimini there is a good road traversing a monotonous,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}flat district, and passing S. Apollinare in Classe, the pine-forest, the small{% endraw %}
{% raw %}town of Cervia, Cesenatico, S. Martino, and Celle, altogether a drive ol{% endraw %}
{% raw %}4'/2 hours. — Rimini, see Baedeker''s Central Italy.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}49. From Bologna to Florence.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}82 M. Railway in 4-6 hrs. (fares 14 fr. 90, 10 fr. 45, 7fr. 45 c; express{% endraw %}
{% raw %}16 fr. 40, 11 fr. 50 c). — A boldly-constructed line. Fine views of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valleys and ravines of the Apennines (generally to the left), and after¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}wards of the rich plains of Tuscany.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bologna, see p. 306. The train skirts the slopes of the Monte{% endraw %}
{% raw %}della Guardia (p. 322), near the Reno, which it soon crosses. On{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an island in the Reno, not far from Bologna, the Second Trium¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}virate was concerted by Octavian, Antony, and Lepidus, B.C. 43.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the Reno contracts. Here on 26th June, 1402, the army of Gio¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vanni Bentivoglio was defeated by Gian Galeazzo Visconti, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on 21st May, 1511, that of Pope Julius II. under the Duke of Ur-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}bino, by the French. — On the left, near (12 M.) Sasso, the brook{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Setta falls into the Reno, from which a subterranean aqueduct,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}constructed by Augustus and recently restored, leads to Bologna{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(see p. 307). — 17 M. Marzabotto, with the spacious Villa Aria.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}bouring village of Misano. Between this point andPracchia there are{% endraw %}

{% raw %}the valley expands. 29y2 M. Riola; on the left rise the abrupt{% endraw %}
{% raw %}peaks of Monte Ovolo and Monte Vigese; a landslip from the latter{% endraw %}

{% raw %}is the modernised castle of Savignono, with picturesque environs.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}37 M. Porretta (*Albergo Cavour, unpretending; Palazzino, open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in summer only), a village of 3400 inhab , with mineral springs{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and baths, is much frequented in summer. — Beyond Porretta the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}line enters a narrow and romantic ravine of the Reno, from the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}sides of which numerous waterfalls are precipitated, particularly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in spring, and is then carried by a series of tunnels, cuttings, and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}viaducts to the culminating point where it crosses the Apennines.{% endraw %}

{% raw %}In the Piazza Carignano , near the Piazza Castello, to the S.,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}rises the Palazzo Carignano (PL 36; F, 3), with its curious brick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ornamentation, erected by Guarini in 1680. The Sardinian Cham¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ber of Deputies met here from 1848 to 1860, and the Italian Par¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}liament from 1860 to 1865. The handsome facade at the back,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}towards the Piazza Carlo Alberto, was built in 1871 from the de¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}signs of Bollati and Ferri.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The rooms used by the parliament are now devoted to the Natural{% endraw %}
{% raw %}History Collections formerly in the Academy (open to the public every{% endraw %}
{% raw %}week-day 10-4, in winter 1-3). The collection is divided into the Zoolo¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gical and Comparative Anatomy Section and the Palaeontological, Geological,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and Mineralogical Section. The former contains a fine array of birds{% endraw %}
{% raw %}and insects, and a collection of the vertebrates of Italy arranged in a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}separate gallery. The palseontological division contains a fine collection{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of fossil mollusca from the tertiary formations, and the skeletons of a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}gigantic armadillo (Glyptodon Clavipes) from Rio de la Plata, a Tetra-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lophodon Avernensis, a, Megatherium Cuvieri, and other antediluvian animals.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the Piazza Carignano, in front of the palace , stands the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}finely-executed marble statue of the philosopher and patriot Gio-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}berti (PL 29), by Albertoni, erected in 1859.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The Piazza Carlo Alberto (E. side of the Palazzo Carignano){% endraw %}
{% raw %}is embellished with a bronze monument of King Charles Albert{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 27), designed by Marochetti, and cast in London. The pedestal{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands on four steps of Scottish granite; at the corners below{% endraw %}
{% raw %}are four colossal statues of Sardinian soldiers ; above them are four{% endraw %}
{% raw %}allegorical female figures, representing Martyrdom, Freedom,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Justice, and Independence. The Piazza Carlo Alberto is connected{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with the Piazza Castello by the Galleria Subalpina (p. 63).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the vicinity, at the corner of the Piazza Carignano and the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Via dell' Accademia No. 4, is the Palazzo dell' Accademia delle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Scienze (PL 3; E, 3), containing a picture-gallery and museums{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of natural history and antiquities. The building, formerly the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Jesuit College, was erected by Guarini in 1678. To the right on{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Ground-Floor are the Egyptian, Roman, and Greek sculp¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tures ; on the First Floor, the smaller antiquities; on the Second{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Floor (98 steps), the picture-gallery. These collections are open{% endraw %}
{% raw %}daily 9-4 (the Antiquities in winter 10-4), adm. 1 fr.; on Sun.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}11-3, gratis.{% endraw %}

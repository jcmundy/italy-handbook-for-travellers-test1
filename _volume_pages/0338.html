{% raw %}In the vicinity is the *TeatroOlimpico (PL 51; E, 3 ; fee 72fi\)>{% endraw %}
{% raw %}designed by Palladio, but not completed till 1584, after his death.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}It was inaugurated by the performance of the '(Edipus Tyrannus'{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Sophocles. Palladio is said to have adhered to the directions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}given by Vitruvius with regard to the construction of ancient{% endraw %}
{% raw %}theatres, but the result differs materially from what would have{% endraw %}
{% raw %}been anticipated. The perspective of the stage is very remark¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}able ; it is closed by a facade adorned with statues, through three{% endraw %}
{% raw %}doors in which a glimpse of the distant landscape is obtained.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The original orchestra in front of the stage is 5 ft. below the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}present wooden floor.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}We now return to the Corso and proceed by the first cross-street{% endraw %}
{% raw %}on the right to the church of S. Corona (PL 12; E, 3), a brick{% endraw %}
{% raw %}edifice with a plain Lombard facade. It contains Five Saints by{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Bart. Montagna (2nd altar to the left), a Madonna of the 14th cent.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}with angels by Fogolino (4th altar to the left), a *Baptism of Christ{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by G. Bellini (5th altar to the left; fine frame), and an Adoration of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the Magi by P. Veronese (5th altar to the right).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The street opposite leads to the church of S. Stefano (PL 29;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}D, 3), which contains, over the 3rd altar to the left, a large *Altar-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}piece by Palma Vecchio, the Madonna with SS. Lucia and George,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}an admirable example of the latest period of the master, among{% endraw %}
{% raw %}whose finest creations the two saints must be reckoned.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Opposite, at the corner to the left, stands the Palazzo Thiene{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 48; D, 3), the front of which was designed by Palladio, while{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the back part (Banca Popolare), facing the Via Porta, is an early-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Renaissance structure. On the other side of the way rises the Pa¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}lazzo Porto Barbarano (PL 34) , also by Palladio (1570), and a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}little farther on, to the right, is the Gothic Palazzo Porto-Colleoni{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(PL 45), with a handsome portico. We now retrace our steps to the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Corso and turn to the right into the Contrada S. Lorenzo, in which{% endraw %}
{% raw %}stands the Palazzo Valmarano (PL 49; D, 3), by Palladio. At the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}end of this street is the fine Gothic church of S. Lorenzo (PL 19 ;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}C, 3), containing (on the left) the tomb of Bart. Montagna(jp. 215),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by whom the altar-piece on the 3rd altar to the right, representing{% endraw %}
{% raw %}SS. Lorenzo and Vincenzo, was painted.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Near the end of the Corso, on the left, is the Palazzo Loschi,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}which contains a Bearing of the Cross by Giorgione. — On the way{% endraw %}
{% raw %}back to the station we observe on the right the Romanesque tower{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of the old church of SS. Felice e Fortunato.{% endraw %}

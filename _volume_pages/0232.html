{% raw %}Cathedral. MILAN. 20. Route. 131{% endraw %}
{% raw %}52 pillars, each 12 ft. in diameter, the summits of which are ad¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}orned with canopied niches with statues instead of capitals. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pavement consists of mosaic in marble of different colours. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}vaulting is skilfully painted in imitation of perforated stone-work.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Interior. By the principal inner portal are two huge monolith col¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}umns of granite from the quarries of Baveno (see p. 29). The band of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brass in the pavement close to the entrance indicates the line of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}meridian. Right Aisle : Sarcophagus of Archbishop Aribert (1018-1045),{% endraw %}
{% raw %}above which is a gilded crucifix of the 11th century. Monument of Otto{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Visconti (d. 1295) and Johannes Visconti (d. 1354), both archbishops of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Milan. Gothic monument of Marco Carelli (d. 1394). Tomb of Canon{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Yimercati, by Bambaja. Right Transept (W. wall): Monument of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}brothers Giacomo and Gabriele de' Medici, erected by their brother{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Pope Pius IV. (1564), the three bronze statues by Leone Leoni (Aretino).{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Tickets for the roof (25c, see below) are obtained near this monument;{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the staircase leading to the dome is in the corner of the side-wall. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}altar of the Offering of Mary (E. wall of S. transept) is adorned with fine{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Reliefs by Bambaja, with a relief of the nativity of the Virgin by Tan-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}tar dini at the foot. Adjacent is the Statue of St. Bartholomew by Marco{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Agrate (end of 16th cent.), anatomically remarkable, as the saint is{% endraw %}
{% raw %}represented flayed, with his skin on his shoulder, and bearing the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}modest inscription 'non me Praxiteles sed Marcus finxit Agrafes'.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}The door of the S. Sacristy (to the right, in the choir) is remarkable{% endraw %}
{% raw %}for its richly sculptured Gothic decorations. The ""Treasury here (adm.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}1 fr.) contains silver statues and candelabra of the 17th cent.; the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}enamelled Evangelium of Abp. Aribert; a diptych of the 6th cent.; book-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}covers adorned with Italian and Byzantine carving of the early middle{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ages; ivory vessel belonging to Bishop Godfrey; a golden Pax; and lastly{% endraw %}
{% raw %}a statue of Christ by Cristofano Solari.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the ambulatory, a little farther on, is a sitting figure of Martin V.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}by Jacopino da Tractate (1421). Then the black marble Monument of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Cardinal Marino Carracciolo (d. 1538), by whom Emp. Charles V. was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}crowned at Aix-la-Chapelle in 1520, by Bambaja. The fourth of the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}handsome new Gothic confessionals is for the German, French, and English{% endraw %}
{% raw %}languages. The stained glass in the three vast choir windows, comprising{% endraw %}
{% raw %}350 representations of scriptural subjects, were executed by Alois and{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Giov. Bertini of Guastalla during the present century; most of them are{% endraw %}
{% raw %}copies from old pictures. Before the N. Sacristy is reached, the Statue{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of Pius IV. is seen above, in a sitting posture, by Angelo Siciliano. The{% endraw %}
{% raw %}door of this sacristy is also adorned with fine sculptures in marble.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In the centre of the N. Transept is a valuable bronze ^Candelabrum,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}in the form of a tree, executed in the 13th cent., and decorated with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}jewels, presented by Giov. Batt. Trivulzio, in 1562.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Left Aisle: Altar-piece, painted in 1600 by Fed. Baroccio, represent¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}ing S. Ambrogio releasing Emp. Theodosius from ecclesiastical penalties.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Upon the adjoining altar of St. Joseph, the Nuptials of Mary, by F. Zuc-{% endraw %}
{% raw %}chero. The following chapel contains the old wooden Crucifix which S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Carlo Borromeo bore in 1576, when engaged, barefooted, in his missions{% endraw %}
{% raw %}of mercy during the plague. Adjacent, the Monument of Abp. Arcimboldi{% endraw %}
{% raw %}(ca. 1550), and by the wall the statues of eight Apostles (13th cent.). Not{% endraw %}
{% raw %}far from the N. side door is the Font, consisting of a sarcophagus of S.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Dionysius, but appropriated to its present use by S. Carlo Borromeo.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In front of the choir, below the dome, is the subterranean Cappella{% endraw %}
{% raw %}S. Carlo Borromeo (p. 167), with the tomb of the saint; entrance opposite{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the doors to the sacristy, to the N. and S. of the choir (open in summer{% endraw %}
{% raw %}5-10, in winter 7-10 a.m.; at other times lfr.; for showing the relics of{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the saint 5fr.).{% endraw %}

{% raw %}The Political History of these districts during the middle ages re¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}cords continual struggles for precedence among several rival powers. As{% endraw %}
{% raw %}long as the power of the emperors was in the ascendant, they kept the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}pretensions of the popes in check. During the exile of the popes at{% endraw %}
{% raw %}Avignon, the dismemberment of the papal dominions seemed imminent,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}but after protracted combats was prevented by Cardinal d'Albornoz, a{% endraw %}
{% raw %}valiant Spaniard, who was sent to Italy by Innocent IV. in 1353. Even{% endraw %}
{% raw %}those princes, however, who consented to acknowledge the papal supre¬{% endraw %}
{% raw %}macy, still continued practically independent. Alexander VI,, who was{% endraw %}
{% raw %}elected pope in 1492, and his son Gesare Borgia at length put an end to{% endraw %}
{% raw %}this insubordination; they extirpated the dynasties of the Romagna with{% endraw %}
{% raw %}fire and sword, and from that period the papal fiefs began to be gradually{% endraw %}
{% raw %}converted into a state in the modern sense. Under Julius II. and Leo X.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}the papal supremacy was farther extended to Modena, Parma, and Piacenza.{% endraw %}
{% raw %}In 1545 Paul III. Farnese invested Pier Luigi, his natural son, with the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}two last as a duchy, which, on the extinction of the Farnese in 1731, came{% endraw %}
{% raw %}into the possession of the Spanish Bourbons. In Modena and Reggio, the{% endraw %}
{% raw %}house of Este maintained its supremacy in spite of the papal pretensions,{% endraw %}
{% raw %}while Ferrara in 1597 was incorporated with the states of the church.{% endraw %}
